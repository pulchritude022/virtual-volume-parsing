## Notes & context
★★ **The Inch planting turns the corner.** After weeks of pressure (imgs 191–198), **the Inch parish finally compears, undertakes to meet the Synod's committee at Wigton, and "promises to give all satisfaction … that nothing might be wanting for the encouragement of their minister to stay among them."** Meanwhile the material machinery moves: **the Sheriff has undertaken to put Mr John Dick in possession of the glebe "by an instrument,"** with the presbytery threatening to "use diligence in law" if he stalls. A vacancy dispute visibly resolving.

★★ **The war behind the poor-roll.** A quietly poignant administrative note: the presbytery's charity fund is **"the money belonging to the widows and orphans of the soldiers who died in the public service"** — the human cost of the 1648–50 wars recorded as a line of parish accounting. Coupled with the drive for **constables to restrain the "country-poor" from wandering and to "dismiss the strange-poor,"** it sketches a society straining under displacement.

★ **Loose ends.** **Arisemman (Andrew Kennedy) is "seriously sick"** — his case stalled; **Margaret McLourie** evades citation "from house to house" and is to be summoned from the pulpit; **George Adestoun has returned from Ireland** (img 196) and is to be cited; the **robbery process** (Thomas Baillie) still cannot be extracted from the Committee of War. Also: **Mr John Dick preached on Galatians 6:7** ("Be not deceived; God is not mocked: for whatsoever a man soweth, that shall he also reap") — an apt text for a presbytery policing penitence.

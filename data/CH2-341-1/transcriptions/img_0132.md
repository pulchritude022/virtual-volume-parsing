# CH2/341/1 — Image 132 (folios 131v–132)

Left page (foliated **131a** = folio 131 verso) continues the **26 April 1648**
sitting. It completes **Ardwell's supplication** (he asks to be shown the process
and the acts of the Provincial Synod — including one **holden at Wigtown, 19
October 1647** — before answering, promising to answer the next presbytery day),
and records the presbytery's reply: the clerk is to **draw up the whole process
since the October Synod** and present it, Ardwell to be summoned again. Then: a
report on the **James McMillan paternity matter** (Mr [Blair] spoke with [Mr
Gomrie], a day to be set by Mr John Scott to bring McMillan to compear); and ★ a
**petition from Irish parishioners (of "the Maghis") to transport Mr John Dick to
Ireland**, presented by **John [Kennedy?] of [Barsell?]** with a supporting letter
from the presbytery in Ireland, a roll, and a commission — which the presbytery
takes to consideration and defers (all parties to be heard). The right page (folio
132) closes the 26 April sitting (letters to be written to the **Earl of
Cassillis** and the **laird of Garthland** about Dick; a **Sabbath-night scandal**
by **John [McCulloch?] of [Ardloch?]** — sackcloth penance in Kirkmaiden then
[Clanrie?]; preaching appointments) and then opens the **sitting at Inch, 17 May
1648** (absents; **Mr John Dick's exercise** approved; the paternity witnesses
still failing to compear).

## Notes & context
- ★★ **Petition to transport Mr John Dick to Ireland.** Alongside the Livingstone-
  to-Ancrum call (img 130), a second minister of this presbytery is sought
  elsewhere — here **an Ulster parish ("the Maghis")** petitions, through a
  commissioner **John [Kennedy?] of [Barsell?]**, backed by a letter from **the
  presbytery in Ireland**, a parish roll, and a formal commission, to have John
  Dick transported to Ireland. The presbytery defers pending a hearing of all
  parties, and the Moderator is to write to the **Earl of Cassillis** and the
  **laird of Garthland** about it. A vivid record of the two-way traffic of
  Presbyterian ministers across the North Channel in 1648.
- ★ **Ardwell plays for time (concluded).** His supplication asks to see the full
  process and the Synod acts — including one **holden at Wigtown, 19 October 1647**
  — before answering. The presbytery orders the clerk to draw up the whole process
  since the October Synod. The case continues to grind without resolution.
- ★ **Sabbath-night scandal — John [McCulloch?] of [Ardloch?].** Referred up by a
  kirk session for "scandalous carriage" in striving on a Sabbath night to [free]
  a young woman (Jonet [Corsan?]); confesses; ordered **sackcloth penance in
  Kirkmaiden and then [Clanrie?]**. A discipline case distinct from the Ardwell
  adultery matter (note the recurrence of the McCulloch surname among Rhins
  gentry).
- **Mr John Dick's exercise** approved at Inch, 17 May 1648 (text ~[—] 9:20) —
  consistent with his active status while both his Scottish glebe and the Irish
  call are pending.
- **Paternity case:** the McMillan matter is worked through intermediaries (Mr
  [Gomrie], Mr John Scott) to get James McMillan to compear; the witness woman
  again fails to appear at Inch.

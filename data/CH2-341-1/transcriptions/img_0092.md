# CH2/341/1 — Image 92 (folios 91v–92), 15 April 1646 — a cross-channel bigamy; Glenluce referred to the Synod; the kirk waits for Ireland

Left page (folio **91a**): the **Glenluce parishioners petition yet again for "a qualified minister to
serve the cure"** — the presbytery **refers the point to the Synod** and has **Mr James Blair write [to
a party] that he may be present at the Synod** about the Glenluce charge; **John McDowell and Effie
Adair have begun to give signs of repentance**; the **ordinance to repair the kirk of Stoneykirk is
renewed "till the laird of Garthland and Mr John Livingstone return out of Ireland"**; **Mr Alexander
Turnbull to preach at Stranraer the first Sunday** (supplying Livingstone's vacant pulpit); and every
brother to have a **collection ready at the Synod.** Right page (folio **92**): **★ Margaret Cobland
compeared and confessed a bigamy** — married some years to **David McWatters in Stoneykirk**, she left
him and went to Ireland, where in **Donegal** she fell in adultery with **James Fullerton, an
Englishman**, and then (after her first marriage) married **John Couper**, with whom she has lived
**fourteen years**; the presbytery **orders them to separate and Margaret to satisfy at the parish kirk
of Inch**; **a woman could not travel to the presbytery, being in childbed**; **John McFee to be
summoned from the pulpit in Stranraer**; a **privy trial (all approved)**; and the next meeting at the
Synod (**Hutcheson, clerk**).

## Notes & context
- **★★ A cross-channel bigamy — the life of Margaret Cobland.** A vivid human story compressed into one
  minute: **married to David McWatters in Stoneykirk, Margaret Cobland left him, crossed to Ireland,
  fell in adultery with an Englishman (James Fullerton) in Donegal, and then married John Couper, with
  whom she lived fourteen years** — a bigamy the presbytery now unwinds, ordering **separation and her
  public satisfaction at Inch.** It captures the **traffic of ordinary people back and forth across the
  North Channel** (Galloway ↔ Donegal), the fragility of marriage in a mobile, war-torn world, and the
  kirk's reach over the marital past. A rich genealogical/social node — names **Margaret Cobland, David
  McWatters, James Fullerton, John Couper** (all uncertain; handle with care) — and a strong candidate
  set-piece for the wiki.
- **The presbytery waits on Ireland.** Two entries confirm and turn on the **Garthland–Livingstone
  Irish absence** (img 0091): the **Stoneykirk repair is deferred "till they return out of Ireland,"**
  and **Turnbull supplies Livingstone's Stranraer pulpit.** The parish machinery visibly idles while
  its leaders are away with Argyll.
- **Glenluce escalated to the Synod.** The perennial **Glenluce vacancy** (parishioners "desiring a
  qualified minister to serve the cure") is now **referred up to the Synod** — the local court unable to
  break the finance deadlock (imgs 0084–0091).
- **Everyday texture.** A woman **excused "in respect she was in childbed"**; adultery penitents
  (McDowell, Adair) beginning their penance; a pulpit summons for John McFee — the ordinary human grain
  of the record.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `maryed… to one David McWatters… fell in adulterie… maryed John
  Couper… ordained to separate` = a bigamy case resolved by enforced separation and penance; `in the
  countrie of Donnegall` = Co. Donegal, Ulster; `ane Englishman` = an English settler in Ireland; `in
  childbed` = in confinement after childbirth (a valid excuse); `summond fra pulpite` = cited by
  proclamation; `severally tryed… all approven` = the privy trial passed. **Left page stained** —
  provisional; right page fairly legible. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1646.

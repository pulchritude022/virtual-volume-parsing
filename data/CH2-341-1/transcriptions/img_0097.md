# CH2/341/1 — Image 97 (folios 96v–97), 15–29 July 1646 — Thomas Adair's signed £100 bond; a son's sworn deposition against his father; the Kirkmaiden visitation begins

Left page (folio **96a**): **Mr Alexander Turnbull to write to the presbytery in Ireland that [the
fugitive] may be sent back again to satisfy**; **★★ Thomas Adair in Glenluce, advised and confessing the
guilt of fornication with a woman with whom he had committed fornication for the space of [some] years,
is ordained to give signs of repentance in sackcloth at the kirks of Glenluce and Kirkmaiden under pain
of excommunication; and, further, ordained to abstain from the company of Margaret [—] in all suspect
places — which to do, the said Thomas Adair binds and obliges himself, under penalty of failing, to pay
one hundred pounds money, "as witness his hand," [signed] Thomas Adair**; **Mr Hew Hutcheson reports he
preached at Glenluce**; **Mr James Bell to preach at Glenluce 19 July**; and **★ Margaret Mc[Cristine?]
in Glenluce appeared and confessed she fell in adultery with Thomas [Morton?] some three-quarters of a
year since, and is enjoined per order to begin her repentance in sackcloth at Glenluce**. Right page
(folio **97**): **★★ James Torrane younger, being taken sworn on the matter of his father's suspected
adultery, deposed: (1) that one night he came to his father's [door] and found it locked, and his father
and Margaret [—] being within, [they] answered both at once; (2) that he saw them at another time coming
both from the bed together; (3) that on another night the said woman came out of the chamber from his
father**; **the brethren reported the fast was kept**; **the names of widows, orphans and adulterers to
be given in the next presbytery**; **★ the brethren [published] the [excommunication] of the Earl of
Seaforth**; **★ the collection for the succour of Argyll to be given in the next presbytery day**; **John
Vans[?] for his disobedience to the Inch session to be summoned to the next presbytery**; **the 29 July
appointed for the visitation of Kirkmaiden, and the 30 July for Portmontgomery**; then the record opens
**★ the visitation at Kirkmaiden, 29 July 1646** — Mr Alexander Turnbull preached on his ordinary text
(1 Cor. 15:19–20), approved; absent Mr James Blair (continued on the next opening).

## Notes & context
- **★★ A signed bond of caution — Thomas Adair's £100 obligation.** A remarkable document embedded in
  the minutes: **Thomas Adair of Glenluce**, confessing fornication, not only submits to public penance
  in sackcloth at two kirks but **subscribes a formal bond obliging himself, under a £100 (Scots)
  penalty, to abstain from the company of Margaret [—] "in all suspect places," with his own signature
  ("Sic subscribitur, Thomas Adair").** A rare survival of a signed lay caution/bond in a kirk record —
  excellent for the wiki (the mechanism of the "bond of caution" against relapse; a named person with an
  autograph). Note the tension with img 0093, where a Thomas Adair's partner is named **Marion** (his
  servant) — confirm whether one man/two partners, or two men. (Confirm the woman's surname.)
- **★★ A son's sworn deposition against his father.** In the **Torrane adultery** (imgs 0094–0095),
  **James Torrane younger is taken sworn and testifies in three specific articles against his own
  father** — the locked chamber door, the pair emerging from bed together, the woman leaving the chamber.
  A striking, harrowing piece of intra-family testimony and of kirk evidence-procedure (numbered articles
  of deposition). A strong set-piece; the alleged partner is a **Margaret [—]** (surname stained). A
  compelling, if painful, wiki node.
- **★ The Earl of Seaforth's excommunication published; the Argyll collection.** The **intimation of
  Seaforth's excommunication** (ordered img 0096) is **reported as done**, and **the collection for "the
  succour of Argyll" is to be handed in** — the two national threads of img 0096 both progressing. Plus
  the **register of widows, orphans and adulterers** being compiled.
- **★ Two visitations appointed — Kirkmaiden (29 July) and Portmontgomery (30 July).** The presbytery
  moves into a **round of parish visitations**, opening with **Kirkmaiden on 29 July 1646** (Turnbull
  preaching 1 Cor. 15:19–20). Visitations are rich sources (they inventory the state of a parish — kirk
  fabric, manse, glebe, school, discipline, the minister's and elders' conduct) — watch the next
  openings for the Kirkmaiden and Portmontgomery visitation reports.
- **A second Glenluce confession.** **Margaret Mc[Cristine?]** confesses adultery with **Thomas
  [Morton?]** "three-quarters of a year since," enjoined to public penance — the ordinary run of Glenluce
  discipline alongside the Adair bond.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `binds and obliges himself, under paine of failzie, to pay ane
  hundreth lib money. Sic Subscribitur, Thomas Adair` = a signed penal bond (£100 Scots) of abstention;
  `abstaine fra ye company of… in all suspect places` = the standard non-association clause; `being taken
  sworne, Deponed: 1… 2… 3…` = a witness's sworn deposition in numbered articles; `intimat ye
  excommunicatioun of ye Earle of Seafort` = the published sentence against the royalist earl;
  `contributioun for ye succor of Argyle` = a war-relief collection; `visitatioun` = a formal parish
  inspection. **Both pages faded, the right stained at the outer margin** — provisional. Capital **W** for
  *w/wh*. Year **1646**.

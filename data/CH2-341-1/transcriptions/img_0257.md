# CH2/341/1 — Image 257 (folios 256a–257)

## Folio 256a (left page)  *(confidence: low)*

*The volume's penultimate folio, under the **14 April 1652** sitting. The brethren report they have **read the presbytery's warning "against the snare of their times" from the pulpits, as recommended, and will do it "with all diligence."** **Turnbull and Crookes** report they had **no occasion to meet with the Laird of Garthland since the last meeting** and are ordained to confer with him again before the next; the process continues under the Synod's ordinance. **Cristian Matronnall**, of the parish of Glenluce, who before her session confessed adultery with **Andro Murray in Balnegapill** — he a married man, she unmarried — compears, acknowledges the sin, and is remitted to the session of new Glenluce. Then the **protestation dispute resumes: the presbytery wishes the Protesters' paper "considered but not inserted in the register till after the Synod," but the brethren (Turnbull, Park, Lawrie) refuse to delay and refuse to remove** — the Synod being "about the transacting of differences amongst brethren." Faded, browner up top.*

## Folio 257 (right page — final folio of the volume)  *(confidence: low)*

Subscribed **"M[r Ja:] Blair, clerk to the presbytery,"** and revised by the Synod clerk: **"June 8, 1652. This book revised & examined [by] Ro: Ferguson, Cl[erk of] Sy[nod]."** The volume ends here.*

## Notes & context
★★★ **The volume ends on the Resolutioner–Protester dispute — and on the Synod clerk's signature.** A fitting close to CH2/341/1. The final folios are dominated by the struggle over **Mr Alexr Turnbull's protestation** against the presbytery's approval of the late General Assembly (imgs 247–256). The presbytery, "unwillingly," agrees to **defer inserting the Protesters' paper (and its own answer) in the register until after the Synod;** and Turnbull, backed by **Park and Lawrie**, makes his last stand — demanding "the whole relation of their business should be inserted in the register, **or all buried**," and **protesting against the delay.** The book then closes in the ordinary way, subscribed by the clerk **Mr James Blair**, and — the volume's terminal act — **revised and examined by the Synod clerk Robert Ferguson on 8 June 1652** (the same Ro. Ferguson, Cl. Synodi, whose revision note appears at img 242). The register that opened years earlier ends amid the great schism of the Kirk, still unresolved, referred upward to the Synod.

★ **The last discipline entries.** Even at the end, the machinery grinds: the presbytery confirms it has **read its anti-union warning from the pulpits "with all diligence"** (imgs 254–255); the **Laird of Garthland** is (once more) to be conferred with (Turnbull and Crookes had "no occasion" to meet him); and a final adultery case is disposed of — **Cristian Matronnall**, who confessed adultery with the married **Andro Murray in Balnegapill**, acknowledges her sin and is remitted to her session at new Glenluce. (The surname "Matronnall" recurs — cf. Isobell Matronnall, imgs 243–249.)

★ **Corpus complete.** This is the final opening (folios 256a–257) of the ~257-image volume; the transcription run for CH2/341/1 ends here.

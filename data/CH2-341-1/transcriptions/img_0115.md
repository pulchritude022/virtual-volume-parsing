## Notes & context
- **★★ New Luce gets its minister — Crichton admitted.** The climax of the whole Glenluce arc (imgs
  0101–0114): **Mr John Crichton is admitted to the ministry of the New kirk of Glenluce**, "according to
  the order of the Kirk of Scotland," the edict read at the kirk, he having preached and been examined and
  produced. After the June-1646 GA act dividing Glenluce into Old kirk + New kirk at Balneil (img 0106),
  the heritors' resistance and default (imgs 0107–0111), the forced funding through the civil courts, and
  Crichton's full course of trials (exercise, common head, popular sermon, examination in theology and the
  tongues — imgs 0108–0113), **the new parish of New Luce is now planted with its first settled minister.**
  A key wiki node.
- **★ The McColby women return from Ireland.** The bed-scandal thread that opened with **Agnes McColbie**
  (pregnant, fled to Ireland, deferred — img 0094) now resurfaces: **Elizabeth and Anna Mc Colby, "returned
  out of Ireland," appear and are required to satisfy for their adulterous and scandalous carriage**, in a
  tangled case that seems to involve **bedding within a forbidden degree** (a "sister['s son]" reading) —
  which would make it an **incest case**, exactly the sin the 5 May act targeted (img 0112). The names and
  kinship are badly faded; a priority for the reviewer. (Note the spelling drift McColbie → Mc Colby.)
- **★ The Ardwell slander names the Lady Freugh.** The defamation matter from img 0113 sharpens: **Mr [—] of
  Ardwell has given in a bill of complaint of slander against the Lady Freugh (wife of the Laird of Freugh),
  charging her as the slanderer of him**, and refuses to purge himself until his bill is heard. A **report
  from Ireland** is bound up in it, and "she" (the woman at the centre) is hoped to be sent over; the
  presbytery **suspends all proceeding against the Lady Freugh until the author of the scandal is cleared**
  — a rare glimpse of a gentry-level reputation quarrel spilling into the church court, tangled with the
  cross-channel traffic to Ulster.
- **Routine strands.** The **fugitives summoned from every pulpit** (Adair + James Mc[—], img 0114) reported
  done; the standing **act against concealers of adultery**; the **exercise** (Livingstone/Hutcheson, 1 Cor.
  3:3–4); the perennial **war-widows/orphans list** (imgs 0096, 0112, 0114); and continued
  **correspondence with the presbytery in Ireland**.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `admitted to ye ministrie of ye New kirk of Glenluce, according to ye
  order of ye kirk of Scotland… ye edict before read` = the formal induction/admission of a minister;
  `returned out of Ireland… required to satisfie for all their adulterous and scandalous carriage` =
  citation of returnees to answer discipline; `bill of complaint of slander against ye Lady Freugh…
  suspendit till ye author of yt scandal be cleared` = a defamation cause held over; `made… added [the
  exercise]… and Was approved` = the weekly exercise/expound routine. **Both pages heavily faded; the right
  torn on the outer margin** — names and figures provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1647**.

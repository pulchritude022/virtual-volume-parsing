# CH2/341/1 — Image 102 (folios 101v–102), 16 September 1646 — a Commission letter to excommunicate Montrose's royalists; a formal call to Mr John Crichton to plant Glenluce

Left page (folio **101a**): (16 Sept, continued) absent Mr Alexander Turnbull and the elders of Kirkmaiden,
Leswalt, Portmontgomery and Inch; **★ Mr John Crichton preached on the ordinary of the exercise (1 Thess.
2:7–9) and was approved** [his trial sermon as candidate]; the Leswalt elder's absence excused; then **★★
a letter from the Commission of the Kirk** was presented, requiring: **(1) the keeping of a fast; (2) the
intimation of the excommunication — of the Earl of Seaforth, and of a list of malignants**: [the Earl of]
Airlie [Ogilvy], David Graham [sometime of Fintry?], Patrick Graham son to the laird of Inchbrakie,
[Donald] Robertson sometime tutor of Struan, John Stewart sometime of Ballechin [and another] Stewart,
[Lord] Napier [and others] — **(3) a contribution for the distressed brethren of Argyll**. The presbytery
then answers the particulars point by point: **(1) the fast has been kept already/formerly; (2) Seaforth's
excommunication has been intimated already, and the rest is to be intimated next Sabbath; (3) there are
none within their bounds guilty in the rebellion; (4) the contribution for the distressed of Argyll is
gathered, all but one [parish/brother]; (5) two brethren's labour is given to Mr [John] Lindsay, and the
rest as [directed]**. Right page (folio **102**): a set of **"Remember" reminders** (Turnbull to write of
[charging] Marion Mure; John Vans has satisfied; the Justices [in their charges]; Thomas Morton; Agnes
McColbie; Margaret Mc[Cristine?]; to make inquiry for Marion [—]; **James Torrane's matter delayed till
further trial**); then **★ the brethren appointed to settle Glenluce reported progress toward agreement,
and a further commission (the laird of [Ardwell], Mr James Blair and Mr John Livingstone) is appointed to
settle fully with James Ross about the mortification**; **★★ the which day James Ross presented a call to
Mr John Crichton to be minister of Glenluce, subscribed by the lairds and parishioners; the presbytery
desired Mr John Crichton to consider the call; he desired space to advise; and [the presbytery] delayed
[his] answer**; and **★ Alexander McDowell in Stoneykirk compeared before the presbytery in sackcloth**
[continued on the next opening].

## Notes & context
- **★★ The 1646 mass excommunication of the royalists reaches Galloway.** The **Commission of the Kirk**
  orders the Stranraer presbytery to intimate the excommunication not only of **the Earl of Seaforth**
  (imgs 0096–0097) but of a roll of the leading **Montrose/royalist "malignants"** — recognisably **the
  Earl of Airlie (Ogilvy), David Graham [of Fintry?], Patrick Graham younger of Inchbrakie ("Black Pate",
  Montrose's cousin), [Donald] Robertson tutor of Struan, John Stewart of Ballechin, and Lord Napier**
  (Montrose's brother-in-law). This is the Kirk's post-Philiphaugh spiritual purge of the royalist
  leadership being published from Galloway pulpits — a vivid, datable national-history node. The names are
  faint/uncertain (please confirm against the printed Acts of the 1646 General Assembly).
- **★ The presbytery's dry replies.** The point-by-point answers are revealing: the fast **already kept**,
  Seaforth **already intimated** (the rest next Sabbath), **"none within their bounds guilty in the
  rebellion"** (the Rhins had no active royalists), and the **Argyll contribution gathered "all but one."**
  A snapshot of a loyal Covenanter presbytery reporting compliance up the chain.
- **★★ The Glenluce vacancy resolves — a call to Mr John Crichton.** The long saga (imgs 0071, 0084–0101)
  reaches its climax: **James Ross of Barneill himself presents a formal call to Mr John Crichton to be
  minister of Glenluce, subscribed by the lairds and parishioners**, and the presbytery arranges to
  **settle the mortification** with Ross at last. Crichton (whose trial sermon is approved on the facing
  page) **asks time to advise** before accepting. After years of the money-and-patronage deadlock, the
  planting is finally in motion. A pivotal moment and a firm new name — **confirm "Crichton."**
- **★ A Stoneykirk penitent — Alexander McDowell.** **Alexander McDowell in Stoneykirk** does public
  sackcloth penance before the presbytery (note the McDowell surname, ubiquitous in the Rhins; not
  necessarily kin to McDowell of French/Garthland). Continued overleaf.
- **The housekeeping list.** The **"Remember" bullets** neatly index the live discipline caseload (Marion
  Mure, John Vans, Thomas Morton, Agnes McColbie, Margaret McCristine, James Torrane) — a clerk's docket
  of unfinished business.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `a letter… fra ye Commissioun of ye kirk… for keeping a fast… for
  intimatioun of the excommunicat` = the standing Commission of the General Assembly directing the
  presbyteries; `there are none Within their bounds guiltie in ye rebellioun` = no local royalists to
  proceed against; `presented a call to Mr John Crichton to be minister… subscribed by ye lairds and
  parochiners` = a formal parish call to a named minister; `Desired space to advyse` = asked time to
  consider (the standard response to a call); `compeired… in sackcloth` = public penance. **Both pages
  heavily water-stained** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1646**.

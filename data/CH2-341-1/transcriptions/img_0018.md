# CH2/341/1 — Image 18 (folios 17v–18), March 1643

Left page (folio **17a**) is the **Glenluce new-church business**: a sederunt at
Glenluce (15 March), rival **protests** over whether siting a church means dividing
the parish, and the presbytery — having ridden the moors of Luce — siting the church
at **Balneil, where the Luce and Crosswater meet** (with a bridge). Right page
(folio **18**): a fallback site (**Cairns of Barnshangan**); then, at the **25
March** sederunt, the presbytery **keeps Mr John Livingston from going to Ireland
with General Major Monro**; and at **30 March**, Power supplicates yet again as his
process nears "a whole year."

### Modern English (paraphrase)
[James] Ross gave in a suit about building, in the parish of Glenluce, for a new
erection; the presbytery appointed **15 March** to that effect. **Presbytery held at
Glenluce, Tuesday 15 March 1643**; Mr John Man preached, approved. **Mr George Dick
and Gillespie**, for themselves and adhering parishioners, **protest** that siting a
new church should imply no division of the parish — it should remain one entire
eldership, the ministers sharing the charge. **James Ross of Balneil protests to the
contrary**, since the Act of Parliament provides that large parishes be divided.
Having ridden to the **moors of Luce** and gathered information, the presbytery
declares the **house of Balneil, at the meeting of the Luce and the Crosswater**, to
be the place for the church — provided the petitioners give sufficient security, by
30 March, for a **bridge over the Crosswater**.

## Notes & context
- **John Livingston and Monro's Ulster army — a firm historical anchor.** The
  presbytery, on the **Council of Scotland's** request, weighs whether **Mr John
  Livingston** should **go to Ireland with General Major Monro**, and decides to
  **keep him at Stranraer**. This matches the historical **John Livingston (1603–
  1672), minister of Stranraer 1638–1648**, and **Major-General Robert Monro**, who
  commanded the Scottish Covenanter army in Ulster from 1642. It confirms the
  clerk's identity and dates the Ireland business to spring 1643. Prime material for
  Livingston's biographical page.
- **Glenluce's new church, sited.** The presbytery fixes the site at **Balneil,
  where the Luce and Crosswater meet** (with a required bridge), and names a
  fallback at the **Cairns of Barnshangan near Knockbrex** — concrete parish
  geography. The **protest** by **Mr George Dick & Gillespie** (keep one eldership)
  vs. **James Ross of Balneil** (divide, per Act of Parliament) shows the local
  politics of parish subdivision under the 1640s teind/erection legislation.
- **Power's process drags.** A year on, Power renews his supplication demanding an
  answer and the closing of the process — the case is grinding toward resolution.
- **New people/places:** **General Major Monro**, **Mr John Man**, **Gillespie**,
  **James Ross of Balneil**, **Andrew Agnew of Killumpha**, **William Kennedy of
  Gillespie[?]**, **Charles Turnbull**; places **Balneil**, **Crosswater**, **moors
  of Luce**, **Cairns of Barnshangan**, **Knockbrex**, **Killumpha**.
- **Palaeography applied:** hasty hand → low confidence; `liberat ... fra going` =

  = carried on/prosecuted; `remeids` = remedies. Dates "1642" are Old Style (= 1643).
  `y`+superscript → *th-*.

### Modern English
**The presbytery, having signified to the Laird of Garthland that he was appointed by the last General Assembly to complete [his process] before the Commission of the Kirk — and he being desired to give satisfaction for his sin in accession to the unlawful Engagement — and now finding that there is loss and trouble to the place of their meeting, and having (through other impediment) supplicated that the presbytery would join in speaking with the [board], to send to the Commission, that he might witness his willingness to appear and know what further is to be done concerning him** — the presbytery appoints **Turnbull and Lawrie to write accordingly.** Next meeting at Stranraer this day twenty days.

**The presbytery *pro re nata*, 7 November.** All the brethren present except Turnbull, Dick, and Bell; the ruling elders absent except [that] of Stoneykirk; convened at the old kirk of Glenluce. **This meeting being occasioned by a letter sent from the brethren that were with the associate forces, requiring some of our number to bide still along, working with them — the letter was read and considered.** **The presbytery appoints our brother Mr John Crosse to repair to the associate forces … and to remain with them** …

## Notes & context
★★★ **Ministers rotating as chaplains to the Western Association army.** The *pro re nata* sitting of **7 November 1650** is called by **"a letter sent from the brethren that were with the associate forces, requiring some of our number to bide still along, working with them"** — and the presbytery details **Mr John Crosse to repair to the "associate forces"** (the **Western Association army**, raised in the south-west after Dunbar). This is the Rhins clergy staffing the chaplaincy of the Western Association host — the force behind the **Western Remonstrance** of October 1650 — a vivid parish-level trace of that movement (cf. John Scott's chaplaincy, img 204).

★★ **Gilbert Kennedy flees to Stirling; Garthland edges toward satisfaction.** The scandal-ridden **Gilbert Kennedy of Aviolen** (imgs 203–212), having "slipped off to be a quarter-master" (img 211), is now reported **"gone to Stirling"** — where the beaten Covenanter army had regrouped after Dunbar — and the presbytery writes there that he be made to clear himself. Meanwhile the **Laird of Garthland** (the leading local Engager, imgs 180–195) moves to comply: appointed by the last GA to complete his process before the **Commission of the Kirk**, he supplicates the presbytery to write on his behalf "witnessing his willingness to appear."

★ **Discipline under the war.** The evasive **Margaret McClounie** (fornication) still cannot be found; **Arisemman (Andrew Kennedy)** is recovering, his paternity co-defendant **Marioun** held over till he can attend (cf. img 189); **George Adestoun** cited a third time; **William Baillie** willingly confesses his Engagement guilt and names "the persuaders."

- **Lowest-confidence readings (Dad):**
  - **Both folios are faint**, the left especially. The **"associate forces" / Mr John Crosse chaplaincy** and **"Gilbert Kennedy gone to Stirling"** are the priority readings and are moderately secure; much connective wording is reconstructed.
  - The "Deanie parish disorderly persons" clause (foot of 212a) is largely conjectural.
  - Garthland's supplication wording (the "board" / Commission) is partly reconstructed.
  - "Marioun" = the Marion of the 1650 paternity case (img 189), held to Arisemman's recovery.

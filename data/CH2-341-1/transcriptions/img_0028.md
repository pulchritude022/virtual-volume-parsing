# CH2/341/1 — Image 28 (folios 27v–28), April 1643 — presbytery's answers, reasons 4–9

The presbytery's **rebuttal of Power's appeal continues** — the mirror of imgs
0023–0025. Left page (folio **27a**) finishes reason 3 and answers **reasons 4, 5,
6**; right page (folio **28**) answers **reasons 7, 8, 9**. Highlights: the court
insists it admitted **no woman, beggar, distracted person, or the pursuer's tenant**
as witness (answering 4); denies any **malice or undue delay**, blaming Power's own
tactic of objecting to enrolled witnesses (answering 6); defends examining witnesses
on the **defendant's own interrogatories** (answering 8); and dismisses reason 9 with
a Latin flourish — it were "superfluous **cramben**" (reheated cabbage) "**toties…
denuo deponere**" (to depose the same over and over).

### Modern English
[Answer to reason 3, concl.:] …until Power condescends upon particulars; and though
divers sorts of persons were summoned, the presbytery was "so circumspect and tender"
that **neither woman, beggar, distracted (deranged) person, nor the pursuer's own
tenant was admitted as a witness.**

**To reason 4:** it is too general — "we know not whom he means"; let those it
concerned answer for themselves; and "we believe no minister would be so rash" to do
such a thing **without some order of session or presbytery.**

**To reason 5:** both the equity of Power's desires and the court's answers "may be
seen in the process," as also its "indifferent and impartial carriage towards either
party."

**To reason 6** (the malicious-prosecution charge): whatever the accuser's malice or
presumption, "**we know not; but certainly there is no occasion of encouragement given
by us to him.**" As for the many diets and long time the business has taken — "what we
may say in the sight of God, we are not to blame for it"; the presbytery's
"**indefatigable attendance**… from our own particular charges, many days," testifies
as much; and truly, since the beginning, "**there hath been more undue delay occasioned
by the appellant** than by presbytery or accuser." As for granting the pursuer liberty
to bring new witnesses to new diets — that is true, "but at first we urged the accuser
to **close the roll of his witnesses**," whereupon he protested that if any of the roll
were rejected on exception he might add "as many in their room"; and Power, "**labouring
still to object against the witnesses enrolled**… as it were of purpose to draw on
delays," forced the presbytery to grant new diets and summon new witnesses, "that so
the pursuer might have the benefit of his protestation."

### Modern English
**To reason 7:** it "smells of no more moderation than the former," for the presbytery
"still urged the pursuer to give in particular instances," whereupon the defendant was
"particularly posed" (questioned), as his answers in the process testify — though often,
being posed, **Power would not answer until he was acquainted with the witnesses'
depositions, which should have been kept close till the closing of the business.** As
for his claim that they received particular instances from the accuser "since the
closing of the libel" — "we know of none that we have taken notice of."

**To reason 8:** being general, a general answer serves — the proceedings "have been…
already **tabled** and approved" by the last Synod, and it were "**both ill and long to
spend time and trouble the Synod… with needless repetitions**"; and since the last
Synod no witness has been examined but on particulars, on which Power was "particularly
posed before they were examined." As for the **interrogatories**: there being "sure
form that witnesses should be examined upon the **defendant's interrogatories**,"
especially since **every member of the presbytery had liberty to interrogate the
witnesses himself** for clearing the truth — and by all appearance those on "that seat"
were "no way deficient in interrogating anything that might tend to the clearing of the
defendant."

**To reason 9:** "**We have been wearied in answering**" the first point, concerning his
allegation that the court received general accusations against him; and the instances
are so full that it were "**a superfluous cramben, toties … denuo deponere**" (a
superfluous reheated cabbage — to depose the same thing over and over again). As for
refusing him an **extract of the whole process** — "**we did not refuse, but only
continued the same till the process were closed**"; and if he can instance any
"wearying oppressions" by any of the presbytery, "let them be censured"; if not,
[the court stands justified]. And as for the paper Power gave in for his own clearing —
"first, it was but his own bare [assertion…]"

## Notes & context
- **The rebuttal, complete (reasons 4–9).** With img_0027 (protestation + reasons 1–3)
  and this opening (reasons 4–9), the presbytery has now answered **Power's entire
  appellation point by point** — a systematic defence of its own procedure, to be laid
  before the Synod. Reading it against imgs 0023–0025 gives both sides of the same
  dispute, the essential material for a fair biography of Power *and* of the court.
- **The court's strongest points:** (4) it admitted **no woman, beggar, deranged person,
  or the pursuer's tenant** as a witness — directly rebutting Power's most vivid
  complaint (img_0023); (6) the **delay was Power's own doing**, through serial
  objections to the enrolled witnesses; (8) witnesses were examined on the **defendant's
  interrogatories** and every member could question them. These are concrete
  procedural claims a reviewer can weigh.
- **A telling admission (reason 7):** Power "**would not answer until acquainted with
  the witnesses' depositions**" — the court frames this as obstruction; Power framed the
  same facts (img_0024) as being denied the process-extract. Same events, opposite spin.
- **Literary Latin.** The clerk reaches for **"crambe(n) … toties … denuo deponere"** —
  an echo of Juvenal's *crambe repetita* ("cabbage served up again," i.e. tiresome
  repetition). A rare flash of humanist learning in the register; worth quoting in the
  wiki.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `posed` = questioned/examined; `condiscend upon
  particulars` = specify the charges; `trall on delayes` = spin out / protract delays;
  `roll of his Witnesses` = the closed list of prosecution witnesses; `interrogators` =
  interrogatories; `cramben` = (acc.) reheated cabbage, proverbial for tedium; `toties…
  denuo deponere` = to depose so many times over again. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates
  Old Style. `y`+superscript → *th-*.

  5. Whether reason 9 (Power's own "paper given in for clearing himself") continues onto

## Notes & context
- **★ Crichton finishes his trials.** Having done the exercise (img 0109) and made the exercise (img
  0110), **Crichton now preaches his "popular sermon" (1 Cor. 4:1–2) and is approved** — the last of the
  standard ordination trials (exercise, common head, popular sermon). The Moor kirk of Glenluce (New Luce)
  is close to having its first settled minister.
- **★ Office rotation — Turnbull moderator.** The **post-Synod election** brings **Mr Alexander Turnbull**
  to the moderator's chair (Hutcheson continuing clerk) — the usual annual rotation after the Synod.
- **★ The Abbey tacksman warned off.** The **removal of Patrick Mc[—] from "the Abbey and teinds of
  Glenluce"** (the litigation of imgs 0100–0101) advances: **Livingstone has "caused give lawful warning to
  him to flit and remove"** — the formal legal step of warning a tenant out. The temporal side of the
  Glenluce planting being tidied up.
- **★ A district act against incest and adultery.** A new disciplinary measure: **the presbytery, alarmed
  at "the abounding of the enormities of incest and adultery," orders every kirk session to require
  [voluntary submission] of every delinquent** — evidence of a perceived rise in grave sexual scandal in
  the Rhins in 1647 and a coordinated presbytery-wide response (continued overleaf).
- **The wider web.** The presbytery keeps up its **correspondence with the presbyteries of Ireland and
  Ayr**, presses in the **Argyll relief contribution**, revives the **war-widows/orphans register** (cf.
  img 0096), and pursues the **Wigtown divinity-bursary conference** — the outward-facing business of a
  well-connected court.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `preached a popular sermon… and Was approved` = the final ordination
  trial; `chosen moderator… continued clerk` = the post-Synod re-election; `caused give lawfull Warning…
  to flit and remove` = the legal warning to quit a holding; `correspondence… With ye presbyteries in

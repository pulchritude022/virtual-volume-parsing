## Notes & context
★★★ **The Laird of Garthland at the stool — the Engagement thread resolved.** The climax lands: **James McDowall, Laird of Garthland**, the county's principal Engager (tracked since imgs 180–223), **"came to Stranraer upon the Lord's Day, and there, before the congregation, gave a full and free confession of his guilt in accession to the late unlawful Engagement, with willing obedience and great sorrow and grief for his fall"** — and the **Session of Stranraer declares him satisfied.** The leading local gentleman-malignant has publicly submitted to the Kirk's discipline, completing the arc begun with the Commission's reference (img 220).

★★ **Robert Campbell still not cleared — and rebuked for contumacy.** Mr Andrew Stewart's Donaghadee testimonial (img 223) **"does not clear him, but leaves him suspect,"** and even queries the grounds on which a testimonial was refused. The presbytery **lays "the sin of his rude and contentious carriage" before Robert Campbell** to his face; he undertakes a "satisfactory answer" next meeting. The long duel with the "What! malignancy in me!" man (imgs 210–223) grinds toward a reckoning.

★ **Discipline hardening.** **Margaret McClounie**, thrice admonished and still defiant, is now **put to the first step of process** (toward the greater sentence). **Arisemman** dodges his citation again. And the presbytery, unable to break the **flat denial** of the two adultery-suspects, punts the question of oath-taking to a written pro-and-contra deliberation — a careful, almost judicial hesitation over compelling an oath.

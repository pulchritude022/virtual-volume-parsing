# CH2/341/1 — Image 113 (folios 112v–113), 5–12 May 1647 — Glasgow tries to take Livingstone; Crichton examined in theology & the tongues and his New-Luce admission arranged; a slander case at Ardwell

Left page (folio **112a**): (5 May, continued) the **tail of the act against incest and adultery** — the
delinquent's composition ("gift") to be settled between this and the next presbytery day, otherwise the
presbytery takes the gift for the use of the poor; the satisfaction to be intimated in every parish kirk
and not released without the presbytery's advice; and for time coming every such delinquent to make offer
to the presbytery before being admitted to give public signs of repentance. Then **★★ the same day, three
letters presented to the presbytery — one from the town council of Glasgow, another from the [Glasgow kirk]
session, and a third from the presbytery of Glasgow — desiring the transportation of Mr John Livingstone to
the Blackfriars kirk of Glasgow**; the presbytery, finding "the weighty [consequence] of the said letter,"
**resolved to delay the answer till the morrow**. Then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 12 May 1647** — absent
the ruling elders of Kirkcolm(?) and Portmontgomery, excused for their former absence; **★ Mr John Crichton
handled the common head [*De causa impulsiva electionis*] (disputing theses 17 & 28), was tried in theology
and in the Hebrew and Greek tongues, and was approved** — the examination portion of his ordination trials.
Right page (folio **113**): **a day appointed for the admission of [Mr John Crichton] to the ministry of
the New kirk of Glenluce** (to the New kirk and benefice, according to the acts of the General Assembly);
**Mr John Livingstone, Mr James Blair, the Laird of Garthland and [the Laird of Freugh] appointed to meet
for the New kirk on Monday to settle the business of the said kirk**, and the business of **Patrick Mc[—]
anent the Abbey [teinds]**; **the Laird of Freugh appointed commissioner of the presbytery**; then a
**slander/defamation matter touching [—] of Ardwell** (a reported scandal that "arose upon a [flying]
sentence," referred to 22 April, feared to be "spread through the country," with Mr Alexander Turnbull
tasked to deal with it and the party to be required to come and confess whether it be true or untrue)
[much of this page torn/faded].

## Notes & context
- **★★ Glasgow tries to take Livingstone.** The headline item: **the town council, kirk session, and
  presbytery of Glasgow all wrote seeking the transportation of Mr John Livingstone — the eminent
  Covenanter preacher, minister of Stranraer — to the Blackfriars kirk of Glasgow.** The presbytery
  recognised "the weighty consequence" and put off its answer "till the morrow." This is the second
  documented attempt in this volume to draw Livingstone away from the Rhins (cf. img 0096, the earlier
  fight to keep him at Stranraer against a call). Historically Livingstone did decline such calls and
  remained at Stranraer until 1648; this minute captures the local reluctance to lose him. **How the
  presbytery answered is likely recorded on the following opening.**
- **★ Crichton finishes his trials — the examination.** Having done/made the exercise (imgs 0109–0110) and
  preached his popular sermon (img 0112), **Crichton is now examined on the common head, in disputed theses,
  and orally "in theology and in the Hebrew and Greek tongues," and approved** — the scholarly examination
  that completes the standard ordination trials. The New kirk of Glenluce (New Luce / the Moor kirk / at
  Balneil) is now ready for his admission.
- **★ Settling the New kirk.** A committee — **Livingstone, Blair, the Laird of Garthland, and the Laird of
  Freugh** — is appointed to meet "for the New kirk" and settle its business, together with the still-open
  **Abbey teind** matter with Patrick Mc[—] (the tacksman warned to flit, img 0112). This is the practical
  planting of the new parish's temporalities running in parallel with Crichton's admission.
- **★ A slander case at Ardwell.** A defamation/scandal matter concerning **[—] of Ardwell** (Ardwell in
  Stoneykirk parish) — a report that "arose upon a [flying] sentence" and, the presbytery fears, has
  "spread through the country." **Mr Alexander Turnbull is charged to deal with it**, and the party is to be
  required to come and confess whether it is true or false. The names and specifics are torn/faded — one
  for the reviewer to pursue on the original.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `desyring… ye transportation to Master John Livingstone to ye
  Blak[friar] kirk of Glasgow` = a call seeking to move a minister to another charge; `tryed in Theologie
  and in ye Hebrew and Greek tongues, and Was approved` = the examination trial (languages + divinity);
  `admissioun… to ye ministre of ye New kirk of Glenluce… be acts of ye Generall assemblie` = induction to
  the new parish authorised by the GA; `arose upon a [flying] sentence of a slander… spread throu ye
  countrey… confess if it be [untruth] or if it be [sooth]` = a defamation cause. **The right page is torn
  away along its whole outer margin and heavily faded — much of folio 113 is provisional.** Capital **W**
  for *w/wh*. Year **1647**.

## Notes & context
★★★ **Stranraer presbytery's Resolutioner declaration for the late General Assembly — entered in three articles.** The climax of the Assembly-lawfulness thread (imgs 244–247). At the clerk's request the presbytery records "the particular judgment of the several members" in a formal **three-point declaration: (1) asserting and holding that "the late General Assembly begun at St Andrews and continued at Dundee," being lawfully constituted, together with all former assemblies, is to be approven — explicitly rejecting the charge of an "unlawful and illegal constitution"; (2) promising to assist the ecclesiastical authority derived from that Assembly and to perform their duties by their presbytery; (3) to maintain it, conform to the public judicatories.** This is a **Resolutioner** adherence-declaration in its clearest form — and the presbytery further **carries the fight to the Synod, sending "an application" via the moderator and Mr John Park desiring the Synod to approve the Assembly's ordinances and "condemn a conspiracy against the Kirk."** Against it stands the recorded **Protester dissent — the brethren who "did protest, by a judicial testimony, to condemn the proceedings of the said late General Assembly"** (Turnbull and Mr James, img 247). The national Resolutioner–Protester schism fully documented at the local level, both sides on the page.

★★ **A section landmark and the turn into 1652.** The declaration closes with a bold marginal **"NB"** and the register turns the year: the next meeting is set **at the old kirk of Stranraer, 4 February 1652,** and a **January 1652 sitting** opens with the usual sederunt and exercise (Mr John Park supplying for the absent Mr John Dick; Mr James Lawrie appointed). The disciplinary and doctrinal routine resumes under the new-year, new-officer regime (Bell moderator, Blair clerk, img 242).

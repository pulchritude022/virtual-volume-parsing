# CH2/341/1 — Image 51 (folios 50v–51) — Power's letter to Garthland (in full); a Glenluce visitation (June 1642); the register resumes (Aug–Sept 1643)

Left page (folio **50a**, verso of 50): the **completion of Mr Gilbert Power's letter to the
laird of Garthland** (begun at the foot of img_0050) — a remarkable **first-person Power
document**, conciliatory but wounded, signed "**Gilbert power**"; then a short
**visitation of the kirk of Glenluce, 8 June 1642**, examining the **ruling elder of
Glenluce** (Mr Hugh Hutcheson preaching). Right page (folio **51**, badly water-stained down
the outer edge): the **forward-running 1643 register resumes** — the presbytery **chooses
commissioners to the General Assembly** (Mr John Livingston, Mr George Dick, and John
Campbell, ruling elder); **Power protests** touching an election and the presbytery's
prejudice; **James Gordoun and Michael McKilras complain that "Mr Gilbert" refused to
proclaim/marry them**, and the presbytery orders him either to marry them himself or let them
go elsewhere; then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 6 September 1643** (Turnbull's common head *de
anima*; many ruling elders absent "because of the harvest"; Mr James Blair presents a letter
of recommendation touching the Earl of Galloway). *(This opening confirms the June-1642
process/extract material and the July–Sept 1643 register run side by side in the book.)*

### Modern English
[Power's letter, concluded:] "…you would know that **not only what right belonged to me, but
my whole estate would have helped the good estate of your kind forebears**, since I would
have hazarded all for the love your predecessors bore me. **But when your son joined with so
malicious a man, and was thus instigated, doing all the malice that ever his father or he
carried against me** — and for the **true respect I had to your house, which I would not
wound but strengthen** — my mind knowing assuredly that if I were out of the way they should
more easily prevail, what less should I do than seek my own, where **I shall suffer an
undeserved change**. Sir, if you had wounded me any other way, it would **not have pierced so
deeply as in my tender name and the credit of my ministry**; I wonder at the matter. I shall,
God willing, **make good the breach thereof, and will not be vindictive**. Sir, if you desire
any time or place to speak with me, I will give you satisfaction in all points, God willing;
professing [myself] of your favour, I will obey your [request], and shall, God willing,
**remain your loving pastor till death**. *So subscribed —* **Gilbert Power**."

**A visitation of the kirk of Glenluce, 8 June 1642** — [touching] the **ruling elder of
Glenluce.** That day **Mr Hugh Hutcheson preached** on his ordinary text (verses 10–11) and
was found orthodox. Mr Hugh, examined on what he knew of the members of the [Glenluce]
session, answered that **they gave good account in discipline; he knew nothing but that they
were of a civil life**, and was informed they were careful [to attend to their duty].

## Notes & context
- **Power's letter to Garthland — a rare first-person Power document.** The concluded letter
  (begun img_0050) is **Power in his own voice**: aggrieved, protesting that Garthland's
  attack wounds him "in my tender name and credit of my ministery," yet **conciliatory** — he
  will "make good the breach… and will not be vindictive," offers to meet, and signs "**your
  loving pastor till death, Gilbert Power.**" He **blames the laird's son** for joining "so
  malicious a man" (the chief instigator — cf. Uchtred McDowell of French / the accusers) and
  frames his ruin as an "undeserved change." Prime material for Power's **person-page**: it
  humanises the deposed minister and states his own theory of the case (a personal vendetta,
  not just the nine crimes). Worth quoting at length there. See [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]].
- **Power still on the rolls into Sept 1643.** Even after the July-1642 deposition and the
  1643 Synod appeal, **"Mr Gilbert power" is still listed among the absent members** on 6 Sept
  1643, and still **protests** at the presbytery — consistent with img_0049 (he "sat amongst
  them," appealed, declined the court). The register treats his status as **unresolved
  pending the Synod**, not settled. Flag the tension for the timeline: *deposed by process
  (1642) yet still nominally a member (1643).*
- **A marriage denied.** **James Gordoun and Michael McKilras** complain that **"Mr Gilbert"
  refused to proclaim the banns and marry them** though lawfully contracted; the presbytery
  orders him to do it or let them marry elsewhere. If this "Mr Gilbert" is **Power**, it is a
  vivid instance of a suspended/deposed minister still expected to serve his cure while the
  appeal drags on — and refusing. (Confirm it is Power and not another "Mr Gilbert.")
- **Commissioners to the 1643 General Assembly.** The presbytery elects **Mr John Livingston,
  Mr George Dick, and John Campbell (ruling elder)** as commissioners to the **General
  Assembly** — the **1643 GA at Edinburgh** (August 1643) that adopted the **Solemn League
  and Covenant**. Stranraer's local delegation to the great national moment; a solid node for
  Livingston's and Dick's biographies and the national-events timeline.
- **A Glenluce visitation (8 June 1642).** A clean **kirk-visitation vignette**: the
  presbytery examines the **ruling elder of Glenluce**, **Mr Hugh Hutcheson preaching**; the
  session found orthodox, of "civil life," and careful — ordinary discipline running in
  parallel with the Power crisis. Note this is **June 1642**, part of the 1642 material bound
  next to the 1643 register (manuscript order ≠ date order).
- **"Because of the harvest."** The 6 Sept 1643 absences are excused **"because of the
  harvest"** — a lovely agrarian-calendar detail (rural elders at the September in-gathering).
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `de anima` (Latin) = "concerning the soul," the set
  theological common head for the exercise; `Common head` = the appointed commonplace;
  `proclamations` = the reading of banns before marriage; `posed` = questioned/examined;
  `remonstrance` = account/representation; `allogit` = alleged/pleaded; `Sic Subscribitur` =
  "thus subscribed" (the notarial tag before a copied signature). **Right page water-stained
  down the outer margin** — many readings provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*.

## Notes & context
- **★★ The birth of a parish — Glenluce split into Old Glenluce and the New Kirk at Balneil.** This
  formal recital is the constitutional charter of the long Glenluce saga: after the King's provision (a
  minister "in place of the late bishop," **1000 merks stipend + the teinds of Glenluce Abbey + 200 merks
  for a schoolmaster**), and the presbytery's and parishioners' wish for **a new kirk and a divided
  parish**, disputes stalled everything — until **the General Assembly of June 1646 ordained the split:
  the OLD kirk of Glenluce (funded by "the late mortification") and a NEW kirk erected at Balneil (funded
  by "the old local stipend" of 420 merks)**, with the mortification's surplus (after building the new
  kirk) employed by the presbytery. This is the founding of what became **New Luce parish** (at Balneil,
  on the Water of Luce), carved out of Glenluce — a genuine, datable ecclesiastical-geography event and
  the resolution of the vacancy/finance deadlock that has run since img 0071. **A headline node for the
  wiki** (the Glenluce/New Luce division; the mortification and royal grant). Confirm the figures (420 /
  1000 / 200 merks), the year of the royal grant (1633?), and "Balneil."
- **The temporal pieces fit together.** The recital ties up the earlier threads: **James Ross of
  Barneill's mortification** (imgs 0084–0102), **the Glenluce Abbey teinds** (imgs 0100–0101), and now a
  further **assignation by Mr Alexander Hepburn of a sum to the kirk of Glenluce** (left page) — all the
  endowments being marshalled to fund the two new charges.
- **★ A fallen elder's heavier penance.** **Patrick [Cathcart?]**, confessing adultery, receives an
  *extra* public penance at Stranraer **"because he had once been a ruling elder"** — office-holders held
  to a higher account, a nice discipline datum (cf. the relapse-offender escalation, img 0093).
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `provyded… a minister in place of ye late bishop… 1000 merks of
  stipend, With ye… Abbey of Glenluce` = the post-Episcopal royal endowment of the charge; `there sould
  be a new kirk erected, and ye parish divyded… approved by ye Generall assemblie` = the canonical
  division of a parish; `ye old kirk… provyded by ye late mortificatioun… ye now kirk erected at Balneil…
  provyded With ye old locall stipend` = the funding split between the two charges; `mortificatioun` = a
  perpetual endowment; `assignatioun of a certane sowme` = a legal assignment of money. **Both pages
  heavily water-stained/torn** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Years **1633 / 1646 / 1647**.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 53 (folios 52v–53), Nov–Dec 1643 — clerkship handover; a presentation; routine planting business

**⚠ Badly damaged opening.** Both pages are heavily **water-stained** and suffer strong
**ink bleed-through** from the facing leaves; the lower half of the left page and most of the
right page are ghosted. This is a **low-confidence** transcription — flagged for re-imaging.

Left page (folio **52a**): the tail of a sederunt — **the Earl of Galloway exhibits a
presentation** (to a kirk) in favour of **Mr James Blair**, which the presbytery **admits
*salvo iure*** (saving all parties' right); **[Andrew] Agnew summoned a second time (*pro
2do*)** for disobedience to a session; then the **formal handover of the register**: **Mr
Alexander Turnbull delivers the presbytery book, subscribed with his own hand, to Mr Hugh
Hutcheson the succeeding clerk, to be kept "in safe custody."** Then **Presbytery at Stranraer,
22 November 1643** (Blair exercises on Col. 3:22–24). Right page (folio **53**): the Agnew
summons continued (*pro 3o*); **Mr John Livingstone appointed to preach at Kirkcolm** and sort
the vacant parish's business; a **visitation appointed for 20 December**; **Presbytery at
Stranraer, 7 December 1643**; and **James Ross of Balneil, in Glenluce parish, gives in a
supplication** (touching supply/planting — Mr James Blair to be sent to preach).

## Notes & context
- **The clerkship changes hands — a rare glimpse of record-keeping.** The formal minute of
  **Turnbull delivering the register, "subscribed with his own hand," to Hugh Hutcheson, the
  succeeding clerk, "to be kept with the presbytery books in safe custody,"** is a small
  archival gem: it explains why *this very volume* survives, and confirms the **clerk rotation
  (Nov 1643)** already seen at img_0052. A nice provenance/reference note for the wiki's
  "about the record" material. See [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]] and
  [[mccollough-genealogy-project]].
- **A presentation *salvo iure*.** The **Earl of Galloway presents Mr James Blair** to a
  vacant kirk (Portpatrick or Kirkcolm — page damaged), which the presbytery admits **"salvo
  iure"** — a lawyerly formula reserving everyone's rights, echoing the Kirkcolm patronage
  reservation at img_0050. The patron–presbytery dance over planting continues.
- **Planting Kirkcolm and Glenluce.** **Livingstone to preach at Kirkcolm** (and marshal the
  subscribing parishioners); **James Ross of Balneil supplicates for Glenluce**, asking that
  **Blair be sent to preach** — both vacant parishes are being worked toward settlement. The
  **Kirkcolm** thread (Porterfield's death, the Earl of Galloway as patron) and the
  **Glenluce** thread (the mortification/Ross sub-plot) both run on.
- **Discipline: the Agnew summons.** An **[Andrew] Agnew** is being summoned in escalating
  stages (*pro 2do*, then *pro 3o*) for **disobedience to a kirk session** — the standard
  three-fold citation before a contumacy censure. New (uncertain) name and a procedural
  example.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `salvo iure` (Latin) = "the right being safe" — a
  reservation of all parties' rights; `pro 2do` / `pro 3o` = "for the second / third [time]"
  (successive summonses); `in safe custodie` = the clerk's charge of the records; `planting` =
  settling a minister in a vacant parish; `parochiners` = parishioners; `supplicatioun` = a
  petition to the court. **Both pages badly water-stained with heavy show-through** — this is
  one of the least legible openings so far. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1643.

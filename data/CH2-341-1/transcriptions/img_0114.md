## Notes & context
- **★ Two fugitive adulterers hunted from every pulpit.** A vivid discipline item: **Thomas Adair (formerly
  of Stranraer — the man of the £100 bond to shun Margaret Hannay, img 0097) and James Mc[—] (lately of
  Glenluce), both suspected of adultery and both fled, are to be summoned "out of the pulpit of every parish
  kirk in the presbytery."** The public reading of summonses from every pulpit is the standard weapon
  against absconders. Adair's reappearance here — now himself a fugitive — is a notable thread to track.
- **★ The Argyll relief collection accounted — to Provost Porterfield.** The long-running **collection for
  the distressed people of Argyll** (imgs 0096, 0102, 0109, 0112) is now **delivered/remitted to George
  Porterfield, provost of Glasgow**, with a **per-parish breakdown** of the sums (Kirkcolm £33 13s; Leswalt
  £26; Inch £10 6d; Portmontgomery £30; Kirkmaiden £50 14s; Stoneykirk c. £80) — a rare quantified snapshot
  of each Rhins parish's giving for the war-ravaged Argyll (the aftermath of Alasdair MacColla's 1644–47
  campaigns). Figures provisional.
- **★★ The Glasgow call for Livingstone — deferred.** Following the three Glasgow letters (img 0113), the
  presbytery **"could not give answer" and referred the matter**, invoking a standing **protest touching the
  transportation and the vacant kirks in Ireland depending before the General Assembly** — i.e. the local
  court is stalling, unwilling to release its star preacher, and pointing to competing Irish claims. And,
  tellingly, **it immediately sets a rota to supply Stranraer's pulpit every Lord's day** — Turnbull, Dick,
  Hutcheson, Bell, Livingstone himself, and others — bracing for the possibility of his departure. (He did
  in fact remain at Stranraer until 1648.)
- **★ The New kirk's stipend secured by bond.** At the **26 May sitting held at the New kirk of Glenluce**
  itself, Livingstone and Blair report their diligence, and **James Ross of Balneil gives bond (800 merks)**
  — with the presbytery's act binding the heritors for **600 merks and thirty** — **"for making up the
  stipend of the New kirk, according to the act of the General Assembly."** This is the funding of New Luce
  finally being nailed down by written obligation after the heritors' default (imgs 0107–0111): James Ross
  (the presbytery's ally, whose letter reported the default) now personally bonds the stipend.
- **The moderator's sermon and Crichton.** Turnbull (the new moderator) **preaches on Song of Solomon 3** at
  the Glenluce sitting, and **Crichton "repeated a preaching"** there — the settlement of the new minister
  proceeding in the very kirk he is to serve.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `fugitive be summoned out of pulpit in every parishe kirk` = citation
  of absconders read from every pulpit; `contributioun collected for ye distressed people of Argyle…
  delyvered… to George Porterfield provost of Glasgow` = the Argyll relief remitted with parish accounts;
  `ye presbyterie could not gyve answer… a protest for ye transportation… depending before ye… assemblie` =
  deferring the Glasgow call pending higher-court process; `Stranrawer be supplied ilk Lord's day` = a
  pulpit-supply rota; `James Ross of Balneill to gyve bond of 800 merks… for making up of ye stipend of ye
  New kirk` = securing the new parish's stipend by written bond. **Both pages faded; the right torn on the
  outer margin** — figures and some names provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1647**.

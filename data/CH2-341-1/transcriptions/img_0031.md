# CH2/341/1 — Image 31 (folios 30v–31), May 1642 — citation to Glenluce; the Glenluce visitation

Top of the left page (folio **30a**) finishes the **11 May 1642** business: a **copy of
James Gordon's libel** is to be sent to **Mr Gilbert Power** with a **citation to
appear at Glenluce on Thursday 19 May**, and a warrant to **John Man or Robert
McClearnon** to summon Gordon's witnesses. The rest of the opening is the **visitation
of the kirk of Glenluce, 19 May 1642** — a model parish visitation: the minister
examined, the **Session book reviewed**, the **elders' duties** (visiting/praying with
the sick, catechising by quarters) enjoined, the congregation's verdict on their
minister taken (edified — except **James Rosse**, who "does not approve him"), and an
act for **new seating in the kirk**.

### Modern English
[The minister answered] as to family worship, "he knows no other." He desired the
presbytery to **exhort the elders to unanimity**, and to "**take some course about the
fabric of their preaching house.**" The minister removed and the elders called in, they
were all exhorted to the forementioned duties, then asked **what they knew of their
minister's preaching and discipline — whether he was diligent, whether they were
edified, and of his life and conversation.** They answered they **thought him diligent
in preaching and discipline, were edified by him, and knew nothing against his life and
conversation.**

**James Rosse** was called (having removed); being **no elder**, he was interrogated
what he knew of the minister, and **whether he did not approve him in everything**; posed
wherein he did not approve, he **craved a convenient time to answer.** Since the
presbytery conceived that **Rosse's not approving the minister intimated something
reflecting on the minister's credit**, and Rosse **refused to instruct the particulars**,
it is appointed that he **give in the particulars of his disapproval on Wednesday come a
fortnight, at the visitation of [Leswalt]** or elsewhere as the presbytery thinks fit;
and to that effect he was summoned **apud acta** (by the act itself). Finally, the
presbytery, considering how the Glenluce parishioners might best be seated, thought it
fitting that **seats be built on the north side of the kirk, from the end of William
Gordon's seat to the eastmost end of [Crongs?] seat**, to be built first by **William
[…].**

## Notes & context
- **A model parish visitation.** Amid the Power case, this is the routine machinery of
  the kirk: a **presbyterial visitation of Glenluce** (19 May 1642) — Session-book audit,
  the minister examined on his diligence, the **congregation asked to testify** for or
  against him, elders' duties (sick-visiting, praying with the dying, **catechising and
  family-visiting by parish quarter**) enjoined, and **kirk seating** allocated. Useful
  context for how the presbytery governed a parish, and a foil to the contentious Power
  visitation.
- **James Rosse — a dissenting parishioner.** Alone in the congregation, **James Rosse
  (not an elder)** declines to approve the minister "in every thing," but **refuses to
  specify**; he is summoned *apud acta* to bring particulars at the next visitation
  (**Leswalt**). A small drama of local discontent worth a wiki note — and a name to
  track.
- **Power's citation to Glenluce.** The 11 May act sends **James Gordon's libel** to
  Power with a citation to Glenluce (19 May) and a warrant to **John Man / Robert
  McClearnon** to summon Gordon's witnesses — dovetailing with img_0030 (Gordon an
  accuser by 1642). The visitation day doubles as a diet in the Power/Gordon process.
- **Name to confirm:** the **minister of Glenluce** reads "Mr George [Park]" here but the
  surname is not certain and could be **Dick** (the newly-chosen clerk is a different
  "Mr George Dick"). Memory has **Mr George Park** as Glenluce's minister — flag for the
  reviewer.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `apud acta` = "in the presence of the court" (a
  summons made in open session, needing no further citation); `remonstrat` = set forth,
  represented; `fabrick of their preaching house` = the church building's fabric;
  `reflex upon the credit` = casts doubt on the reputation; `instruct the particulars`
  = prove/specify the details; `come a fourteen dayes` = a fortnight hence. Capital **W**
  for *w/wh*. Dates Old Style. `y`+superscript → *th-*.

  4. `[Crongs] seat` (the eastern pew boundary) and `William [ ]` (the builder) — names.
  5. `at the visitation of [Lesswalt]` — confirm the next visitation place (Leswalt).

# CH2/341/1 — Image 133 (folios 132v–133)

The heart of the **Ardwell adultery-scandal** arc, at the **Inch sitting of 17 May
1648**. The Moderator reports he has written to the **Earl of Cassillis** and the
**laird of Garthland**, and that **John [McCulloch?] of [Ardloch?]** did his
sackcloth penance at Kirkmaiden. Then **Alexander McCulloch of Ardwell**, asking
for the extract of his process (which the clerk had not fully completed), gives in
a **long supplication** (transcribed in full across both pages): ★ it names the
slanderer as **Jonet Agnew, Lady Freugh**, who "affirmed that **Jean [Fyfe]** was
absolutely guilty of the said fact"; Ardwell demands the reasons and grounds of
the act ordering him to purge by oath, and **all the proceedings since the
Provincial Synod at Wigtown, 19 October 1647**; he asks either to be absolved
without oath (there being no presumptions agreeable to the acts of the General
Assembly) or to be granted **process on his own libel of complaint against Lady
Freugh** (referred from the session of Stranraer) for her "manifest untruth." The
**presbytery's answer**: it will supply the whole process, but will **neither
absolve him nor grant process against Lady Freugh unless he purges himself by
oath**, per the General Assembly act and the Synod of Galloway acts (Wigtown, Oct
1647 and April 1648); he is summoned again. The page ends with ★ a **letter from
the Commission of the Kirk (Edinburgh, 28 April 1648)** on the Engagement crisis —
the Commission's dealings with the Estates of Parliament, the breaches of the
treaties and Covenant, and demands to be sent to the Parliament of England.

### Modern English
[Ardwell's supplication, concluded:] "...be granted when it is desired. 2. I
desire your wisdoms, if you have no presumptions agreeable with the acts of the
General Assembly, that you would absolve me without an oath from the said scandal,
and hold me no longer under it, since there is no reason therefor... 3. I desire
that you would be pleased to grant me **process upon the libel of complaint given
in by me to you, by way of reference from the session of Stranraer, against the
Lady Freugh**, which libel may be read before you and referred for trial of such a
manifest untruth... that there is any [ground] of guiltiness in me of the said
fact. Which I humbly crave, etc."

**The presbytery answers** that they are most willing the proceedings should be
known, both to him and to others; and therefore appoint the clerk to write out the
whole process and deliver it to the laird of Ardwell before the next presbytery,
by which he will perceive the grounds and reasons why they urge him to confess or
purge himself by oath, conform to the act of the General Assembly and the advice
of the Provincial Synod of Galloway (holden at Wigtown in October last, and the
act of the Synod of the said province holden at Wigtown in April last). And
therefore they can **neither absolve him unless he purge himself by his oath, nor
grant him process upon his libel against the Lady Freugh**; and therefore they
summon him again to compear before the presbytery, either to confess or purge
himself by his oath.

The same day, a **letter from the Commissioners of the Kirk, dated at Edinburgh,
28 April 1648**, was presented, the tenor whereof follows: *"Reverend brethren, In
our letter of the last of [March] to the Synod (which we suppose all of you have
seen and perused), we acquainted you with our desires given in to the Honourable
Estates of Parliament about that time; and since, after some conference had and
some papers interchanged between their Lordships and us upon those desires — of
which we had no satisfactory answer — they were pleased to fall upon the
consideration of the breaches of the treaties and Covenant, and have resolved to
send demands to the Parliament of England for reparation therein; [some] of those
demands concerning religion, having first voted the same before..."* (continues).

## Notes & context
- ★★ **Lady Freugh identified as Jonet Agnew.** This page finally names the
  "Lady Freugh" of the whole Ardwell arc: **Jonet Agnew**. She is the woman who
  "affirmed that Jean [Fyfe] was absolutely guilty of the said fact" — i.e. the
  source of the adultery-slander against Ardwell. (The Agnews of Lochnaw were the
  hereditary Sheriffs of Galloway; "Lady Freugh" would be an Agnew married into
  the Freugh estate, or an Agnew heiress of Freugh.) A key genealogical anchor for
  the eventual wiki person-page.
- ★★ **Ardwell's counter-suit.** Ardwell does not merely resist purgation — he
  demands **process against Lady Freugh for slander** ("a manifest untruth"),
  referred up from the **session of Stranraer**. The presbytery refuses to
  entertain the counter-suit until he first purges himself by oath — a neat
  illustration of church-court leverage.
- ★ **Full documentary record.** This opening copies (a) Ardwell's supplication
  verbatim, (b) the presbytery's formal answer, and (c) the Commission of the
  Kirk's political letter — the clerk is building an evidentiary dossier, exactly
  the kind of primary material the wiki person/event pages will draw on.
- ★ **Commission of the Kirk letter (28 April 1648).** The national backdrop: the
  Commission (Robert Douglas moderator) is at loggerheads with the Estates over
  the **breaches of the treaties and the Covenant** (the Engagement), and resolves
  to send demands to the **Parliament of England** — the churchmen's resistance to
  the Engagers on the eve of the 1648 invasion of England.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 131 (folios 130v–131)

Left page (foliated **130a** = folio 130 verso) finishes the mid-April sitting and
opens the **sitting of 26 April 1648**: Blair's report on the still-absent witness
woman; **Bonqin's** report that he has not yet reached the Lieutenant-Colonel his
brother about **Mr John Dick's gleib**; the Moderator's report that he has written
to Wigtown (widows/orphans roll); **Ardwell** compears but again asks for delay
before purging himself; the next meeting appointed for **26 April**, when Mr James
Lowrie is to give his popular sermon (**Titus 2:7–9**). The page then opens the
**26 April sitting**: absents (ruling elders of Stranraer, Inch, Leswalt); Lowrie's
popular sermon **approved**; and **Mr Alexander Turnbull chosen moderator, Mr John
Craig clerk**. The right page (folio 131) continues the 26 April sitting and — its
headline content — **enters verbatim the act of the Provincial Synod of Galloway
(holden at Wigtown, 18 April 1648, sess. 2)** on the Ardwell adultery-scandal: the
presbytery had used all diligence, even **sending a man to Ireland to search for
Jean Fyfe** (who has fled); the Synod, given "the flagrancy of the scandal,"
orders **Alexander McCulloch of Ardwell either to confess or purge himself by
oath**, failing which the presbytery is to **proceed against him**. Ardwell then
presents a **supplication** asking for an extract of the act from the register.

> **Naming note.** The presbytery's own name is written with an ornate initial and
> is not cleanly legible here; on the identification of this volume (NRS CH2/341 =
> **Presbytery of Stranraer**) it is rendered "Stranrawer" below, flagged.

## Notes & context
- ★★ **Synod of Galloway act on the Ardwell scandal (Wigtown, 18 April 1648).**
  This entry copies the higher court's ruling into the presbytery register and is
  the pivot of the whole Ardwell arc (running since img 113). Key confirmed facts:
  (a) the accused is **Alexander McCulloch of Ardwell**; (b) the alleged partner is
  **Jean Fyfe**, who has **fled to Ireland** — and the presbytery actually **sent a
  man over to search for her**; (c) the Synod endorses the presbytery's diligence
  and orders Ardwell to **confess or purge by oath**, with **process (toward
  excommunication) to follow if he refuses**. This resolves the long ambiguity
  about the partner's identity and the reason the case kept stalling (the witness
  unreachable in Ulster).
- ★ **Ardwell's supplication:** rather than swearing or confessing, Ardwell plays
  for time — asking for a certified extract of the act, the standard delaying
  tactic of a heritor with legal advice. The case will continue.
- ★ **Mr James Lowrie's ordination trial** proceeds: popular sermon on **Titus
  2:7–9**, approved (following his Stonykirk call, img 130).
- ★ **Court officers, 26 April 1648:** **Mr Alexander Turnbull** chosen moderator,
  **Mr John Craig** clerk — note this is *Alexander* Turnbull as moderator (the
  earlier scribe/clerk was John Turnbull); worth tracking whether these are two
  distinct Turnbulls.
- **Marion / Jonet paternity thread:** the witness woman still fails to appear;
  now **Jonet [—], Marion's sister**, is also to be summoned. Process delayed
  again.
- **Bonqin & the gleib:** "Bonqin" (a heritor designation) is charged with Mr John
  Dick's glebe but is waiting on his brother the Lieutenant-Colonel — a glimpse of
  how parish endowment depended on individual lairds then away at the wars.

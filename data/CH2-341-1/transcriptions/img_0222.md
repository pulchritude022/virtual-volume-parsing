### Modern English

## Notes & context
★★ **"The trouble of the time" — the war reaches the minute-book's margins.** Two entries here register the disruption of early 1651 (Cromwell's army occupying the south-west after Dunbar): Mr John Scott, "by reason of the **distraction** he had been troubled with," forgot to cite a delinquent; and Mr John Dick could not confront the adultery-suspects **"because of the trouble of the time."** These offhand phrases are the war intruding directly into parish discipline — cases stalled not by evasion but by the general upheaval.

★★ **Garthland's repentance performed.** The **Laird of Garthland** (imgs 220–221) writes that he **carried out his public repentance at Stranraer "last Lord's Day"** for his accession to the Engagement; the presbytery, satisfied with his "good will," holds him to complete satisfaction **in both his parishes** per the earlier act — the leading Engager's submission now substantially done.

★ **The Ireland-malignants and the routine.** **John Campbell** remains fled; **Robert Campbell**, still obstinate under his first admonition (img 221), is set for a **second** unless he produces his testimonial. A **12 February** diet was adjourned to the **26th**, and the sitting keeps the ordinary exercise (Turnbull, Scott).

## Folio 245a (left page)  *(confidence: low)*

*Still the 24 December 1651 sitting. **The old-Glenluce kirk repair: Mr John Crosse and Mr John Dick to press the heritors — "to lay it upon the conscience [of the heritors] before the congregation" — and report; no answer yet received.** The **John Stewart of Bargowan** matter (via a letter from Mr Maxwell) — Stewart appointed to appear before the presbytery. On correspondence expenses, the brethren are to get names from their sessions. **Robert Campbell's public confession is continued at his request "because of much English [company] in the kirk"; Mr John Park to require his obedience before the next meeting, else the first public admonition.** Then **Mr John Dick and Mr James Lawrie report their conference with Alexr McDowall — who acknowledged no guilt but "was found not sensible of any scandalous carriage with that woman."** A witness against Little McDowall in Stoneykirk is summoned. Water-stained. Side-margin captions faded.*

## Notes & context
★★ **The Gilbert Neilson / Cristian McDowall adultery — a witness deposition.** The Kirkmaiden Neilson cohabitation-scandal (Gilbert Neilsone, first seen img 218) is now under formal proof: **John Morison is sworn and deposes that Gilbert Neilson and Cristian McDowall were "guilty," and that Neilson thereafter "departed"** (fled the country) — the familiar Galloway pattern of the guilty man slipping to Ireland. Morison himself is noted as having gone to Ireland and been tried "by the session." A fresh witness-deposition entry in the volume's long adultery-discipline series.

★★ **Robert Campbell delays under cover of "English company," and Alexr McDowall denies.** Two obstinate men: **Robert Campbell asks that his public confession be "continued for a space, because of much English company in the kirk"** — an unusually candid glimpse of the Cromwellian garrison's presence in the congregation — but then fails to appear when sent for, so **Mr John Park is to require his obedience or move to the first public admonition.** And **Alexr McDowall, conferred with, answers only "not guilty" and is "not sensible of any scandalous carriage with that woman."** Two parallel refusals to submit.

★ **The Glenluce kirk repair, still stalled.** The **old-Glenluce heritors** (imgs 237–245) again return **no answer** on repairing the ruinous kirk; **Mr John Dick is to "lay it upon their conscience before the congregation"** — the presbytery escalating from committee to pulpit pressure. The **John Stewart of Bargowan** matter (the excommunicate thread, imgs 240–243) is pursued via Mr Maxwell.

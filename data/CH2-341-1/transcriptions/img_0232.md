# CH2/341/1 — Image 232 (folios 231a–232)

## Folio 231a (left page)  *(confidence: low)*

*The 21 May meeting closes; the stated meeting of **4 June 1651** opens (exercise by Lawrie, Park). The presbytery, **finding the Inch heritors have not built the manse as undertaken, appoints Mr John Dick to uplift the [valuation money] of the manse himself** so he may make use of it. Then **John Campbell, present, is set up before the congregation** (per the presbytery's ordinance) to acknowledge his guilt in his accession to the late unlawful Engagement in Ireland, his disobedience, and his uncomely carriage. ## Notes & context
★★★ **John Campbell to the stool — but "gave further evidence of his perverseness."** The violent Ireland-malignant of the sworn deposition (imgs 216–217), long "fled the kingdom" (imgs 212–222), has returned and is **set up before the congregation to acknowledge his accession to the Engagement in Ireland, his disobedience, and his uncomely carriage** — but his performance fails: **"instead of obedience and repentance … he gave further evidence of his perverseness and wickedness."** Rebuked and "in some measure convinced," he is **ordered to redo his public repentance next Lord's day.** The presbytery's most notorious hold-out grudgingly at the stool, still resisting.

★★ **The Stoneykirk / Portmontgomery boundary decided — Kidork and Barnsalloch stay with Stoneykirk.** Garthland's teind-supplication (img 231) is answered: weighing the reports from both kirks and **the perambulation held at Portmontgomery (1649, reported 16 Jan 1650)**, the presbytery **concludes "it most fit that the lands of Kidork and Barnsalloch remain joined to Stoneykirk"** — reversing the pull toward Portpatrick and keeping the disputed lands (and their teinds/stipend) in Stoneykirk. The long Rogele/Barnsalloch boundary question (imgs 182–183, 192, 214) reaches a settled judgment.

★ **The Inch manse forced through.** With the Inch heritors again defaulting on the manse (cf. imgs 206, 209), the presbytery **empowers Mr John Dick to uplift the valuation money himself** and use it — a pointed step in his contested tenure at Inch (the transportation fight, imgs 227–229). Margaret Morion's and John McNish's cases are held over.

- **Lowest-confidence readings (Dad):**
  - **Both folios are heavily water-stained; the boundary paragraph on folio 232 is faint** and its connective wording reconstructed, though the outcome (Kidork and Barnsalloch remain with Stoneykirk) is secure.
  - **"the lands of Kidork and Barnsalloch"** — the two place-names are best readings (Kidork uncertain).
  - **John Campbell "gave further evidence of his perverseness"** and the re-appointment to the stool are the priority readings and moderately secure.
  - **"Kennedy of Culhorn"** (Kirkmaiden ruling elder) and the exercise text references are partly conjectural.

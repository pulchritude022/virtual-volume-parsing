# CH2/341/1 — Image 144 (folios 143v–144)

Left page (foliated **143a** = folio 143 verso) completes the ★ **visitation of the
old kirk of Glenluce (26 October 1648)** with the presbytery's **injunctions to the
session**: provide a **sand-glass** and a **sackcloth** (the kirk lacks both);
stop meddling with **fines/penalties** — leave that to a civil magistrate per the
act of Parliament; **repair the fabric of the kirk**; and **plant a school**. The
account of collections and penalties gathered "since Parliament was treasurer" is
found unclear, so **Mr John Scott and John Crosse** (with the bailies of
[Kirkchanyan] and [Balminnoch]) are appointed to audit it. Then an **adultery
referral** — **John Neilstoun and Grissell Steinsoun**, confessing, remitted back to
the session — and ★★ an **act suspending the members of the old Glenluce session who
complied with the late unlawful Engagement** from sitting, until censured, per the
Commission of the Kirk. The right page (folio 144) has a new **extraordinary
sitting at Stranraer, 14 November 1648**, dominated by ★ the **paternity probation
of Marion [Ant—?]**: the long-delayed witnesses **Jonet and Cathrin McBryd** at last
compear, are rebuked for disobedience, and depose on oath about Marion's delivery —
Cathrin recounting a man, **Peter Malcolm**, coming to speak privately with Marion.

### Modern English
The same day, the elders being called in, they and the minister were both **rebuked
for want of a sand-glass** for the use of the kirk, and for **want of a sackcloth**
(so that any notorious offenders may show signs of repentance); they are appointed
to provide one. They were likewise rebuked for **meddling with penalties/fines**,
and are appointed to leave that to a civil magistrate, conform to the act of
Parliament. They were also rebuked for not **repairing the fabric of the kirk**, and
desired to use diligence therein. They were likewise rebuked for **not having a
school**, and desired to plant one with all diligence.

Concerning the **account of the collections and penalties gathered since Parliament
was treasurer** — which account is in no way clear, neither in the manner nor the
delivery — the presbytery appoints **Mr John Scott and John Crosse**, [with] the
bailie of [Kirkchanyan] and [Balminnoch], to wait on the said account and labour to
clear it.

The same day, **John Neilstoun and Grissell [Steinsoun]**, referred by the session
of [—] to the presbytery for adultery, were brought before the presbytery,
confessed their adultery, and are referred back to the session to receive directions
about their satisfaction. The same day, the presbytery, considering that ★★ **many
of the session of the old kirk of Glenluce were guilty of compliance with the late
unlawful Engagement**, think fit to **suspend them from sitting in session** until
they be censured and rebuked, according as the Commission of the Kirk shall appoint.

### Modern English

**Presbytery held at Stranraer, 14 November.** The presbytery being **extraordinarily
convened**, there was no exercise. Absent: Mr Alexander Turnbull and Mr John Scott.
**Mr James Blair reported** that the process was to have gone forward, per the
presbytery's appointment, against **Jonet McBryd and Cathrin McBryd** — who were
present with **Marion [Ant—?]** at the time of her delivery — for their disobedience
to the presbytery; but that the two women [now signified] they were willing to come

**inability and sickness**, and that they were coming that day to Stranraer.

They being called were both **sharply rebuked for their disobedience**, and being
sworn, **Jonet McBryd deponed** that Marion, at the time she bore [the child],

## Notes & context
- ★ **Visitation injunctions to the Glenluce session (26 Oct 1648).** A textbook
  cluster of parish-material demands: a **sand-glass** (to time sermons), a
  **sackcloth** (penitential garment for offenders), the boundary between kirk
  discipline and civil **fines** (to be left to the magistrate per Parliament), the
  **fabric of the kirk**, and the **planting of a school** — a vivid inventory of
  what a mid-17th-c. Galloway parish was expected to have and was found lacking.
- ★★ **Session members suspended for Engagement compliance.** Following Mr John
  Scott's complaint (img 143), the presbytery formally **suspends the
  Engagement-complying elders of the old Glenluce session** from sitting until
  censured, per the **Commission of the Kirk** — the local enforcement of the 1648
  purge reaching down to individual elders.
- ★ **Audit of collections/penalties "since Parliament was treasurer."** A financial
  scrutiny of the parish poor-box and fines, appointed to Scott, **John Crosse**,
  and local bailies — of interest for parish finance and for the (uncertain)
  place-names **[Kirkchanyan]** and **[Balminnoch]**.
- ★ **Paternity probation — the McBryd depositions.** The witnesses who defied the
  presbytery in October (img 142) finally compear (pleading sickness), are rebuked,
  and depose: Jonet on the near-full-term birth; **Cathrin** on the infant's death
  within a day and — the evidentiary heart — a man **Peter Malcolm** who came to the
  croft and spoke privately with Marion, then went toward the port. Marion herself
  reportedly defended her honesty but **named no father**. The case (running since
  imgs 128/139) remains unresolved.
- **New adultery referral:** **John Neilstoun & Grissell [Steinsoun]**, confessing,
  remitted to the session for satisfaction.

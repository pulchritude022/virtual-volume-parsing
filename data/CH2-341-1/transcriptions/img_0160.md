# CH2/341/1 — Image 160 (folios 159v–160)

The **witness phase of Balneil's trial — and it goes entirely his way.** Left page
(foliated **159a** = folio 159 verso): Balneil finishes his own answers (art. 10, the
"miner," and an **art. 11** about counsel at **Kirkcudbright** that the Earl of Galloway
should be employed by the oath of the shires — he says **Callendar had written to my
Lord Galloway offering him a party**, and that he himself "would rather be [dead] than
have a hand in that business"); then a panel of witnesses — **Major McDougall, John
Kevan, Robert Gunnokat, John McMillan, William Gordon, George McDougall, How
McRalstoun** — is sworn in his presence. ★★ **Major McDougall deposes that Balneil did
NOT advise them to spare their [dues], and that they took the protection from Callendar
of their own accord, in no way at Balneil's desire** (clearing arts. 6–7). The right
page (folio 160) continues: **Robert Gunnokat** and **William Gordon** confirm Balneil
only asked **John Kevan** to read the Estates' observations (Kevan refused) and that
**Balneil did not read them himself** (art. 8); and — most tellingly — ★★ **on art. 2,
William Gordon and John McDougall both depose that Balneil did not press his tenants,
but told them "if any of you will not join, I am content, I will bear with you."**

### Modern English
**[Art. 10]** (that he fled from the colonel in the Engagement) — *"refers himself to the
foresaid paper."* **[Art. 11]** *Charged with counselling at Kirkcudbright that the Earl of
Galloway should be employed by the oath of the shires:* he answers he knew nothing of it;
that **Callendar wrote to my Lord Galloway offering him a party to raise**, and my Lord
declined to bring it to the church; and that he himself **"would rather be [dead] than
have a hand in that business."**

The church, having considered his answers, called **Major McDougall, John Kevan, Robert
Gunnokat, John McMillan, William Gordon, George McDougall, How McRalstoun**, who were
**sworn in Balneil's presence**. The rest removed, **Major McDougall**, examined on art.
6, deponed that ★★ **Balneil had NOT advised them to spare their [dues]**; and on art. 7,
that ★★ **they took the protection from Callendar of their own accord, in no way desired
so to do by Balneil**. **John Kevan**, on art. 8, deponed that **Balneil desired him to
read the observations, but he refused**; that Balneil **offered him no money**; that he did
not hear the alleged encouraging speech; and that **Balneil did not read them himself**.

### Modern English
**Robert Gunnokat**, on art. 8, deponed that **Balneil desired John Kevan to read the
observations, but he refused**; that he heard no encouraging speech; and that **Balneil
did not read them himself** — he "put them by, as if he had been reading." **John
McMillan**: not present, knew nothing but by report. **William Gordon**: saw a copy in
Balneil's hand but **did not hear him read it**, only that he asked John Kevan to read it.

The same **William Gordon**, on art. 2 (whether Balneil dealt with his tenants to serve in
the Engagement, or encouraged them with a bill), deponed that ★★ **Balneil did not
encourage them with a bill, but said to the tenants, "If any of you will not join, I shall
be well content."** **John McDougall**, on art. 2, deponed that **Balneil said to the
tenants, "If any of you will take the refusal and not join, I am content, I will bear with
you."**

## Notes & context
- ★★ **The witnesses clear Balneil.** This is the evidentiary heart of the case: sworn in
  Balneil's presence, the panel — **Major McDougall, John Kevan, Robert Gunnokat, John
  McMillan, William Gordon, John McDougall** — testifies, article by article, that he
  **did not press the Engagement**: he neither advised sparing dues (art. 6), nor desired
  the Callendar protection (art. 7 — "of their own accord"), nor read or pushed the
  Estates' observations (art. 8), nor biled his tenants to serve (art. 2). On the contrary,
  he **told his tenants they need not join** ("I am content, I will bear with you"). Taken
  with the session's "ingenuously declared the truth" (img 155) and the committee's "nothing
  to say against him" (img 158), Balneil is comprehensively exonerated.
- ★ **"I would rather be dead."** Balneil's own art.-11 answer — that when **Callendar
  offered Galloway a party to raise**, he "would rather be dead than have a hand in that
  business" — is a vivid line for his person-page and dates his stance.
- ★ **A McDougall cluster.** The witnesses include **Major McDougall, George McDougall,
  John McDougall** and **John Kevan** — worth disambiguating for the prosopography (Major
  McDougall is the officer to whom Ardwell allegedly paid for a "place," img 146).
- ★ **A model of church-court fact-finding.** The methodical, per-article, per-witness
  examination — with everything minuted — is an excellent specimen of 17th-c. presbyterian
  judicial procedure, and the counterpoint to the committee's summary fining that Balneil
  complained of (img 148).

The parish caught in the middle of the volume's founding drama: see
[Mr Gilbert Power's deposition and reposition](entity:event/mr-gilbert-power-s-deposition-and-reposition)
for the case that dominates Stoneykirk's presence in this record.

## A parish, and a session, split

Stoneykirk's own kirk session petitioned *against* its minister — describing itself as "a
desolate people" no longer comforted by his ministry — while a separate group of
parishioners (George McCulloch and six others) petitioned *for* his restoration. The
tumult reportedly spilled into physical disruption: a "tumult at Stoneykirk" is recorded in
the immediate aftermath of Power's fall, and his own daughter Marion is recorded disturbing
a later service [entity:person/mr-gilbert-power].

## A slow, genuine reconciliation

By the mid-1640s, an aged and infirm Power and his heritors work out a detailed,
point-by-point reconciliation — arrears paid, the kirk repaired, a colleague funded largely
out of Power's own stipend — a quieter final chapter than the case's opening years would
have predicted.

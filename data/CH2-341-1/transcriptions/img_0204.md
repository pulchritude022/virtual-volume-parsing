The several brethren reported they had kept the fast.** **Compeared John Fouler, and confessed his gross scandalous behaviour in shedding of blood and offering to rob; appointed to profess his guilt in sackcloth before the congregation at Glenluce next Wednesday.** The presbytery, considering the vagabonding of the people found in the presbytery, resolves to meet at Glenluce next Wednesday, Mr John Scott to preach.

**Presbytery at Glenluce, 24 July [1650].** All the brethren in the country present. **Mr John Scott preached on 1 Thess 2.** This day **John Fouler, having stood in sackcloth and been rebuked for his gross offence, is appointed to profess his repentance next Sabbath at Portpatrick, where the offence was committed, and on satisfaction to be released.** **Mr John Scott is appointed to attend the gentlemen and soldiers who agree to go from the bounds toward the army, for the space of a month; and Mr James Lawrie with him a while.** **Mr John Scott reported he found no meeting of the Synod's committee, though he had done diligence attending at Wigton; and that he attended the meeting for the trial of Mr William Thomson, but nothing was yet done therein.** **The presbytery did unanimously report to the committee that no scandalous person should be received in the forces they were to send out.** The next meeting to be at Stranraer …

## Notes & context
★★★ **The Kirk goes to war — an army chaplain and the purge of the ranks.** Two vivid war data. First, **Mr John Scott is appointed "to attend the gentlemen and soldiers … agreeing to go from the bounds toward the army, for the space of a month"** — a Rhins minister sent as **chaplain to the levied men marching to the Covenanter host** (with James Lawrie to help "a while"). Second, the presbytery **"unanimously reports to the committee that no scandalous person should be received in the forces they were to send out"** — the local enforcement of the notorious **purging of the army** before Dunbar. Coming from a sitting weeks before the battle (3 Sept 1650), this is the parish-level texture of the 1650 mobilisation.

★★ **The General Assembly's line on the "malignants from Ireland."** The commissioners returning from the (July 1650) General Assembly report its **mind that those "under the scandal of malignancy and lately come from Ireland to reside in the bounds" are to be proceeded against by process "according to the degree of their guilt."** The presbytery accordingly orders **enquiry into all "who had any accession to the malignant war in Ireland"** — a window onto the flow of royalist/Irish-war veterans into Galloway and the Kirk's determination to discipline them. (Cf. the McDowall officer "long in Ireland," img 197; Frank Wilson taken in the Loch Ryan frigate, img 197.)

★ **Discipline continues under the war.** **John Fouler**'s bloody assault-and-robbery (img 200) is worked through the penitential machinery — **sackcloth at Glenluce, then public repentance "at Portpatrick, where the offence was committed."** A new fornication case (**Margaret Comen**) is cited. And the **trial of Mr William Thomson** at Wigton is still stalled ("nothing was yet done therein").

## Notes & context
★★ **The robbery case resolved against Thomas Baillie.** The sworn witnesses close the "poor Englishman" robbery (imgs 184–217): **David Kennedy deposes that Baillie broke off, told the others "I have nothing to do with this man, ride you on," rode forward and left them — and that the scandal of robbery arose upon Baillie** himself. With Nisbet swearing he knew nothing (img 217), the evidence points the finger squarely at Baillie.

★★ **Death intervenes in the Kennedy adultery — and the year turns.** The **Gilbert Kennedy of Aviolen** adultery cases (imgs 205–215) thin out: **Margaret Morion "was removed by death,"** leaving **Janet Williamson**, whom Dick is to pursue and, if she keeps denying, **put to her oath**. This opening also crosses into **1651**: the stated sitting of **1 January 1651** opens the presbytery's record of the year of Cromwell's occupation of the south-west, still in the ordinary rhythm of the exercise (Bell and Blair on 2 Thess 2).

★ **Engagement satisfaction and a Kirkmaiden cohabitation.** **Thomas McDowall** (Garthland's brother, imgs 212–215) completes his public repentance for the Engagement "a second time" and is **admitted to the ordinance** — the malignancy discipline reaching a resolution. And **Gilbert and Margaret Neilsone**, Kirkmaiden parishioners, confess disobedience and are referred to their session, which is to try **"the scandal of their cohabiting together."** The Commission of the Kirk's **warning** (img 216) is reported read.

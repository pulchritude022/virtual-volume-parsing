# CH2/341/1 — Image 64 (folios 63v–64), October 1644 — **two witchcraft references** remitted to the sessions

Both pages are the **forward register**, and both carry **witchcraft accusations** — this in the
peak years of the **1643–44 Scottish witch-hunt**. Left page (folio **63a**): **Mr George Dick
presents a reference from the Session of Glenluce touching Marioun [—], a Glenluce parishioner
suspect of witchcraft**; depositions of witnesses are begun, but the presbytery, judging it
**more conveniently done in the session**, **remits it back to the Glenluce Session**; then
**Presbytery at Stranraer, 2 October 1644** (John and George Dick exercise; Blair and Bell
reprehended for absence; the Leswalt/Stranraer penitent **Bertoun** beginning repentance). Right
page (folio **64**): **Mr Alexander Turnbull presents a reference from the Session of Kirkmaiden
for the examination of persons suspect of witchcraft — Marioun McCormick(?), Alexander
Semple(?), and another** — with witness depositions; the presbytery again **remits it to the
Kirkmaiden Session**; a **privy trial** approves the members; **George Dick to handle the common
head**; the **Synod** meets in October; signed **Hutcheson, Clerk.**

## Notes & context
- **★ Two witchcraft references — the 1643–44 witch-hunt reaches the Rhins.** In a single
  October-1644 opening the presbytery handles **two separate witchcraft accusations**: one from
  the **Session of Glenluce** (**Marioun [—]**) and one from the **Session of Kirkmaiden**
  (**Marioun McCormick?, Alexander Semple?, and another**), each with **depositions of
  witnesses.** This falls squarely in the **great Scottish witch-hunt of 1643–1644** (one of the
  largest in Scottish history). Tellingly, the presbytery, though it **"took pains,"** in both
  cases **remits the examination back to the kirk session** — the session did the ground-level
  investigation (precognition), the presbytery/ civil magistrate the trial. **A significant,
  sombre new strand** for the wiki: an events node on **witchcraft accusations (1644)** and
  person-stubs for the accused (names uncertain — treat with care and dignity). See
  [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]] and [[mccollough-genealogy-project]].
- **Discipline continues.** The **Bertoun** penitent (img 0062) and a **Leswalt fornicator** (img
  0063) are both **beginning their public repentance** — the slow machinery of satisfaction
  grinding on. (Note the forename of "Bertoun" reads variously — **Helen** at img 0062, **Hew(?)**
  here — reconcile; possibly two different Bertouns.)
- **Ordinary governance.** The weekly **exercise** (John/George Dick), a **privy trial** (all
  approved — now including Power, cf. img 0063), a **common head** set for George Dick, and the
  **October Synod** — the settled rhythm now that the Power crisis is resolved.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `reference fra the Session` = a matter referred up from a
  kirk session for advice/decision; `suspect of Witchcraft` = under suspicion (not yet convicted);
  `depositiones of Witnesses` = sworn witness statements (the precognition); `remitted… to the
  Session` = sent back down to the session to handle; `common head` = the set theological
  commonplace; `privie tryell` = the members' private examination. **Handle the witchcraft names
  with special caution** — several are faded and these were real, vulnerable people. Capital **W**
  for *w/wh*. Dates 1644.

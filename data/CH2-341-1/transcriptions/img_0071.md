# CH2/341/1 — Image 71 (folios 70v–71), April 1645 — building the new kirk of Glenluce; Blair too in Ireland

Left page (folio **70a**): a **repentance case** (John Bertoun, to be received on signs of true
repentance); then **James Ross of Barneil's supplication (10 April)** about **building the new kirk
of Glenluce** — the **1200 merks appointed for it will not suffice**, and there are **bygone
arrears of stipend**; the presbytery **enacts that his necessary disbursements above the 1200 merks
be allowed to him before any minister is admitted to the kirk**, he to bring in his account at the
next Glenluce visitation; and it grants a supplication **to bring in [a minister] to preach at the
new kirk.** Right page (folio **71**): the disbursement arrangement concluded (Ross to be written
for when the kirk is settled); a **privy trial** (all approved); the next meeting set **at the
Synod** (George Dick to handle the common head, thesis ready); signed **Hutcheson, clerk to the
presbytery of Stranraer**; then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 29 April 1645** (Turnbull & George Dick
exercise, Col. 4:15–17), with **Mr James Blair absent — "who is [gone] for [the public] in
Ireland."**

next Sabbath's preaching, signs of true repentance.] **And anent the supplication of James Ross of

## Notes & context
- **A new kirk for Glenluce — the money.** The **building of the new kirk of Glenluce** is under
  way, funded at **1200 merks** — which **James Ross of Barneil** (the local financier/undertaker,
  cf. the Glenluce mortification thread, imgs 0044, 0047) says **will not suffice**; the presbytery
  **enacts that his necessary disbursements above the 1200 merks be reimbursed before any minister
  is admitted**, on audit, with his account due at the next Glenluce visitation. A concrete
  church-building finance vignette, and it ties the long **Glenluce planting/mortification** thread
  to a physical new church. Place/person nodes: **new kirk of Glenluce**, **James Ross of Barneil.**
- **★ Blair too in Ireland.** With **Livingstone** already sent to Ireland (img 0070), **Mr James
  Blair (minister of Kirkcolm) is now also absent "for [the public service] in Ireland" (April
  1645)** — both leading Rhins ministers deployed across the North Channel at once. This underlines
  the **Stranraer presbytery's role as a staging post for Scottish ministers to Ulster** in the
  mid-1640s, and dates Blair's Irish service. Note for his person-page.
- **Ordinary governance.** A **privy trial** (all approved), the **Synod** meeting arranged (George
  Dick's common head/thesis), and the clerk's subscription (**Hutcheson, clerk to the presbytery of
  Stranraer**) — the settled rhythm.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `1200 merks` = the building fund (a merk = 2/3 £ Scots);
  `bygone arrears of stipend` = unpaid past stipend; `necessarie disbursements` = necessary outlays;
  `allowed… before the admission of any` = credited before a minister is settled; `mak the samyn
  forthcuming` = produce it/account for it; `compt`/`compter` = account/accountant (audit); `for
  the publick in Ireland` = on public (church/state) service in Ireland; `penult Day of Aprile` =
  the second-last day (29 April). **Folio 71 outer edge trimmed** — provisional. Capital **W** for
  *w/wh*. Dates 1645.

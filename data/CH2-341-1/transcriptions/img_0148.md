# CH2/341/1 — Image 148 (folios 147v–148)

Two threads from the **30 November 1648** sitting. Left page (foliated **147a** =
folio 147 verso): the presbytery resolves to ★ **write to the presbytery of Wigtown
to try what passed between the Earl of Galloway and the Sheriff** — whether the
Sheriff counselled my Lord "to arrest of money," and his dealing to make his brother
lieutenant-colonel; then ★★ **James Ross of Balneil presents a supplication** (its
full tenor entered): the shire committee charged him with **divers points of
malignancy** on information he was never shown, **refused to give him a copy of the
articles in writing**, and **fined him** — he asks the presbytery to read the
articles so he may clear himself. The presbytery answers it awaits direction from the
**Commission of the Kirk**. The right page (folio 148): a ★ **jurisdictional tussle
over Balneil's papers** — the committee will grant only a "sight" of his process, not
a copy, and sends **Francis Hay** and another with it — and the ★ **Glenluce glebe
and manse controversy**: commissioners are sent to **Garthland** (who declines to
arbitrate), then **Blair and Crosse** are empowered to deal with **Balneil** to
settle the long want of a glebe and manse that injures the minister.

### Modern English
[continued:] The presbytery think it expedient to **write to the presbytery of
Wigtown**, showing and acquainting them what was done, and recommending the [matter]
to them — particularly, concerning the **Sheriff of Galloway**, that they would **try
what passed between the Earl of Galloway and the Sheriff**, and whether or not he had
been a counsellor to my Lord "to arrest of money," as the report is; and the rather,
because the Sheriff confessed the one part of it — namely his dealing with my Lord to
make his brother lieutenant-colonel — and that they would advertise the presbytery
what they find.

The same day, ★★ **James Ross of Balneil presented a supplication** (its tenor
following): *"Unto the Reverend the Moderator and remaining brethren and ruling
elders of the presbytery of Stranraer, I, James Ross of Balneil, humbly show that,
whereas the committee of the shire, upon what information I know not, lately charged
me with divers points of malignancy — whereunto I, being most willing to answer,
have lately several times written and supplicated that the **doubles [copies] of
their articles laid to my charge might be given me in writing**, that I might have
advised thereon and answered in writing again — notwithstanding the same was refused
me, **but I was troubled and also fined**. And I, now remembering it may concern me
in my reputation to be vindicated from all such aspersions before all persons, but
especially before your wisdoms... therefore I humbly crave that you would **read the
said articles, that I might clear myself before you**; and, after my answer thereto,
you might render satisfaction."*

The presbytery, having read and considered the supplication, answer that they **await
direction from the Commission of the Kirk** how to proceed with such as shall be found
guilty of compliance with the late unlawful Engagement...

### Modern English
The presbytery, considering that the **double [copy] the committee holds against
Balneil** might give them more light in their own process against Balneil, appoint Mr
John Dick and Mr John Crosse to **crave of the committee a sight of the same**. The
commissioners, returning, reported that when they desired a **copy** of the articles
and answers, ★ **the committee would not grant a copy — only a "sight" of it** — and
would send two of their number with it to show it to the presbytery. The same day,
[—] and **Francis Hay** came from the committee and showed **James Ross of Balneil's
process** to the presbytery, and took it back with them.

The same day, **Mr John Crosse and Mr James Lowrie** were appointed to **attend
Garthland** and settle the ★ **controversy of the manse and glebe at the old kirk of
Glenluce** (with one at the new). They reported that they had met Garthland at the old
kirk of Glenluce, but that **nothing was done, nor would Garthland take it upon him**
to compose the differences between the two chosen by the presbytery and **James Ross
of Balneil**, since they were not agreed on the way of dealing with the **teinds**.
The presbytery, considering the great injury the minister sustains through the **long
want of a glebe and manse**, appoint **Mr James Blair and Mr John Crosse to deal with
Balneil** for settling the controversy, giving them full power to treat, if they find
Balneil's offer reasonable and for the good of the kirk, and to report their diligence
at the next meeting.

## Notes & context
- ★★ **James Ross of Balneil — a due-process grievance.** Balneil's supplication is a
  rare first-person voice in the register: a heritor charged with malignancy who was
  **fined without being shown the articles against him**, and who appeals to the
  church court for a fair hearing. It documents the friction between the summary civil
  purge and ordinary notions of due process — and the presbytery's own caution (it
  waits on the Commission of the Kirk).
- ★ **Committee vs presbytery over the papers.** The civil committee guards its
  process — granting the presbytery only a supervised "**sight**" of Balneil's
  articles, not a copy, and escorting the papers back. A concrete instance of the two
  jurisdictions negotiating control of evidence in the purge.
- ★ **The Glenluce glebe and manse controversy.** The material problem flagged at the
  visitation (imgs 143–144) now has a named counterparty: **James Ross of Balneil**
  (evidently a heritor/titular of the teinds), with **Garthland** declining to
  arbitrate. **Blair and Crosse** are empowered to negotiate a settlement — the
  minister is said to suffer "great injury" from the long want of glebe and manse.
- **Galloway/Sheriff thread continues:** the presbytery formally asks Wigtown
  presbytery to investigate the **Sheriff's** dealings with the **Earl of Galloway**
  (money-seizure counsel; the brother's lieutenant-colonelcy) — cross-presbytery
  cooperation in the purge.

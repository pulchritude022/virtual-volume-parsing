# CH2/341/1 — Image 82 (folios 81v–82), August 1645 — the "Remedies" concluded (rooted in 1596 & 1638); the Directory begun; a fast kept

Left page (folio **81a**): the **Remedies** list continues (items ~9–14) — make use of the state of
the parish for private prayer; ministers to profit in all company and not drink healths to excess; to
**"cherish the smoking flax, the weak beginnings in the ways of God" and courageously oppose the
oppression of the godly**; as the Lord is now calling all to [repentance], ministers to give
themselves to prayer "in and out of season" and to reading and meditation on Scripture; and the
**prophesying (exercise) of ministers to be observed and not deferred.** Right page (folio **82**):
the list ends (items 15–16) — weak-hearted brethren to be strengthened, "the younger reverencing the
elder, and the elder not despising the younger"; **and, "finally, for the corruption of the ministry
and the remnant thereof, we refer the brethren to the acts of the General Assembly at Dunblane in
1596, revived in the late Assembly at Glasgow in 1638, to be found in the printed acts"** — subscribed
**"Sic subscribitur, J. [Ker?]."** Then **ordinary business resumes**: the brethren report they have
**begun the practice of the Directory on the appointed day**, and that they **kept the fast on 26 July
and the Lord's day following**; **John Corbett in Stranraer, summoned a second time and not appearing,
to be summoned a third**; and **George Gutting, disobedient to the Stranraer session, enjoined to obey
it.**

## Notes & context
- **★ The catalogue's pedigree: 1596 Dunblane → 1638 Glasgow.** The engrossed document (imgs
  0079–0082) closes by **referring the brethren "to the acts of the General Assembly at Dunblane in
  1596, revived in the late Assembly at Glasgow in 1638."** This is a precise and important
  self-placement: the 1645 "catalogue of sins and remedies" consciously **revives the famous 1596
  Assembly's programme of ministerial self-examination and covenant-renewal** (the Dunblane/Edinburgh
  reformation of manners), itself re-enacted by the **great Glasgow Assembly of 1638** that launched
  the Covenanting revolution. A strong historical anchor for the theme node — the "causes of wrath"
  tradition running 1596 → 1638 → 1645. Subscription **"J. [Ker?]"** (the Assembly/Commission clerk —
  verify).
- **★ The Directory in practice.** Following its adoption (img 0077), the brethren now **report they
  have "begun the practice of the Directory on the appointed day"** — the concrete implementation of
  the new form of worship in the Rhins parishes (mid-Aug 1645).
- **The fast kept.** Confirmation that the **26 July humiliation** (img 0078) was duly **observed in
  the several parishes**, with the following Lord's day.
- **Routine discipline resumes.** **John Corbett** (Stranraer) cited a second/third time; **George
  Gutting** enjoined to obey the Stranraer session — the ordinary rhythm returning after the great
  engrossed document.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `cherish the smoaking flax` = nurture weak/new faith (Isa.
  42:3, Matt. 12:20); `instant in prayer in and out of seasone` = 2 Tim. 4:2; `the prophecying… of

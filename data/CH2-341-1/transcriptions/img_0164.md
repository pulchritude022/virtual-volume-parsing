## Notes & context
Three consecutive Stranraer sittings (20 Feb, early March, 19 March 1649), all still turning on the Covenant-renewal apparatus. Two of the three open with the striking note that **"there was no exercise, the kirk being extraordinarily convened"** — i.e. these were special business meetings, not the ordinary weekly exercise, a sign of how much administrative pressure the renewal drive put on the presbytery.

★ **The "conditions of the Kirk" go to Edinburgh.** The subscription terms the brethren were tasked to draft (imgs 162–163) are now **"all drawn up in one roll by the clerk,"** and **Mr James Currie is deputed to carry them to the Commission of the Kirk at Edinburgh.** In parallel, the presbytery **charges every kirk session to return the names of all persons "debarred" [from the Covenant] within its bounds** — the local census of Engagement-compliers that the whole 1648–49 purge depends on. The brethren also report **keeping the fast appointed by the Commission's act.**

Discipline:
- An adultery case from **New Glenluce** ([Torquil?] Mc[…] with **Margaret McDougall**) — the man failed to appear before the presbytery in sackcloth, was sharply rebuked, and referred back to his session.
- **Thomas Adair** of old Glenluce — the recurring fornicator (partner **Margaret Hannay**, running since the 1646 £100 bond, imgs 97–98, 142) — is once more **put to sackcloth** for fornication, referred up from the old-Glenluce session.
- **Patrick McKie**'s process continues to be delayed (softening line, cf. img 163).
- **Balneil's** referral to the Commission of the Kirk (img 162) is noted again as standing.

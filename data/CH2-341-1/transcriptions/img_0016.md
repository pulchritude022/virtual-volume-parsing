# CH2/341/1 — Image 16 (folios 15v–16), Jan–Mar 1643

Left page (folio **15a**) closes French's enlarged paper and the presbytery's
**deferral** — peremptory summonses to be issued for "the last day of probation" —
then the clerk **J. Livingstone** signs, and a new sederunt opens (**Stranraer, 18
Jan 1643**). Right page (folio **16**), in a hastier hand, runs three short
sederunts (**18 Jan, 9 Feb, 2 Mar 1643**): a Colossians exercise series, national
church-state business (valuation sub-commissioners; **Irish-refugee relief**), and
the Power case simmering (**[John] McCulloch** gives in a paper).

### Modern English (paraphrase — low confidence)
[French concludes:] though no crimes deserving deposition are yet proven, and some
things manifestly true could not be brought to light for want of witness evidence,
yet Power's behaviour has been such that he should never again be an instrument of
God to that place. He also entreats the presbytery to consider the "delinquent's"
contempt in **preaching in his own house and close though silenced**, and to appoint
some of their number to give a word of comfort to that "poor destitute people" on
Sabbaths. *Subscribed: French younger.*

Given the thin attendance and the late hour, the presbytery **defers** the Power
business and French's last paper to the next day, and orders the clerk to issue
**peremptory summonses** to the witnesses for the next presbytery day — "the last
day of probation," barring witness disobedience. Next meeting this day twenty; Blair
to make, Turnbull to add. *Signed: J. Livingstone, clerk.*

**Presbytery at Stranraer, 18 January 1643.** Mr James Bower made and [Turnbull]
added on Colossians 2:21–22; approved. [Absences and next-day rota follow, in a
faded/hasty hand.]

### Modern English (paraphrase — low confidence)
**Presbytery at Stranraer, Wednesday 9 February 1643.** Mr Alexander Turnbull made
on Colossians 3:1–2; the second speaker Mr George Dick absent, and the Ruling Elders
of Inch, Kirkmaiden, Glenluce, Stranraer — as also both **French and Mr Gilbert
Power** [present]. Letters were read from the **commissioners appointed by the
General Assembly to attend the Parliament**, desiring the presbytery to send a list
of names from which the parliamentary committee for church matters might choose
**sub-commissioners for valuations** (of teinds); the presbytery named the list and
had the clerk subscribe and send it. A paper given in by **[John] McCulloch
concerning Mr Gilbert Power** was delayed to next day, Power being absent. Next
meeting this day twenty (George Dick to make, John Dick to add, Colossians 3:3).

**Presbytery at Stranraer, Wednesday 2 March 1643.** Mr John Dick added on
Colossians 3:2–3, approved; Mr George Dick should have made but had not been warned.
Absent: Turnbull and the elders of Stranraer, Inch, Kirkmaiden. The Moderator
**intimated the contribution, throughout the whole kingdom, for the help of poor
people coming out of Ireland.**

## Notes & context
- **The volume's rhythm shows.** Alongside the Power case, the presbytery does its
  ordinary work: a rolling **exercise series through Colossians 2–3** (Bower,
  Turnbull, the two Dicks), attendance-keeping, and — twice now — **relief for
  refugees "coming out of Ireland"** (the 1641 Irish Rising drove Protestant
  settlers back across the North Channel to Galloway). The kingdom-wide contribution
  and the **valuation sub-commissioners** (teind valuations, a major 1640s
  parliamentary programme) locate this presbytery inside the Covenanter church-state.
- **Power case, still open.** French wants Power censured for *preaching while
  silenced* and pushes for "the last day of probation"; **[John] McCulloch** (yet
  another McCulloch touchpoint) lodges a paper about Power, deferred in his absence.
  Note both French and Power attend 9 Feb.
- **Clerk identified:** the record is signed **J. Livingstone** — John Livingston,
  the presbytery clerk (elected at folio 2, img_0002).
- **New/So-far figures:** **Mr James Bower** (exercise, 18 Jan), **Mr George Dick**
  (possibly minister at **Colmonell**), **[John] McCulloch** (petitioner re Power).
- **Palaeography applied:** the lower-left and right pages are in a **hastier
  secretary hand** (a different scribe's stint) — hence low confidence; `Iland` =
  Ireland (the "Island"); `subcommissioners for valuations` = teind-valuation
  commissioners; `peremptor summonds` = final/peremptory summons. Dates written
  "1642" are **Old Style** (= 1643, the year changing on 25 March). `y`+superscript
  → *th-*.

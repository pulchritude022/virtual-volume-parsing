# CH2/341/1 — Image 52 (folios 51v–52), Sept–Nov 1643 — the register runs on; the Stranraer kirk visitation; Power "not approven"

Both pages are the **forward-running 1643 register**. Left page (folio **51a**): the tail of a
**September 1643** sederunt — Blair to treat with **the Earl of Galloway**; a **General
Assembly recommendation for a collection for the poor captives "with the Turks"**; a
**correspondence in a trial**; then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 27 September 1643** (exercise
Col. 3:18–19), a **letter from the Committee of Estates** (the 1643 levy), and the beginning
of the **Visitation of the kirk of Stranraer, 4 October 1643** (Livingstone preaching). Right
page (folio **52**): the **Stranraer visitation continued** — the parishioners give their
minister (**Livingstone**) "**a large testimonie**"; the session book is revised (Communion
elements found paid from the poor's box — to be got another way); the ministers and elders
are **privily tried and approved — except Mr Gilbert Power**, "**because of sundrie
miscariages to the presbytery, and slanders yet lying on him untaken away.**" Then
**Presbytery at Stranraer, 1 November 1643** (office rotation: **Mr George Dick moderator, Mr
Hugh Hutcheson clerk**). *(Right page water-stained down the outer margin.)*

### Modern English (partial — page browned)
[Sept 1643 business:] **Mr George Dick to preach at Kirkcolm** till the next presbytery; the
parish to put in its grievance in a subscribed report; **Mr James Blair (with another)
ordained to treat with the Earl of Galloway**, per his letter. A **proclamation** to be read
in the kirks; a **General Assembly letter recommending a collection for the poor captives
"with the Turks"** [Scots taken by Barbary corsairs], read; a **letter from Wigtown
presbytery** seeking correspondence in a trial (**Turnbull to keep it**). The presbytery to
meet **27 September** (exercise, Col. 3:18).

**Presbytery at Stranraer, 27 September 1643.** Livingstone and Hutcheson exercised on Col.
3:18–19, approved; ruling elders of Glenluce, Portpatrick, Kirkmaiden absent, **excused
because of the harvest.** **A letter from the Committee of Estates** about the **levy of a man
by every minister** [the 1643 war effort], read and answered. The ministers reported reading
the proclamation in their parishes; **Turnbull reported his correspondence with Wigtown** in a
trial, to all parties' satisfaction. The presbytery to meet at Stranraer **for a visitation of
the kirk on 4 October.** **Visitation of the kirk of Stranraer, 4 October 1643:** Mr John
Livingstone, minister, preached on his ordinary text (Luke 22:51), approved.

## Notes & context
- **Power "not approven" — the privy trial names his stain.** The **October 1643 Stranraer
  visitation** ends with the ministry and elders privily tried and approved — **"except Mr
  Gilbert power… because of sundrie miscariages to the presbytery, and slanders yet lying on
  him untaken away."** This is the register's own summary of Power's unresolved status: still
  a member, but explicitly **not cleared** — the deposition-process and the appeal have left
  him in limbo. A crisp line for his person-page timeline (and it dates the ongoing stain to
  **Oct 1643**). See [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]].
- **A model kirk visitation.** The **Stranraer visitation (4 Oct 1643)** is a textbook
  example: parishioners examined on the minister *in his absence* (a "large testimony" for
  **Livingstone**), then the minister on the elders; the **session book audited**; a call for
  a **schoolmaster/session-clerk**; and discipline of **"tigling" (tippling/drinking).** Good
  "how a parish was governed" material for a glossary/method node.
- **Communion elements from the poor's box — a small scandal.** The audit found the
  **elements of the Communion (bread and wine) had been paid out of the poor's collection**,
  and ordered them **"procured another way."** A telling detail of parish finance and the
  ring-fencing of poor relief.
- **The national war reaches the register.** A **letter from the Committee of Estates** about
  the **levy of "a man by every minister"** (the 1643 mobilisation for the Solemn League and
  Covenant / the army into England), plus a **General Assembly collection for captives "with
  the Turks"** (Scots seamen enslaved by **Barbary corsairs**) — the presbytery as a conduit
  for national demands. Nodes for the events timeline.
- **Office rotation.** **Mr George Dick becomes moderator and Mr Hugh Hutcheson clerk (1 Nov
  1643)**, relieving Turnbull — the normal half-yearly rotation continues.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `8ber` = October (the "-ber" months numbered from
  March: 7ber Sept, 8ber Oct, etc. — here "October"); `tigling`/`tippling` = habitual
  drinking; `poors collectioun` = the parish poor-box; `captives With the Turks` = Scots held
  by North-African (Barbary) corsairs, ransomed by national collections; `de anima` = the
  Latin common head "concerning the soul"; `severallie tryed` = each examined in turn (the
  privy trial); `quho` = who. **Right page water-stained down the outer margin** — many
  readings provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1643.

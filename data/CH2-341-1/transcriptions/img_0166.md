## Notes & context
★★ **Provenance — the Synod signs off.** This opening carries the **Synod of Galloway's formal attestation: "At Wigtown, the 27 of April 1649, this register, being revised by the Synod, is approved,"** subscribed by the Synod clerk (*Clericus Synodi*). Like the earlier synodical approvals noted in the running spine (e.g. img 143), this authenticates the presbytery book up to this point — a useful anchor for the volume's chain of custody. Note this clerk's habit of **side-margin captions** ("Controversie handled", "Moderator", "Clerk", "caise reexamined", "Balneilis supplicatioune", "correspondence with the Commission"), which makes the 25 April sitting unusually easy to index.

★ **Post-Synod election (25 April 1649):** Mr John Crosse handled the common-head controversy *de statu primi hominis* (Eph 4:22–24) and was chosen **moderator**; **Mr Hew Hutcheson chosen clerk**. (A joint sitting at Wigtown on 17 April preceded it.)

★★ **The Balneil affair turns sour.** Reconsidering Balneil's supplication (given in 4 April, img 165), the presbytery fastens on his phrasing about "your Commissioner" — i.e. **Mr Alexander Turnbull**, who had carried the process to the Commission of the Kirk. Turnbull "confesses himself to be reflected upon" in Balneil's words (that Turnbull, "not being satisfied… gave in reasons to the contrary" and had the process remitted back). The presbytery **interprets this as a slighting reflection on Turnbull** and ordains the moderator to **admonish Balneil.** So Balneil's bid to be reponed to the eldership has backfired into a personal grievance between him and Turnbull — the case, unresolved on its merits, now carries a fresh charge of disrespect. Strong biographical material for both the **James Ross of Balneil** and **Mr Alexander Turnbull** person-pages.

Discipline: **Thomas Adair** now under an explicit **excommunication warning** if he relapses into fornication; his partner **Margaret [Dunn?]** summoned. **Patrick McKie** to be received on testimonial at the next presbytery, "as Balneil" (his case paired with Balneil's). Correspondence with the Committee/Commission and the outstanding **roll of the debarred** pressed again.

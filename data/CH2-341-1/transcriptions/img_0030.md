# CH2/341/1 — Image 30 (folios 29v–30), 11 May 1642 — the Power witnesses examined

The back-filled register continues the **11 May 1642** presbytery. This is the
**actual examination of witnesses in the Power case** (a year before the appeal we
read at imgs 0022–0028): **Mr Gilbert Power was lawfully cited to object to the
witnesses but compeared not**, so they were sworn and examined. The depositions
centre on a **poinding dispute** — Power's goods "driven by French to Wigtown cross,"
where Power **swore the goods were his own** and recovered them. Interleaved:
Irish-refugee relief acts, and an order to copy **James Gordon's libel** — placing
Gordon among the accusers as early as 1642.

### Modern English
[**Marton**, concluding:] posed on French's accusation against Mr Gilbert Power, he
declared he "heard of such a business, but it was all by report"; he heard there were
admonitions given, but what was done he knows only by report; asked about other
particulars, he **craved time to recollect his memory** — granted, and ordered to give
in anything he knows of the libel's particulars; he was interrogated touching **"the
making of the committee of the Synod at Mochrum,"** and that under his hand.

**Patrick Cabrach**, asked if either party gave him "sinistrous counsel," answered no;
posed on what he knows concerning [Martinmas?] and **the goods Mr Gilbert brought back
after they were driven by French to Wigtown cross**, declared he **was at the gate of
Wigtown, met Mr Gilbert, and told him that if he had not ridden fast he would have
missed the goods**; and he heard Mr Gilbert **swear the goods to be his own proper
goods**, and that he got them again. **John McDowell**, likewise, posed on **Mr
Gilbert's goods — whether [poinded] as his own or not, at Wigtown cross** — declared he
**heard him swear the goods to be his own**, whereupon **he got the goods back out of
French's hand.**

**Relief acts:** an act for **£40** for Mr George Dick, to be given to "a honest
Christian in distress"; an act for **50 merks** to the **distressed people in Cumber who
came from Ireland**; and **200 merks** ordained to the town of Stranraer, to be
distributed as before. **French came in and desired to pass from all witnesses but those
already sworn**; and the clerk is ordained to **send a copy of the libel given in by
James Gordon** against [Mr Gilbert Power…]

## Notes & context
- **The Power process, seen from inside.** These are the **sworn examinations** that the
  April-1643 appeal (imgs 0022–0025) was arguing about — now recovered in the
  back-filled register. Power was **lawfully cited but compeared not** to object, so the
  witnesses were sworn in his absence. A reviewer can finally test his appeal-claim of
  unfair witnessing against the actual record of who deposed and on what.
- **The Wigtown-cross poinding.** The core factual episode: French **poinded/drove Mr
  Gilbert Power's goods to Wigtown market cross** (for the debt/horning), and Power
  **publicly swore the goods were "his own proper goods"** and recovered them — witnessed
  by **Patrick Cabrach** and **John McDowell**. This is likely the factual root of the
  "perjury" charge in French's libel (a disputed oath at Wigtown cross).
- **James Gordon an accuser since 1642.** The clerk is to copy **"the libel given in by
  James Gordon against [Power]"** — confirming Gordon was among the accusers **as early
  as May 1642**, not just in the April-1643 paper (img_0025). Strengthens the
  "coordinated faction" reading.
- **Chronology reminder.** This opening (11 May 1642) sits *earlier* than the appeal
  material at imgs 0022–0028 — the register from img_0029 runs the whole process forward
  in date order (see [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]] / the img_0029 note). Expect
  overlap with events already summarised, now in strict sequence.
- **Relief business continues:** £40 (via Mr George Dick) to a distressed Christian; **50
  merks to Irish refugees in "Cumber"** (Cumberland? or a local place); 200 merks to
  Stranraer — the ongoing 1641-Rising refugee relief.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `sinistrous councell` = biased/corrupt advice (a
  standard question to test witness impartiality); `posed` = questioned; `poind` = to
  distrain/seize goods for debt; `gate of Wigton` = road/street of Wigtown; `sould a
  mist` = should have missed; `compt making` = drawing up an account; `committie of the
  Synod at Mochrum` = a Synod commission that met at Mochrum. Capital **W** for *w/wh*.
  Dates Old Style. `y`+superscript → *th-*.

## Notes & context
★★★ **The Laird of Garthland submits — the leading local Engager makes public satisfaction.** This is a narrative climax of the whole Engagement thread. **James McDowall, Laird of Garthland** — the principal Wigtownshire malignant, tracked since imgs 180–213 (his brother Mr Thomas McDowall already satisfied, imgs 212–218) — at last **compears in person with a reference from the Commission of the Kirk**. The registered Commission act (here dated **25 November 1650**, subscribed by its clerk) orders him **"received to satisfaction for his accession to the late unlawful Engagement against the kingdom of England,"** referred to the presbytery/committee to try his repentance, and then **proceeded to public satisfaction.** The presbytery, satisfied with his **"ingenuous confession … with willing obedience and grief,"** appoints him **to stand in the place of public repentance** — the local gentry-Engager brought to the stool.

★ **The Kennedy adultery frustrated, and routine discipline.** The **Gilbert Kennedy of Aviolen** adultery (imgs 205–219) hits an evidentiary wall: both women (**Margaret Morion** and **Janet [Williamson]**, with a third, "Christian") **contradict themselves**, so the oath is forborne and Dick is to **confront them with Kennedy himself.** **Margaret McClounie** gets her **second public admonition** (obstinate, so a third looms); **Arisemman** is still convalescing (cf. imgs 213–219).

- **Lowest-confidence readings (Dad):**
  - **Both folios are stained;** the Garthland reference and satisfaction are the priority readings and moderately secure, but the connective phrasing of the presbytery's act (right foot) is reconstructed.
  - **The reference's date "At [—] the 25 of [November] 1650"** — the place is lost in the stain and the month is a best reading; the Commission of the Kirk sat at Perth/Stirling in this period. Verify.
  - **The clerk's subscription "And. [Ker]"** — very uncertain; identify the Commission clerk who signed.
  - **The women's names** — "Margaret Morion," "Janet [Williamson]," and a "Christian" — are entangled and partly conjectural across imgs 218–220; reconcile at review (see also the img 218 "removed by death" note).

### Modern English
**Presbytery at the old kirk of Glenluce, for visitation, 1 November 1649.** Mr John Scott preached his ordinary (2 Thess 1:7), approven.

**Mr John Scott being removed, the elders were severally examined whether they were satisfied in their minister** — in his doctrine, life, conversation, and exercise of discipline — and answered they were very well satisfied. Asked whether any elder among them was scandalous, they answered they knew none of their elders scandalous. **The minister, called in, was asked whether he was satisfied with his elders** — whether they convened cordially with him in exercising discipline and were free of scandal — and answered he knew none of them scandalous, and had no complaint for want of their concurrence.

**Donald Gifford and [—] Crosse, called, did not compear; ordained to be summoned a second time.**

**The session-books, read and tried, were found in form, except that several material points were omitted and not inserted.** The minister, asked why, answered they had but lately received a new session-book and had not filled it up before it was produced; and that the fault was the clerk's, not his. **The presbytery appoints the book to be written over again.**

### Modern English
**Presbytery at Stranraer, 7 November 1649. Session 1.** The edict for Mr John Park's admission to the kirk of Stranraer had been read and delivered at the congregation, returned and indorsed. **The edict being called, none compeared to the contrary; all professed that they earnestly craved Mr John Park's preaching to them, and that it was their earnest desire the presbytery fulfil [it] by admitting him their minister.**

**Session 2.** Mr John Scott preached Col 4:3. **Mr John Park was admitted to the kirk of Stranraer, according to the ordinance of the Synod.** Absent: [the lecturer/elders of Kirkmaiden?], Inch, etc.

Mr James Blair, appointed to meet with some brethren of Wigtown and Kirkcudbright about the widows and orphans, reported that they attended [and] were appointed to the brethren of the presbytery of Kirkcudbright. **The presbytery appointed Mr Alexr Turnbull, with Sir Robert Adair, to put the act of Parliament anent widows and orphans in execution.**

The presbytery appoints **Mr Alexr Turnbull, Mr John Scott, and Mr John Dick to deal with the heritors of the new kirk of Glenluce, that Mr John Crosse may be satisfied concerning his manse.**

**Jonet McArtour, called, compeared; appointed to stand in the public place of repentance in sackcloth until she gives signs of repentance.**

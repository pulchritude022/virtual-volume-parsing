**Mr John Scott** enters the volume as a candidate and becomes one of its busiest working
ministers. The register follows his **presentation and admission to the charge of Old
Glenluce (Old Luce)** — the presentation delivered, accepted, and his admission carried
through the usual edict and trials — as part of the long
[division and planting of Glenluce](entity:event/the-division-and-planting-of-glenluce).

## A working parish minister

Once settled, Scott is one of the ministers whose absence or presence the court most often
notes, because so much local discipline runs through his session. Business is repeatedly
deferred "by reason of Mr John Scott … being absent," since he is the minister of the parties
concerned — for instance in the [continued cases] of Margaret McClounie and the Arisemman
business [img_226]. He takes his turn in the exercise and sits regularly on the presbytery's
discipline.

## In the record

Scott's steady, high count of appearances reflects a parish minister deep in the ordinary
machinery rather than a figure of the great controversies — a useful counter-weight, in the
wiki, to the ministers remembered mainly for the schism. He appears prominently in the
co-occurrence [network](charts.html) among the core working brethren.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 101 (folios 100v–101), 10–16 September 1646 — the Glenluce Abbey teind-tack settled; a named candidate (Mr John [Crichton?]) to plant Glenluce at last; Agnes McColbie awaited from Ireland

Left page (folio **100a**): (tail of 10 Sept) the **removal of Patrick Mc[—] resolved by compromise** — if
he removes and gives possession willingly, the commissioners are to **discharge him of all bygone rents
for the Abbey and give him a tack till the next [term], 1647**. Then **★ Presbytery at Stranraer, 16
September 1646** — absent Mr Alexander Turnbull and the elders of Kirkmaiden, Leswalt [and others]; **Mr
John Livingstone handled the common head [on election/reprobation], Eph. 1:4–6, and was approved**; the
elders of Glenluce and Portmontgomery absent the day before excused; **★ Mr John Dick reported he had
written to the presbytery in Ireland concerning Agnes McColbie, and received an answer that as soon as she
is recovered she should be sent over**; and **John Vans[?] in Inch compeared and, for his frequent
disobedience to the presbytery and session of Inch, was ordained to stand in sackcloth at the kirk of
Inch till he give signs of repentance, binding himself for his future obedience**. Right page (folio
**101**): **★ the Abbey business concluded** — Mr John Dick (in name of the presbytery) [gave up] the
Abbey, and read a **tack** granted by **Thomas Ker[?] and his brother** to the commissioners (Dick,
Turnbull, Livingstone), **set to Patrick Mc[—], of the whole Abbey and teinds of Glenluce till the next
[term]**, together with a **discharge to Patrick of all bygone mails [rents]**; the presbytery **approved
the proceedings of the moderator and remnant commissioners, and the tack and discharge foresaid**, which
are **delivered to the commissioners to be kept**; **★★ a supplication given in by the laird of Ardwell
and other heritors desiring the settling of what is unsettled, for the providing of the inner kirk of
Glenluce and the planting of it with Mr John [Crichton?]** — whereupon the presbytery **ordained Mr James
Blair, Mr John Livingstone and the laird of Garthland to meet at Glenluce before the next presbytery to
settle the unsettled matters about the glebe and the disbursements for the fabric of the new kirk, and to
report**; and **the case could not be disposed this day for the approaching night, but is to be [taken
up] the next presbytery day, [Mr John Crichton?] to preach** [continued overleaf].

## Notes & context
- **★★ The Glenluce planting finally moves — and a named candidate.** After two years of deadlock (the
  mortification/stipend fight, imgs 0071, 0084–0100), the **heritors (the laird of Ardwell and others)
  petition to settle and plant Glenluce "with Mr John [Crichton?]"** — the **first named candidate** for
  the long-vacant charge. A commission of **Blair, Livingstone and Garthland** is to meet at Glenluce to
  settle the glebe and the new kirk's building costs, and **Crichton is to preach** (as probationer). A
  key turning point; a firm (if faint) new name to track. **Confirm the surname "Crichton"** (it could
  read Crichton/Crombie/Crukbie).
- **★ The Glenluce Abbey teinds put on a footing.** The mysterious "Abbey" of imgs 0100–0101 is now clear
  as **Glenluce Abbey**: the presbytery, holding the abbey's teinds toward the Glenluce stipend, has been
  litigating the **removal of the sitting tacksman Patrick Mc[—]** — now resolved by **granting Patrick a
  fresh tack (from Thomas Ker[?] and his brother) of "the whole Abbey and teinds of Glenluce" and
  discharging his bygone rents**, the presbytery keeping the writs. This ties the Abbey litigation (the
  Edinburgh errand, img 0100) directly to **funding the Glenluce planting** — the temporal machinery
  behind the vacancy. (A rich estate/teind node; confirm Ker and Patrick.)
- **★ Agnes McColbie — the Irish presbytery answers.** The cross-channel pursuit (img 0100) bears fruit:
  the **Irish presbytery replies that "as soon as she is recovered she should be sent over"** — Agnes
  McColbie (still convalescing/confined) to be extradited for her trial once fit. A neat illustration of
  functioning **Scotland–Ulster kirk cooperation.**
- **★ A poor-box and a disobedience penance.** **John Vans[?] in Inch**, for **repeated disobedience to
  the presbytery and Inch session**, is put to **public sackcloth penance** and bound over for future
  obedience — discipline reaching contumacy itself, not just sexual scandal.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `a tack… of ye Whole Abbey and teinds of Glenluce… a discharge…
  of all bygane mailes` = a lease of the abbey's teind-revenues and a release of arrears of rent;
  `desiring… ye planting of it With Mr John [Crichton]` = petitioning to have a named minister settled;
  `to settle… about ye right and building of ye glebe… ye fabrick of ye new kirk` = arranging the glebe
  and the new church's construction accounts; `common head, de electione et reprobatione` = the set
  doctrinal topic (predestination); `stand in sackcloth… for his often disobedience` = public penance
  for contumacy; `for ye approaching night` = because darkness was falling. **Both pages heavily
  water-stained** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Years **1646/1647**.

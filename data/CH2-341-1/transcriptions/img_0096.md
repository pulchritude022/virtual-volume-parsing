## Notes & context
- **★★ The excommunication of the Earl of Seaforth (July 1646).** A major national-history datum: the
  presbytery orders **intimation from every pulpit that the Earl of Seaforth is excommunicated "for his
  great perfidious dealing and lifting up of arms against the cause of God."** **George Mackenzie, 2nd
  Earl of Seaforth**, was a serially side-switching royalist magnate in the north; his excommunication
  by the Kirk in 1646 is here being published in Galloway. Shows the presbytery relaying the supreme
  courts' censures of the great royalists — a companion to the local "malignants" process. (Confirm
  "Seaforth".)
- **★ A collection for "the distress of Argyll."** The brethren are to **contribute for the succour of
  the distress of Argyll and send the money to [Porterfield?] in Glasgow by the last of July** — relief
  for the devastation of Argyll (the MacDonald/Irish and Montrose ravaging of Argyll's country, 1644–45).
  A concrete instance of national war-relief organised through the presbyteries, and a link back to the
  Argyll/Ireland thread (imgs 0091–0092). (Confirm the Glasgow agent's name.)
- **★ Listing the war widows and orphans.** An order to **collect the names of the widows and children
  of those "fallen in the wars"** and send them to the **agent of the kirk** — early organised relief
  for war bereaved, and a poignant social-history datum of the Civil-War toll on Galloway families.
- **★★ The fight to keep Livingstone — a transportation opposed.** **Mr John Livingstone** (Stranraer's
  celebrated minister) has been **called before the committee of the kirk to discuss the planting of the
  parish of [Rowtane]** — i.e. another parish seeking to call him — and **the presbytery sends Turnbull
  and the laird of Garthland to Edinburgh to OPPOSE his transportation.** A vivid, firmly dated (12 Aug
  1646) episode in Livingstone's much-contested career, and evidence of how highly Stranraer prized him.
  (Confirm the parish name "[Rowtane]" — possibly Row/Rhu.)
- **Ordinary business.** The **weekly pulpit supply** of Stranraer during Livingstone's absences (Blair,
  Livingstone, Turnbull in rotation), the **kirk fabric to be repaired by 20 August**, the **session
  book called for** to deal with unsatisfied delinquents, **Agnes McColbie continued**, and the
  **John Dick preaching 15 July** (John 3) — the settled rhythm around the crises.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `intimatioun… fra pulpit… excommunicat for his… perfidious
  dealing, and lifting up of armes against ye caus of God` = a published sentence of excommunication
  against a royalist; `succor of ye Distresse of Argyle… send ye silver to… Glasgow` = a war-relief
  collection; `names of ye Widowes and children of those yt have fallen in ye Warres… sent to ye agent
  of ye kirk` = a register of war bereaved for relief; `called… before ye committee of ye kirk to
  Discusse the planting of ye paroche… to oppose his transportatioun` = a contested translation of a
  minister; `supplied ilk Sabbath… With a minister` = pulpit supply by rotation. **Both pages faded** —

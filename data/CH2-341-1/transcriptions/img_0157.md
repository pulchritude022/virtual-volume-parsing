# CH2/341/1 — Image 157 (folios 156v–157)

The **27 December 1648** sitting resolves several threads. Left page (foliated **156a**
= folio 156 verso): ★ **the Glenluce glebe-and-manse controversy is settled** — Blair
and Crosse report they have agreed terms with **Balneil**, writs to be drawn up; ★★
**the Park↔Airieman bill collapses** — Park produces the committee records showing
**Airieman had acknowledged before the committee that he knew nothing in the glebe to
charge Park with malignancy, and that it rested on misinformation**; **Dick** kept the
fast and **How Hutcheson renewed the Covenant** at Stranraer; **Blair** pressed the
**Sheriff** for a glebe and manse for **Mr John Dick** (Inch); a charitable collection
is granted; and ★ **witnesses against Airieman are sworn**. The right page (folio 157)
records their depositions: ★★ **William Lin [of Larg] and Thomas Gledstone testify that
Airieman said the ministers "ruled the kingdom," that Mr John Levingstoun was a
[hypocrite] who "ruled like a bishop," and that "were it not for Argyll and the
ministry there would not have been so much trouble in the land."** Then: **Balneil's**
case is **delayed to 2 January**; **Patrick McKie** delayed; the ★ **paternity case** —
**Marion** still names only **James McMillan**, whom the witnesses' depositions rule out,
so she is to be processed as obstinate; and the **widows-and-orphans money** (the Sheriff
had consigned it to **Thomas Kennedy**).

## Notes & context
- ★★ **Airieman's malignant speeches, in his neighbours' words.** The depositions of
  **William Lin [of Larg]** and **Thomas Gledstone** give unusually vivid direct reported
  speech of Covenanter-era political talk: that **the ministers "ruled the kingdom,"** that
  **Mr John Levingstoun "ruled like a bishop,"** and that **but for "Argyll and the
  ministry" there would have been less trouble**. This is prime evidence of anti-clerical,
  anti-Argyll sentiment among the Engager gentry — and a serious slander case against
  **How Ronald of Airieman**.
- ★★ **The Park↔Airieman feud resolved for Park.** Airieman's own committee admission —
  that he had nothing to charge Park with and acted on misinformation — collapses his bill;
  both are ordered to make satisfaction, Airieman for the slander. A clean outcome for
  **Thomas Hay of Park**.
- ★ **Glenluce glebe/manse settled with Balneil.** The material dispute running since img
  143/148 is agreed by Blair and Crosse — a concrete resolution for the plantation and for
  Balneil's estate.
- ★ **The paternity case turns.** **Marion** obstinately names only **James McMillan**, but
  the witnesses' timing evidence (the premature birth, imgs 139/144) makes him impossible;
  she is to be **processed as obstinate**. This effectively exonerates James McMillan and
  reframes the case as one of concealment.
- **Widows/orphans money:** the fund is being consigned through the **Sheriff** to **Thomas
  Kennedy** — a step toward the long-pending relief.

## Notes & context
★★★ **The presbytery's rebuttal of Balneil — the other side of the case.** Facing Balneil's eight-point appeal (img 201), the presbytery enters its **formal answer**, and it is combative. Its arguments: (1) the appeal is **procedurally void** — "given in unsubscribed, and without a written appeal"; (2) Balneil is appealing from the **wrong court** — his case was long since **remitted to the Commission of the Kirk (with his sentence appealed in July 1649)**, so any appeal must run from the Commission, not the presbytery, "which has no power to meddle with it"; (3) he **neglected to appeal in time** and now, only because the presbytery presses him, "begins to quarrel"; (4) it is **"not legitimate to quarrel"** at his reasons **"so long *post sententiam latam*"** (after sentence), and to allow it would be a **"dangerous preparative"** licensing every man "pursued for malignancy" to reopen his process; and (5) the decisive moral point — unlike the true penitents (the Engagers who "supplicate in a penitential way"), **"Balneil, notwithstanding his confession and carriage, stands still to his justification,"** i.e. refuses genuine repentance. Together imgs 201–202 preserve a **full adversarial exchange — appellant's grounds and court's answer — in a Covenanter malignancy prosecution**, an unusually complete record of the legal-theological machinery of the 1649–50 purge.

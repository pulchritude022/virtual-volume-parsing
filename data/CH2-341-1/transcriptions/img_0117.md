bill of complaint against the Lady Freugh as slanderer** (referred to the presbytery by the session of

## Notes & context
- **★★ The Ardwell affair resolved into shape — an adultery-by-rumour and a compurgation.** The slander
  matter of imgs 0113 and 0115 now stands fully exposed: **Alexander McCulloch of Ardwell** (of the Ardwell
  estate in Stoneykirk parish) has been **defamed as an adulterer with a woman named Jean Fyfe**, and blames
  **the Lady Freugh** (wife of the Laird of Freugh) as **the author of the slander**. He lodges a **bill of
  complaint against her**. The presbytery, faced with a "flagrant scandal of long standing" and a key witness
  **stuck in Ireland** (she "should have been sent over with one John [—]" but is not), puts the classic
  church-court machinery to work: **the Lady Freugh is asked whether she will undertake to prove the
  adultery — she declines** — and so McCulloch is offered **compurgation ("purge himself"): to swear his
  innocence** since no proof is forthcoming. A vivid, well-documented case of how reputation, gentry rivalry,
  and cross-channel movement collided in the kirk court — excellent material for person-pages on **McCulloch
  of Ardwell**, **the Lady Freugh**, and **Jean Fyfe**, and for a glossary node on **compurgation/purgation**.
- **★ The Stoneykirk visitation.** A second visitation in as many days (Kirkmaiden 30 June, img 0116; now
  Stoneykirk): **Dick preaches and is approved; the elders are exhorted to recruit "qualified men to be
  elders"; the session book is visited and approved.** The instruction to seek new elders is a nice detail
  on how sessions were replenished.
- **Threads moving.** **Anna Mc Colby** (of the McColby scandal, imgs 0115–0116) now **gives signs of
  repentance and humbles herself publicly** — the case resolving toward satisfaction; **Euphame Mc Comb**'s
  **adherence/desertion** case hardens (she flatly refuses to return to her husband, and her master the
  **Laird of Logan** wants her off his land); and the **Ayr and Wigtown correspondence** continues (Dick;
  Bell to Wigtown).
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `bill of complaint… against ye Lady Freuch as slanderer` = a formal
  defamation complaint; `no hope of her… getting over out of Ireland… not certainly known what she speaks` =
  a witness unavailable across the channel; `either to attest… or to purge himself… seeing ye Lady Freuch…
  Wold not undertake to prove ye fact` = offering compurgation when the accuser will not prove the charge;
  `try if they could find qualified men to be made elders` = recruiting session elders at visitation;
  `session booke Was then visited and approved` = the register passed at inspection; `adhere to her husband…
  she Wold not` = a desertion/adherence cause. **Both pages faded; the right torn on the outer margin** —
  the alleged partner ("Jean Fyfe"), the Ardwell forename, and the Stoneykirk date are provisional. Capital
  **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1647**.

### Modern English
The brethren who conferred with **the Laird of Garthland report that he promises to keep the Commission of the Kirk's diet in May.** The presbytery thinks fit to **delay censuring Robert Campbell and his son until the Synod**, wishing the Synod's advice. Allan Gill has satisfied the session of Kirkmaiden.

**Mr James Boyd compeared and gave signs of repentance for his fornication and disobedience**; referred to the Stranraer session for censure.

**Mr John Crosse signified that Allan Gill had carried himself most scandalously at the time of examination — railing at Carleton, and upbraiding and mocking the minister when questioned about his knowledge of the principles of Christianity.** Allan Gill, called, compeared; **ordained to stand in the public place of repentance next Lord's day at Kirkmaiden for his scandalous carriage at the examination.** George Alexstone summoned a second time.

To advise at the Synod concerning [marriage], maintenance, and persons coming from Ireland (as Capt Stewart).

### Modern English
Mr John Crosse reported that **Margaret [Flowand] McCredie and Jonet McCredie**, guilty of a lapse in fornication, had already stood twice in the public place of repentance, and that Margaret at several times gives signs of repentance; he leaves it to the presbytery what further should be done. Crosse to revisit them.

**John Lillie, commissioner from Queensferry, produced the summons indorsed, summoning the presbytery and the minister of Inch to compear and hear/see the minister [Mr John Dick] transported from the kirk of Inch to the kirk of Queensferry. The presbytery, considering it, found that the summons was not formally deduced [served], and therefore thought fit not to enter on the matter of Mr John Dick's transportation until the summons is formally deduced. John Lillie appealed from this sentence to the Synod**, and all his papers were returned to the moderator.

The presbytery, considering it necessary to have the Synod's advice about putting the **act of Parliament concerning the poor** in execution. **John [Currie] appeared before the presbytery in sackcloth and gave signs of repentance**; remitted to the session of [Inch] for his injunctions. The brethren, severally removed, had their privy trials approved. **Mr James Bell, clerk.** [End of presbytery.]

## Notes & context
★★ **Mr John Dick's transportation to Queensferry is blocked — on a technicality.** The Queensferry call (img 186) is defeated for now: **the presbytery finds the summons "not formally deduced" (improperly served) and refuses to enter on Mr John Dick's transportation** until it is. **John Lillie, Queensferry's commissioner, appeals to the Synod** — so the fight over Dick moves up a court, exactly as the Livingstone-transportation disputes did. The presbytery is defending its minister of Inch by procedural means.

★ **The Engagement satisfactions wind toward the Synod.** Garthland promises to keep the **Commission's May diet**; Robert Campbell's censure is **held over for the Synod's advice**; and the presbytery decides to consult the Synod on **marriage, ministers' maintenance, incomers from Ireland (Capt Stewart), and the execution of the poor-relief act** — a tidy agenda for the coming Provincial Assembly.

★ **Discipline vignette — Allan Gill's outburst.** A colourful case: **Allan Gill "carried himself most scandalously at the time of examination — railing at Carleton and upbraiding and mocking the minister"** when catechised on the principles of Christianity, and is put to public repentance at Kirkmaiden. Also: **James Boyd** (fornication/disobedience) and the two **McCredie women** (repeated fornication) making their repentances; **John Currie** appears in sackcloth. Clerk this sitting = **Mr James Bell**.

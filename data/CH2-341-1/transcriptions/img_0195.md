### Modern English

## Notes & context
★★★ **A named witch-trial: Agnes Little, alias "Green-bittle."** The 1650 witch-hunt (ordered imgs 189, 191) now has a defendant. A **vagrant woman "haunting within the bounds but having no constant residence in any one parish"** is delated for witchcraft by **three ministers at once (Portpatrick, Stoneykirk, and New Luce)**; she **utterly denies** ("in less or more"); the presbytery cannot get caution and so has the **magistrate of Stranraer ward (imprison) her** pending trial, and orders the brethren to **draw up the formal delations of her "manifold malefices" before witnesses, toward obtaining a [judicial] commission.** This is the classic machinery of a Scottish witch process — presumption, denial, warding, the gathering of *malefices*, and the petition for a commission of justiciary — captured in a single sitting. Her by-name **"Green-bittle"** ("bittle" = a beetle/mallet, or a nickname) is a vivid piece of local memory. To follow as the corpus continues.

★★ **Garthland referred back down to the presbytery.** The **Laird of Garthland** (the McDowall Engagement penitent, imgs 180–181, 185–186), via a letter from **Mr James Blair at Edinburgh**, having narrowly missed the **Commission of the Kirk** (it "had risen ere he came"), **renews his accession to the Covenant** and petitions that his case be **referred back to the presbytery** — the reconciliation of the leading local Engager entering its last stage.

★ **The duel and Engagement satisfactions close out.** **Gilbert Kennedy of Aviolen** has performed his sentenced **public confession at Leswalt and Stranraer** (imposed at img 190) — confirming the opponent's designation as **Kennedy of Aviolen** (imgs 188, 190) over the "Cochrane of Chappell" of imgs 184–186. **Alexr Agnew in Dindinnie received to the Covenant** (cf. img 190). The subscribed Engager declarations are lodged with the new clerk **Mr James Bell.**

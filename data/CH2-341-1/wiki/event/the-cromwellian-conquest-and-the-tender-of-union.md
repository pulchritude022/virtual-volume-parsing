By 1651 the English army had overrun Scotland, and in 1652 the Commonwealth moved to absorb
the conquered kingdom outright — the "**Tender of Union**," an offer (backed by force) to
incorporate Scotland into a single Commonwealth with England. The Stranraer presbytery's
response, recorded in the volume's closing folios, is one of the more remarkable survivals in
the book: a formal **pulpit-protest against the union itself.**

## A warning and declaration against the union

In the spring of 1652 the presbytery **draws up a "warning and declaration," revises it, has
it read "in face of presbytery," and ordains it read from every pulpit** on a Lord's day
[img_254–255]. Its targets are named plainly: "the snare … of the English," "the foundation
of union betwixt the kingdoms," and "unlimited obedience to the [usurped] authority and
arbitrary command." It is cast in the full Covenanting idiom — a catalogue of the national
sins and "causes of wrath" behind the disaster, closing with a peroration to "be strong in
the Lord … and bring them again to the New Jerusalem in the latter days" [img_255].

## Read from the pulpits "with all diligence"

The declaration is not merely drafted and filed: at the following sitting the brethren report
that they have read it from the pulpits "as recommended," and undertake to continue doing so
"with all diligence" [img_257]. A local court, in a conquered country, using its one
remaining instrument — the Sabbath pulpit — to warn its people against submitting to the new
order.

## The last months under a new power

The union business runs in the same weeks as the
[Resolutioner–Protester schism](entity:event/the-resolutioner-protester-schism) and the
winding-up of the [Dunbar prisoners' relief](entity:event/the-dunbar-prisoners-relief), so
the volume's final openings read as a court trying to keep the ordinary machinery of
discipline turning while the political ground gives way beneath it. The register ends in
1652, its supervision signed off by the Synod clerk on 8 June — the last dated act of the
book.

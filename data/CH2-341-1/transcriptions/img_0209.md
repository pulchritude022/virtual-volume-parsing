## Notes & context
★★ **The manse at Inch, itemised and valued (21 Aug 1650).** A rare, precise piece of **material/economic history**: a sworn valuation by **James Harper, mason, and George McDowall, carpenter** of the building works Mr John Dick raised at his own charge on the Inch benefice since his entry — **55 merks (stonework: dwelling-house and chamber), 105 merks (timber: roofs, doors, windows), 36 merks (a barn built off the ground), 30 merks (thatching all the houses)** — some **226 merks** of improvements. The kind of exact costing of a 17th-century Scottish glebe/manse rarely preserved. (The building was itself interrupted by "the troubles of the time," img 208.)

★★ **The Ireland malignancy vetting continues — the Lord of Ardes and George Munro.** **William Young, "sometime a captain of foot in Ireland,"** is examined whether he served **"under the command of the Lord of Ardes [the Montgomery Viscount of the Ardes, Ulster] or George Munro while they were carrying on a malignant design"** — he denies it, and must produce a testimonial from the Ulster parish where **"Mr Anthony and Mr Thomas Dobbes"** served as ministers. Together with imgs 207–208 this builds a clear picture of the Kirk scrutinising the stream of soldiers back from Ulster, distinguishing "malignant" royalist service (Ardes/Munro) from acceptable Parliament service (Wilfred Campbell, img 208).

★ **The Gilbert Kennedy scandal — a forged/suborned bill.** The adultery case (img 208) turns on documents: **Jonet McMillan's "bill"** to the session is judged to be **in Gilbert Kennedy's own hand** (she admits writing it at his prompting, via one **John Kennedy "commonly styled Rottenwoss"**); her husband distances himself. **Kennedy fails to compear** and is cited with certification. And a slander offshoot: **Margaret Dalgleish** confesses "rashly and foolishly" reproaching **Mr John Scott** and his ministry — sackcloth at Glenluce. The presbytery also debates whether to copy **Balneil's long "observations"** into the register (continues next opening).

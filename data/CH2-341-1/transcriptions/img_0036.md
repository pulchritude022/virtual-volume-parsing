# CH2/341/1 — Image 36 (folios 35v–36) — Gordon's remaining witnesses; the register resumes at May 1643

Left page (folio **35a**) finishes the witness depositions on **James Gordon's
accusation** (25 May 1642): **Hugh Wallace, Thomas McNaught, and Alexander Cunning**
corroborate the Sabbath-alehouse and the **subscribing of the Covenant by Mr James
Adamson**; then **Mr Alexander Turnbull** deposes on the **"lying/bribes" charge** —
Power had said that Turnbull, **Mr John Livingston, and Mr George Dick** "had gotten
**buds** [bribes]… to be his enemy" (from **Ardwell** and **Killaster**), which Turnbull
denies. Right page (folio **36**) closes the inserted 1642 material with a **notarial
instrument** (about impeding the presbytery's sitting) and then — crucially — the
**live register resumes: "Presbytery at Stranraer, 19 May 1643."** *(The right page's
ink is badly browned; much is low-confidence.)*

### Modern English
**Hugh Wallace**, sworn (no sinistrous counsel), declared **"in everything as Uchtred
[McCulloch] did,"** adding he saw Power tender **Mr James Adamson the Covenant to
subscribe, which Adamson did.** **Thomas McNaught**, on oath, deponed on the
**Sabbath-breaking "just as the other two did"**; on receiving **Mr James Adamson**, he
heard Power speaking to him and Adamson made a confession, but **whether Power gave him
the Covenant he could not see, being aloft in a loft**; and Power **laboured a parcel of
ground beside his glebe.** **Alexander Cunning**, sworn, declared he heard Power **read
two or three verses of a psalm**, then speak to the people about **Mr James Adamson**,
who made a confession, and **Power brought the Covenant to the table and Adamson
subscribed it.**

**Mr Alexander Turnbull**, sworn, declared **the "bribes" charge**: Power had said that
**Mr John Livingston, Mr George Dick, and Turnbull himself "had gotten buds [bribes] to
be his enemy"** — as for Livingston, [Power] would tell nothing "till the business was
closed"; that **Mr George Dick "ran in the boys of promise, which was a bribe"**; and
that Turnbull's **own bribe was to have been "a stone, or a half [of wool], from Ardwell,
and somewhat from Killaster"** — which Power "would not name till further trial, because
Mr Alexander **denied he did take any bribe.**"

## Notes & context
- **Structural pivot: the back-fill ends; the live register resumes at 19 May 1643.**
  The dated heading **"Presbytrie at Stranrawer May 19. 1643"** marks the end of the
  inserted 1642 material (imgs 0029–0036) that clerk **Mr George Dick** was ordered to
  copy in (img_0029: "from 30 March 1642 till… 18 April [1643]"). From here the register
  is the **current, forward-running record**. Confirms the chronology note in
  [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]]: manuscript order jumps back to May 1642 at img_0029
  and rejoins "the present" (May 1643) here. **Roles have rotated**: by 1643 **Mr John
  Dick is moderator** and **Mr Alexander Turnbull is clerk** (in 1642 it was Turnbull
  moderator / George Dick clerk).
- **The "bribes/lying" charge, fully deposed.** Turnbull's evidence is the concrete form
  of Gordon's article 3 (img_0025): Power allegedly said **Turnbull, Livingston, and
  George Dick took bribes** ("buds" — a stone/half-stone of **wool** from **Ardwell**,
  "somewhat from **Killaster**"; George Dick "ran in the boys of promise"). Turnbull
  **denies** it. So the "lying" charge = Power's alleged slander that his own brethren
  were bought — a serious intra-presbytery accusation that helps explain why the court
  closed ranks against him.
- **Mr James Adamson received into the Covenant.** Three witnesses independently confirm
  Power **tendered the Covenant to Mr James Adamson**, who **made a confession and
  subscribed** — the "receiving a conform minister-refugee from Ireland" charge
  (Ellaster, img_0019). **Mr James Adamson** is very likely that refugee minister (and
  possibly Power's kinsman); a strong candidate identification — flag to confirm.
- **An attempt to impede the presbytery.** The notarial instrument (top of folio 36)
  records someone trying to **impede the "down-sitting" of the presbytery**, with the
  **Sheriff of Galloway younger (Agnew of Lochnaw)**, **Adair of Kinhilt**, and two
  **McDowells** as witnesses — the Power faction's gentry allies. Worth a wiki note on
  how the dispute spilled into obstruction.
- **New people/places:** **Patrick Agnew of Sheuchan** (ruling elder, 1643), **Adair of
  Kinhilt**, **Patrick McDowell of Creichton**, **John McDowell of Crossis**; places
  **Sheuchan**, **Kinhilt**, **Ardwell**, **Killaster**.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `buds/budds` = bribes; `hersell` (uncertain) and
  `ran in the boyes of pronis` = an obscure idiom for taking an inducement ("the boys of
  promise"); `buird` = table/board; `in re nata` = on a matter newly arisen; `took
  instruments` = had a notary record a formal protest. **Right page ink badly browned** —
  the lower half is largely conjecture. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates Old Style.

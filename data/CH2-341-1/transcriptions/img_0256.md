# CH2/341/1 — Image 256 (folios 255a–256)

## Folio 255a (left page)  *(confidence: low)*

*The **aftermath of Mr Alexr Turnbull's protestation** (img 255). The presbytery takes his desire to a conference and finds **"the said brethren were not all of one judgment concerning it": Mr Alexr Turnbull declares he "counted it to be a matter of conscience" and "could neither come nor go" upon the paper; Mr James Lawrie desires first to be present and would confer "upon the whole matter from the beginning"; Mr John Park declares his own purpose** — the local Resolutioner/Protester fault-line laid bare in a procedural wrangle over what may be entered in the register. The presbytery defers: any answer to be "read, considered and applied at the next meeting." Then a new sitting opens: **"A presbytery at Stranraer, the 14 April 1652"** (Mr John Dick to make the exercise). Sederunt begins. Faded, browner ink up top.*

## Notes & context
★★ **The Protester protestation goes into the register — and splits the brethren three ways.** The most telling entry is the handling of **Mr Alexr Turnbull's protestation** (img 255): the presbytery agrees it be **inserted in the register with its own answer**, but a conference exposes that **"the said brethren were not all of one judgment."** The three positions are minuted with unusual candour — **Turnbull holds it "a matter of conscience" and will not budge; Lawrie wants to re-argue "the whole matter from the beginning"; Park states his own qualified position** — a rare, granular snapshot of how the national Resolutioner–Protester rift played out as day-to-day procedure inside one presbytery. (This again groups Park and Lawrie oddly with the Protester Turnbull; the alignment remains a puzzle — see img 255.)

★ **Absent elders disciplined; a scandal bound over to the Synod; McDowall's adultery still open.** Under the new **14 April 1652** sitting: the presbytery cracks down on **ruling elders "frequent and often absent,"** ordering their ministers to compel attendance; **Mr James Blair's conference** [with Turnbull and a Steuart] ends unsatisfied, so the moderator is to deal with **Mr William McDonald** and else refer it to the **Synod**; and **Alexr McDowall "in Drumfad," posed on his adulterous scandal [with Cristian McDowall], is "not sensible"** and the process is continued once more (imgs 246–253) — the volume's most persistent disciplinary thread, still unresolved this near the end.

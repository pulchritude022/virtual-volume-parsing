# CH2/341/1 — Image 34 (folios 33v–34), 25 May 1642 — Marton's deposition signed; Power's protestation

Top of the left page (folio **33a**) closes **John McCulloch of Marton's deposition** —
"This I testify to be true, according to the oath I have made" — **subscribed
"Myrton,"** confirming the deponent as **of Myrton**. The presbytery then reconvenes at
**Stoneykirk, 25 May 1642** (**Mr George Dick** preaching, supplying the suspended
Power's pulpit). **Mr Gilbert Power demands a copy of his whole process**, taking
**notarial instruments in Gilbert Moore's hand**; the moderator takes counter-
instruments. Power then **gives in a Protestation** — the text (both pages) **appeals
to the General Assembly**, complaining that French keeps **shifting the accusers' names
"pro re nata,"** and demanding the whole process be transmitted and that nothing
prejudge him meantime.

### Modern English
[Marton's deposition, concl.:] "…because I knew nothing of it. This I testify to be
true, according to the oath that I have made." **Subscribed: Myrton.**

**Mr Gilbert being called, and the messenger not present to verify the summons**, the
presbytery orders the clerk to issue a new summons to **25 May at Stoneykirk**.
**Presbytery at Stoneykirk, 25 May 1642; Mr George Dick preached.** **Mr Gilbert came in
and desired a copy of his whole process, taking instruments in the notary Gilbert
Moore's hand** of his desire; and the moderator, in the presbytery's name, **took
instruments in the same notary's hand of the court's not refusing** his request —
provided he give in his desire under his hand, as had been their practice from the
beginning.

**Mr Gilbert Power gave in a Protestation**, as follows: "**Protestation of Mr Gilbert
Power, minister at Stoneykirk, to the presbytery of Stranraer.** Forasmuch as I have been
accused before you by **Uchtred McDowell of French and his adherents** of divers grievous
crimes — for which, in matter as in manner of procedure, I have been forced to **appeal
from you, and from the Provincial Assembly of Galloway, to the next General Assembly** —
craving that it might be ordered that my pursuer and his adherents produce the whole
process, protestations, supplications, appellations, and every other **adminicle**
[supporting document] thereto; and they, having given their appearance the…"

### Modern English
"…before the said General Assembly, **that the matter might be put to a final decision
there.** And because I find the said pursuer, his adherents, plotters, and conveeners to
this in continual action before you — **running the complaints sometimes in one, sometimes
in another of their names** (which have mostly been given in by the principal pursuer in
his own name, and thereafter, under his hand, **in the name of another and of a third
person**), which, as they meet with difficulties, they **change *pro re nata*** [as the
occasion arises] — therefore I did press that my appellations and protestations be
brought before the General Assembly to insist there. Notwithstanding these appellations
and protestations, **you are pleased to proceed still, contrary to all equity and form
above used.**

"Wherefore I am forced, anew — for preventing all seeming dispensations — to **repeat my
whole former supplications, appellations, and protestations**, and declare that I adhere
to them and their whole reasons; and to desire again (without prejudice to my former
desires) that **the process, with its pursuers and adherents, be ordered by you to be
produced and conveened before the General Assembly as it presently stands**, and they
appointed to insist there; that **you in no way prejudge anything already proceeded, but
that I be heard there to give in my lawful defences and answers, and to object** as I
shall have occasion and think expedient; and that **no further be proceeded in, nor I
prejudged, by your [matter / procedure]…**"

## Notes & context
- **"Myrton" confirmed.** Marton's deposition is **subscribed "Myrton"** — settling that
  the deponent laird is **John McCulloch of Myrton** (the principal McCulloch seat, near
  Monreith), the same man written "of Marton" throughout. Anchors the
  [[mccollough-genealogy-project]] to a documented family.
- **Power escalates to the General Assembly.** Having been suspended (1642) and losing at
  presbytery/Synod, Power now **protests and appeals to the General Assembly**, on two
  grounds a modern reader recognises: (a) the whole process should be **transmitted intact**
  to the higher court; (b) meantime the presbytery must **not prejudge** him. The 1643
  appellation we read earlier (imgs 0022–0025) is the mature form of this same strategy.
- **The "shifting accusers" complaint — Power's sharpest point.** He alleges French
  **launders the same complaints through different names** — his own, then "another," then
  "a third person" — **changing them *pro re nata*** as each meets difficulty. This is
  Power's counter-narrative to the "three coordinated accusers" (French, Ellaster,
  Gordon): from his side, it is **one man litigating under three masks.** A crucial
  interpretive tension to carry into the wiki.
- **Notary Gilbert Moore.** Power's demand for a process-copy is formalised by **taking
  instruments in the notary Gilbert Moore's hand** — and the moderator takes counter-
  instruments in the same notary's hand. A named local notary; note the careful
  paper-trail both sides keep.
- **Mr George Dick supplies Stoneykirk.** With Power suspended, **Mr George Dick preaches
  at Stoneykirk** (25 May 1642) — supporting that the "Mr George" who is *minister of
  Glenluce* (img_0031) is a **different** man (Park?), while Dick is the clerk who supplies
  the vacant Stoneykirk pulpit.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `pro re nata` = as the matter newly arises / to suit
  the occasion; `adminicle` = a supporting/accessory piece of evidence (Scots law);
  `forsameikle as` = forasmuch as; `took instruments` = had a notary formally record a
  protest/demand; `of new` = anew; `abou wsed` = heretofore used. Capital **W** for *w/wh*.
  Dates Old Style. `y`+superscript → *th-*.

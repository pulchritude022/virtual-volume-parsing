## Notes & context
★★★ **The Presbytery of Stranraer's warning-and-declaration against the Cromwellian union (1652) — to be read from every pulpit.** The most historically significant document in this stretch of the volume. Having already **"given public warning that the people beware of the snare contained in the declaration of the English,"** the presbytery here **formally adopts a "warning and declaration," drawn up by a committee and revised by the whole presbytery, and orders it "read publicly in each several pulpit … upon a Lord's day."** Its text — a Covenanting manifesto — laments a "day of darkness … and hard oppressions," warns of "the sinful snare … so openly spread for the entangling of souls in this day of temptation," reasserts the obligations of the **Solemn League and Covenant** and the "received government" of the Kirk (the Book of Church Government), and — crucially — sets its face against **"the foundation of union betwixt the kingdoms"** and against **"unlimited obedience to the [usurped] authority and arbitrary command."** This is the Rhins clergy's minuted, congregation-facing resistance to the **English incorporating union / Tender of Union of 1652** — a vivid local counterpart to the national struggle, and a rare survival of the actual pulpit-warning ordered against the Commonwealth.

★ **Ordinary discipline around the manifesto.** The declaration is framed by routine business: **Agnes McKelue** of new Glenluce, cited for fornication, repents and is remitted to her session; and the **Laird of Garthland** process (the Rhins Engager, imgs 220–253) continues, with **Turnbull, Crookes, and Lawrie** appointed to confer with him at Garthland — a reminder that the presbytery pursued its old penitents even as it braced the whole community against the new English power.

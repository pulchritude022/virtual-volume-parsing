The regional magnate whose patronage rights and estate arrears the presbytery has to
navigate on several unrelated fronts across the volume — appearing both as a cooperative
patron and, elsewhere, as a name the presbytery has to press for money.

## Patron of Kirkcolm

As patron of **Kirkcolm**, the Earl of Galloway is the party the presbytery presses to pay
stipend/teind arrears owed to the estate of the late **Mr John Porterfield** (minister of
Kirkcolm), threatening to refer the matter to the Lords of Session if he does not
[img_0055–0056 region]. He is also asked, in the Power case's aftermath, to help broker
peace between the restored Power and his Stoneykirk parishioners [img_0062].

## A name on the 1648 Engagers register

The same title appears, separately, on the presbytery's named 1648 register of Engagement
compliers presented to the civil committee — one of several regional grandees whose wartime
choices the Kirk party's post-Preston purge sought to reckon with
[entity:event/the-post-engagement-purge-of-malignants, img_146]. Note that "the Earl of
Galloway" and "Earl of Galloway" are merged onto this one page; whether every mention refers
to the same office-holder across the volume's ~1641–1652 span (a title can pass to an heir)
has not been independently verified.

Minister of **Kirkmaiden** and the volume's most persistently present figure — clerk,
moderator, disputant, and the man Mr Gilbert Power accused (falsely, on the presbytery's own
finding) of taking bribes.

## Clerk, then moderator, then commissioner

Turnbull's offices rotate with everyone else's across the decades: clerk during the live
1642 register (with George Dick as moderator) [img_0043], then moderator by 1643 with John
Dick as clerk [img_0034]. By 1649 he serves as the presbytery's own **commissioner to the
Commission of the Kirk** in the Balneil case — a role prominent enough that when Balneil's
supplication is read as containing a slight against "your Commissioner," it is read as a
reflection on Turnbull specifically, and the moderator is directed to admonish Balneil over
it [img_166, entity:event/james-ross-of-balneil-s-malignancy-process].

## Accused, and cleared, in the Power case

Power alleged, among his procedural counter-charges, that Turnbull (along with Livingstone
and George Dick) had taken "buds" (bribes) from Ardwell and Killaster. Turnbull denied it,
and nothing in the surviving folios substantiates the charge — it reads as part of Power's
broader strategy of discrediting the men judging him
[entity:event/mr-gilbert-power-s-deposition-and-reposition].

## A theologian among administrators

Turnbull is also one of the presbytery's more active disputants in the regular "common
head" exercises that structure weekly presbytery business — assigned, for instance, *de
redemptione* — and is repeatedly sent on diplomatic errands: to Edinburgh with Garthland to
resist Livingstone's transportation [img_96], and to keep correspondence with the
presbytery in Ireland on the Kirk's behalf [img_139].

## Notes & context

This opening contains the **body of the 18-article libel** that McDowell of French
(younger) laid against **Mr Gilbert Power, minister of Stoneykirk**, subscribed
"French younger" — followed by the opening of **Power's written answers**. It is
the documentary heart of the scandal that has run since img_0002.
- **The whole libel is now on the page.** French (younger) of that Ilk charges Mr
  Gilbert Power, minister of Stoneykirk, with (compiling from img_0006 + this
  opening): cloaking adultery, usury, oppression, sowing sedition, ordinary lying,
  Sabbath-breaking, engrossing/profiteering (butter), drunkenness, carding/gambling,
  calumny, perjury (twice), cloaking fornication, wrongful eviction, writing a
  false testament, and oppressing a poor widow into a 500-merk bond. An unusually
  complete indictment of a Covenanter-era minister.
- **Cross-references confirmed / extended:**
  - *False testament* → **umquhile Peter McCulloch** (the "Peter McCulloch" of
    img_0003, now confirmed dead: "umwhyle" = the late).
  - *500-merk bond / widow* → **Charles Campbell** (whose ruling-elder commission
    Power tried to reject in img_0006 — because Campbell is a party!) and his wife
    **Margaret [?]**, the poor widow.
  - *McHum's oxen* → the deposition of img_0004–0005.
  - *Usury / oppression* → the corn payments and annualrent of img_0002, 0005.
- **New named figures:** the lairds of **Ardwell** and **Garthland** (the latter
  deceased, with a deathbed line quoted), **Francis Hay** (butter), **Captain
  Maxwell** (perjury venue), "the **old Sheriff**" and **Logan** (alleged
  witness-suborners), **Elias McFadzen** of Kirkmaiden (evicted), **Robert Tarbret**
  (the adulterer Power allegedly cloaked).
- **Power's defence** opens by *denying* the adultery-cloaking charge, pointing out
  he himself repeatedly cited Robert Tarbret to session and presbytery — i.e. he
  prosecuted the sin rather than concealed it. His full answers continue on the
  next opening(s).
- **Palaeography applied:** `sawing` = sowing; `umwhyle`/`umquhile` = the late,
  deceased; `evidents` = title deeds/writs (Scots law); `by the strong hand` = by
  force/violence; `ingrossed ... to the dearth` = engrossed (cornered) a commodity
  and sold at scarcity price; `carding` = playing cards/gambling; `calumniator` =
  slanderer; `Sic subscribitur` (Latin) = thus subscribed. `y`+superscript → *th-*.

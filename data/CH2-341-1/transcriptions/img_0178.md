### Modern English
**Presbytery at Stranraer, *pro re nata* [specially convened], 25 October 1649.** Mr John Crosse chosen moderator, Mr James Bell clerk. Absent: Mr Alexr Turnbull and his ruling elder, and the ruling elders of Stoneykirk, Portpatrick, and the new kirk of Glenluce.

**The same day the Provost, Bailies, and Council of the town of Stranraer supplicated the presbytery that, since the Synod had transported Mr John Park from Mochrum to Stranraer, the presbytery would use all expedition to receive the said Mr John as minister at Stranraer.** The presbytery, taking the supplication to consideration, **ordained that the edict be read and served next Lord's day, and [Park] admitted on Wednesday se'nnight, being the 7 of November.**

Mr John Dick appointed to preach at Stranraer next Lord's day. **Mr John Scott appointed to preach at Stranraer at the admission of Mr John Park to the kirk of Stranraer, Wednesday 7 November.** The next presbytery to be at the new kirk of Glenluce on Wednesday next, the country being cited for the **visitation of both the old and new kirks** (per the Wigtown appointment). Mr James Bell appointed to go to Mochrum se'nnight [to supply Park's old charge].

### Modern English
**Presbytery at the new kirk of Glenluce, for visitation, [31] October 1649.** Absent: Mr James Blair, who had a meeting appointed at Wigtown concerning the widows and orphans, per the Synod's ordinance; and the ruling elders of Kirkcolm, Leswalt, Portpatrick, Stoneykirk, and Inch.

Mr John Crosse preached his ordinary, Hosea 6:5, and was approven.

**Mr John Crosse being removed, the session and elders were called and asked particularly concerning his carriage in doctrine, government, and discipline** — whether they were edified in his doctrine, whether he was honest in life and conversation, whether he kept up discipline diligently, whether he was painful in catechising and visiting the sick. **The said Mr John was approved by them all unanimously in every particular.** The session-books delivered to Mr John Dick and Mr James Lawrie to be revised.

Mr John Crosse, asked about his elders' lives and keeping of the eldership, answered that none of them were scandalous, nor did any neglect family worship.

**Margaret McDowell, called, compeared; ordained to be rebuked … next presbytery day, being convicted of incest, adultery, and fornication.** Jonet Martin, guilty of fornication, did not compear; ordained to be summoned a second time. The session being tried, the book approved. Mr James Dick reported he had preached at Stranraer as ordained.

## Notes & context
★★★ **Stranraer's pulpit is filled at last — Mr John Park admitted.** The long vacancy that opened when **Mr John Livingstone was transported to Ancrum in 1648** (img 130) closes here: **the Synod transported Mr John Park from Mochrum to Stranraer, and the town (Provost, Bailies, Council) supplicated for his speedy admission.** The presbytery orders the edict read and served, and sets the **admission for Wednesday 7 November 1649** (Scott to preach). Park — the Wigtown correspondent of imgs 168–176, whose call the town pushed from August — becomes minister of the presbytery seat. A milestone comparable to the 1647–48 plantings of the two Glenluce charges.

★★ **Visitation of the New Kirk of Glenluce (New Luce), 31 October 1649.** The visited minister is **Mr John Crosse ("Mr John Crookes")**, examined and unanimously approved on doctrine, life, discipline, catechising, and visiting the sick; his elders reported none scandalous and all keeping family worship. *(Note: earlier files had Mr John Crichton admitted to the New Kirk of Glenluce in 1647, img 115 — this visitation treats **Crosse** as the New-Luce minister, so Crichton may have moved on; flag for reconciliation.)* Session-books remitted to Dick and Lawrie to revise.

★ **Discipline:** **Margaret McDowell convicted of incest, adultery, and fornication** — a grave triple charge, to be rebuked/censured next presbytery day; **Jonet Martin** (fornication) summoned a second time. Also: **Mr James Blair away at a Wigtown meeting on the widows-and-orphans fund** (per Synod ordinance), showing the poor-relief administration running in parallel with the plantings.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 128 (folios 127v–128)

Continuation of the sitting of **22 March 1648** (Glenluce presbytery, clerk
John Turnbull). The left page (foliated **127a** = folio 127 verso) carries four
acts: (1) the long-running **Ardwell business** — the Irish presbytery having
sent no answer, Ardwell is ordered to purge himself by oath at the next court or
confess; (2) a note that many brethren went to **Glenluce Abbey** and gave
possession to **Mr John Scott**; (3) a recommendation about the ministers' pay;
and (4) the taking of a **roll of widows and orphans** in each parish, with a
letter to be sent to the kirk of Wigtown and Kirkcowan. The right page (folio
128) records **Mr John Levingstoun** presenting two letters from the
**Commission of the Kirk** together with acts of the General Assembly (including
the **Declaration against the malignants**) to be read; the **Marion [—]**
paternity case referred up from the Glenluce session (she names one **James
McMillan**, a soldier, as a child's father, but the session's probation finds she
has not spoken truly); and two acts in favour of **Mr John Dick** and for
providing gleib and manse at the old and new kirks of Glenluce.

### Modern English
Concerning Ardwell's business, the Moderator declared that he had received no
answer from the presbytery in Ireland. The presbytery, considering that no
further discovery had been made to prove him guilty beyond the ground of
suspicion, and that he ought to purge himself by oath or acknowledge the fault —
therefore the presbytery, proceeding according to the appointment of the General
Assembly and the advice of the Provincial Synod of Galloway, finds it fitting
that he must purge himself without further delay, or else confess; and therefore
ordains him to be so notified by the said John McDuall, officer to the presbytery,
who by these presents is appointed to summon him to the next court, either to
purge himself by oath or to confess.

The same day the Moderator declared that many of the brethren went to the Abbey
of Glenluce and gave possession of the same to Mr John Scott.

The Moderator declared that, although it did not please the brethren, they should
not omit to deal with the heritors about the ministers' pay; they recommend to him
to write to the brethren [about it].

The same day the Moderator enquired of the brethren whether they had the names of
the widows and orphans in their several parishes, who gave in the same for the
most part; and those appointed to gather them in were to have them ready
("conditioned") against the Synod. And the Moderator is appointed to write to the
kirk of Wigtown, desiring them to have their names ready, and also to advertise
the [plantation/minister] of Kirkcowan to be present on the day of the Synod.

### Modern English
The same day Mr John Levingstoun presented to the presbytery a letter from the
Commission of the Kirk, together with the act of the General Assembly, which was
declared to the Moderator and by them recommended to the brethren.

The said Mr John presented another letter, together with an act of the General
Assembly appointing them to publicly read the Declarations emitted by them
concerning [the army], and also the Declaration against the malignants. The said
Mr John declared that he had given them to the several brethren of the presbytery
to be read. The Moderator asked the brethren whether they had received and read
the same; all answered they had received and read them.

The same day Marion [—], referred by the session of Glenluce to the presbytery,
was called, compeared, and being questioned concerning the guilt of a child,
stoutly affirmed that one James McMillan, a soldier, was the child's father. But
the church was assured that this did not hold true against James McMillan; the
probation was truly taken of the same, and there they found that she had not
spoken truly. She is summoned again to compear at the next court, and in the
meantime the minister and session of the old parish of Glenluce are appointed to
deal with her to bring her to confession of the truth.

The presbytery, considering that Mr John Dick, minister at [Kirkcowan], has so
long wanted a gleib, appoint [certain brethren] to deal with the parish to provide
a gleib and a manse for the said Mr John Dick.

They were also appointed for [setting right] the Abbey of Glenluce, and providing
the gleib and manse at the old kirk of Glenluce, with one at the New kirk.

## Notes & context
- ★ **Ardwell case advances but stalls again.** The Irish presbytery (where the
  key witness Jean Fyfe was said to be) has still sent **no answer**. With "no
  farther discoverie" beyond suspicion, the presbytery falls back on the standard
  remedy: **purgation by oath** (canonical compurgation) *or* confession, with
  John McDuall (the presbytery officer) to summon Alexander McCulloch of Ardwell
  to the next court. This is the procedural resolution of the multi-year
  Ardwell / Lady Freugh / Jean Fyfe adultery-slander thread running since img 113.
- ★ **Possession of Glenluce Abbey given to Mr John Scott** — the physical
  completion of the Old-kirk planting begun at imgs 119/126/127. Ties off the
  Glenluce parish-division arc.
- ★ **Widows-and-orphans roll**: the presbytery is compiling parish-by-parish
  lists of widows and orphans (poor relief / kirk-session welfare), to be
  "conditioned" (readied) against the Synod. Letters to Wigtown and Kirkcowan.
- ★ **Commission of the Kirk letters & the Declaration against the malignants**:
  Mr John Levingstoun (minister of Stranraer) relays two letters plus General
  Assembly acts appointing the public reading of the Kirk's declarations
  (Engagement-crisis politics, spring 1648) — the anti-malignant/anti-Engager
  stance. Continues the political thread from imgs 122/124/125.
- ★ **Paternity case (Marion [—] vs James McMillan)**: a session-referred
  fornication/paternity case; she names a soldier, James McMillan, as father, but
  the session's probation finds against her testimony. Remitted back to the
  Glenluce minister and session to bring her to confession.
- **Mr John Dick** appears here as a minister wanting a gleib (glebe) — probably
  minister at Kirkcowan (he preached at Scott's admission, img 127). Two acts
  provide for gleib and manse at both the old and new kirks of Glenluce.

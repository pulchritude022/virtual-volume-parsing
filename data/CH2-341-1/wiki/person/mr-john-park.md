**Mr John Park** is one of the ministers who dominate the presbytery's final years. He
appears steadily through the later openings taking his turn in the **exercise** rota,
sitting in judgment on the ordinary discipline business, and — by 1652 — serving as
**moderator** of the court [img_255–256].

## In the last controversies

Park is caught up in the volume's closing storm, the
[Resolutioner–Protester schism](entity:event/the-resolutioner-protester-schism). When
[Mr Alexander Turnbull](entity:person/mr-alexander-turnbull)'s protestation splits the
brethren, Park is one of the three who will not simply fall in with the presbytery's line,
declaring his own qualified position in the conference over whether the Protesters' paper
should be entered in the register [img_255–257]. His appearing alongside Turnbull and
[Mr James Lawrie](entity:person/mr-james-lawrie) here is one of the alignment puzzles the
record leaves — elsewhere he acts with the Resolutioner majority — and is flagged for review
in the page notes.

## Role in the court

As moderator in the last sittings he presides as the register winds toward its close, and is
named to make the exercise at the next (post-Synod) meeting [img_257] — a meeting the surviving
book never reaches.

## A note for the reviewer

Park's exact parish within the bounds is not asserted here; it should be confirmed against the
sederunts and the visitation records before being stated. His prominence in the co-occurrence
[network](charts.html) reflects how constantly he sat with the core ministers.

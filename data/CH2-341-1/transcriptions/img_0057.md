## Notes & context
- **"De capite ecclesiae et infallibilitate papae" — controversial divinity in the pulpit.**
  Blair's set common head — **"On the Headship of the Church and the infallibility of the
  Pope"** — is a **flagship anti-Roman Covenanter theme**: it asserts **Christ (not the Pope,
  and not the king) as sole Head of the Church** and refutes **papal infallibility**. Paired
  with the earlier *capite ecclesiae* exercise (img 0047), it shows the presbytery drilling its
  ministers in the **polemic of 1643–44** (the Westminster Assembly / Solemn League years). A
  strong glossary/theme node on **the Headship of Christ** and anti-papal controversy. See
  [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]].
- **Power still the standing exception.** At the **privy trial** the presbytery approves every
  minister and elder **except Mr Gilbert Power, "who was not desyred"** — i.e. deliberately
  **not called/approved**, exactly as at the Stranraer visitation (img 0052, "not approven…
  slanders yet lying on him"). The register keeps marking Power as the unresolved outlier into
  spring 1644. A consistent, quotable thread for his person-page.
- **Cleaning up after Blair's admission.** **Livingstone "settled the Kirkcolm session" and
  subscribed there** — the practical aftermath of planting a minister: reconstituting the kirk
  session and its book. And **Livingstone & Blair are to treat with the Earl of Galloway about
  "satisfying Mr John Porterfield according to the old friendship."** If this Porterfield is
  the **former Kirkcolm minister (d. c.1642) or his heirs**, it likely concerns **stipend
  arrears / the vacancy's ann**; the phrase "the old friendship" hints at a negotiated
  goodwill settlement with the patron. Flag the Porterfield identity (dead minister's estate?
  a living kinsman?) for the refining pass — it may correct the earlier death-date note.
- **Vacancies still juggled.** Kirkmaiden supply rolls on (Livingstone, Hew Hutcheson, John
  Dick), and Kirkcolm's new session is bedding in — the presbytery's spring-1644 workload.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `de capite ecclesiae et infallibilitate papae` (Latin) =
  "concerning the Head of the Church and the infallibility of the Pope" (a controversial-head
  for the exercise); `settling the Session` = reconstituting the kirk session; `privatlie
  tryed` = the members' private examination on doctrine and life; `was not desyred` = was not
  called/invited (to the trial or its approval); `pro 2 / pro 3` = second / third summons;
  `made [the exercise]` = delivered the set sermon. **Both pages faded** — provisional. Capital
  **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1644.

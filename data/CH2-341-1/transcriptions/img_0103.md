## Notes & context
- **The settled autumn rhythm.** After the eventful September (the excommunication letter, the Glenluce
  call), this opening is largely **routine governance**: a **privy trial**, the **post-Synod election**
  (Livingstone moderator, Hutcheson clerk — the same pairing as after previous Synods), and the
  **censure of elders who skipped the Synod** — the ordinary machinery of a presbytery keeping its
  members to their duties.
- **★ Thomas Morton does his penance.** The **Thomas Morton / Margaret Mc[Cristine?] adultery** (imgs
  0097–0102) reaches the sackcloth stage: **Morton confesses and begins public repentance at Glenluce,
  and is bound to shun her company** — the standard resolution, mirroring Thomas Adair's (imgs 0097–0098).
  Note the two Glenluce couples running in parallel: **Adair + Margaret Hannay**, and **Morton + Margaret
  McCristine**.
- **★ The two cross-cases pressed.** **Agnes McColbie** is again to be **written for, to Ireland, "to be
  returned for further trial"** (imgs 0100–0101), and **Marion Mure** — the woman in the **Torrane
  adultery** (imgs 0094–0099) — is now herself **to be summoned** (the man, James Torrane elder, having
  been discharged from her company). The kirk pursuing both parties across parish and channel.
- **A Glenluce elder to mind Thomas Adair.** The **ruling elder of Glenluce (the [French?] elder)** is set
  to **oversee Thomas Adair's penance** — local eldership tasked with enforcing the presbytery's
  discipline on the ground.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `chosen moderator… continued clerk` = the post-Synod re-election of
  officers; `having given in reasons for their absence fra ye Synod, Wer excused… ye rest… to be censured`
  = accountability for attendance at the superior court; `compeired in sackcloth… confest his falling…
  ordained to abstaine fra ye company` = public penance plus a non-association order; `to be returned for
  further tryall` = extradition of an offender from Ireland; `suspect of adulterie… to be summond` = a
  citation on scandalous report. **Both pages heavily water-stained** — provisional. Capital **W** for
  *w/wh*. Year **1646**.

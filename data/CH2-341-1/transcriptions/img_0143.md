# CH2/341/1 — Image 143 (folios 142v–143)

A **two-day kirk visitation of Glenluce, 25–26 October 1648** — the physical and
disciplinary follow-through on the long-running Glenluce plantation. Left page
(foliated **142a** = folio 142 verso): a note that ★ **the presbytery book, being
read, is approved by the Synod**; then the ★ **visitation of the New kirk of
Glenluce (25 October)** — Mr John Dick chosen moderator, Mr John Hutcheson clerk;
the standard visitation formula (minister removed, elders asked whether they are
satisfied with his doctrine, conversation and dispensing of the ordinances; elders
removed, minister asked about the elders); the minister regrets the long delay in
**settling the glebe and manse**, and the parishioners of the new parish are
exhorted about the **maintenance/stipend of the minister**. The right page (folio
143): a ★ **glebe dispute** — the parishioners complain the glebe has been troubled,
and **Ardwell** is named as concerned in it; **Mr John Scott** and **Mr James
Lowrie** are appointed to attend Ardwell about the difference. The presbytery then
adjourns to the morrow (**26 October**) for the ★ **visitation of the old kirk of
Glenluce**: Mr John Scott's people give him a good testimony, but ★★ **the minister
complains of most of his session for complying with the late unlawful Engagement**,
and of their slackness to repair the kirk.

## Notes & context
- ★ **Presbytery book approved by the Synod.** The note "This book, being read, is
  approved by the Synod" is a procedural certification that the Stranraer
  presbytery's register was produced at, and passed by, the Synod of Galloway — a
  useful datum for the register's own provenance/oversight chain.
- ★ **Two-day visitation of Glenluce (25–26 Oct 1648).** A formal kirk visitation
  in the classic form: on each day the minister is removed and the elders examined
  on his doctrine/conversation/dispensing of the ordinances, then the elders are
  removed and the minister examined on them and on the people. This is the
  disciplinary and material audit of the parish — the plantation of Glenluce (New
  and Old kirks) followed to its ground.
- ★ **Glebe and manse still unsettled at the New kirk.** The minister of the new
  parish "much regrets" that, after so long, the **glebe and manse** are still not
  settled; the parishioners are pressed on the **maintenance/stipend of the
  minister** — the recurring material problem of the Glenluce plantation (cf. imgs
  128–129 fabric survey and gleib).
- ★ **Glebe dispute touches Ardwell.** A dispute over the glebe/ground of the new
  kirk implicates **Ardwell** (Alexander McCulloch); **Mr John Scott** and **Mr
  James Lowrie** are appointed to attend him and take up the difference — another
  thread in the long Ardwell file, here a property matter rather than the adultery
  scandal.
- ★★ **Session complied with the Engagement.** At the old kirk of Glenluce, **Mr
  John Scott** (minister) reports his only complaint of his session is that **most
  of its members complied with the late unlawful Engagement**, and were slack to
  repair the kirk — a concrete parish-level trace of the 1648 Engagement purge
  reaching the eldership, and of church-fabric neglect.

## Notes & context
★★ **The Portpatrick / Portmontgomery boundary dispute measured on the ground.** The 28 May perambulation (imgs 191–192) turns technical: the presbytery, still unsatisfied about the **distance of Barnsalloch and Argrenan** from the rival kirks, appoints named men from each parish to **physically measure the distance "by the ordinary passage-way"** and report to Stranraer on 12 June — a concrete instance of a parish-boundary/annexation case (Stoneykirk vs Portpatrick, imgs 182–183) being settled by survey. A **second, afternoon sitting** is held the same day "at the kirk of the Inch," which proves a poorly-attended washout ("a very slack meeting … few heritors or elders present").

★ **The Engagement penitents close out.** A confessing Engager (from context the **William Baillie** of imgs 189, 191 — a Lieutenant of horse) "professed his guilt for his accession to the … unlawful Engagement and promised watchfulness," and is set to confess at **Old Glenluce** and, on subscribing the GA Declaration, be received to the Covenant.

★ **A contempt-of-court scene.** **George Adestoun** (= **George Alexstoune**, the man "disobedient to the Session of Stoneykirk" at imgs 188, 190) appears and **"behaves himself very impertinently and in an insolent manner"**; the presbytery orders public repentance at Stoneykirk and, notably, calls in the **civil magistrate** to restrain his "stubborn and insolent behaviour" — the kirk reaching for the secular arm. **Mr John Park** is to press **James Kennedy** to produce him.

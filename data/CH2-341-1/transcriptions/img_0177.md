### Modern English
**Presbytery at Stranraer, 10 October 1649.** Mr John Dick made and Mr James Lawrie added to the exercise (2 Thess 1:1–2), approven. Absent: Mr John Scott and the ruling elders of Kirkmaiden.

**The brethren reported they were putting the act for the poor into execution.** The reports about Balneil and Patrick McKie are delayed because of Mr John Scott's absence. Mr James Lawrie preached at Stranraer as appointed. Mr James Bell reported **Roger Campbell had given obedience.** Thomas and James McConeill in Kirkcolm, called, did not compear and are to be cited again.

**Concerning the extract of Balneil's process — because the clerk had given out a copy without the consent of the presbytery, the clerk was rebuked; and an act made that hereafter no presbyterial act in a process shall be given out by the clerk without the presbytery's consent.**

The ministers and ruling elders being removed [in turn], the privy trials were held and severally approved; only such of the ruling elders as were found to miss the presbytery meetings were rebuked. **Alexr Turnbull, clerk pro tempore.**

**At Wigtown, the [—] of October 1649: the Synod, having revised this book, approves the same. [Subscribed] [Ja. —], Clericus Synodi.**

## Notes & context
★★★ **The Balneil case reaches its resolution — a public confession by articles.** This is the payoff of the whole 1648–49 Balneil arc (imgs 148–176): **James Ross of Balneil's own numbered articles of confession** are copied in, each a first-person act of contrition — that he slighted the anti-Engagement supplication; **reasoned for the levy in the Kirkcowan Committee**; took a protection without declaring it; **offered money to have the Committee of Estates' observations read against the Kirk's Declaration** and, when no one would, read it himself; was "so forward for the Last unlawful Engagement"; and caused a man apprehended and sent to Wigtown. **The presbytery orders him to make this confession publicly before the congregation of old Balneil (old Glenluce) next Sabbath**, Scott to receive it — exactly the "public satisfaction … then admit him to the Covenant" the GA directed (img 175). A parallel confession by **Patrick McKie** (a servant who rode out in the rebellion) is ordered the same way. After a year of the process being unclosable, it closes as **repentance, not condemnation.**

★★ **The clerk rebuked — a records-security act.** A revealing governance moment: **the clerk had given out a copy of Balneil's process without presbytery consent, and is rebuked; a standing act is made that no presbyterial act in a process may be issued by the clerk without the presbytery's consent** — the court tightening control of its own paper trail (the same process whose custody was fought over in imgs 169, 173).

★ **Section boundary — Synod of Galloway attestation.** The opening closes with the **Synod's revision-and-approval attestation ("At Wigtown, [—] October 1649 … the Synod, having revised this book, approves the same," subscribed Clericus Synodi)** — a natural volume/section marker, mirroring the April-1649 attestation of img 166. Also: the **act for the poor put into execution**, Roger Campbell now obedient (closing his img 176 bribery case), and routine privy trials (absentee elders rebuked). Clerk this stretch = **Alexr Turnbull pro tempore**.

### Modern English (paraphrase)
The presbytery met at Stranraer on Wednesday 3 November 1641. Mr John Livingston
was chosen clerk. In a matter involving the younger laird of French, witnesses were
summoned and questioned. The witnesses called — John McCloy [elder?] of Lochans and
John McKim[?] — appeared to be re-examined, having previously been sworn in the
presence of Mr Gilbert.

John McDowall of Lochans, being questioned, [affirmed] his earlier deposition and
[denied] that he had been suborned (coached) by either party. Asked whether he had
borrowed about 28 or 29 merks — [or rather] two merks upon a 100-merk [bond?] — from
Mr Gilbert, and had held it about 9 years; asked what annualrent (interest) he paid
Mr Gilbert, he answered that he began by paying in money, so much per year. Asked
whether Mr Gilbert demanded the money back and would not let him keep it unless he
paid the yearly annualrent, he said Gilbert did not press it but left it at his
option. Asked whether, if he kept the money, he always paid a boll of meal per £100
merks yearly [to Balingel's(?) management], in the year 1620 he did keep the money
until that time and paid a boll of meal for the two [merks] until then. Asked whether
in the years 1621 and 1623 he paid him a boll of meal for the merks, he answered
"Yes, I did." Asked whether [meal] was cheapest in those years [etc.], he answered he
does not remember [remainder torn/illegible].

### Notes & context
- **Document structure:** Folio 1 is the register's title page and the roll of the
  eight constituent parishes of Stranraer Presbytery. Folio 2 opens the minutes proper:
  the first sederunt (3 Nov 1641) and election of the clerk, then immediately a witness
  examination — the presbytery acting as a court taking depositions.
- **Subject:** a dispute over a loan and its **annualrent** (interest), paid partly in a
  **boll of meal** — a common way to disguise interest in an era when the kirk policed
  usury. Money is in **merks Scots** (1 merk = 13s 4d).
- **Palaeography applied:** `wis` = was; `wonsday` = Wednesday; `begouth` = began (Scots);
  `mikle` = much; `boll` = grain measure; `annual` = annualrent; `Interrogat` = questioned;
  `compeared` = appeared in court; `wt`/superscript expanded. Names show heavy spelling
  variation (`M'Cloy`, `M'dowal`).

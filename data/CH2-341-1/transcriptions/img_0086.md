# CH2/341/1 — Image 86 (folios 85v–86), Nov–Dec 1645 — lay commissioners chosen for a petition to Parliament; a murder-suspect fugitive pursued to Ireland

Left page (folio **85a**): Mr John Dick to handle the doctrine and give in the exercise on 3 December;
then a **presbytery of 17 November** at which **the presbytery chose commissioners to present a petition
to Parliament** — the leading Rhins lairds **Alexander McDowell of Logan, Uchtred McDowell of French,
Alexander McCulloch of Ardwell, Thomas Hay of [—], and John Cathcart of Carleton** — to undergo the
said commission; then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 3 December 1645** — an exercise (Mr John Livingstone
made and added, 1 Thess. 1:3, approved; **Mr John Dick, who should have added, forgot he was appointed,
so is ordained to make next day**, and Hutcheson to add; absent Mr James Bell). Right page (folio
**86**): the elders for **Stranraer, Portmontgomery, Kirkmaiden, and Glenluce admitted**; **Mr John
Livingstone reports he preached at Glenluce** (his supply turn), the Glenluce penitent having failed to
enter to repentance as ordained; **Mr John Dick reports he summoned [Mr Corbie?] from the pulpit a
second time, and is ordained to summon him a third**; **★ Mr John Livingstone appointed to bear a
letter to the presbytery in Ireland, as soon as he has occasion, to try whether [Mr Corbie?], suspect
of murder, is residing there**; the brethren report **the fast kept in their several congregations on
23 November**; and the **next meeting appointed for the last day of December.**

## Notes & context
- **★ A murder-suspect fugitive pursued to Ireland — and a likely correction to img 0085.** The fugitive
  whom the presbytery is summoning from the pulpit (pro 2/pro 3) and pursuing by letter to **"the
  presbytery in Ireland"** is here twice named **"Mr [Corbie?]" and described as "suspect of murder."**
  This is the same pulpit-summons + Ireland-letter matter recorded on folio 85 (img 0085), where I read
  the name as **"John Ferguson, fugitive out of the kingdom."** On the clearer folio 86 the surname
  reads short (Corbie/Colbie/Corbett), and the crime is **murder** — so **img 0085's "Ferguson" is
  probably a mis-reading**, and this is likely a **distinct case from the earlier John Ferguson** ("the
  heinous fact," imgs 0069–0070, whose witnesses were women). **Flag for review:** the murder-suspect
  fugitive of imgs 0085–0086 is **Mr [Corbie?]**, not (necessarily) Ferguson. (A "John Corbett in
  Stranraer" also appears at img 0082 — possibly the same family.) Either way, it is another instance
  of the presbytery using its **Ulster church links to pursue a serious criminal across the North
  Channel.**
- **★ The Rhins lairds as commissioners to Parliament.** A striking secular-political entry: the
  presbytery **chose leading heritors — McDowell of Logan, McDowell of French, McCulloch of Ardwell,
  Thomas Hay, John Cathcart of Carleton — as commissioners to present a petition to Parliament.**
  Several are the very lairds prominent in the Power affair (**French**, **Ardwell**), here acting for
  the presbytery in the national arena (late 1645, the Covenanter Parliament). A good cluster of named
  gentry for person/place nodes (Logan, French, Ardwell, Carleton) and evidence of how kirk and local
  gentry cooperated in petitioning the state.
- **Glenluce supply and a stalled penitent.** **Livingstone preached at Glenluce** (his supply turn,
  img 0085), but the appointed **penitent failed to enter to repentance** — the vacancy's discipline
  drifting for want of a settled minister.
- **A November fast.** The **fast kept on 23 November** in the several congregations — another 1645
  observance.
- **Human detail.** **Mr John Dick "forgot he was appointed" to add the exercise** — a small,
  humanising slip duly minuted (and penalised with the next turn).
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `commissioners to present the petition to the parliament` = lay
  delegates (heritors) sent with a presbytery petition to Parliament; `suspect of murther… residing
  there` = a murder suspect thought to be in Ireland; `summond fra pulpite pro 2 / pro 3` = second/
  third public citation from the pulpit; `gave in their commissions and were admitted` = elders'
  credentials received; `both made and added` = delivered and seconded the exercise. **Left page
  stained** — provisional; right page fairly legible. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1645.

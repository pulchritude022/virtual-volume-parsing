# CH2/341/1 — Image 127 (folios 126v–127), 1–22 March 1648 — ★★ Mr John Scott received as minister of Old Glenluce and given possession of the Abbey; Alexander McIlwrick returned from Ireland

Left page (folio **126a**): (1 March, continued) **the kirk officer of Glenluce, at the command of the
presbytery, went to the kirk door and warned any who had anything to object against the admission of Mr John
Scott to the ministry at Glenluce to compear presently before the presbytery; but none at all compeared;
therefore the presbytery appointed Mr John Dick, according to the last meeting's appointment, to preach and
receive the said Mr John Scott**; **★★ Mr John Dick preached (text Job 13:7?), and after sermon did receive
Mr John Scott to be minister at the old kirk of Glenluce, in the ordinary way and form used in this kirk in
receiving and admitting transported ministers**; **Mr John Crichton reported that, per the presbytery's
directions, he had preached at Stranraer**; **★ the said day Mr John Crichton reported that Alexander
McIlwrick was returned from Ireland, and desired to know how the presbytery would take further way with him;
the presbytery appoints that he shall, the next Sabbath, give signs of his repentance in the parish of [one
Sunday], and upon signs of repentance to be received — for reason that it be to the [—] of the [Assembly] of
the New kirk of Glenluce**; **the cause of Ardwell's business is referred to the next presbytery**; and **the
presbytery, considering that some of the names of the widows and orphans within the parishes were not yet
given in, appointed them to bring them in the next day**. Right page (folio **127**): **★ the presbytery,
being certainly informed that the Earl of Cassillis had appointed [notice] to be given to the feuars… that
[resided] in that region… appoint the moderator to speak with [them]… and to appoint a day that the feuars
shall come and receive the same**; **★ the said day the presbytery resolved that immediately after the rising
of the presbytery, the moderator and remanent brethren shall go with [Mr John Scott to] the Abbey of
Glenluce, and give Mr John Scott possession of the same**; **★ Mr Alexander Turnbull is appointed to have the
next common head — the subject is the rest of these questions *De redemptione* not handled by Mr James Blair;
and Mr John Crichton is to follow after him, and to handle these questions *De natura et viribus liberi
arbitrii***; the next meeting appointed at Stranraer this day twenty days (Mr John Livingstone to make, Mr
Hew Hutcheson to add). Then **Presbytery at Stranraer, March 22, 1648** — absent Mr Hew Hutcheson and the
ruling elder of Portmontgomery, both of them being sick; **Mr John Livingstone both made and added (because
of Mr Hew Hutcheson's absence); the text was 1 [John] 4 v. [—]10; he was approved**; and the excuses of the
absentees approved.

## Notes & context
- **★★ Old Glenluce planted — Scott received, and given possession of the Abbey.** The saga closes: after the
  edict was served with **no objectors**, **Mr John Dick preaches and formally receives Mr John Scott as
  minister of the old kirk of Glenluce "in the ordinary way and form used in receiving and admitting
  transported ministers"** — and, the same day, **the whole presbytery walks with him to the Glenluce Abbey
  and gives him possession** (the manse and glebe of his benefice, imgs 0119–0120). Both Glenluce charges are
  now filled and physically installed (Old/Scott, New/Crichton). This is the concrete end of the parish
  division begun in 1646.
- **★ McIlwrick comes home.** The fugitive **Alexander McIlwrick** (fled to Ireland from his sackcloth, img
  0122) has **returned**, and the presbytery — rather than punishing his flight — orders him to **give signs
  of repentance and be received at the New kirk of Glenluce.** The cross-channel discipline chase (Adair,
  McCredie, the McColbys, McIlwrick) at last yields a returnee.
- **★ The Earl of Cassillis and the feuars.** A temporal-administration item: notice from **the Earl of
  Cassillis** (the great Ayrshire/Galloway Covenanter magnate, patron connected to Stranraer's stipend, img
  0123) to the **feuars** — the moderator to arrange a day for them to "come and receive the same" (likely
  the Stranraer stipend/feu-duty settlement flowing from Livingstone's Edinburgh success).
- **Doctrine on the roster.** The common heads roll on: **Turnbull continues *De redemptione*, Crichton
  takes up *De natura et viribus liberi arbitrii*** ("on the nature and powers of free will") — the
  Calvinist curriculum of the weekly exercise. And **Ardwell is deferred yet again** — still unresolved.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `Warned any Who had any thing to object against ye admissioun… none
  compeared` = the edict's proclamation at the kirk door; `receive Mr Johne Scott… in ye ordinar Way and
  forme… in receiving and admitting transported ministers` = the reception of a translated minister; `goe
  Wt ye Abbey of Glenluce, and gyve Mr Johne Scott possessioun of ye same` = symbolic infeftment/possession
  of the manse and glebe; `De natura et viribus liberi arbitrii` = the doctrinal topic of free will. Firm
  hand; the admission sermon-text (Job 13:7?), the Cassillis/feuars clause, and the 22 March exercise text
  are provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1648**.

A long-running slander case that shows the presbytery's discipline machinery grinding
slowly against a laird who kept appealing every adverse step upward — and shows how far a
scandal could reach when a key witness fled the jurisdiction entirely.

## The accusation

**Alexander McCulloch of Ardwell** stood accused of adultery with **Jean Fyfe**. Ardwell's
defence was not that the affair never happened but that he had been defamed as guilty of it
by **the Lady Freugh**, who he said had "affirmed Jean Fyfe absolutely guilty" without
proof [img_133]. He wanted permission to counter-sue her for slander; the presbytery refused
both absolution and a counter-suit unless he first purged himself by oath [img_118, img_133].

## Appeal to the General Assembly, and a witness who vanished

Rather than accept that, Ardwell **appealed to the General Assembly** — the same escalation
route Mr Gilbert Power had used a few years earlier — and the case was provisionally
remitted back down to the presbytery, with GA commissioners named to advise [img_118]. The
presbytery examined Ardwell's own tenants and cottars as witnesses and referred the matter
further up to the **Synod of Galloway** [img_121]. The Synod's own act (read into the
record at Wigtown, 18 April 1648) confirmed the accused as Ardwell, the partner as Jean
Fyfe — by then **fled to Ireland** — and, citing "the flagrancy of the scandal," ordered
Ardwell to confess or purge himself by oath, on pain of the presbytery proceeding against
him regardless [img_131].

The presbytery went so far as to send a man to Ireland to search for Jean Fyfe and wrote to
the Irish presbytery to help find her [img_122, img_138–139] — a reminder of how thoroughly
this Ulster-facing presbytery's reach extended across the North Channel for both relief work
and discipline. Ardwell answered the Synod's order with yet another supplication, this time
merely asking for a register extract — a further delaying tactic [img_131–132].

## Resolution

By late 1648 Ardwell finally **declared himself willing to purge by oath**, and a date was
set [img_142]. The case then folds into the same autumn stretch of business as the opening
moves of the [Engagement purge](entity:event/the-post-engagement-purge-of-malignants) and
the [Balneil malignancy process](entity:event/james-ross-of-balneil-s-malignancy-process) —
1648 was a busy year for presbytery discipline on every front at once.

## People central to this case

[Alexander McCulloch of Ardwell](entity:person/alexander-mcculloch-of-ardwell) ·
the Lady Freugh (an Agnew of Freuch by marriage; not yet a standalone entity page) ·
Jean Fyfe, the accused partner, who fled to Ireland and was never produced.

## Notes & context
★ **Visitation of Inch concluded, with a glebe designation (7 June 1649).** The standard reciprocal examination (elders vouch for minister **Mr John Dick**; minister vouches for elders, urging more frequent weekday meetings for prayer and discipline; the minister's **maintenance** flagged as under-provided and remitted to the presbytery per "the act of the Kirk"). The concrete legal act is a **designation of the Inch glebe** — a boundary perambulation naming the kirk-yard dyke and **Alexr McBlain's house** — with formal possession given, expressly reserving a fuller later survey.

★ **Possession of the Stoneykirk glebe.** The presbytery also ordains **Turnbull and Dick to give Mr James Lawrie possession of the glebe and kirk-land of [Clayyaird] and [Castletoune]** (Stoneykirk) — tidying the same glebe/possession housekeeping across two parishes on one day.

★ **Presbytery at Stranraer, 20 June 1649 — storm-thinned.** Turnbull makes and Scott adds the exercise (1 Thess 5:20–22, approven). The clerk **Hutcheson being absent, Turnbull is chosen clerk for the day**, and the absents are excused **"by reason of extraordinary storms"** — a second weather note within a fortnight (cf. the flooding of img 170). Mr John Scott is again ordained to keep the **Ireland correspondence** via **Mr Andrew [Kennedy]** (continuing the img 170 thread).

### Modern English
The same day the presbytery ordained **Allan Gill to satisfy at Kirkcolm for profaning the Lord's day**; George Alexstone to appear next presbytery.

**Act anent the election of moderator and clerk:** because some ministers were not satisfied with the manner of the moderator-and-clerk election of 20 October 1649 (it having been at a *pro re nata* meeting), the presbytery re-nominates the persons to be elected, to continue until the next Synod, and **unanimously ordains that henceforth the moderator and clerk be chosen only at an ordinary presbytery meeting** [not at an emergency sitting].

**John Lillie presented two papers — one from the Town and Council of Queensferry, with a call from that town's kirk session, to bring a call to Mr John Dick, minister at Inch — to prosecute the transportation of Mr John Dick from us to the kirk of Queensferry.** The presbytery took the papers to consideration.

Margaret [Chalmers] and Jonet Mc[—], for unchaste and scandalous [carriage], compeared, were convicted, and remitted for punishment; already stood two Sabbaths in the public place of repentance.

### Modern English
**Presbytery at Stranraer, 10 April 1650.** Absent: ruling elders of old Glenluce, Kirkcolm, Leswalt, Portmontgomery. Mr John Crosse handled the common head *De Ecclesia, sic forma Constitutiva*, approven (theses not disputed). The brethren reported they had kept the fast.

**Mr John Scott to speak with James Ross of Balneil and [the tenants] of Glenluce, and to get a commission to apprehend the persons who robbed the poor English traveller** on the way within the bounds of the old kirk.

## Notes & context
★★ **Another attempt to poach a Rhins minister — Mr John Dick to Queensferry.** John Lillie brings **a call from the town and kirk session of Queensferry to transport Mr John Dick (minister of Inch) to Queensferry** — the third such raid on this presbytery's ministers in the volume (after Glasgow's bid for Livingstone, img 113, and the successful Livingstone→Ancrum transportation of 1648, img 130). The presbytery takes it to consideration. (Whether Dick was actually transported would show in later folios.)

★★ **The Engagement penitents make their public repentance (10 April 1650).** The satisfaction machinery reaches its ceremonial climax: **Major John McDowall subscribes the Declaration and is ordained to give signs of repentance before the Stranraer congregation**; **Lt-Col [Alexr Stewart] has already done so at [Kirkinner]**; and, notably, **Gilbert Cochrane of Chappell subscribes and agrees to public repentance at the kirk of Chappell** — folding the near-duel (imgs 184–185) into the same discipline. The "Declaration" they all subscribe is the anti-Engagement / anti-malignant national declaration.

★ **A governance reform.** An **act reforming moderator/clerk elections** — prompted by dissatisfaction with the *pro re nata* election of 20 Oct 1649 — ordains that **moderator and clerk henceforth be chosen only at ordinary meetings**, a small constitutional tightening. Plus routine discipline (Allan Gill, sabbath-breaking; two women for scandalous carriage) and the ongoing hunt for the **robbers of the poor English traveller** (a commission to apprehend them).

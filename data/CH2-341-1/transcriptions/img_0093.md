# CH2/341/1 — Image 93 (folios 92v–93), 13 May 1646 — post-Synod election; the magistrates and malignants; a relapse adulterer

Left page (folio **92a**): **Presbytery at Stranraer, 13 May 1646** — an exercise (Mr Alexander Turnbull
made, Mr John Dick added, 1 Thess. 2:1–2, approved); absent Mr James Blair and elders; **★ Mr John Dick
chosen moderator and Mr Hew Hutcheson continued clerk** (the post-Synod election); elders of Stranraer,
Leswalt, Glenluce and Inch admitted; **the brethren report they had used diligence in dealing with the
magistrates for the trial and punishing of malignants per the act of Parliament**; the **act about
repairing the kirk of Stoneykirk exhibited**; **Mr Alexander Turnbull reports he preached at Stranraer**
[supplying Livingstone]; and **an Inch adulterer, ordained to satisfy before the Inch session, had in
the meantime become fugitive.** Right page (folio **93**): diligence to be used to find where the
fugitive resides; **Mr Alexander Turnbull reports he spoke the laird of French, who promised to give
public place / cooperate with the discipline**; **★ Thomas Adair in Kirkmaiden compeared in sackcloth,
confessing adultery with Marion [—] his servant — and, being a relapse offender, is further enjoined to
do public penance at the kirk of Stranraer**; **Marion McChesant, displeased and unable to travel, is
to satisfy when recovered**; the brethren report **the fast (appointed the last day of April) was
kept**; **Mr John Dick to preach at Glenluce on 24 May**; and a **Thomas Adair, adulterer in Glenluce,
to be summoned to the next presbytery.**

## Notes & context
- **The magistrates enlisted against malignants.** The **process against malignants** (imgs 0087–0091)
  now involves the presbytery **"dealing with the magistrates for the trial and punishing of malignants
  per the act of Parliament"** — the kirk courts and civil magistracy acting together to enforce the
  anti-royalist legislation, complementing the JP commissions of img 0091.
- **A relapse adulterer's heavier penance.** **Thomas Adair** (Kirkmaiden), confessing adultery **with
  his own servant** and being a **relapse offender**, is given an **extra day of public penance at the
  presbytery seat (Stranraer)** — a good illustration of how discipline escalated for repeat offenders,
  and of the master–servant dimension of sexual scandal.
- **The laird of French cooperating.** Notable that **the laird of French** (Uchtred McDowell — the
  chief anti-Power accuser of the 1642–43 case) here **cooperates with the presbytery's discipline** at
  Inch, promising to "give public place" — a small sign of the old antagonist working within the kirk's
  order in 1646.
- **Ordinary rhythm.** The **post-Synod election** (John Dick moderator, Hutcheson clerk), the **fast
  kept** (last day of April), **Turnbull supplying Stranraer** (Livingstone still in Ireland), and the
  **Glenluce supply** (John Dick to preach 24 May) — the settled weekly business, plus the recurring
  chase after **fugitive offenders** and excuses (a woman "displeased and unable to travel").
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `dealing With magistrates for tryall and punishing of malignants,
  according to the act of parliament` = kirk–civil cooperation to enforce the anti-malignant statute;
  `a relapse offender` = a repeat sinner (given heavier penance); `give publick place… before the
  Session` = provide the setting/occasion for public discipline; `in respect she Was in childbed / not
  able to travell` = valid excuses; `keepe the fast appointed the last Day of Aprile` = observance of an
  appointed fast. **Both pages faded** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1646.

Head of the **MacDowall of Garthland** family and the presbytery's most consistently chosen
lay (ruling-elder) commissioner to national business — a layman trusted with the same
Edinburgh errands as the presbytery's ministers.

## The presbytery's man in Edinburgh

Garthland is repeatedly named a General Assembly commissioner (e.g. alongside Livingstone
and Turnbull in 1642 [img_0043]) and is sent, specifically, to **oppose Livingstone's
transportation** to Glasgow — a lay elder dispatched on exactly the kind of errand usually
given to ministers [img_96]. In 1646 he is "necessitated to accompany the **Marquess of
Argyll** to Ireland," a trip important enough to stall both the Glenluce planting and the
Stoneykirk repair until his return [img_91].

## Family, not just office

The Garthland family's presence in the record is not limited to the laird himself: a
Garthland is recorded recommending a young ministerial candidate, **Mr James Lowrie**, to
the presbytery [img_123], and (per the 1648 Engagers register) MacDowall of Garthland
himself appears as a named compiler in the post-Engagement purge — the same family supplying
both the presbytery's most trusted lay commissioner and, a few years later, a name on its
own malignancy watch-list [entity:event/the-post-engagement-purge-of-malignants, img_146].

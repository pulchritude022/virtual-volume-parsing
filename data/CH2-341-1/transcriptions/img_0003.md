### Modern English (paraphrase)
The deponent (John McDowall of Lochans) continues: rents were paid at Martinmas
— a boll worth 4 shillings and some lambs — the sum being about 20 merks; he
always paid the annualrent (interest) but does not recall which year the money
was first borrowed (the second or third year after a "tack"/lease from Lord
Ochiltree[?], dated 1611). Asked whether, at his young daughter's baptism, he or
his wife gave Mr Gilbert gifts in lieu of interest, he denies it.

Then **Alexander [MacCulloch?] submitted a written paper of specific charges**:
(1) **oppression** — seizing by force much of the parish's grain, and in 1633
breaking open Patrick Wilson's barn doors and extorting corn (witnesses Patrick
Wilson, John Wilson, [Alexander] McDowell); (2) **perjury** and dealing in goods
"bought and sold" improperly (witnesses include the younger Sheriff of Galloway,
George Neilson, John McKinna); (3) **falsifying a testament** — forcing a bond of
500 merks, and knowledge of **the false writing of Peter McCulloch's testament**
around the time of the deceased's death. In defence, William Campbell was examined.

### Modern English (paraphrase)
The presbytery resolved to consult the Commissioners of the Kirk in Edinburgh:
**Mr Alexander Turnbull** was appointed to go to Edinburgh with the three papers
submitted by the Stranraer elders and to report "the present tumult," seeking the
commissioners' advice. Every minister and session was to inquire who had been
present at the tumult of Stranraer and report by the next meeting. **Marion
[Tolber/Pollock?]** was ordered summoned a second time to appear on 21 September.
The next meeting — a visitation — was set for Stranraer, 21 September 1642.

A new sederunt then opens: **Presbytery at Stranraer for visitation, 21 September
1642.** Mr John Livingston preached (on Luke 19); the parish elders, questioned
about their minister's doctrine, discipline, life and conversation, declared
themselves well satisfied; the minister had no complaint against the elders.
James Blair … ; Marion [Conochie?] was absent and was to appear again.

  grain, and a forced 500-merk bond. The deponent is McDowall of Lochans; the

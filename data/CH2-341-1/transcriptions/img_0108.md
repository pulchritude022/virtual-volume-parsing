# CH2/341/1 — Image 108 (folios 107v–108), February 1647 — Mr John Crichton accepts the call to the Moor kirk of Glenluce and is entered to trials; an ultimatum to the dissenting heritors

Left page (folio **107a**): **Presbytery at Stranraer, [—] February 1647** — **there was no preaching, the
brethren not being able to convene till [late] by reason of the great storm**; absent Mr Alexander
Turnbull, Mr James Blair, and the elders of Kirkmaiden, Inch and Stoneykirk; **John Mc[—] to be summoned to
the next presbytery**; **Mr John Livingstone reported he had intimated Mr Alexander Hepburn's assignation
to John Baillie of Little Imrogart and Hew Kennedy of [Ardgirvane?]**; **Mr John Livingstone reported that
Patrick [Cathcart?] in [Culhorn?], adulterer, had entered to give signs of repentance at Stranraer**; then
**★ the which day compeared James Ross of Barneill and desired that, according to the supplication given in
by him and others of the parish of Glenluce — there being a call to Mr John Crichton to the ministry at
the [Moor] kirk of Glenluce — the presbytery would in like manner give him a call and enter him to his
trials; the presbytery inquired of James Ross what assurance Mr John Crichton might have that [the stipend
with] the modification of locality would make out 800 merks; James Ross promised he would use his course
for obtaining the teind[-duties] in the lands of the parishioners of Glenluce**. Right page (folio **108**):
**★★ James Ross [promised] to give his bond for assuring the modification, after satisfying the presbytery,
and withal desired [Crichton] to convene with them to that effect; the presbytery gave Mr John Crichton a
call to the ministry at the Moor kirk [of Glenluce], which the said Mr John accepted; and the presbytery
ordained him [to be entered to his trials] upon the 24 day of February (1 Thess. 2:19–20, Mr John Dick to
preside)**; then the presbytery **recommended to John McDowell [of —], ruling elder of Glenluce, that the
heritors who disagreed from the act of the General Assembly anent the maintenance of the two kirks — if
they will be content to comply with the order the presbytery propounded to them on Thursday last (to refer
the arrears of the mortification to law) — should promise as much to the moderator by Tuesday next; which
if they do not, the presbytery ordained the moderator to send with all diligence to the Commission of the
General Assembly the act agreed upon on Thursday last, and to write to the Commission to desire their
concurrence in petitioning [the Lords], or what other way they think good, for planting both the kirks**;
and **a letter was directed to the Commission of the General Assembly, desiring a [warning] in every
several congregation the next Sabbath** [re the excommunication].

## Notes & context
- **★★ Mr John Crichton accepts — the Moor kirk of Glenluce (Balneil / New Luce) gets its minister.** The
  climax of the Glenluce saga: **James Ross of Barneill presents the call, promises a bond to assure the
  stipend (with the "modification of locality" to reach 800 merks), and Mr John Crichton accepts the call
  to "the Moor kirk"** — the moorland new kirk at Balneil that becomes **New Luce**. The presbytery
  **enters him to his trials (24 Feb, 1 Thess. 2:19–20, John Dick presiding)** — the formal path to
  ordination. After years of vacancy, the parish is being planted. **A headline node** (Crichton = first
  minister of the New/Moor kirk; the 800-merk stipend and its "modification of locality"). Confirm the
  surname and "Moor kirk."
- **★ The stick behind the settlement — an ultimatum and the courts.** Alongside the call, the presbytery
  gives the **dissenting Glenluce heritors (img 0107) until "Tuesday next"** to accept referring the
  mortification arrears to law; failing that, it will **send the act to the Commission of the General
  Assembly and petition the Lords** to force the funding of both kirks. The carrot (a called minister) and
  the stick (litigation) advancing together.
- **A storm stops the kirk.** A weather vignette: **"there was no preaching, the brethren not being able
  to convene… by reason of the great storm"** — the February 1647 winter in the Rhins interrupting the
  presbytery itself.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `gave Mr John Crichton one call… Whilk ye said Mr John accepted…
  ordained him to be entered to his tryals` = the call, acceptance, and entry to probationary trials of a
  minister; `modificatioun of localitie… make out 800 merks of stipend` = the legal fixing (modification)
  of a stipend out of local teinds; `give his band for assuring ye modificatioun` = a bond guaranteeing
  the stipend; `refer ye arrears of ye mortificatioun to law` = to litigate the endowment arrears; `ye
  Moor kirk` = the new kirk on the moor at Balneil (New Luce). **Both pages heavily water-stained/torn** —
  provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1647**.

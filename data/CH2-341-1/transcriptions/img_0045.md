# CH2/341/1 — Image 45 (folios 44v–45), Nov–Dec 1642 — Kirkcolm falls vacant (Porterfield's death); the Glenluce provision

Left page (folio **44a**, faded): the **parish of Kirkcolm is now vacant through the death
of Mr John Porterfield, its late minister** — pulpit supply is arranged **per vices** (Mr
John Livingston on **20 Nov**, Mr Hugh Hutcheson on the **first Sabbath of December
1642**). Also: a Stoneykirk supplication about **electing a ruling elder** (meeting 29
Nov); the neighbouring presbyteries' commissioners **cannot meet "through the distemper of
the weather and great floods,"** so a new meeting is set for **14 Dec**; and referrals
(James Rosse's absence abroad; **Marion Power** "with child") are **delayed**. Right page
(folio **45**, badly browned): the presbytery arranges the **provision of Glenluce** via a
**mortification** (handled through **Mr George Dick**, who is to go to **Edinburgh** about
it), and appoints **Mr James Blair** to the "common head" / the controversy.

### Modern English (partial — page faded)
Considering the state of **Kirkcolm, now vacant through the death of its late minister Mr
John Porterfield**, the presbytery ordains the pulpit **supplied by turns (*per vices*)** —
**Mr John Livingston to preach there 20 November, Mr Hugh Hutcheson the first Sabbath of
December 1642.** A **Stoneykirk supplication** about **electing a ruling elder** to attend
the presbytery: a meeting set for **Tuesday 29 November** (*Alexander Turnbull, Clerk*).
The **neighbouring presbyteries' commissioners could not meet, "through the distemper of
the weather and great floods,"** so a new meeting about the **Stoneykirk business** is set
for **14 December**, the Synod's committee to be advertised. Referrals **delayed**: **James
Rosse's** absence abroad; and the matter of **Marion [Power?]**, she being **with child**.
Further business touching a **committee of church affairs** (Mr James Blair to desire
attendance of gentry — an **Alexander Agnew**, the Sheriff, **Adair**, and others — to give
obedience) and the **exercise of the Synod of "Ayr and Galloway."** *(Much of this page is
faded.)*

## Notes & context
- **Mr John Porterfield is dead — Kirkcolm vacant.** A firm, datable biographical fact:
  **Mr John Porterfield, minister of Kirkcolm, died by November 1642**, and the presbytery
  arranges **pulpit supply by turns**. This **resolves the Glenluce-minister confusion**
  (imgs 0031, 0039): Porterfield was minister of **Kirkcolm**, not Glenluce — he merely
  *preached at* the Glenluce visitation as a neighbouring brother. Note also his earlier
  role in the leaked-deposition affair (img_0023). His person-page can now be closed off
  with a death date.
- **The Glenluce provision resolved by the presbytery itself.** Having rejected **James
  Rosse's** presentation of **Mr James Smith** (img_0044), the presbytery takes the
  **Glenluce mortification** in hand — arranging a **presentation to Mr George Dick** for
  the kirk's provision, on condition he **renounce manse/glebe claims and take the
  mortification only**, and **sends him to Edinburgh** to secure it. Whether this settles
  **Mr George Dick at Glenluce** (as minister) or merely makes him the endowment's agent is
  **unclear in the faded text — flag.** A key patronage/endowment thread for Glenluce.
- **Weather stops the courts.** The neighbouring-presbytery commissioners **cannot travel
  "through the distemper of the weather and great floods"** (winter 1642) — a vivid,
  human detail, and a reminder of how physical conditions governed church governance.
- **Marion Power "with child."** A referral about **Marion Power** (Gilbert Power's
  daughter, the Lord's-day-disturbance case, img_0043) is **delayed because she is
  pregnant** — if the reading holds, a poignant human note and a reason her censure was
  postponed. **Confirm the name (Power vs French).**
- **National church business.** The **committee of church affairs**, gentry summoned to
  "give obedience," and the **exercise of the Synod of Ayr and Galloway** — the presbytery
  is embedded in the wider 1642–43 Covenanter mobilisation.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `per vices` = by turns/in rotation; `allanerlie` =
  only, solely; `mortificatioun` = a perpetual endowment; `distemperatnes of the weather`
  = foul weather; `pass from all clame` = renounce every claim; `the work of god's kirk` =
  the church's mission. **Both pages badly browned** — this opening is among the least
  legible; treat most readings as provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. **Dates 1642.**

# CH2/341/1 — Image 90 (folios 89v–90), 18 March 1646 — heavier penalties ordered; a named malignant suspect; Glenluce stalls again

Left page (folio **89a**): **Presbytery at Stranraer, 18 March 1646** — an exercise (Mr James Blair
made and added, 1 Thess. 1:9, approved); absent the elders of Kirkcolm and Kirkmaiden; **★ a named
malignant suspect** — reported that **[George Cathcart?] of Colmonell has his residence [near] French,
and being suspected a malignant, Mr James Blair is to speak him and try whether he has given
satisfaction to the presbytery of Ayr; if not, Mr James Bell to summon him**; the brethren **still
could not be informed of any malignants** in their charges; **Mr John Dick reports he has written to
the presbytery in Ireland concerning John Pirie, the fugitive adulterer**; and the **acts of Parliament
against malignants to be put in practice and registered.** Right page (folio **90**): **★ an act that
kirk sessions shall no longer restrict themselves to small customary penalties but extend fines to the
act of Parliament, so far as the offenders' ability will permit**; the **Glenluce planting stalls for
James Ross of Barneill's absence** (the former ordinance renewed); the **ordinance to deliver the witch
processes to the commission renewed**; **John Cloud (adulterer) beginning to give signs of repentance**;
**John McDowell not yet entered but willing**; **Effie Adair, like diligence**; and the **ordinance
about repairing the fabric of the kirk of Stoneykirk** [renewed].

## Notes & context
- **★ Heavier discipline fines — aligning sessions with the act of Parliament.** A revealing
  church-finance/discipline reform: the presbytery orders **kirk sessions to stop levying "small
  customary penalties" and instead extend fines to the level set by the act of Parliament, scaled to
  the offender's ability to pay.** Evidence of the 1640s drive to make discipline bite (and to fund the
  poor/kirk from scandal fines), and of the interplay between civil statute and kirk-session practice.
  A good glossary/theme node (scandal penalties; the act of Parliament on fines).
- **★ A named malignant suspect.** The **process against malignants** (imgs 0087–0089) finally names a
  target: **[George Cathcart?] of Colmonell, residing near French**, suspected a malignant — Blair to
  test whether he has already **satisfied the presbytery of Ayr** (Colmonell being in Carrick/Ayr
  presbytery), else Bell to summon him. Shows the cross-presbytery handling of a suspected royalist
  sympathiser and gives a named person to track.
- **Threads renewed, not resolved.** The **Glenluce planting stalls again** on **James Ross of
  Barneill's absence** (the mortification/stipend deadlock, imgs 0084–0089); the **witch processes** are
  still to be delivered to the commission for execution; the **Stoneykirk repair** ordinance is renewed;
  and the **adultery penitents** (Cloud beginning, McDowell willing, Adair) inch along. A vivid picture
  of a court doggedly re-minuting the same unfinished business week by week.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `sall not restrict them selves to so small customary penalties…
  but extend… to the act of parliament` = raise discipline fines to the statutory level; `suspected a
  malignant… satisfactioun to the presbytery of Ayr` = a royalist sympathiser who may have already done
  penance in his home presbytery; `no progress… in respect of… his absence; renewes the ordinance` =
  the matter continued for want of a key party; `processes… to be put in execution against the witches`
  = the witchcraft prosecution to proceed; `beginning to give signes of repentance` = starting public
  penance. **Both pages faded** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1646.

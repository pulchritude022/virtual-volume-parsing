Laird of **Ardwell**, accused of adultery with Jean Fyfe and — more precisely, by his own
account — of being wrongly defamed as guilty of it. His name recurs across the whole
volume in two quite different registers: as a defendant fighting a slander case for years,
and, separately, as a named Engagement-complier in the 1648 purge.

## The adultery-slander case

See [The Ardwell adultery-slander case](entity:event/the-ardwell-adultery-slander-case) for
the full narrative: an accusation, an appeal to the General Assembly, a Synod order to
confess or purge by oath, and years of delaying supplications before Ardwell finally agrees
to purge himself in late 1648 [img_118, img_131–133, img_142].

## A name that recurs in earlier discipline, too

An earlier folio records **Ardnell/Ardwell's** deposition as the source implicating **Robert
Tarbret** and **John McCulloch** in the bribe at the heart of the Mr Gilbert Power case — a
reminder that "Ardwell" as a title covers more than one generation of the family across the
volume's ~1641–1652 span, and that name matches spanning decades should be treated with some
caution [entity:event/mr-gilbert-power-s-deposition-and-reposition, img_0002].

## Named among the 1648 Engagers

Separately from the adultery case, "the laird of Ardwell" appears on the presbytery's 1648
register of named Engagement compliers, laid before the civil committee as part of
[the post-Engagement purge](entity:event/the-post-engagement-purge-of-malignants) [img_146].

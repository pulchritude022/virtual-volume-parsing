# CH2/341/1 — Image 65 (folios 64v–65), November 1644 — **a witchcraft commission sought from the Privy Council**

**A grave page.** The presbytery, having read the **depositions against several persons "suspect
of witchcraft,"** finds **"material points of witchcraft"** and resolves to **supplicate the
Lords of His Majesty's Privy Council for a commission to apprehend, try, and execute the law
against them.** These were real, named, vulnerable people; the names are transcribed here for the
record and flagged as uncertain.

Left page (folio **64a**): **Presbytery at Stranraer, [—] November 1644** — exercise (George Dick
/ Livingstone, Col. 4), elder commissions admitted (Stranraer, Leswalt, Portmontgomery), **Mr
Alexander Turnbull chosen moderator**, **Hew Hutcheson continued clerk**; the **fast reported kept
in the several kirks**; and a **letter to the General Assembly's commissioners** desiring the
presbytery send the **information about "the young woman that delated [informed against] the
witches."** Right page (folio **65**): the presbytery **reads and considers the depositions of
"famous witnesses" against Issobell Broughan (Stranraer), Marioun Russell (Culroy), Jonett Mc[—],
[—] Bleane, and Marioun Shennan (parishioner in Glenluce)**, finds material points of witchcraft,
and **draws up a presbyterial act and a supplication to the Privy Council for a commission** to
apprehend and try them.

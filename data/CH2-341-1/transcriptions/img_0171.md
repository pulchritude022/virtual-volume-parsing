## Notes & context
★★ **Mindork's sworn deposition — the malignant "speech" at last put in writing.** This delivers the payoff of the img 168–170 hunt: **the laird of Mindork's signed, sworn declaration** (dated Stranraer, 24 May 1649, "Sic subscribitur Mindork") that he personally **heard James Ross of Balneil say publicly, at supper at the Earl of [Ballabary?]'s table in [Blaketoune?], that "the Sectaries had done with the ministry in England … and would take them to live upon the people"** — i.e. reported approval of the New Model / Independent purge of the Presbyterian clergy. Delivered openly before many witnesses. This is exactly the kind of corroborating hearsay the presbytery needed to strengthen its case beyond "Balneil's own confession" (img 168). Further witnesses (Park Bennoch, Robert Clark, an Andrew of Stoneykirk) are to be cited.

★★ **The 1649 General Assembly commissioners + a national supplication.** The presbytery elects its commissioners to the General Assembly — **Mr James Blair, Mr John Dick, and Sir Robert Adair (ruling elder)** — and deputes **Mr John Crosse** to carry a multi-point **supplication to Parliament**: for arrears/moneys, a **settled judicatory to remedy adulteries**, the plantation business before the Commission, relief in uplifting ministers' maintenance because **"the country [is] impoverished by the Irish army and ill crops this last year"** (a rare, dateable economic note — post-1649 Galloway distress), and the **annexation of the parishes of [Kirkinner?]/[Kirkmadrine?]**.

★ **Visitation of Inch, 7 June 1649**, opens at the foot of the page — Mr John Dick (minister of Inch) preaches his ordinary (Micah). Clerk this sitting = **Jo. Hutcheson**.

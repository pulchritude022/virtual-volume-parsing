# CH2/341/1 — Image 17 (folios 16v–17), Feb–Mar 1643

Left page (folio **16a**) entrusts the **Irish-relief money** to four Stranraer men
(overseen by Livingston), works through the outstanding **Power witnesses** (the
**Sheriff younger** — French's brother-in-law — pleads imperfect memory; French's
tenants forborne), and opens a **marriage/desertion case** (Andrew Muir & Christian
Pyper). Right page (folio **17**): that case is remitted to the Session; **George
McCulloch and six others petition for Power's restoration**; and a letter from the
**Lords of Secret Council** to **Mr John Livingston** about **Ireland**. Hasty hand
throughout — tentative.

Mr George Dick delivered to the presbytery [a sum] from the collectors of the
**collection for Ireland's people**, which the presbytery orders delivered to **James
[Glover?], John Kennedy, Hew Cochran, and Robert Wilson** in Stranraer — with **Mr
John Livingston as overseer** — two of them, by Livingston's advice, to distribute
it and keep an account. The **Power witnesses** French named still to examine are:

forbear them to avoid all suspicion of partial deposition. The **Sheriff younger**,
present, refuses to depose (he is French's brother-in-law); the presbytery, confident
he would be impartial notwithstanding kinship, urges him — but he pleads **imperfect
memory** and asks a continuation, which is granted, till the next meeting (Tuesday at
Glenluce, on the Glenluce division per the Act of 29 Dec 1642). **Andrew Muir of
Inch**, fallen in fornication (or, some allege, adultery) with **Christian Pyper of
Stranraer**, he having (as alleged) a **wife living in Ireland**, referred by the
Session…

### Modern English (paraphrase — low confidence)

## Notes & context
- **A community divided over Power.** Alongside French's relentless prosecution,
  **George McCulloch and six other parishioners petition for Power to be restored
  to preach** — the first clear sign of *parish support* for the suspended minister.
  The presbytery holds all three papers (Power's, French's, the parishioners') till
  the process closes.
- **John Livingston and Ireland — a national figure.** "**Mr John Livingston**,"
  the presbytery clerk and (from folio 2) its first clerk, is almost certainly the
  famous Covenanter **John Livingston, minister of Stranraer (1638–1648)**, who had
  earlier served in Ulster. The **Lords of Secret Council** writing to seek advice
  about his "**direction to Ireland**" fits his known role in Scottish–Irish church
  affairs of the 1640s. A strong candidate for a full biographical page.
- **A hard marriage case.** **Andrew Muir (Inch)** and **Christian Pyper
  (Stranraer)**: he has a wife gone 19 years, reportedly childbearing and remarried
  in **Ireland**, status unknown. Unable to prove adultery/desertion/divorce, the
  presbytery refuses the adultery reading and remits them to the Session to be
  disciplined merely as fornicators — a window on how the kirk handled **desertion
  and presumptive bigamy** amid the disruption of the Irish wars.
- **Irish relief, administered.** The kingdom-wide collection is now concrete money
  handed to **James [Glover?], John Kennedy, Hew Cochran, Robert Wilson** of
  Stranraer, under Livingston's oversight.
- **New people:** **George McCulloch** (Power's supporter — another McCulloch),
  **Andrew Muir**, **Christian Pyper**, **the laird of Marton[?]**, **Thomas Agnew**,
  **[Archie] Neilson**, **John Given** (witnesses), the four relief-distributors, and
  the **Lords of Secret Council**.
- **Palaeography applied:** hasty secretary hand (a different scribe) → low
  confidence; `satisfie` = perform church discipline/penance; `excepted against` =
  objected to as a witness; `Iland` = Ireland; `Lords of Secreit councell` = the
  Privy Council of Scotland; `overture` = a formal proposal/reference. Dates "1642"
  are Old Style (= 1643). `y`+superscript → *th-*.

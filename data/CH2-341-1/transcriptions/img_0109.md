# CH2/341/1 — Image 109 (folios 108v–109), 24 February 1647 — Crichton's trial sermon approved; the Argyll contribution to Provost Porterfield of Glasgow; a multi-kirk penance; the dissenting Glenluce heritors again

Left page (folio **108a**): (tail) a letter presented desiring **a contribution be collected for the
distressed people of Argyll**, the brethren promising their diligence. Then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 24
February 1647** — absent Mr Alexander Turnbull and the Kirkmaiden elder; **★ an exercise (Mr John Dick made,
and Mr John Crichton added, 1 Thess. 2:19–20, both approved)** [Crichton's trial sermon]; the former
absences of Mr James Blair and the elders of Stoneykirk and Portmontgomery excused; **John Mc[—], called
and not appearing, to be summoned pro 3**; **the brethren present reported they had read the warning and
made intimation of James Ogilvy's [excommunication]**; **the contribution collected [for the poor] to be
kept in the hands of the [treasurer] till delivered for the end it was collected**; **★ the presbytery
ordained the moderator and Mr [James] Bell to receive the contribution for the distressed people of Argyll,
and to return a discharge from George Porterfield, provost of Glasgow, to whom it is to be delivered**; and
**Mr Hew Hutcheson appointed to preach at Stranraer the next Sabbath**. Right page (folio **109**): it is
ordained that **Stranraer be supplied for a time by the presbytery (Mr John Dick, Mr [James] Bell, Mr
Alexander Turnbull, Mr John Livingstone, Mr James Blair)**; **★ the which day compeared Margaret Agnew in
sackcloth, confessing her fall in adultery with [—] Campbell in [Miltoun]; and, because she should [avoid]
a scandalous [life], she was enjoined to give signs of repentance — first at Inch, 2nd at Stranraer, 3rd at
Leswalt, 4th at Portmontgomery — and then to compear at [Kirkmaiden] till she be satisfied**; and **★ the
which day the presbytery, having several times formerly desired the gentlemen and heritors of Glenluce to
make payment of their [share] and use diligence as appointed for payment of the arrears of the mortification
per the act of the General Assembly (and they still refusing), at last this day — there being present Thomas
Hay of Park, Hew Kennedy of [Sorbie?], John Cathcart of Genoch, [Hew] Kennedy of [Ardgirvane?], John
McDowell of [—], [Uchtred] Baillie of Meikle Imrogart, John Baillie of Little Imrogart and [—] Kennedy of
[Ardolan?] — the presbytery desired them to comply and make payment, or give surety, to James Ross of
Balneil before the next day; which if they shall do, and it be reported accordingly by James Ross to the
presbytery, the presbytery engages itself that it shall not oppose these heritors' having their
proportional relief of their [own share]**.

## Notes & context
- **★ Crichton's trial sermon approved.** On the appointed day (24 Feb), **Mr John Crichton "adds" the
  exercise (1 Thess. 2:19–20) and is approved** — the first of his ordination trials passed (cf. the entry
  to trials, img 0108). The planting of the Moor kirk of Glenluce (New Luce) moving step by step.
- **★ Confirmed: the Argyll contribution goes to George Porterfield, Provost of Glasgow.** This resolves
  the tentative "[Porterfield?] in Glasgow" of img 0096: the **relief collection for the distressed people
  of Argyll** (ravaged in the 1644–45 war) is to be **delivered to George Porterfield, provost of
  Glasgow**, with a discharge returned. **George Porterfield** was indeed a leading Glasgow Covenanter and
  Provost — a firm, checkable identification and a good node linking Galloway's charity to the national
  relief effort. (Note: distinct from the late *Mr John* Porterfield, minister of Kirkcolm.)
- **★ A four-kirk penance circuit.** **Margaret Agnew**, confessing adultery with **[—] Campbell of
  Miltoun**, is sentenced to **do public penance in sackcloth successively at Inch, Stranraer, Leswalt and
  Portmontgomery, then at [Kirkmaiden]** — an unusually itinerant, humiliating sentence (penance at every
  kirk of the district), showing how the kirk escalated shame for grave/repeated scandal.
- **★ The dissenting Glenluce heritors, named again, get a deadline.** The **stand-off over the
  mortification arrears** (imgs 0107–0108) continues: with the recalcitrant lairds **present in person**
  (Thomas Hay of Park, Kennedy of [Sorbie?], **Cathcart of Genoch** — cf. img 0105 — Kennedy of
  [Ardgirvane?], McDowell, the two Baillies of Imrogart, Kennedy of [Ardolan?]), the presbytery gives them
  **until "the next day" to pay or give surety to James Ross of Balneil**, promising in return **not to
  oppose their proportional relief**. A vivid negotiation between kirk and lairds over church money, and a
  second, corroborating roll of Glenluce heritor-names for the wiki.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `made intimatioun of James Ogilvie… fra excommunicatioun` = the
  published sentence against the malignant James Ogilvy (of Airlie, img 0102); `to returne a Discharge fra
  George Porterfield, provost of Glasgow` = a receipt from the Glasgow provost for the relief money; `give
  signes of repentance: first at Inch, 2 at Stranrawer…` = a circuit of public penance through several
  kirks; `make payment… or give suretie… before the next Day` = a deadline to pay or find caution;
  `proportionall relief of their awne pairt` = a rateable share/rebate. **Both pages heavily
  water-stained/torn** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1647**.

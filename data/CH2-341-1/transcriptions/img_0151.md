# CH2/341/1 — Image 151 (folios 150v–151)

The purge turns inward and downward. Left page (foliated **150a** = folio 150 verso):
**Mr John Crookes** reports his correspondence with Wigtown presbytery (which has
"done nothing" about the Sheriff); ★ **the presbytery examines its own members one by
one for Engagement compliance — all approved**; ★ **the Sheriff of Galloway is content
to delay taking the Covenant** until cleared of my Lord Galloway's charge that he
counselled him "to arrest of money"; **Blair and Lowrie** are sent to Wigtown (19
December) to try the Galloway/Sheriff matter and get a copy or sight of **Balneil's**
process; and **Mr John Crosse presents the paper of the Session of old Glenluce (12
December 1648)** listing the ★★ **"variance" (Engagement compliance) of the several
gentlemen of Glenluce**, referred up so the presbytery can declare who is debarred
from renewing the Covenant. The right page (folio 151) enters that paper: its
preamble (per the last General Assembly's act, sessions are to take **exact trial**),
then the ★★ **declaration of Thomas Hay of Park** — a detailed, half-faded narrative
of how he came to take **a protection/warrant, dealing with Duke Hamilton and the
Earl of Callendar** (with **Mr John Levingstoun's** advice) — and the beginning of a
second gentleman's examination.

### Modern English
**Mr John Crookes** reported that he had kept correspondence with Wigtown presbytery
and presented their written answer to the presbytery's letter; and being asked by the
moderator whether he had any answer to the church's letter concerning **the Sheriff**,
he answered that Wigtown presbytery had **done nothing in that business**. He is
approved for his diligence.

★ The presbytery think fit, **before entering upon the renewing or taking of the
Covenant, to try their own number** — what variance any of them had in relation to
the unlawful Engagement; the brethren being removed one by one, **all were approved**.

Concerning **the Sheriff of Galloway**: in respect he is content to **delay his taking
of the Covenant** until he may be cleared of that expression my Lord Galloway had of
him — namely, that he had been a counsellor to him "to undergo the charge in the act
of Engagement."

The presbytery, informed that both the committee and presbytery would convene at
**Wigtown, 19 December**, appoint **Mr James Blair and Mr James Lowrie** to keep
correspondence with Wigtown presbytery and with some of the [committee], to show to my
**Lord Galloway** and try whether the Sheriff had counselled him to seize money; and
also to desire from the committee **a copy — at least a sight — of Balneil's process**.

The same day, **Mr John Crosse presented a paper** containing the **variance of the
several gentlemen within Glenluce** in relation to the unlawful Engagement, referred by
the Session to the church, its tenor following: *"The Session convened at the old kirk
of Glenluce, 12 December 1648. The same day, the minister regretted before the Session
that [so] many are to be debarred from renewing the Covenant, by virtue of an act of
the Commission of the Kirk..."* (continues).

## Notes & context
- ★★ **The Session of Glenluce's own examination paper.** This is the grassroots
  bottom of the whole purge documented on imgs 144–150: the **kirk session physically
  interrogating each heritor** and recording his declared "variance," so the presbytery
  can decide who is debarred from renewing the Covenant. The minister even "regrets" how
  many are to be debarred — a striking human note about a whole gentry class caught by
  the post-Engagement discipline.
- ★★ **Thomas Hay of Park's fuller story.** Where img 149 gave Park's supplication, this
  gives his **examined declaration**: he says he had no hand in raising the Engagement,
  refused money for his "proportions" and would only give men, and then — pressed —
  **resolved to seek a warrant/protection from Duke Hamilton, and considered going to
  the Earl of Callendar**, on **Mr John Levingstoun's** advice about how to get it in
  writing. The two accounts should be read together for his person-page (note the
  tension between "no hand in it" and the warrant-seeking).
- ★ **Self-examination of the presbytery.** Before administering the Covenant to
  others, the ministers **try themselves** one by one — all cleared. A neat instance of
  the court applying the discipline to its own members first.
- ★ **The Sheriff's scruple.** The Sheriff of Galloway **postpones taking the Covenant**
  until cleared of the money-seizure charge — evidence that Covenant-renewal was being
  used as a live test of political loyalty.
- New name: **William Ronald(s) of [—]** — another of the recurring Ronald(s) surname
  in the Engagement material.

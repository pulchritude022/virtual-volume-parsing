### Modern English
[Craving] their appearance before you, protesting that these my reasonable
desires be not prejudicial to me touching the exceptions I gave in against them
or any of them.

*3.* Item: seeing your wisdoms have taken the deposition of Mr John Porterfield
touching the first seven articles of the libel, that your wisdoms may be pleased,
for your better information in so important a matter, [to write to] the Presbytery
of Wigtown, that they may take the deposition of Mr George [Blair?], Mr George
Galloway and Mr Joseph Cleland — who all were present at that time as well as he
— and that you would send their depositions to your wisdoms under their clerk's
hand; protesting likewise that this my equitable desire be not prejudicial to me
touching the exception I made about the manner of proof of the first seven
articles.

*4.* Item: that your wisdoms would write to the other presbyteries where the
inhabitants belong (or their old presbyteries or synod, if distant), whom the
persons affirm — as his libel will testify — [can prove] the said above points;
or at least desire the said presbytery to peruse them and send to your wisdoms
the extract of what concerns these 11 points.

*5.* Item: when the depositions of the witnesses are closed, before any proceeding
to sentence and final determination of this matter, I humbly crave that your
wisdoms may be pleased to give me the extract of the particulars deponed by your
witnesses that seem to prove any point of the libel given in against me, that I
may have time sufficient to peruse them and give in my answers thereto, [or for]
further information before the members of the judicature and your wisdoms' answer.

*Thus subscribed:* **Mr Gilbert Power.**

The presbytery [delayed] to give answer to this, until the witnesses present —
who have been long detained — be first examined.

## Notes & context

Both pages continue the case against **Mr Gilbert Power**, minister of Stoneykirk.
Left page (folio **4a**) finishes his **supplication** (numbered demands 3–5,
subscribed "Mr Gilbert Power"); the presbytery defers its answer until detained
witnesses are examined. Right page (folio **5**) is dense **witness testimony**:
John McHum (McKim) re-examined on the poinded oxen, a ruling admitting Patrick
Wilson but suspending Robert Neilson, and Patrick Wilson's deposition about years
of corn payments to Power. Badly faded throughout — treat as tentative.
- **Case continues (Power).** This opening is entirely the discipline case against
  **Mr Gilbert Power, minister of Stoneykirk**, remitted from the Provincial
  Assembly on the libel of McDowell of French (see img_0004). Folio 4v completes
  Power's **supplication** (three numbered demands: depose the Wigtown witnesses;
  write to the other presbyteries; and give him an extract of the particulars
  before final sentence — all standard defence procedure), signed **Mr Gilbert
  Power**. The presbytery defers answering until the long-detained witnesses are
  examined.
- **New names — Wigtown witnesses** Power wants deposed: **Mr George Blair[?],
  Mr George Galloway, Mr Joseph Cleland** (all "present at that time"). The
  **Presbytery of Wigtown** is asked to take their depositions — a neighbouring
  presbytery in Wigtownshire.
- **Substance of the libel emerging:** poinding (distraining) of a father's
  **oxen**, whether sold outright or held in **warandice** (security/wadset);
  years of **corn payments** (3 bolls yearly) which tenants say were given "of
  goodwill at the first" but latterly "kept up" unwillingly — i.e. the charge is
  that Power **oppressed** parishioners by extracting payments/interest not truly
  due. Coheres with the annualrent/usury theme of img_0002 and the 500-merk bond
  / false-testament of img_0003–0004.
- **Witness procedure:** absent witnesses to be pursued by their kirk sessions;
  **Patrick Wilson admitted**; **Robert Neilson objected to and his admission
  suspended** (Power "repells" him as being [young?]).
- **Palaeography applied:** `poinded` = distrained for debt; `warandice` = surety/
  security (Scots law); `repells` = rejects (a witness); `compered` = appeared;
  `boll` = grain measure; `awand/awing` = owing; `subscribitur` (Latin) = subscribed;
  `viz.` / `Item` = list formulae. `y`+superscript expanded to *th-*.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 107 (folios 106v–107), 3 February 1647 — the Glenluce heritors split over the parish-division act; the presbytery resolves to sue the dissenters for the kirk's arrears

Left page (folio **106a**): (the Glenluce sitting, continued) a recital that the ordinary dues **might
accrue and appertain to the minister serving the cure at the new kirk [Balneil]**, and about the
**proportioning of the [Balneil] stipend** per the act; but the **arrears, being in the hands of the
heritors and so unprofitable, with the necessary disbursements daily accruing and exhausting the greater
part — to the notable prejudice of the public good and hindrance of the planting of the kirks** — the
moderator, in name of the presbytery, **desired to know whether all the gentlemen and heritors would
adhere to the act of the General Assembly**; **★ whereunto James Ross of Balneil, John McDowell of [—],
John Adair of [Genoch], [Uchtred] McDowell of Barlockart and John Kennedy of [Cruggleton?] agreed; but
Thomas Hay of [—], [—] of Sorbie, Hew Kennedy of [Ardgirvane?], John Baillie of Little Imrogart and [—]
Baillie of Meikle Imrogart dissented, alleging that first they besought to see a way how they might get
relief of their proportional part of 380 merks [that had been] given to Mr George Dick**. Right page
(folio **107**): the presbytery **recommended [the parties] to decide their difference [amicably] if they
thought good, the more advisedly, [by chosen men] — whereunto James Ross of Balneil consented; but the
heritors that dissented refused the act of the General Assembly there also**; **★ therefore the presbytery,
considering their denial of payment of the just arrears due to the kirk, and their refusal to have their
[allowances] satisfied and cleared these many years — and being sensible that the present [grudges] might
hinder the planting of the kirks — ordained and desired that commissioners be sent by them to inform the
Commission of the General Assembly, to crave their assistance and concurrence, together with the
assistance and concurrence of the advocate [and] agent of the kirk, for prosecuting the matter either
legally before the Lords of Session, or by supplication before the [Estates] of Parliament, or any other
judge competent, for recovery of the said arrears and the expenses debursed in obtaining thereof**; and
**the moderator was ordained to read this act in presence of the heritors foresaid, which accordingly was
done**; then **★ the which day compeared Thomas Adair in Glenluce, [enjoined] to give signs of repentance
for his [fault]** [continued on the next opening].

## Notes & context
- **★★ The Glenluce heritors take sides — a named roll for and against.** This is the human drama behind
  the parish-division act (img 0106): the presbytery **polls the Glenluce heritors** on whether they will
  obey the General Assembly, and the record names them by estate. **Consenting: James Ross of Balneil,
  John McDowell of [—], John Adair of [Genoch], [Uchtred] McDowell of Barlockart, John Kennedy of
  [Cruggleton?]. Dissenting: Thomas Hay of [—], [—] of Sorbie, Hew Kennedy of [Ardgirvane?], John Baillie
  of Little Imrogart, [—] Baillie of Meikle Imrogart** — the dissenters balking at their share of the
  **380 merks given to Mr George Dick**. A superb genealogical/estate node for the wiki: a snapshot of
  the mid-17th-century lairds of the Glenluce district and their factions. (All names uncertain — please
  confirm the estates.)
- **★ The kirk turns to the courts.** Faced with the dissenters' **refusal to pay the arrears due to the
  kirk**, the presbytery resolves to **send commissioners to the Commission of the General Assembly** and,
  with the **kirk's advocate/agent**, to **prosecute the arrears "either legally before the Lords of
  Session, or by supplication before the Estates of Parliament, or any other judge competent."** A third
  instance (after the Glenluce Abbey teinds and the Cathcart-of-Genoch mortification) of the presbytery
  using the civil law to secure church funds — and a vivid picture of how the "planting" of a parish
  depended on wringing money from reluctant landowners.
- **The act read to their faces.** A nice procedural touch: **the moderator read the act aloud "in
  presence of the heritors foresaid"** — putting the recalcitrant lairds formally on notice.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `Whether… all ye gentlemen and heritors Wold adhere to ye act of ye
  Generall assemblie` = the loyalty poll on the Assembly's division act; `Did Dissent; alledging… their
  proportionall part of 380 merks` = objecting over their share of the endowment; `prosecuting ye matter…
  before ye Lords of Session, or by supplicatioun before ye Estates of parliament, or any other Judge
  competent` = pursuing recovery in whichever civil forum; `arrears due to ye kirk` = unpaid stipend/teind
  owed; `read this act in presence of ye heritors` = formal intimation. **Both pages heavily
  water-stained/torn** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1647**.

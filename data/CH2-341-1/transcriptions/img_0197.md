## Folio 196a (left page)  *(confidence: medium)*

### Modern English
**Mr James Blair reported he had spoken the Sheriff about possessing Mr John Dick in his glebe; the answer was that, on Mr John's advertisement, he should be possessed any day he pleased.** **Mr John Scott, Mr James Blair, and Mr John Crosse reported they had declared their presbyterial diligence [to the committee]. Mr John Dick passed to the Committee of the Synod, which was well pleased therewith; but because there was no real performance on the parish's part, [the committee] cited the parish *apud acta* to compear at the next meeting at Wigton on the 26th of this instant, and required the presbytery to send commissioners.** Accordingly the presbytery appoints **Mr John Scott, Mr Alexr Turnbull, and Mr James Lawrie, ministers, and Wester McDowall of Freuch, ruling elder**, to attend. **The presbytery, also desired to correspond with the Presbytery of Wigton in the trial of Mr William Thomson (about the 26th–27th), appoints Turnbull and Lawrie to attend.** **Mr John Scott reported he had spoken the Bailie of the Regality about the robbery; and, that punishment be speedily done, it is recommended to the ministers of Luce and Stranraer to get the extract of the process before the Committee of War and deliver it to the said Bailie;** the ordinance renewed that Scott cite **Thomas Baillie** to next meeting. **Report is made that the thanksgiving was kept in all the** [congregations] …

### Modern English
… **congregations of the presbytery.**

**Compeared Frank Wilson; and the presbytery, having heard of his miscarriages in the country and his gross obdurate malignancy — being popishly affected, and having been taken prisoner in the frigate at Loch Ryan — rebuked him publicly and warned him of the evil of his courses; and he professing a purpose to depart the country shortly, the presbytery required him to do it with diligence.** **Item, Mr McDowall (who bore office as [Lt-Col] in the late unlawful wars) again supplicated to be admitted to profess his repentance for his sin in engaging; the presbytery, considering he had long been in Ireland, appointed enquiry to be made into the carriage of his conversation there, and meanwhile ordered him to confer with Mr John Dick and Mr James Lawrie.**

**Compeared James Ross of Balneil, and supplicated to be admitted to subscribe the Solemn League and Covenant. The tenor of his supplication follows:**

> **Unto the Right Reverend the Moderator and remaining brethren of the Presbytery of Stranraer, the humble Supplication of James Ross of Balneil. Humbly sheweth**, that in respect of my great and earnest desire to be admitted to the renewing of the Solemn League and Covenant — and notwithstanding the many supplications I have made to your wisdoms to that effect — yet I am informed that, in relation to an act remitting me to the Commission of the Kirk, made in winter last in my absence, I will now supplicate in vain to have it remitted [back]; and seeing I have no other business that requires my going …

## Notes & context
★★★ **James Ross of Balneil's supplication — his own words.** The long Balneil malignancy case (running since ~img 100) yields a **verbatim first-person petition**: his "great and earnest desire to be admitted to the renewing of the Solemn League and Covenant," his frustration at the act (passed "in winter last, in my absence") **remitting his case up to the Commission of the Kirk**, and his wish to have it referred back to the presbytery so he can finally subscribe. A prize document — the voice of the volume's central penitent, formally begging readmission. (Continues to img 198.)

★★★ **"Taken prisoner in the frigate at Loch Ryan" — a 1650 naval capture.** A striking war datum: **Frank Wilson, a popishly-affected malignant, "taken prisoner in the frigate at Loch Ryan,"** is hauled before the presbytery, rebuked, and ordered to leave the country. A frigate action / capture in **Loch Ryan** (the sea-loch on the Rhins' east side) in the tense summer of 1650 — the kind of local echo of the wider Anglo-Scottish naval war that rarely surfaces in kirk records.

★★ **The planting of Mr John Dick at Inch grinds on.** The Sheriff will put Dick **in possession of the glebe** on request, but the **Synod committee, finding "no real performance on the parish's part," cites the Inch parish *apud acta* to Wigton (26 June)** and the presbytery sends commissioners (Scott, Turnbull, Lawrie, and elder Wester McDowall of Freuch). Also: **correspondence with the Presbytery of Wigton in the trial of Mr William Thomson**, and the **robbery process** (Thomas Baillie) pushed toward the Committee of War.

★ **Another Engager (a McDowall, lately long in Ireland)** re-supplicates to profess repentance; enquiry ordered into his conduct in Ireland. And confirmation the **12 June thanksgiving [for the deliverance from Montrose] was duly kept** in all the kirks.

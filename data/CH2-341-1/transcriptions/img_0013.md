# CH2/341/1 — Image 13 (folios 12v–13), 15 Dec 1642 (Power case)

Left page (folio **12a**) continues the **witness depositions**: John Wilson on the
corn payments; the presbytery declines to ask **Alexander McDowell** whether he
fornicated with **Marion Power** (his wife) before marriage; then McDowell — **Mr
Gilbert Power's son-in-law** — deposes on collecting annualrent in bere from
**David McCulloch**. Right page (folio **13**): French moves to **censure Power for
preaching while suspended**; Power supplicates, defending the **£5 "budd"** in the
Tarbret matter as a conditional intermediary payment.

## Folio 12v (left page, marked "12a")  *(confidence: medium)*  — depositions of John Wilson & Alexander McDowell

```
Alexander McDowell was sworne in Mr Gilberts presence[.] Interro-

he received, in name & behalf of Mr Gilbert his father in law,
from David McCulloch, halfe a boll of bodded [= malted] beir for the

I received halfe a boll of beir from David McCulloch, &

that yeir that I received it ———
```

### Modern English
[The last woman] "knows not what an oath means," and says the Synod's mind was
that no women should be admitted witnesses. The presbytery continues the discussion
of the exceptions against the Sheriff younger and the three women to a fuller day.

**John Wilson**, sworn: denies being suborned. Asked whether over the last 20 years
Power oppressed him by taking 3 half-pecks of corn yearly — he answers he saw Power
get this corn before he (Wilson) came of age, and since has measured it himself and
paid it willingly, because the rest of the parish paid it. Asked whether Power broke
up (or caused break up) his father's barn door and took out the corn — he was not
present; his mother was angry that "the lass" put her hand to the boll of corn, then
measured and gave it herself; this he heard but did not witness.

The presbytery **declines French's request** to ask **Alexander McDowell** whether
he fornicated with **Marion Power, his wife**, before marriage. McDowell, sworn:
not asked to say anything but truth; asked whether, **in the name of Mr Gilbert his
father-in-law**, he received from **David McCulloch** half a boll of malted bere for
the **annualrent of 47 merks** in 1639 and 1640 — he answers he received half a boll
of bere from David McCulloch, but once only, in the spring, within these 4 years
since he came to his father-in-law's; whether it was malted he cannot tell.

## Folio 13 (right page)  *(confidence: medium)*  — censure motion; Power's Tarbret defence

### Diplomatic transcription

```
I humblie crave that Mr Gilbert may be censured for the

Mr Gilbert power gave in a paper the tenor wherof
followes[.]     Mr Gilbert powers humble supplicatioun to      [margin: Mr Gilbert & answer to Tarbrets matter]
the presbiterie of Stranrawer[.]     Seing it hath
pleased your ws, at the desyre of my accuser, to sus-
pend me from the exercise of my ministrie, upon con-
sideration of that article of his lybell for cloaking of
adulterie & taking budds [= bribes] therfor, Instanced by him in
the mater of Robert Tarbret, I humblie crave that
your ws would more graithlie [= thoroughly] consider my answer
given in already to that instance[.] wherunto, for your
better informatioun, I add these following, viz[.] when
Ardnell & John McCulloch came to me & dealt for the said
Robert Tarbret (declaring that he would never confesse
his fault, but would purge by his oath), promising to cause
him satisfie publictlie for his disobedience, & to [undergo]
the [merits] of penaltie thereupon[.] wherof Ardnell urged me to
receive fyve punds, which I took at his earnest desyre,
but told them plainly that I would give them no resolute
answer till first I had acquainted the Session therewith,
& followed their advyse[.] which (I am confident) nather
Ardnell nor [John] will deny, being posed on their oaths[.]
Thereafter the said Robert Tarbret, by my knowledge,
without any direction ather from the Session or me,
went to the publick place of repentance[.] & therfor I
spoke nothing to him, but after the publick worship of God [...]
```

### Modern English
**French's paper:** he craves that Power be **censured for contemning the
presbytery** — for preaching, since his suspension, in his own house and close —
and be suspended till sentence; also that a letter be written to the **laird of
[Mardrocht?]** to come to the next presbytery to witness the libel. *Subscribed:
French younger.*

**Power's supplication:** seeing the presbytery has suspended him on the article of
cloaking adultery and taking **budds** (bribes) in the Robert Tarbret matter, he
asks them to reconsider his answer, adding this: when **Ardnell and John McCulloch**
came dealing for Tarbret — declaring Tarbret would never confess but would purge by
oath, and promising to cause him satisfy publicly for his disobedience and undergo
the penalty — **Ardnell urged Power to receive £5, which he took at Ardnell's
earnest desire**, but told them plainly he would give no resolute answer until he
had acquainted the Session and followed its advice (which neither Ardnell nor John
will deny on oath). Thereafter Tarbret, of his own accord and without direction
from Session or Power, went to the public place of repentance; so Power spoke
nothing to him [but after public worship…].

## Notes & context
- **Genealogy — a family knot around Power.** This opening yields concrete kinship:
  **Alexander McDowell is Mr Gilbert Power's son-in-law**, married to **Marion
  Power** (so almost certainly Power's daughter). McDowell collected Power's
  **annualrent in bere-barley from David McCulloch** — a **McCulloch** paying
  interest, directly relevant to the family research focus. And in the Tarbret
  defence, **John McCulloch** (with "Ardnell" = the laird of **Ardwell**, itself a
  McCulloch estate) acts as go-between. Three McCulloch/MacCulloch touchpoints on
  one opening: **David McCulloch** (debtor), **John McCulloch** (Tarbret's agent),
  and Ardwell.
- **Power's defence of the bribe.** He reframes the "budd" (bribe): the £5 was
  pressed on him by Ardnell as part of a deal to have Tarbret purge by oath and do
  public repentance; Power says he took it only provisionally, pending Session
  advice — and Tarbret did public repentance anyway. This is his answer to the very
  charge on which he was suspended (img_0012).
- **Contempt while suspended.** French escalates: Power has kept **preaching in his
  own house and close** despite suspension — so French seeks a contempt censure.
- **Witness-competency law.** The objection to the women stands on two grounds:
  *interest/character* ("common") and *incapacity* ("knows not what an oath means",
  "ignorant"); the Synod's rule "no women should be admitted witnesses" is invoked.
  The presbytery defers to a fuller meeting.
- **Palaeography applied:** `budd`/`budds` = bribe(s) (Scots); `graithlie` =
  thoroughly/properly; `bodded beir` = malted bere barley (bere = 4-row barley);
  `boll` = grain measure; `come to perfection` = come of age; `closse` = close (the
  yard/enclosure by a house); `posed on their oaths` = questioned under oath.
  `y`+superscript → *th-*.

     Machrimore / Mochrum?).

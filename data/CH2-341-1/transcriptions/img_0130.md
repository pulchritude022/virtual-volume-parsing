# CH2/341/1 — Image 130 (folios 129v–130)

Left page (foliated **129a** = folio 129 verso) continues the **Stranraer sitting
of 5 April 1648** with a run of clearly-headed acts: (1) the presbytery appoints
**Mr James Blair** and another to have the woman who was present with Marion (the
img 128–129 paternity probation) compear at the next meeting; (2) **Katherine
McFinnen**, adulteress, who had fled the kirk's censures to Ireland, compears **in
sackcloth**, acknowledges her adultery with **John Sloman**, and is enjoined to
give signs of repentance; (3) the widows-and-orphans roll (Wigtown) is again
pressed on the Moderator; (4) a recommendation about **Mr John Dick's gleib**;
(5) a **letter from the Commission of the Kirk with new Catechisms** and copies of
sundry propositions; (6) **Ardwell** compears but his case is again delayed; and
(7) ★ the **parishioners of Stonykirk give in a unanimous call to Mr James
Lowrie** to be their minister. The right page (folio 130) closes the sitting with
a privy trial ("all approven"), opens a further **April sitting**, records **Mr
James Lowrie's trial exercise** (approved), and then the headline event: ★ a
**call to transport Mr John Levingstoun (Livingstone) to the kirk of Ancrum**,
petitioned by the **Earl of Lothian** and the Ancrum parishioners via **Thomas
Dickson, writer in Edinburgh**, with a presentation to the benefice — to which the
presbytery replies that it will require due process (all parties heard) and makes
a verbal appeal to the Synod.

The same day, the presbytery, considering that the woman who was present with
Marion and [—] of the soldiers might do much for clearing the truth, the rest of
the presbytery therefore appoint Mr James Blair and [Alexander —] to cause the

special considerations it is delayed until the next presbytery, and he is summoned
again to compear that day.

James Lowrie** to be their minister, which call he accepted; the presbytery

## Notes & context
- ★★ **Call to transport John Livingstone to Ancrum.** This is a historically
  significant entry: **Mr John Levingstoun (John Livingstone, 1603–1672)**, the
  celebrated Covenanter minister of Stranraer, is here being called away to
  **Ancrum** (Roxburghshire) at the instance of the **Earl of Lothian** (William
  Kerr, 3rd Earl), who presents him to the benefice. Livingstone was in fact
  transported from Stranraer to Ancrum in 1648, so this minute captures the
  opening move of that translation. The presbytery does not simply consent: it
  asserts its power to transport, insists on due process ("all parties... might be
  heard"), and the Earl's agent takes instruments and appeals to the Synod —
  standard procedure in a contested transportation.
- ★ **Call to Mr James Lowrie (Stonykirk).** The young candidate flagged in
  earlier openings receives a unanimous parish call to Stonykirk and undergoes his
  trial exercise (approved). Track his admission in subsequent sittings.
- ★ **Katherine McFinnen's penance:** a completed discipline case — flight to
  Ireland to escape censure, then return, sackcloth, confession of adultery with
  John Sloman, and enjoined repentance. A vivid instance of the cross-border
  (Galloway ⇄ Ulster) flight-from-discipline pattern recurring in this volume.
- **Commission of the Kirk new Catechisms:** distribution of the newly-produced
  catechetical material (the Westminster Larger/Shorter Catechisms were approved
  by the GA in 1648) plus "propositions" — the Kirk pushing doctrinal standards
  down to presbytery level during the Engagement crisis.
- **Ardwell** delayed again — consistent with the img 128 order to purge by oath;
  the case keeps rolling forward without resolution.

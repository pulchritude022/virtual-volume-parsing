**Concerning the first article** — whether his commission by the Parliament was obtained [by fair means] or no; and if any of those who subscribed that commission disclaimed it afterward; and whether any dissuaded Balneil from taking that commission before the Parliament as a means to advance the design of malignancy.

**Concerning the second article** — referred to Mr John Dick and Mr John Crosse [to gather] declarations.

**Concerning the third article** — (1) whether any advice was required of the Committee members at Kirkcowan, on which Monday; (2) whether Balneil reasoned first about taking it; (3) whether the act of debarring was mentioned, read, or understood by that day; (4) whether the same was professedly administered and related to the act of debarring; (5) **what number rose and what number sat, free, in the Committee when the same was urged upon the Committee men** [i.e. a division/vote].

## Notes & context
★★ **The Balneil probation laid bare.** This opening is the presbytery's own **structured checklist of the still-uncleared "presumptions"** in James Ross of Balneil's malignancy process — a rare window on how a 1649 kirk court actually built (or failed to build) a case under the Act of Classes. Article by article they specify what must be proved: *how* his parliamentary commission was obtained and whether co-signatories later disclaimed it; whether anyone dissuaded him from taking it "as a means to advance the design of malignancy"; and — most concretely — for the Kirkcowan Committee episode, **who rose and who sat "free" when the act of debarring was urged** (a remembered division of the room). The telling admission is at the top of the right page: **"if any further can be heard than Balneil's own confession"** — i.e. beyond his own words, the evidence is thin.

★ **The hunt for corroborating "speeches."** The one new thread is hearsay of malignant **speeches allegedly spoken "at the Earl of [—]'s table in [Blaketoune?]"**, reported at second hand via **Mr John Park, the Wigtown-presbytery correspondent** (this fixes img 167's "Mr John Park" as the Wigtown correspondent). The **laird of Mindork** is to be brought in to declare what he heard, and the Wigtown correspondence is to chase it further. So the case is now reaching outside the presbytery's bounds for witnesses — a sign of how determined (or how stuck) they are.

Routine: preaching rota (Dalgleish reported, Blair next); **George [Alexstoune?]** cited a second time for disobedience to the Stranraer session; **Mr John Crosse "late clerk"** (he handed over on 25 April, img 166) delivers the papers about the psalm books and a kirk commission.

## Notes & context
★★ **Balneil begins his public satisfaction.** The GA's remit (img 175) is now put in motion: **the extracted Balneil process is referred to the Commission of the Kirk**, Scott is to advertise Balneil to appear, and by the September sitting **Balneil compears and is "ordained to answer the following articles contained in his own confession"** — beginning with "the unseasonable carrying of a [cross-petition] to Parliament … to advance the malignant design." The prosecution has become a **structured confession-by-articles**, the mechanism for public satisfaction. (Continues onto img 177.)

★★ **The 1649 acts put in execution locally.** A clean audit trail of the acts brought back from the GA/Parliament (img 175): the **fast of 26 August kept**, the seasonable warning and the **act for absolving Engagers** read; **Turnbull delivers the widows'-and-orphans' act to Sir Robert Adair**; **Scott delivers the witch-trial Commission to the justices** — both "to be put in execution." So both the poor-relief machinery and the 1649 witch-hunt are formally launched in the Rhins.

★ **A bribery-and-perjury case: Roger Campbell.** A striking secular-crime item: **Roger Campbell (Kirkinner) confesses he gave Alexr Agnew 4s 6d Scots "to let him alone"** [to drop a matter], then **denied it on oath** — so he is to satisfy, be absolved, and be **"complained on to the Judge ordinary"** (referred to the civil court for the perjury/bribery). A vivid glimpse of small-scale corruption and the kirk↔civil interface.

★ **The call to Mr John Park for Stranraer advances** — the Provost consents, and Dick and Scott carry the process to Wigton presbytery. **Florence McCredie** (fornication) confesses and is put to repentance; note also **Marion McCredie** is fugitive to Ireland (untraceable "because of the troubles in Ireland"). Weekly **common heads resume** (Scott, *de materia Ecclesiae*; Dick next, *de forma visibilis Ecclesiae*).

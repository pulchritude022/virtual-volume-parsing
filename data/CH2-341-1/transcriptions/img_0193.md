### Modern English
… [George Adestoun to be free] from molestation by any pursuit for civil debt. **The moderator desired to speak a word of exhortation to the people tomorrow.**

**Presbytery meeting at the kirk of Inch, 29 May.** All the brethren present; no ruling elder present except the elder of Kirkmaiden. **There was no sermon, because there was no timely gathering of the people to hear.** The parish being called, only some heritors and elders compeared — **Patrick Agnew of Sheuchan, Sergeant Lin of [—], John [McA]dair of Crainoch, and a few commoners.** **Being asked why they did not attend the Synod's committee at Wigton, they answered that it did not proceed from any slighting of the committee, but because they thought it not necessary to attend, since [—] John Lillie, commissioner for the [commissary], would not appear.** The few of the parish present **professed their willingness to contribute to their utmost in remedying the grievance they had, and some offered a proportion of a sum** judged sufficient to accommodate the kirk. The presbytery, finding such a unanimous and loyal concurrence of that parish for a speedy removal of the said grievance, first appointed them to prepare a fuller and more satisfactory answer to the Synod's committee — in particular to carry with them their several proposals of the money for accommodating the kirk — and further appointed the following act to be read publicly the next Sabbath in the hearing of the whole parish.

## Notes & context
★★★ **The planting of Mr John Dick at Inch — a full parish-vacancy case.** The two-day Inch visitation (imgs 191–193) is really about **settling a minister on the vacant parish of Inch**: the presbytery and the **Synod of Galloway's committee "empowered by acts of the Synod to settle differences"** are pressing the reluctant parish, which is **"very far behind in offering itself to obtain its minister."** The presbytery formally **cites the parish to appear before the Synod committee at Wigton on 5 June 1650, with certification**, and moves to **put Mr John Dick in possession of the glebe** (Blair, Crosse, and Park deputed to attend the committee "in the matter of the conveening of Mr John Dick"). A textbook 17th-century Scottish planting dispute — congregational foot-dragging, presbytery/Synod pressure, the glebe as the material anchor.

★★ **The Balneil malignancy case escalates to the Commission of the Kirk.** The long James Ross of Balneil affair reaches its national ceiling: **the Declaration against Balneil and the whole extracted process (with the General Assembly's act) are to be handed to him via Mr John Scott, so that "the said James may address himself to the Commission of the Kirk"** — i.e. Balneil's case is being referred up to the GA's standing Commission, and he must answer there. (Continues imgs 189, 191.)

★ **The robbery of the "poor stranger" pressed again.** The presbytery **renews its recommendation to the bailie of the regality of Glenluce** to apprehend the robber of the "poor stranger" (the Englishman of imgs 184, 186, 188; robber named Thomas Baillie at img 188).

# CH2/341/1 — Image 87 (folios 86v–87), Dec 1645–Jan 1646 — the letter to Ireland sent; ★ the process against "malignants" begins

Left page (folio **86a**): **Presbytery at Stranraer, the last day of December 1645** — an exercise (Mr
John Dick made, Mr Hew Hutcheson added, 1 Thess. 1:4–5, approved); absent Turnbull and the elders of
Portmontgomery, Glenluce, and Kirkmaiden; **Mr James Bell's earlier absence excused**; **Glenluce to
be supplied per the first ordinance**; **Mr John Dick reports he summoned [Mr Colbie?] from the pulpit
a third time**; **Mr John Livingstone reports he has not yet written to the presbytery in Ireland but
will at the first occasion**; and **the next meeting appointed for 28 January 1646.** Right page (folio
**87**): **Presbytery at Stranraer, 28 January 1646** — an exercise by Mr John Dick, approved; absent
Turnbull and elders; **Mr John Livingstone reports that, per the presbytery's order, he has now written
to the presbytery in Ireland concerning [Mr Colbie?]**; and **★ a letter from the Commission of the
General Assembly (dated 11 December 1645) desiring a fast to be kept on 4 February 1646, with another
of the same date "concerning the censuring of malignants"** — the presbytery **appoints the fast for
the first Wednesday of February and orders every brother to try diligently whether any in his parish
be guilty of the particulars mentioned in the act [against malignants], and to report next
presbytery.**

### Modern English
**Presbytery at Stranraer, 28 January 1646.** Exercise by Mr John Dick (1 Thess.), approved; absent
Turnbull and the elders of Inch, Glenluce, Portmontgomery. **Mr John Livingstone reported that, per the
presbytery's order, he had now written to the presbytery in Ireland concerning [Mr Colbie?].** **★ A
letter was presented from the Commission of the General Assembly, dated 11 December 1645, desiring a
fast to be kept on 4 February 1646; and another of the same date, concerning the censuring of
malignants.** The presbytery, having considered both, **appointed the fast for the first Wednesday of
February, concerning the censuring of malignants, and ordained that every brother try diligently
whether any in his parish be guilty of the particulars mentioned in the act, and report his diligence
at the next presbytery.**

## Notes & context
- **★ The process against "malignants" reaches the Rhins (Jan 1646).** A significant national-discipline
  moment: the **Commission of the General Assembly (letter of 11 Dec 1645) orders a fast (4 Feb 1646)
  and the "censuring of malignants,"** and the presbytery sets **every minister to search his parish
  for any "guilty of the particulars mentioned in the act."** "Malignants" were those who had aided
  the royalist/Montrose cause; after Philiphaugh (img 0083) the victorious Covenanters turned to
  **purging the disaffected through the kirk courts** — parish-level inquisition into complicity with
  the King's party. A strong theme node (**the process against malignants**; the 1646
  fast-and-censure) and evidence of how national politics drove parish discipline. Watch for named
  malignants in the following openings.
- **The Ireland letter finally sent.** After deferring (folio 86a), **Livingstone reports he has now
  written to "the presbytery in Ireland" about [Mr Colbie?]** — closing the loop on the cross-channel
  pursuit of the murder-suspect fugitive (imgs 0085–0086).
- **Glenluce still supplied, not settled.** The vacancy continues under supply "per the first
  ordinance" — the planting unresolved into 1646.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `commissioune of the Generall Assemblie` = the standing
  Commission of the Assembly; `fast to be kept the first Wednesday of February` = an appointed weekday
  fast; `the censuring of malignants` = the disciplining of royalist sympathisers; `try diligentlie if
  any… be guiltie of the particulars mentioned in the act` = examine the parish against the act's list
  of malignant offences; `summond fra pulpite pro 3` = third public citation. **Right page faded
  below** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1645–46.

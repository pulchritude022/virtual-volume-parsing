### Modern English
… [William declared] that he had no intention in going but to hinder and impede the combat.

**The presbytery, considering William's confession, required him to swear, upon oath, that he bared [—] only to hinder the combat and not with intention to join in it; the nature of an oath and the danger of false swearing being represented, he, being sworn, declared upon oath that he behaved himself only to hinder the combat.**

**Gilbert Kennedy is appointed, per the act of the General Assembly, to make confession of the said scandalous behaviour on two several Sabbaths — first at the kirk of Leswalt, then at the kirk of Stranraer — being the 26 of May and the [2] of June.**

**William Young, for his appearance of siding in the combat, to be publicly admonished to be more circumspect in his walking**, with a public declaration that he meant nothing to Gilbert but to hinder the combat. William Broune to be present; the moderator to write that **George Montgomerie may appear and answer for the said scandal**.

Mr James Lawrie reported he had attended the Committee at Stranraer; **George Alexstoune to be permitted to appear before the presbytery without molestation**. **Mr John Scott reported he had represented to the Commission of the Kirk that advice be had about the removing of foreign malignants residing within the bounds.**

## Notes & context
★★ **The Engagement satisfactions completed — with a national echo.** **John McDowall is received to the Covenant**; and **both Alexr Agnews** (a Lieutenant of foot "in Dindinnie" and a Lieutenant-Colonel) confess and subscribe the General Assembly's Declaration, their guilt specified as **"going along to Stirling with George Monro and his adherents"** — i.e. serving in **General George Monro's Engagement army** (the force that marched into Scotland/toward Stirling in 1648). A precise dateable link between the Rhins gentry-soldiery and the national Engagement campaign.

★★ **The duel adjudicated (with a sworn-oath scene).** The Montgomerie combat resolves: **Gilbert Kennedy confesses he received a written challenge from George Montgomerie (carried by William Broune) and bared his sword**; **William Young deposes** to the whole affair; and the presbytery, **"the nature of an oath and the danger of false swearing being represented," puts William [Young] on oath** that he went only to hinder the combat. **Kennedy is sentenced to public confession on two Sabbaths — at Leswalt (26 May) and Stranraer (2 June)** — per the GA act; **William Young admonished**; **George Montgomerie summoned** to answer. (This firmly makes the duel opponent **Gilbert Kennedy**, not the "Gilbert Cochrane of Chappell" read at imgs 184–186 — those need reconciling.)

★ **Foreign malignants** again referred to the Commission of the Kirk (cf. img 188) — the vigilance against incomers a running 1650 theme.

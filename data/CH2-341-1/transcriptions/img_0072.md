# CH2/341/1 — Image 72 (folios 71v–72), May 1645 — a baptism petition; Power preaches and is approved, but his kirk is ruinous and his stipend unpaid

Left page (folio **71a**): **Mr John Dick chosen moderator** and **Mr Hew Hutcheson continued clerk
till the next Synod**; **John McIlroy, suspect of adultery, to be summoned publicly from the
pulpit** — by **Mr John Dick at the kirk of Inch** and by **Mr George Dick at Glenluce** — to appear
before the next presbytery; an **act renewed**; the brethren inquired whether anything had been
**collected for John Dalrymple** (nothing collected); a **letter from the Synod and another from the
commissioners of the kirk desiring a contribution be uplifted for the relief of [the distressed]**,
**George Dick appointed to uptake it**, to be gathered in every parish; and **a petition by John
Bryson in Stranraer that his child might be baptized**, notwithstanding some breach of the kirk, in
respect of **Mr Gilbert Power's infirmity.** Right page (folio **72**): the child ordered baptized;
the **14th day appointed** for a Stranraer meeting; then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 14 May 1645**,
where **Mr Gilbert Power preached on his ordinary (Matthew) and was approved** — but he **complains
that the fabric of the kirk is ruinous** and asks the presbytery to find a way to repair it, and
**declares he was not paid of his stipend and could get no [satisfaction].** Absent: **Mr James
Blair (in Ireland)**, **Charles Campbell** ruling elder.

## Notes & context
- **★ Power still in the pulpit — and still shortchanged (May 1645).** A poignant late glimpse of
  **Mr Gilbert Power**: despite age and infirmity (and the stalled negotiations for a helper, imgs
  0066–0069), he **still preaches his ordinary and is approved by the presbytery** — but in the same
  sitting he **complains that his kirk is physically ruinous** and asks for repairs, and **declares
  he is not paid his stipend and can get no satisfaction.** The reponed minister, restored in
  principle, is left with a crumbling church and unpaid wages — the material precariousness behind
  the whole helper/colleague push. A telling entry for his person-page and the volume's arc. See
  [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]].
- **A baptism withheld for Power's infirmity.** The **Bryson petition** shows the pastoral fallout
  of Power's decline: a Stranraer parishioner's **child could not be baptized "in respect of Mr
  Gilbert Power's infirmity"** (and "some breach of the kirk"), so the presbytery had to intervene
  and **order the baptism.** Concrete evidence that Power's incapacity was disrupting ordinary
  sacramental life in the bounds — the practical reason a helper was so pressed for.
- **Discipline and a national collection.** **John McIlroy** (the adultery case running since img
  0070) is now to be **summoned publicly from the pulpit at both Inch and Glenluce**; and a
  **contribution/collection** is ordered up on letters from the **Synod and the commissioners of the
  kirk** for the **relief of [the distressed]** (very likely the distressed British/Protestants in
  Ireland, given Blair's and Livingstone's Irish deployment, imgs 0070–0071) — **George Dick** to
  uptake it in every parish.
- **Blair in Ireland, confirmed again.** **Mr James Blair absent "for [Ireland]"** corroborates img
  0071's note that Blair (minister of Kirkcolm) had gone to Ireland in spring 1645.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `suspect of adultery` = under suspicion of adultery;
  `summond fra pulpit` = cited by public proclamation from the pulpit; `contributioun… uptaking… in
  ether parochine` = a collection to be gathered up in every parish; `on his ordinar` = on his set
  (assigned) text; `fabrick of the kirk… ruinous` = the church building is dilapidated; `not payed
  of his stipend… could get no satisfactioun` = his stipend was in arrears and unremedied. **Folio
  72 is faded/ghosted throughout** — the lower paragraph is largely illegible and provisional.
  Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Date 14 May 1645.

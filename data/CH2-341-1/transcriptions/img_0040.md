# CH2/341/1 — Image 40 (folios 39v–40) — the Session-book charge; Stoneykirk's own Session petitions against Power

Left page (folio **39a**) is largely **washed out**; the surviving foot records the
presbytery considering the **falsifying/vitiating of the Session book** — a **paper
Power gave in that the Session book contradicted** — and finding "the register to be
wronged" (the point of collusion). Right page (folio **40**) is the striking turn: a
**supplication subscribed by the Kirk Session of Stoneykirk — Power's own parish** —
read and ordered inserted, **begging the presbytery to proceed to trial and censure
notwithstanding Power's appeal.** The Session says Power has used "**endless delays… to
elude your trial**," that his carriage "**smells of some sense of guiltiness**," and that
they, "**a desolate people**," have had "no comfort by his ministry."

### Modern English
That day **[—] McDowell gave in a supplication subscribed by the Kirk Session of
Stoneykirk**, which was read before the presbytery and ordered inserted in the process,
as follows: "**Unto your Wisdoms humbly means and shows** that, whereas **Mr Gilbert
Power has now, of a long time, been under process before you for several scandalous
faults and miscarriages unbeseeming a minister of Christ** — alleged in **the libel given
against him by Uchtred McDowell of French** — of which libel and all its points **we
hoped Mr Gilbert would fully vindicate and clear himself**, removing any scandal or
offence we might have conceived. To that end **we expected you would use all possible
diligence in closing the process**, that we — "**a desolate people**" — might be refreshed
by your care. But considering both your own labour and Mr Gilbert's own conduct, **we
have perceived many endless delays used by Mr Gilbert only to protract time** — as we may
now clearly see by **his appeal to the General Assembly, to elude your trial and
censure**. We cannot but profess that his conduct "**smells of some sense of guiltiness
in him.**" Therefore we **humbly supplicate that, notwithstanding any appeal or
protestation of his, you proceed to trial and censure.** And withal we declare that his
carriage has forced us to conceive sundry prejudices against him, and we have received
**no comfort by his ministry**; so that, unless his innocency appear "**in a most obvious
manner, and some extraordinary work of** [God / grace]…"

## Notes & context
- **Power's own congregation turns on him — a decisive shift.** The **Kirk Session of
  Stoneykirk** — the elders of Power's *own parish* — now **formally petitions the
  presbytery to convict him**, calling themselves "**a desolate people**," his delays a
  sign his cause "**smells of guiltiness**," and confessing they have "**no comfort by his
  ministry.**" This is the mirror image of the **pro-Power petition** (George McCulloch +
  6, img_0017): the parish is **split**, and by June 1643 the *official* Session voice is
  **anti-Power.** A headline development for Power's biography and for the parish's story.
- **"To elude your trial."** The Session reads Power's **appeal to the General Assembly**
  exactly as the accusers do — as **delay tactics** — where Power framed the same appeals
  as a demand for due process (imgs 0022–0025, 0034). Both readings should sit side by
  side in the wiki.
- **The falsified Session book, adjudged.** The faded left-page entry is the presbytery's
  finding on the **Session-book falsification** charge (article 4; the Robert Tarbret
  back-dated £5 entry): a **paper Power gave in contradicted the Session book**, and the
  court **"found the register to be wronged"** — i.e. accepted that the record had been
  tampered with. A concrete adverse finding, if the reading holds.
- **The petitioner.** The Stoneykirk supplication was **given in by a McDowell** (elder or
  Session clerk) — the surname is legible but the forename is lost; note that McDowells sit
  on both sides (French *is* a McDowell), so this need not mean French's kin.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `vitiating` = corrupting/falsifying (a record);
  `point of collusion` = the alleged fraudulent agreement behind the false entry; `meanes
  and shewes` = the formulaic opening of a supplication ("humbly represents and shows");
  `desolat people` = a congregation left without effective pastoral care; `smelles of… 
  guiltinesse` = bears the odour of guilt. **Left page top badly washed out.** Capital
  **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1643 as written.

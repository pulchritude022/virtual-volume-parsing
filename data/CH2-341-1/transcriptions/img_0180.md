### Modern English
[Garthland] **shows that, being convinced that the late expedition into England [the Engagement] was a course contradictory and disobedient, carried on against the appointment of God, contrary to the declarations and acts against malignants and to the Covenant and the articles of the Solemn League — I signified my grief for my accession to it to some ministers of God**, conversed with them, and always desired to satisfy the godly ministers, **intimating my readiness to follow such ways as should be judged expedient for satisfying and removing the scandal.** But the matter being continued to the General Assembly, and **now considering the door opened by the [Assembly's] act and process concerning the manner of satisfaction for such as are willing to amend — I find myself obliged to embrace the opportunity, and to make application to the Committee of the Kirk, manifesting my sorrow for any sinful accession I had** [to the Engagement]. **I humbly refer myself to the judgement of this presbytery**, content to submit my resolution, desiring full satisfaction be made … [with] the laird of [Logan?] … in the fellowship of the presbytery. Humbly wishing … as it concerns the salvation of your cause, etc. **Sic subscribitur, Garthland.**

## Notes & context
★★★ **The Laird of Garthland repents the Engagement.** A major node: **MacDowall of Garthland — one of the leading lairds of the Rhins**, repeatedly a General-Assembly ruling elder and the presbytery's lay ally through the 1640s (imgs 89, 91, 118, 146) — now **submits a first-person penitential supplication for his "accession" to the 1648 Engagement**, the "expedition into England" he calls "contradictory and disobedient … against the Covenant and the Solemn League." He offers to make **public satisfaction** and refers himself to the presbytery's judgement, seizing "the door opened" by the 1649 GA's act on the manner of satisfaction (img 175). Coming days after Balneil's own confession (img 177), this shows the post-Engagement reckoning **reaching the very top of local society** — the presbytery's greatest patron doing penance alongside the small fry. Garthland was himself named on the 1648 Engagers' register (img 146), so this closes that loop.

★ **Process housekeeping.** A committee (Turnbull, Dick, Lawrie) confers with Garthland and receives satisfaction before the supplication is formally given in "under his own hand." Also: **Donald Gifford and Alexr Crossie** cited a third time (the old-Glenluce no-shows of img 179); Bell preaches at Mochrum (supplying Park's vacated charge); next presbytery 12 December.

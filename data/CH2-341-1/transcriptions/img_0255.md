## Folio 254a (left page)  *(confidence: low)*

*The **body of the presbytery's warning-and-declaration** (continuing from img 254) — a Fast-day-style enumeration of the "apparent danger of the time" and the sins that have provoked God's wrath, urging the people not to "involve themselves … in the guilt and manifest defection from the Covenant." It rehearses, under numbered heads, the national and local sins: neglect of family worship, "misrule of religion, oppression," wrongs "in the managing of [affairs]," the burdens laid on the people "by the enemies' oppression," and calls to humiliation and "lamenting for our iniquities." Dense, homiletic, and heavily abraded — the diplomatic text below is a partial skeleton; the sense is a covenanting warning against backsliding under the English yoke.*

## Notes & context
★★★ **The warning's covenanting peroration — and Mr Alexr Turnbull's protestation entered in the register.** Two culminations meet on this folio. First, the presbytery's **anti-union warning-and-declaration** (imgs 254–255) ends in a stirring covenanting exhortation — the people to be "steadfast, immovable, always abounding in the work of the Lord," kept from "the errors of the time," and (the closing image) **"brought again to New Jerusalem in the latter days."** Second, and immediately after, the register books a formal dissent: **Mr Alexr Turnbull [with two named brethren] "professed" they were "purposed finally to adhere" to their protestation against the presbytery's minute approving the late General Assembly, and desired it entered in the register "under the clerk's hand"** — the local **Protester** stand (imgs 247, 251–252) hardened into an on-the-record protestation. The marginal caption confirms it: "anent the protestation made in presbytery by Mr Alexr [Turnbull]."

★ **A Fast-day catalogue of sins.** The left folio is the doctrinal heart of the warning — a Fast-style enumeration of the "causes of wrath": neglect of family worship, "misrule of religion," oppression, the burden of "public [exactions]" and "the enemies' oppression," met with calls to humiliation and "lamenting for our iniquities." It reads as a set-piece of mid-century Scottish covenanting homiletics, aimed squarely at bracing the Rhins congregations against defection under the Cromwellian settlement.

- **Lowest-confidence readings (Dad):**
  - **Both folios are densely written and heavily abraded; the body of the warning (left) and much of the peroration (right) are partial reconstructions.** The *substance* — a covenanting warning against defection, closing with "New Jerusalem in the latter days," followed by Turnbull's protestation — is the priority and reasonably secure; the theology's exact wording and the marginal article-numbers (2, 2, 3) are conjectural.
  - **The protestation entry** — that **Mr Alexr Turnbull** desired his dissent against the late-General-Assembly minute recorded "under the clerk's hand" is secure (confirmed by the marginal note). **The two co-professors' names, read as "Mr John Park" and "Mr James Lawrie," are uncertain and surprising** — Park and Lawrie elsewhere act on the Resolutioner side (the Synod supplication, imgs 250–252), so either the reading of the names is wrong or the alignment is more tangled than assumed. Flag for careful verification.
  - **"be strong in the Lord and in the power of his might … New Jerusalem in the latter days"** — best readings; the peroration's cadence is secure even where words are lost.

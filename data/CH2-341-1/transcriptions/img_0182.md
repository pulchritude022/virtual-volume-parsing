## Notes & context
★ **A parish-boundary annexation: the 2-merk-land of Portpatrick.** The heritors of a **2-merk piece of land lying in Stoneykirk parish but near Portpatrick petition to be dis-annexed from Stoneykirk and annexed to Portpatrick.** The presbytery sends **Crosse, Scott, and Currie to perambulate the bounds**, who report (via Bell) that the land is **"much farther from Stoneykirk kirk and only a mile from Portpatrick"** — a textbook parish-realignment on grounds of distance, the kind of quiet administrative reshaping of the Rhins parishes that runs alongside the bigger dramas.

★ **The Engagement reckoning continues into 1650.** Another Engager, **John McDougall — "a Major in the full Engagement"** — comes forward for satisfaction; **Balneil, now gone to Edinburgh "on necessary affairs" and likely to be long away, is remitted to the Commission of the Kirk to be received to the Covenant** (his local satisfaction done, img 181; the final Covenant-admission passes up to the Commission since he's absent). And the **Commission of the Kirk writes requiring an account of "the conditions of the several kirks" and a report on "the act anent the persons at Edinburgh, 7 Nov 1649"** — the national machinery auditing each presbytery's compliance. **James Cochrane of Chappell and George Mundhouse** summoned for opposing the presbytery.

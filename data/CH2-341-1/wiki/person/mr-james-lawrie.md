**Mr James Lawrie** — the name also written **Lowrie** in the manuscript, and merged with that
spelling here — is a minister of the presbytery prominent especially in its later years. He
takes his place in the exercise and discipline business and, like his colleagues, is named to
and censured for the meetings of the court.

## In the schism

Lawrie's most visible moment is in the
[Resolutioner–Protester schism](entity:event/the-resolutioner-protester-schism). In the
conference over [Mr Alexander Turnbull](entity:person/mr-alexander-turnbull)'s protestation,
Lawrie takes a distinct line from both the majority and from Turnbull: he "desired first to be
present" and would be content to confer "upon the whole matter from the beginning" [img_255].
At the last, he is grouped with Turnbull and [Mr John Park](entity:person/mr-john-park) in
protesting against the delay in inserting the paper [img_257] — a grouping that sits oddly
with his apparent Resolutioner role elsewhere, and which the page notes flag for verification.

## A note for the reviewer

Because "Lawrie"/"Lowrie" was read two ways, and because his alignment in the schism is
uncertain, this entity in particular should be checked carefully against the manuscript. His
parish within the bounds is not asserted here.

Minister of **Leswalt** (approved at its September 1644 visitation) and the presbytery's
clerk across several stretches of the record — written interchangeably as "Hew" and "Hugh"
in different folios (this page merges both spellings; see the entity's variant-spellings
line for the raw forms).

## The clerk who approved Power's return

It is Hutcheson who examines and approves Mr Gilbert Power's first weekly exercise (Col
4:3-4) after two years of being passed over — the concrete moment the reposition actually
takes practical effect, not just a paper ruling [entity:event/mr-gilbert-power-s-deposition-and-reposition,
img_0063]. He also signs the privy trial record as clerk during the Ardwell witness-hunt
years [img_121].

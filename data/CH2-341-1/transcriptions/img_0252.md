## Notes & context
★★ **The Laird of Garthland pursued to Glenluce; Wigton commissioners; Turnbull still absent.** The recurring **Laird of Garthland** case (the Rhins Engager brought to repentance in 1650–51, imgs 220–231, resurfacing imgs 250–251) drives both folios: at the **8 March 1652** sitting the presbytery appoints Dick, Park (and Lawrie) to confer with him and renew the process, and sets the **next meeting at Glenluce** to that end; at the **19 March 1652** Glenluce sitting **Dick and Park report they "went to enquire and deal with the Laird of Garthland," the process renewed.** Alongside, **commissioners from the presbytery of Wigton** ([Mr William Maxwell] and another) are recorded absent — the ongoing Stranraer–Wigton correspondence. **Mr Alexr Turnbull is again absent** (cf. his Protester dissent, imgs 247, 251).

★ **A "drawn-up-for-information" order and materiall admonitions.** The 8 March folio is largely procedural: the presbytery **"disposes" the brethren to enquire and report their diligence, appoints materiall admonitions and a visitation, and orders a matter "drawn up for information and satisfaction" of the people before proceeding to discipline** — the ordinary machinery of parish oversight, with a commission (moderator, Mr John Park, Mr Alexr Turnbull) to report next meeting.

- **Lowest-confidence readings (Dad):**
  - **Both folios are very badly damaged; the left (251a) is tightly written and abraded, the right (252) faded and bled-through.** Most specific readings here are conjectural — treat the diplomatic text as indicative. The *shape* (an 8 March sitting on procedure + Garthland, then a 19 March Glenluce sitting reporting on Garthland) is the priority and reasonably secure.
  - **The Glenluce sitting-date "19 [—] March 1652"** — the day "19" and month "March" are best readings; the exact day and the interval from the "8 [March]" sitting (img 251) are uncertain and internally awkward (the appointment text also names "the 8"). Verify against the run of dates.
  - **"the Laird of Garthland"** — secure by context and marginal caption; his specific business is effaced.
  - **"commissioners from the presbytery of Wigton" / "Mr William Maxwell"** — the Wigton correspondence is secure in substance; the commissioner's name is a best reading (cf. Maxwell as Wigton commissioner, img 238).
  - **"Mr John Dick / Mr John Park / Mr James Lawrie / Mr John Crookes / Mr Alexr Turnbull"** — the standing cast; role-assignments in the damaged passages are conjectural.

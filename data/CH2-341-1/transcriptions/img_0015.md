# CH2/341/1 — Image 15 (folios 14v–15), Dec 1642 – Jan 1643

Left page (folio **14a**) is mixed presbytery business: the Glenluce division moves
to a **view of the bounds on 8 March**; **Mr John Dick is presented to the kirk of
Inch** (an ex-bishop's kirk, the paper subscribed by the **Earl of Cassillis**); a
**Synod of Galloway collection** for **Mr Robert McClellan** (relief of "spoiled
Island people" / prisoners of Ayr); then **French re-lodges his whole accusation**.
Right page (folio **15**) is French's **enlarged bill** — new post-suspension
crimes and the charge that Power **falsified the Session book**.

### Modern English (paraphrase)
The presbytery [is to attend] in full number, or by committee, upon **8 March next**
[to view the Glenluce bounds], and appoints the moderator and clerk to subscribe a
declaration on the back of the petition for the petitioners. A paper given in by
**Gilbert McKnellie[?]** for the parishioners, **subscribed by the Earl of
Cassillis**, desires the presbytery to **present Mr John Dick to the kirk of Inch**
— the right of presentation being in the presbytery's hands, it being one of the
(former) **Bishop's kirks** — Mr John having proven his qualification. The
presbytery, having already admitted him at the parish's suit, agrees and appoints a
presentation. Touching the **Synod of Galloway's ordinance for a collection for Mr
Robert McClellan** (to supply "spoiled Island people"), the presbytery orders the
parishes whose collections for the **prisoners of Ayr** are not yet paid out —
**Leswalt, Kirkmaiden, and Stoneykirk** — to send a competent part to Mr Robert. No
witnesses compeared in the Power business. **French gives in a paper** re-lodging
his whole accusation against Mr Gilbert Power for perjury, usury, cloaking
adulteries and fornications, Sabbath-breaking, sedition, lying, "and a great many
more odious crimes."

### Modern English
[The accusation is as contained above.] Though Mr Gilbert is suspended for the
aforesaid crimes and "at the point of deposition," yet upon the **last Sabbath of
September 1642** he, "most unchristian-like," broke the Sabbath by **drinking in the
alehouse with the tenants of Portspittal**, reckoning their debts and receiving
their money; accused before the Stoneykirk Session, he confessed the drinking,
reckoning, and receiving of money, and when charged with giving them discharges
answered "he had neither pen nor ink to do the same." **(2)** Since the accusation
he has committed sedition (his letter to the laird of Garthland is extant to prove
it). **(3)** Since the accusation he has committed ordinary lying — (i) telling Mr
Alexander Turnbull that [Robert Baxter?] was his enemy; (ii) telling "little
Dunragit" that the presbytery would refer the whole accusation to his own oath —
and many more. Also, that the presbytery send for the **Session books**, which will
show Power **falsified** them in the Tarbret cloaking/bribe matter: whereas he
subscribed last presbytery day that he wrote the penalty in the book the day Tarbret
stood before the Elders, the book shows it written **after he knew he would be
accused** — squeezed in at the end of a page, after penalties that were not received
till more than two years after he got the man's money. French entreats the
presbytery to consider Power's "unchristian carriage" and provide for "that poor
congregation."

## Notes & context
- **The charge sharpens to forgery.** French's most serious new claim is that Power
  **falsified the Stoneykirk Session book** — back-dating the record of the Tarbret
  £5 penalty once he realised he'd be accused (the entry crammed at a page-end, out
  of chronological place). Coupled with fresh **Sabbath-drinking** (reckoning
  tenants' debts in the alehouse — the "usury/merchandise" theme again) and
  **sedition** (a letter to Garthland), French argues Power's misconduct continues
  even under suspension.
- **Beyond the case — church governance in 1642–43:**
  - **Presentation of Mr John Dick to Inch**, one of the former **Bishop's kirks**
    whose patronage fell to the presbytery after episcopacy's 1638 abolition — the
    **Earl of Cassillis** (Kennedy, the leading Covenanter magnate of Carrick/
    Galloway) subscribing the parish paper.
  - A **Synod of Galloway relief collection** for **Mr Robert McClellan** and the
    "spoiled Island people" / **prisoners of Ayr** — almost certainly relief tied to
    the 1641 Irish Rising and its refugees across the North Channel.
  - The **Glenluce parish division** advances to a bounds-viewing on **8 March**.
- **New people/places:** **Gilbert McKnellie[?]**, **Earl of Cassillis**, **Mr
  Robert McClellan**, **[Robert Baxter?]**, **little Dunragit** (person/place —
  Dunragit near Glenluce), **Portspittal** (the Portpatrick tenants). French's own
  forename finally appears (badly faded, "[B.] McDowell younger of French").
- **Palaeography applied:** `coupled & reckoned` = settled accounts/debts;
  `discharges` = written receipts; `budd` = bribe; `at the poynt of deposition` =
  about to be deprived of office; `thir` = these; `moe` = more; `Iland people` =
  Islanders/Irish-war refugees. `y`+superscript → *th-*.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 29 (folios 28v–29) — appeal answers end; the register jumps back to May 1642

Left page (folio **28a**) finishes the presbytery's **answers to Power's appeal**
(reasons 9–11): the court defends its **severity/lenity in suspending** him, denies
refusing his **compurgators**, and calls giving him a process-copy before closing
"altogether inexpedient." Right page (folio **29**) closes the appeal business with a
**probation (trial) of the whole presbytery** — all approved, including **Mr James
Blair** (whom only French accused, and failed to prove) — and then, strikingly,
**reverts to 11 May 1642**: because former clerk **Mr John Livingston has gone to
Ireland** leaving acts un-entered, the new clerk **Mr George Dick** is ordered to
**back-fill the register from 30 March 1642 forward**. From here the minutes are the
retrospectively-inserted 1642 sederunts.

### Modern English
[Answer to reason 9, concl.:] …Power's paper was "but his own bare and simple
assertion," not fit to outweigh sworn depositions; and even if true, the matters were
"**not of that consequence**" as the accusation implied, much less a bar to hearing
probation. As for his complaint of the court's **severity in suspending** him, "our
lenity was as manifest": it had been their duty to suspend him **the moment the libel
was given in**, and though pressed by "some considerable in the parish" and importuned
by the accuser, they held off — "so tender of the man's ministry" — and only suspended
when to refuse further "**would have brought upon ourselves the charge of injustice.**"

**Reason 10:** they have laboured to clear themselves of any partial carriage, to all
men's satisfaction; the ground of the suspension is answered above. As for Power's
claim that they ignored his answers and supplications — "we do not remember we received
any but they got an answer." As for **refusing his compurgators** (oath-helpers): they
remember none — elder or other — who offered to clear him of the point for which he was
suspended; and even had any offered, it is "**most improper**" that, once a witness is
sworn and his deposition on record, anyone be heard to overthrow it "except first he
were accused as a perjured man and proven to be so."

**Reason 11:** to revise the process and give Power a copy under the clerk's hand
**before the process was closed**, "we thought altogether inexpedient."

### Modern English
**On the first presbytery day, a probation (trial) was taken of the whole ministers
and ruling elders** — and all were approved; **Mr James Blair, minister at
Portmontgomerie (Portpatrick),** likewise, "for anything could be known of him by one
or other," except that **the laird of French accused him of some things which he failed
to prove.**

**The first presbytery after the Synod, held at Stranraer, 11 May 1642.** That day **Mr
Robert Montgomerie appeared and was added upon Colossians 3:8–9** (the exercise text),
"by some approved and by some not." That day **Mr Alexander Turnbull was chosen
moderator, and Mr George Dick clerk.** That day the presbytery **ordained the new clerk
Mr George Dick to insert in the register all that former clerk Mr John Livingston had
not entered, since Mr John was gone over to Ireland**; and accordingly Mr George has
inserted the same, **beginning at the acts of 30 March 1642 and continuing until the
last acts closed in the presbytery of 18 April** [1643]. That day it was appointed that
**Mr Alexander Turnbull go to Stoneykirk to convene with the Session there for choosing
a commissioner to the presbytery** — granted on the advice of **Garthland**, without
prejudice to a former act in favour of that Session. [A minister — Power?] gave in a
**supplication to have his place "where the bishops sat," or a seat** [in the kirk]; the
presbytery delayed answer **till the visitation of the parish kirk of Clonkitt.** That
day **Mr Thomas Boyd's letters were produced** by Mr George Dick; and the presbytery
appointed **Mr James Blair (Portmontgomerie), Mr Hugh Hutcheson (minister at …)** [as
commissioners…]

## Notes & context
- **The register turns retrospective — a key structural fact.** With former clerk **Mr
  John Livingston gone to Ireland** (cf. img_0026, called to Ulster with Monro), the new
  clerk **Mr George Dick** is ordered to **back-fill every act Livingston left un-entered,
  from 30 March 1642 onward.** So the folios *after* this point are not a simple forward
  narrative: they are **retrospectively-inserted 1642 minutes**. This explains why the
  next openings jump back to **11 May 1642** even though the Power appeal we just read is
  from April 1643. Flag for the wiki's chronology: **manuscript order ≠ strict date order
  here.**
- **The whole presbytery put on trial.** "Probat trial… of the whole ministers and ruling
  elders" — a post-Synod visitation/scrutiny in which every member was examined and
  cleared. Only **French** dissented, accusing **Mr James Blair** of unproven matters —
  consistent with French's litigious profile (he is behind the Power prosecution too).
- **New people.** **Mr Robert Montgomerie** (a probationer/expectant "added" — i.e.
  admitted to the exercise — on **Colossians 3:8–9**, continuing the Colossians series);
  **Mr Thomas Boyd** (letters/testimonials produced). **Mr Alexander Turnbull** now
  confirmed as **moderator** and **Mr George Dick** as **clerk** from May 1642.
- **The "bishop's seat" supplication.** A minister asks for "**his place where the
  bishops sat, or one seat**" — almost certainly a **manse/kirk formerly held under
  episcopacy** (abolished 1638), the answer deferred to the visitation of **Clonkitt**
  kirk. Likely **Mr John Dick** and the ex-bishop's kirk of **Inch** (per the recurring
  cast) — but the name here is uncertain; confirm.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `probat triall` / `holden probe` = a proving,
  trial (of fitness); `preponderat` = outweigh; `compurgators` = oath-helpers who swear
  to a person's credibility; `obied his deposition` = undo/overthrow the deposition (or
  "abide" — uncertain); `added upon [text]` = admitted to preach the trial exercise on a
  Scripture; `going one` = going on. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates "1642" Old Style.
  `y`+superscript → *th-*.

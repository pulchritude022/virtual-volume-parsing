## Notes & context
★★ **Robert Campbell finally forced to the stool — the last Ireland-malignant broken.** After "so many delays," the defiant **Robert Campbell** (the "What! malignancy in me!" man, imgs 210–219) once more **fails to produce his testimonial and is ordered the first public admonition** "according to the ordinance of the Synod." With John Campbell fled the country and Garthland already at the stool, the presbytery's long campaign against the Ireland-returned malignants is now delivering censures.

★ **Presbytery correspondence and the New-Year routine.** The **Presbytery of Wigton** writes for **correspondence "in the business of Mr Robert [Fingzeardine]"** — inter-presbytery cooperation on a minister's case — and Crosse is deputed to answer. The stated sitting of **15 January 1651** keeps the ordinary form (exercise by Blair and Turnbull). Garthland's re-admission is made conditional on **"evidence of his repentance"** and on his **publishing the General Assembly's declaration** — the malignant gentry made to broadcast the Kirk's line.

★ **The adultery and fornication cases grind on.** The **Gilbert Kennedy of Aviolen** adultery (Morion / Williamson) continues, Dick to **confront the women with their "counsellors"**; and **Margaret McClounie**, obstinate to her session, now **offers obedience and begs the process go no further** — a first sign of submission (cf. imgs 213–220).

- **Lowest-confidence readings (Dad):**
  - **Both folios are water-stained;** the Robert Campbell admonition and the McClounie submission are the priority readings and moderately secure; connective wording is reconstructed.
  - **"Mr Robert [Fingzeardine]"** — the minister's surname in the Wigton correspondence is very uncertain.
  - **"Castle Kennedy … Cardoness"** — the absent ruling-elders' kirks are a best reading.
  - The foot of folio 221 (McClounie's plea) is faint and partly conjectural.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 47 (folios 46v–47), Jan–Mar 1643 — routine business; "capite ecclesiae"; Blair to Ireland

Both pages are the **forward-running 1643 register** (heavily browned). Left page (folio
**46a**): the tail of the **11 January 1643** sederunt — a **Chancellor's Edict** to be
read in every parish kirk, letters from the presbyteries of **Kirkcudbright (William
Glendinning), Irvine, Ayr, and Wigtown** seeking **correspondence in the Stoneykirk
business and the trial of Mr John Chalmer** — then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 8 February
1643**, where **Mr James Blair handles the "common head," the doctrine of *capite
ecclesiae*** (the headship of the Church). Right page (folio **47**): Blair reports his
correspondence in **Chalmer's trial**; **Blair is ordained by the General Assembly's
commissioners to supply a town in Ireland till May**; **Mr George Dick is summoned/sued by
James Rosse**; then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 8 March 1643** (Turnbull exercises on
Colossians 3:15). *(Both pages faded — low confidence.)*

## Notes & context
- **"Capite ecclesiae" — the live theology of 1643.** Mr James Blair's set exercise on the
  **doctrine of *capite ecclesiae* (the headship of the Church** — i.e. Christ, not the
  king, as sole Head) is a **flagship Covenanter theme** in exactly the months the **Solemn
  League and Covenant** was being negotiated (1643). A glossary/theme node linking the
  local register to the national moment.
- **Blair (and Livingston before him) sent to Ireland.** **Mr James Blair is ordained by
  the General Assembly's commissioners to supply a town in Ireland till May [1643]** —
  following Livingston's 1642 Irish service. The Stranraer presbytery is a **staging post
  for Scottish ministers to Ulster** (the Scots army/settlers under Monro). Important for
  Blair's biography and the Scotland–Ulster axis.
- **The Chalmer trial and the correspondence web.** A **Mr John Chalmer** is on trial in
  **Wigtown presbytery** (his sermon "gave no satisfaction"); Stranraer keeps
  correspondence in it, and in the **Stoneykirk business**, with **Kirkcudbright, Wigtown,
  Irvine, and Ayr** — plus a **Chancellor's Edict** read in every kirk. Shows how routine
  inter-presbytery cooperation worked. New names: **Mr John Chalmer**, **William
  Glendinning**.
- **James Rosse's suit against George Dick.** The **Glenluce mortification** fight rumbles
  on: **James Rosse has summoned/sued Mr George Dick** (over the endowment/provision, imgs
  0044–0045). The presbytery backs Dick and its own act. A continuing sub-plot.
- **Calendar:** dates run **January → March 1643** with no Old-Style ambiguity (Scots New
  Year = 1 January; see the note on [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]]) — the register is now
  moving **chronologically forward** through 1643.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `capite ecclesiae` (Latin) = "concerning the Head of
  the Church"; `common head` = the appointed theological commonplace for the exercise;
  `postulad` = requested/petitioned; `remonstrat` = represented, set forth; `caution` =
  security/surety (a legal summons under caution); `plett` = plea/suit; `provydied` =
  provided for. **Both pages browned** — many readings provisional. Capital **W** for
  *w/wh*. Dates 1643.

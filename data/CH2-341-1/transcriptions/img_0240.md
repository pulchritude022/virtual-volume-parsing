## Notes & context
★★ **"A meeting for the outputting of their men" — the levy in the parish.** The single most evocative phrase of the opening: the **Glenluce heritors cannot attend the presbytery "because they were to be at a meeting for the outputting of their men."** In September 1651 — the very weeks of the Battle of Worcester (3 Sep) — the Rhins gentry are busy **raising the parish's soldiers** under the Public Resolutions. Church discipline yields to the war effort, in one plain administrative excuse.

★★ **Cross-bounds discipline: Wigton and Stranraer hunt "malignants and opposers."** The correspondence between the presbyteries (imgs 238–239) bears fruit: **Wigton "earnestly desire that any malignant, or opposer of the public affairs, or scandalous person" within Stranraer's bounds be made known,** and the two bounds trade the names of scandalous persons — **an excommunicate, John [—] of [Barwennan] in Inch, and Alexr Halborn of old Glenluce**, the latter tangled up with money for "the late unlawful Engagement." The Resolutioner net (img 234) drawn tight across parish lines.

★ **John Campbell gets his second admonition; Halborn's drunkenness.** Despite the softening at img 239, **John Campbell "would remain disobedient," so Mr John Park proceeded to a second public admonition,** with a paper of charges read against him. Meanwhile **Alexr Halborn submits to the Glenluce session for his drunkenness** — the small-parish discipline running alongside the great political one.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 98 (folios 97v–98), 29–30 July 1646 — the visitations of Kirkmaiden and Portmontgomery; Margaret Hannay confesses cohabiting with Thomas Adair

Left page (folio **97a**): **the visitation of Kirkmaiden, 29 July 1646** (continued) — absent the elders
of Leswalt, Stranraer, Kirkcolm and Portmontgomery; **the minister and elders being removed, the elders
were called in one by one and interrogated whether they knew any fault or neglect in the minister [Mr
Alexander Turnbull], in respect of his doctrine or discipline, for which he must be admonished, or
whether he gave satisfaction — and every one severally deposed they were well satisfied**; **the minister
being called in and interrogated what concurrence he had from the elders in discipline, answered that
they did concur according to their power**; **the session book being revised was approved**; **the
heritors of Kirkmaiden being present, promised that whatever necessary [expenses] Mr Alexander Turnbull
should lay out on his glebe [and the kirk]… upon account given in to the session, should be refunded…
to the said Mr Alexander Turnbull [or his estate] after his death**; **Thomas Morton, adulterer, summoned
pro 1 and not appearing, to be summoned pro 2**; and **★ Margaret Hannay in Glenluce compeared and
confessed her frequent falling in fornication and cohabiting with Thomas Adair, and was ordained to stand
in Glenluce and make public [repentance] in sackcloth till she give signs of repentance, beginning at
Glenluce the first day**. Right page (folio **98**): **the visitation of Portmontgomery, 30 July 1646** —
Mr James Blair preached on his ordinary text (Matt. 2:21–22), approved; his absence and the elder's
absence the day before excused; **the minister and elders being removed, the elders were severally
interrogated and answered they knew nothing for which the minister [Blair] must be admonished — he gave
good satisfaction**; **the minister being called in, inquired what concurrence he had of the elders in
discipline [and whether he had family worship], answered they did concur, and desired the presbytery
would exhort the people to reverence the ordinances of God — which the presbytery did**; **the session
book being visited was approved**; and **Mr James Blair reported that, per the presbytery's ordinance, he
had preached [touching] discipline** [continued on the next opening].

### Modern English
**[Portmontgomery visitation, 30 July 1646.]** Mr James Blair preached on his ordinary text (Matt.
2:21–22), approved; his absence and the elder's absence the day before excused. **The minister and elders
being removed, the elders were severally interrogated whether they knew anything in the minister (Mr
James Blair) in respect of his doctrine or discipline for which he must be admonished, or whether he gave
good satisfaction; they answered they knew nothing for which he must be admonished — he gave them good
satisfaction.** **The minister being called in was inquired what concurrence he had of the elders in
discipline, and whether he had family worship; he answered they did concur in discipline, and desired the
presbytery would exhort the people to reverence the ordinances of God [and amend their faults]
accordingly — which desire the presbytery did [fulfil], exhorting them.** **The session book, visited,
was approved.** **Mr James Blair reported that, per the presbytery's ordinance, he had preached [at
Portmontgomery] touching discipline** [continued on the next opening].

## Notes & context
- **★★ Two parish visitations — the machinery of oversight.** This opening records the **formal
  visitations of Kirkmaiden (29 July) and Portmontgomery (30 July 1646)**, both following the classic
  form: a sermon; the **minister and elders "removed" and examined separately** (the elders asked whether
  the minister is faultless in doctrine and discipline; the minister asked whether the elders concur, and
  whether he keeps **family worship**); the **session book reviewed and approved**; and the **heritors'
  undertakings** about the fabric and glebe. A superb source for the state of these parishes and for how
  Presbyterian mutual accountability actually worked. Good structural/theme node for the wiki
  ("visitation").
- **★ The minister's glebe — a life interest and a posthumous refund.** At Kirkmaiden the **heritors
  promise that Mr Alexander Turnbull's necessary outlay on his glebe (and kirk) will be refunded on
  account — to him or his estate "after his death."** A concrete glimpse of glebe/manse economics and of
  how a minister's improvements were secured. (A useful glossary node: glebe; heritors' obligations.)
- **★ Margaret Hannay & Thomas Adair — resolving the img 0097 bond.** **Margaret Hannay in Glenluce**
  confesses **frequent fornication and cohabiting with Thomas Adair** — which **identifies "Margaret [—]",
  the woman Thomas Adair was bound (under his £100 bond, img 0097) to shun.** So the Adair case is:
  Thomas Adair ⟷ **Margaret Hannay** (long-term cohabitation/fornication), both now doing public penance
  at Glenluce. Meanwhile **Thomas Morton** (partner of Margaret McCristine, img 0097) is a *distinct*
  adulterer, summoned pro 2. This disentangles the two Glenluce couples.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `the minister and elders being removed… interrogat… every one
  severally deposed` = the visitation's separate examination of minister and eldership; `What concurrence
  he had fra ye elders in Discipline… family Worship` = the standard visitation questions (eldership's
  support; the minister's household devotions); `session booke… visited… approved` = the kirk-session
  register inspected and passed; `necessary expenses… refunded… after his Death` = reimbursement of glebe
  outlay to the minister's estate; `frequent falling in fornicatioun, and cohabiting` = ongoing unlawful
  cohabitation. **Both pages faded, heavily stained** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year

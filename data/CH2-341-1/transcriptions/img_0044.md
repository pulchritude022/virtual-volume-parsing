# CH2/341/1 — Image 44 (folios 43v–44), Aug–Nov 1642 — a disputed Glenluce presentation; Wariston's letter; office rotation

Left page (folio **43a**): the presbytery at Stranraer (**last of August 1642**) **rejects
James Rosse of Balneil's presentation of Mr James Smith to the kirk of Glenluce** (tied to
a **1000-merk mortification** and **the ruins of the [Glenluce] abbey**) — because Rosse
**won't produce the gift**, the presbytery holds itself **"undoubted patron,"** and the
gift's ownership is unclear; a **petition to the Council for Irish-refugee supply**; and
the moderator reports **"a tumult at Stoneykirk"** (unrest after Power's deposition).
Right page (folio **44**): letters from **Lord Wariston** and the **General Assembly's
commissioners**; presbyteries at **Glenluce (17 Oct)** and **Stranraer (16 Nov)** —
**Mr John Livingston, back from Ireland, exercises and is chosen moderator**, with **Mr
Alexander Turnbull** clerk. Signed **George Dick, Clerk.**

### Modern English
**Presbytery at Stranraer, last of August 1642.** The minister of the place gave the
ordinary exercise. That day **James Rosse of Balneil gave in a presentation, presenting Mr
James Smith to the kirk of Glenluce**, tied to **a mortification of 1000 merks for
stipend, the ruins of the abbey, and provision for manse and glebe.** **The presbytery did
not accept the presentation**, for these reasons: **(1)** they would gladly have seen the
**gift and mortification** on which it was grounded, but **Rosse would not produce it**;
**(2)** they conceive **the presbytery to be undoubted patron**; **(3)** they were not sure
whether the gift belonged to the present minister — it having been "**admitted in the
troublesome times upon a contract of the parishioners for the maintenance.**"

That day it was thought good to send **a petition to the Council for supply to those who
came from Ireland**, subscribed by the clerk in the presbytery's name. That day **three
papers were given in by the elders of Stoneykirk**; the presbytery would first **advise
with the Kirkcudbright commissioners and the Synod** before acting on them. That day the
**moderator reported rumours of "a tumult at Stoneykirk"** [→ continues].

### Modern English
That day **a letter sent by My Lord Wariston and Mr Robert [Douglas]** was received and
read; likewise **a letter from the Commissioners of the General Assembly.**

**Presbytery at Glenluce, 17 October 1642.** All absent save **Mr Alexander Turnbull
(moderator), Mr John Livingston, Mr John Dick, Mr George Dick**, and the commissioners
from **Stoneykirk, Inch, Kirkmaiden, and Glenluce.** Those present underwent their **privy
trials** and were approved. **Mr James Blair** was appointed **to have the "common head"
and handle the controversy**; other things referred to next meeting. **George Dick,
Clerk.**

**Presbytery at Stranraer, 16 November 1642.** Absent: Mr John Dick and the ruling elders
of Stoneykirk and Kirkcolm. **Mr John Livingston exercised, and Mr Hugh Hutcheson added,
upon Colossians 3:9–10**, and were approved. The **commissions of the commissioners from
Glenluce, Stranraer, Portpatrick, and Kirkmaiden** were given in and approved. **Mr John
Livingston was chosen moderator, and Mr Alexander Turnbull clerk. [Signed] George Dick.**

## Notes & context
- **A patronage fight over Glenluce.** After Power's deposition, **James Rosse of
  Balneil** tries to **present his own candidate, Mr James Smith, to Glenluce**, backed by
  a **1000-merk mortification** and revenues tied to the **ruins of Glenluce Abbey** — but
  **won't show the deed**, so the presbytery, claiming to be **"undoubted patron,"** rejects
  it. This joins the earlier **Rosse threads** (the withheld mortification, imgs 0039–0040;
  his dodging of citations): Rosse is a **layman contesting the presbytery's control of
  Glenluce's endowment and pulpit.** New name: **Mr James Smith** (would-be minister of
  Glenluce). Glenluce Abbey (Cistercian, dissolved) as a stipend source — a glossary/place
  node.

  after Power's deposition and Marion Power's disturbance (img_0043) — signals a **parish
  in uproar** over losing its minister. A discrete event worth its own wiki entry (the
  aftermath of the deposition).

  of Wariston**, principal Covenanter statesman) **and Mr Robert [Douglas]** (likely the
  eminent minister **Robert Douglas**), plus the **General Assembly commissioners** — the

# CH2/341/1 — Image 70 (folios 69v–70), March–April 1645 — a fugitive pursued across presbyteries; Livingstone sent to Ireland

Left page (folio **69a**): the **Ferguson case escalates** — informed that **John Ferguson (an
"unnatural person") is fugitive and not found**, the presbytery has **Mr John Livingstone write to
the neighbouring presbyteries (Wigtown, Kirkcudbright, Ayr)** to have him apprehended if found in
their bounds; a **Glenluce adulterer** who won't compear is to be **summoned publicly from the
pulpit**; the **Assembly commissioners (Livingstone & Blair) report their diligence** and
distribute the Assembly's acts; and routine **fornication cases** (John Mc[—] and another in Inch;
Hew Bertoun a penitent). Right page (folio **70**): a discipline case involving **Anna Dalrymple**;
then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 2 April 1645**, where **Mr John Livingstone — who should have
handled the common head — was, on a sudden, by a letter from the commissioners of the kirk,
necessitated to go to Ireland**, so **Blair took the exercise** (Col. 4:13–16); the **fast and the
Solemn Warning** read and kept; and more **fornication/adultery summonses** (John McIlroy; Jon
McCraken).

### Modern English
[A discipline case:] a man ordered to abstain from **Anna Dalrymple's** company till he give
satisfaction — he **submitted himself willingly** and promised to do as ordained. **Presbytery at
Stranraer, 2 April 1645.** Absent Turnbull and the elders of Leswalt, Portmontgomery, Glenluce.
**Mr John Livingstone, who should have handled the common head, was on a sudden — by a letter from
the commissioners of the kirk — necessitated to go to Ireland**, and, being to go, warned Blair to
prepare for the exercise. **Blair made and added (Col. 4:13–16), approved**; Turnbull's absence
excused by sickness. The brethren reported reading **the causes of the fast and the Solemn Warning**
and keeping the fast. Adultery/fornication summonses (**John McIlroy**; **Jon McCraken and [—] in
Inch**) — cited a first time, not compearing, to be summoned a second.

## Notes & context
- **A hue and cry across presbyteries.** The **John Ferguson** case (img 0069) escalates: he is
  **"fugitive and not found,"** and the presbytery has **Livingstone write to the neighbouring
  presbyteries — Wigtown, Kirkcudbright, and Ayr — to apprehend him.** A clear picture of
  **inter-presbytery cooperation** in pursuing a serious offender across bounds. The label
  **"unnatural person"** hints the "heinous fact" was an **unnatural crime** (incest or worse — a
  capital matter, hence the wide pursuit); treat soberly, note the uncertainty.
- **★ Livingstone sent to Ireland (April 1645).** A crisp biographical datum: **Mr John
  Livingstone, minister of Stranraer, was "on a sudden, by a letter from the commissioners of the
  kirk, necessitated to go to Ireland"** — the Church of Scotland again deploying its Stranraer
  minister across the North Channel (cf. his and Blair's earlier Irish service, imgs 0047–0048).
  The Stranraer presbytery remains the **hinge to Ulster.** A firm event for Livingstone's
  person-page and the Scotland–Ireland thread.
- **Discipline dominates the ordinary business.** Multiple **fornication/adultery cases** (a
  Glenluce adulterer summoned from the pulpit; **John McIlroy**; **Jon McCraken**; **Hew Bertoun**
  a penitent; a man and **Anna Dalrymple**) — the daily grind of kirk discipline, with the standard
  escalating summonses (*pro 1, pro 2*). Genealogically rich (several named parishioners), if
  handled with discretion.
- **National observance.** The **fast, its "causes," and the "Solemn Warning"** read and kept — the
  1645 national church calling (the Solemn Warning of the Commission of the Kirk), relayed to the
  parishes.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `unnaturall persone` = one guilty of an unnatural (capital)
  crime; `fugitive` = fled from justice/discipline; `deall With the commissioune in these parts` =
  cooperate via the [Privy-Council] commission locally; `summond pro 1 / pro 2` = first/second
  citation; `causes of the fast` = the reasons read out for an appointed fast; `Solemne Warning` =
  a formal admonition issued by the Commission of the General Assembly; `necessitated to go to
  Ireland` = compelled by the kirk's order. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1645.

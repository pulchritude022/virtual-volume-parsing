# CH2/341/1 — Image 60 (folios 59v–60), August 1644 — a kirk visitation approves Blair; Porterfield confirmed the *late* minister

**⚠ Note the reading puzzle** (see Notes): the visited/examined minister clearly reads **Mr
James Blair**, but the visitation heading reads **"the kirk of Portmontgomery"** — while Blair
was admitted to **Kirkcolm** (img 0055). Either "Portmontgomery" here should read **Kirkcolm**,
or the two are the same kirk. Flagged for the reviewer.

Left page (folio **59a**): **At Stranraer, 7 August 1644** — the presbytery appoints
**visitations of the kirk of "Portmontgomery" (21 Aug)** and **of Stranraer (22 Aug)**; then
**Presbytery at "Portmontgomery", 21 August 1644, met for the visitation** — **Mr James Blair
delivers the ordinance (Jonah 4:[?]), is approved**, then **withdraws while the elders are
asked whether they approve him in life, doctrine, discipline, and household** — they approve
him "in all" and report **no scandal.** Right page (folio **60**): the minister's own testimony
(no spiritual deadness; family worship and catechising to be kept); a disobedience case
continued; and — key — **Mr John Livingstone reports he sent the Earl of Galloway a letter for
satisfaction to [the estate of] Mr John Porterfield, *late minister***, and that he spoke the
**laird of Garthland about repairing the fabric of the kirk of Stoneykirk.**

## Notes & context
- **Porterfield confirmed the *late* minister — the money dispute clarified.** The right page
  resolves the Porterfield thread (imgs 0057–0059): **Mr John Porterfield is described as the
  "late minister"** (i.e. **deceased**), and the "satisfaction" the presbytery presses the
  **Earl of Galloway** for is owed **to Porterfield's estate/heirs** — almost certainly
  **stipend/teind arrears** for the vacant years (the Earl being the patron who withheld them).
  This confirms the earlier surmise (Porterfield d. c.1642, minister of Kirkcolm) and reframes
  the dispute as **patron-vs-presbytery over a dead minister's dues.** Update
  [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]]: Porterfield = *late* (deceased), satisfaction = to his
  estate.
- **A model visitation — Blair approved, "no scandal."** The visitation is the classic form
  (img 0052 for Stranraer): the minister **preaches** (Jonah 4), then **withdraws** while the
  elders are asked to approve him in **life, doctrine, discipline, and household** — they
  approve **"in all," with no scandal**; then the elders withdraw and the **minister** is asked
  about **them.** Blair's testimony is a small gem of pastoral ideal: **"no spiritual deadness
  among them," to be maintained by family worship and regular catechising.** Confirms **Blair
  well-settled** in his charge.
- **⚠ Parish-name puzzle (Portmontgomery vs Kirkcolm).** The examined minister is unmistakably
  **Mr James Blair**, yet the visitation heading reads **"the kirk of Portmontgomery."** Since
  Blair was **admitted to Kirkcolm** (img 0055) and the Porterfield line (same page) ties to
  **Kirkcolm**, most likely **"Portmontgomery" here is either an alias for, or a misreading of,
  Kirkcolm** — or the recurring "Portmontgomery / Session of Portmontgomery" (imgs 0053, 0056,
  0058) is a place within Blair's charge. **Do not treat "Portmontgomery" as a separate parish
  with Blair as minister without confirmation.** Flagged for Dad, who knows the Rhins parishes.
- **Repairing Stoneykirk.** **Livingstone spoke the laird of Garthland** (and the **laird of
  Ardwell?**) about **repairing the fabric of the kirk of Stoneykirk** — Power's old parish —
  with the **heritors** to meet and share the cost. The material aftermath of Stoneykirk's long
  troubles.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `handled our ordinance` = preached the appointed
  visitation sermon; `interrogat` = examined; `spirituall deadnes` = spiritual dullness/decay;
  `family worship` = household devotions (a Covenanter emphasis, enforced by the 1647 Directory
  for Family Worship); `catechising` = the minister's regular instruction of the parish;
  `heritors` = the landowners liable for the fabric; `reparatioun of the fabrick` = repair of
  the church building; `satisfactioun to [an] estate` = payment of dues to a deceased's heirs.
  **Right page faint** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1644.

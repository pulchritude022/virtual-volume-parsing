# CH2/341/1 — Image 20 (folios 19v–20), 30 March 1643

Left page (folio **19a**) finishes **Ellaster's article 6** — a detailed charge that
Power, as creditor, ground the elder **laird of French** with **hornings** over the
Martinmas rent (even refusing offered payment, forcing French to Edinburgh in
midwinter) — signed **Andrew McDowell of Killaster**; the presbytery lets the paper
**lie till it advises with the Synod**. Right page (folio **20**) is the **Glenluce
parish-division** procedure: debarment motions, the moor's petition, and a demand to
secure a stipend before dividing.

## Folio 19v (left page, marked "19a")  *(confidence: medium)*  — Ellaster art. 6; paper laid aside

### Diplomatic transcription
```
[at the] terme of his Martimesse, Anno 1640, yet the said Mr
Gilbert tooke his band [= bond] to the register, and charged him
for the same[,] which necessitat him to suspend him, both
for the said tearme and in tyme coming, till the suspen-
sion war discussed[.] yet notwithstanding, the said Mr Gilbert
caused denunce [= denounce] him and regrat him at the horne[.]
Whereof the said laird of French acquainted the pro-
vinciall Synod of his carriage, and they admonished
him not to do the lyke[.] yet immediatlie thereafter,
notwithstanding of the said suspension, he caused charge
for the last Martimesse 1642[,] and, upon the charge,
French offered the money charged for, and he wold not
take it, but caused denunce and regrat French again
at the horne[.] Also, French having [given] him ane band of
four score [= 80] merks not to suspend the said former band, not-
withstanding (both befor the presb. and notars the same
was offered as instruments), yet, contrair Mr Gilberts record, the
said Mr Gilbert regrat that band also, and caused
charge for it, and forced French to suspend that band
also[.] Notwithstanding, he caused denunce and regrat
him the third tyme at the horne[.] and after he forced
French to goe to Edinburgh, in his owne defence, in the
midle of winter[.] he was content thair to take lesse then
was offered him at home[.] And therfor intreats your
ws will, so soone as may be, put both this and the former
accusation to a point, that that poore congregation
may be supplied so soone as the Lord will permit[.]
and in the meane tyme intreats your wisdomes will
appoint some ws of your number sabbothlie to supply, till
the Lord be pleased to provyd ———
        Sic subscribitur   Androw McDowell of Killaster ———
The presb. thinkes good to let this paper of Androw
McDowells ly, without [receaving], giving out, or [rejecting]
of it, till they advyse with the Synod ———
```

### Modern English
[Ellaster's art. 6, concluded:] at the term of Martinmas 1640, Power took French's
**bond to the register and charged him**, forcing French to suspend it, both for that
term and in time coming, until the suspension was discussed; yet Power **caused him
be denounced "at the horn"** (outlawed for debt). French complained of this carriage
to the **Provincial Synod**, which admonished Power not to repeat it — yet
immediately after, Power charged for the Martinmas rent of 1642; when French offered
the money, Power refused it and **denounced him at the horn again**. Though French
gave an 80-merk **bond of suspension** (offered before the presbytery and notaries),
Power — contrary to his own record — charged for that too, forcing French to suspend
it, **denounced him a third time**, and drove him to **Edinburgh in midwinter** to
defend himself, where he took less than was offered at home. Ellaster therefore
entreats the presbytery to bring both accusations to a conclusion, that "that poor
congregation" be supplied. *Subscribed: Andrew McDowell of Killaster.*

**The presbytery thinks good to let Andrew McDowell's paper lie** — neither
receiving, issuing, nor rejecting it — **till they advise with the Synod.**

## Folio 20 (right page)  *(confidence: medium)*  — the Glenluce division procedure

### Diplomatic transcription
```
                                ⑳
James Rosse of Balneil gives in a paper, and thereintill
desyres that Mr George Dick and William Kennedy of
Gillespie should be [debarred], because they protested to the
contrair of his desyre ———
The humble supplicatioun of the heritors and tennents of
the moore of Glenluce[.]  [Unto ... ] his [= this] supplicatioun[,]
[humbly] shewing that whereas your ws, at your last meeting
at Glenluce, did conclude anent the place for building
of ane new kirk, but continued till this day the declar-
atioun what part of that parochin should apperteine
thereto, so that, for the want of division, the worke is
hindered ———
        Therfore we humbly intreat your ws wold
be pleased (according to your former acts) to give first your
declaration of the division, that we may the more redilie
goe about the worke intended[.] and your ws answeare[.]
For that, anent removing of Mr George Dick and W[illia]m
Kennedy of Gillespie[:] The presb. resolvet to let them sit
and vote; and if, in voteing, the voices come to such equalitie
as their removing will cast the mater the one way or the
other, then the presb. will, of new, take their removeall
[or] stay to consideration[.] all agree ———
To the petition, befor the presb. give ane answeare, they
are to desyre the petitioners first to instruct [= prove] the certain-
tie of a stipend, and how it is provyded[.] all agree to this[.]
Anent the first, James Rosse protests against it[.]
Anent the second, he [craves] ws will give band, if the di-
vision be made[.] and desyres agine that Mr George Dick
and Gillespie be removed ———
The presb. delaid the answearing of the petition for di-
vision till the next day, [with] the certaintie of the stipend
& the act of plot [= plat] [ ][.] all agree to this ———
James Rosse protests that there be no nomination
or presentation of a minister till the division goe
throughe ———
```

### Modern English
**James Ross of Balneil** gives in a paper desiring that **Mr George Dick and
William Kennedy of Gillespie be debarred**, since they protested against his desire
[for division]. **The heritors and tenants of the moor of Glenluce** supplicate:
whereas the presbytery at its last Glenluce meeting concluded the *place* for the new
kirk but **continued the declaration of what part of the parish belongs to it**, the
work is hindered for want of the division — therefore they entreat the presbytery to
**declare the division first**, so they may proceed. On debarring George Dick and
Kennedy: the presbytery resolves to **let them sit and vote**, but if their votes
would tip an equal division, it will reconsider their removal (all agree). Before
answering the petition, the petitioners must first **prove the certainty of a stipend
and how it is provided** (all agree). Ross protests on the first point; on the second,
asks a bond if the division is made, and again asks that George Dick and Gillespie be
removed. The presbytery **defers the division answer** to the next day (with the
certainty of the stipend and the **act of plat**). **Ross protests that no minister
be nominated or presented till the division goes through.**

## Notes & context
- **The Power case handed upward.** After a second accuser, the presbytery declines
  to act on **Andrew McDowell of Killaster's** paper and refers the whole question to
  the **Synod** — a sign the local court wants cover for so grave a matter (deposing
  a minister). Note Power himself had complained to the Provincial Synod of French's
  hornings earlier — both sides invoke the Synod.
- **Debt, horning, and "usury" personalised.** Article 6 shows the *mechanism* behind
  the usury/oppression charge: Power holding **bonds "to the register," charging, and
  denouncing debtors "at the horn"** (Scottish diligence — outlawry for non-payment),
  even the elder **laird of French**, three times, refusing tendered payment. This is
  the concrete grievance that made an enemy of the whole French kin.
- **Glenluce, a textbook parish division.** Folio 20 is a clean example of 1640s
  **parish disjunction procedure**: site chosen (Balneil, img_0018), then the
  **boundary declaration**, the fight over **who may vote**, and the rule that a
  **secured stipend and an "act of plat"** (the plan/valuation) must precede
  division — with **James Ross of Balneil** driving it and **George Dick / William
  Kennedy of Gillespie** resisting.
- **New/clarified people:** **William Kennedy of Gillespie** (so "Gillespie" earlier =
  his estate), **Andrew McDowell of Killaster** (= "Ellaster"; second accuser).
- **Palaeography applied:** `at the horne` = denounced rebel/outlawed for debt (put to
  the horn); `band to the register` = a registered bond (summarily enforceable);
  `instruments` = notarial acts; `instruct` = to prove; `act of plot` = act of *plat*
  (the plan/valuation scheme for a new parish); `apperteine` = belong. Dates "1642"
  Old Style. `y`+superscript → *th-*.
- **Lowest-confidence readings for the human reviewer (Dad):**

     "Ellaster" of img_0019 (same man/estate).
  2. `act of plot`/`plat` and the deferred stipend clause (folio 20 foot).
  3. The Martinmas years (1640, 1642) and "four score merks" in article 6.
  4. Whether "regrat ... at the horne" = "denounced/distressed him at the horn"
     (horning) — confidence medium.

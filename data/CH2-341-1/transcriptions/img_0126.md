# CH2/341/1 — Image 126 (folios 125v–126), 16 February–1 March 1648 — ★★ Mr John Scott accepts the presentation to Old Glenluce; the admission edict is served and returned; his settlement proceeds

Left page (folio **125a**): (16 February, continued) **the said day — Mr John Livingstone being absent — Mr
John Crichton both made and added the exercise (1 Thess. 4:6–8) and was approved**; **★ Mr John Crichton
appointed to preach at Stranraer on the Sabbath come eight days**; **★ the presbytery, considering that the
answer to the letter written by the moderator to the presbytery of Ireland anent John Bain was not yet come,
and that many of the brethren and ruling elders were absent, therefore Ardwell's business is delayed**; then
**★★ the said day Mr John Scott compeared and declared that he accepted of the presentation delivered to him
by Mr John Livingstone in the presbytery's name, subscribed by the moderator and clerk of the presbytery, of
the date at Glenluce, September 22, 1647; and the same day the gentry and gentlemen of the old parish of
Glenluce compeared, desiring that — seeing Mr John Scott had accepted of the presbytery's presentation as it
was formed and drawn up by themselves — the presbytery would, with all possible diligence, proceed to
receive and establish the said Mr John Scott, minister at the old kirk of Glenluce; whereupon the presbytery
appointed an edict to be drawn up and subscribed by the clerk of the presbytery, to be read at the most
patent door of the old kirk of Glenluce the next Sabbath, a copy of the same to be affixed and left at the
said door, and the principal copy to be retained duly indorsed and executed unto the next presbytery — which
is by this present appointed to be gotten at Glenluce this day fortnight, being the first of March**. Right
page (folio **126**): **the moderator is appointed to preach and receive the foresaid Mr John Scott,
minister at the said old kirk; or, in case the moderator do not come, Mr John Dick is appointed to preach
and to receive the said Mr John Scott to the ministry at the old kirk foresaid**. Then **Presbytery holden
at Glenluce, March 1, 1648** — absent Mr John Livingstone and all the ruling elders except Kirkmaiden and
Glenluce (old and new); Blair's and Turnbull's former absence excused, and the elders of Kirkmaiden and the
New kirk of Glenluce excused and approved; **★ the said day the presbytery did call for the edict appointed
the last presbytery day, to be read at the kirk door — delivered by the moderator, indorsed and executed
according to [order], found duly [served, with no objection]**; and **★★ the said day Mr John Scott
compeared and [being received], declaring… [reserving] regarding the presbytery… anent the [rights] of the
Abbey and glebe, either by discharge or [otherwise], and any claim… against the said parties, for anything
done by them before the date of this present, to the prejudice of the Abbey; he submitted the point thereof
to the presbytery; and [if] Patrick do not in like manner submit, the presbytery did [ordain] to concur with
Mr John Scott against the said parties, to get [the money] due by [the said] Patrick to the Abbey
[recovered]**.

## Notes & context
- **★★ Old Glenluce planted — Scott accepts and is admitted.** The counterpart-milestone to Crichton's New
  Luce admission (img 0115) reaches completion: **Scott formally accepts the presentation (16 Feb),** the
  **old-parish gentry press the presbytery to "receive and establish" him,** an **admission edict is drawn
  up, read at the kirk door, affixed, and returned duly executed with no objectors,** and on **1 March 1648
  the presbytery meets at Glenluce to receive him** (the moderator, or Dick, to preach and admit). With
  **both** Glenluce kirks now settled (Old/Scott, New/Crichton), the years-long planting saga — division
  (img 0106), funding fights (imgs 0107–0111), Great-Seal mortification (img 0119), Scott's protestation
  (img 0120), the gentry supplication (img 0123), Livingstone's Edinburgh mission (img 0125) — is essentially
  done. A capstone node.
- **★ The Abbey-rights reservation.** Even at his settlement, Scott (with the presbytery) **reserves the
  rights of "the Abbey and glebe" and any claim against parties who damaged the Abbey** — and if **Patrick**
  (the Abbey tacksman warned to flit, imgs 0112–0113) will not submit, the presbytery will **concur with
  Scott to recover the money due to the Abbey.** The temporal loose ends of the Glenluce Abbey teinds still
  being tied off.
- **The admission edict — process detail.** A clean textbook example for the glossary: the **edict**
  (public notice of an intended admission) **read at "the most patent door" of the kirk, a copy affixed, the
  principal indorsed and executed and returned** to the next presbytery — the canonical Presbyterian
  admission procedure, giving the parish a chance to object before a minister is settled.
- **Ardwell stalls; Ireland silent.** The **Ardwell process is delayed** again (the Irish answer about
  **John Bain** still not come, and too many members absent) — the scandal that will not resolve because its
  key witness is beyond reach in Ulster.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `accepted of ye presentatioun… subscryved by ye Moderator and clerk…
  Dait at Glenluce Septr 22 1647` = a minister formally accepting his presentation; `ane edict… to be red at
  ye most patent Dor of ye old kirk… duely indorsed and executt` = the admission edict and its return;
  `proceed to receive and establish ye said Mr Johne Scott, minister` = the act of settling a minister;
  `reserved… ye rights of ye Abbey and gleib… concur… to get yr money due… to ye Abbey` = protecting the
  benefice's temporal rights. Left page firm; right page heavily water-stained — the Abbey-rights clause and
  the receiving-minister arrangement are provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1648**.

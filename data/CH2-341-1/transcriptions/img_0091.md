# CH2/341/1 — Image 91 (folios 90v–91), March–April 1646 — the malignant George Cathcart; JPs to execute the anti-witch act; Garthland & Livingstone sail with Argyll to Ireland

Left page (folio **90a**): (tail of 18 March) **Mr John Dick to summon from the pulpit [Mr Corbie?],
disobedient to the session**; **John Mc[—], disobedient to the Inch session, summoned pro 1 and not
appearing, to be summoned from the pulpit pro 2 under pain of excommunication**; **an adulteress
[—] McFadyen in Kirkmaiden summoned pro 2**; next meeting 15 April. Then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 15
April 1646** — an exercise (Mr James Bell made, Mr Alexander Turnbull added, 1 Thess. 1:10); absent the
elders of Kirkcolm and Kirkmaiden; **Mr James Bell reports he spoke with George Cathcart** [the
malignant suspect, img 0090], who answered [that he would…]. Right page (folio **91**): **George
Cathcart desires a copy of the presbytery's act**, which the presbytery orders delivered to Bell to
show him, then to certify him to the next presbytery; **Mr Alexander Turnbull reports the heritors of
Kirkmaiden have chosen Justices of the Peace within Kirkmaiden, and [named men] for executing the act
of Parliament against [witches/vice]**; the **malignants business is delayed** for the absence of
considerable heritors; and **★ the Glenluce negotiation could not proceed because the laird of
Garthland and Mr John Livingstone — the two commissioners appointed to treat — were necessitated to
accompany the Marquess of Argyll to Ireland.**

## Notes & context
- **★★ Garthland and Livingstone sail with Argyll to Ireland (April 1646).** A striking
  national-history datum minuted as a mere scheduling excuse: the Glenluce negotiation stalls because
  **the laird of Garthland and Mr John Livingstone "were necessitated to accompany the Marquess of
  Argyll to Ireland."** **Archibald Campbell, Marquess of Argyll** — the foremost Covenanter magnate —
  was active around the North Channel/Ulster in 1645–46; here the **Stranraer minister Livingstone**
  (repeatedly sent to Ireland) and the leading Stoneykirk heritor **Garthland** attend him across the
  water. A firm biographical event for Livingstone and Garthland, and further proof of the **Stranraer
  presbytery as the Scotland–Ulster hinge.** (Confirm "Argyll".)
- **★ Justices of the Peace to execute the anti-witch act.** **The heritors of Kirkmaiden have chosen
  Justices of the Peace** and named men **"for executing the act of Parliament against [witches]"** —
  the civil magistracy being organised locally to enforce the witchcraft (and/or vice) statute,
  dovetailing with the presbytery's own witch processes (imgs 0089–0090). A concrete instance of
  kirk–state cooperation in the witch-hunt, and of the 1646 revival of the JP commissions in Galloway.
- **The malignant George Cathcart.** The suspected malignant (img 0090) is now named **George
  Cathcart** and engages with the process — asking for **a copy of the presbytery's act** before
  answering. The court obliges, showing due procedural care even in the political purge.
- **Everything delayed by absent heritors — and by war.** Both the **malignants business** and the
  **Glenluce planting** are held up for want of key laymen — one lot simply absent, the other
  (Garthland, Livingstone) away in Ireland with Argyll. The parish court's work bends around the
  national crisis.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `Desyred… a sight of the act… certifie him to the next
  presbytery` = asked for a copy and to be formally cited next time; `Justices of peace… for executing
  the act of parliament against [witches]` = JPs commissioned to enforce the statute; `necessitated to

# CH2/341/1 — Image 22 (folios 21v–22), 6 April 1643 — Power appeals to the Synod

Left page (folio **21a**) finishes **William McDowell's grain-price deposition**
(the fiars of 1621–23, relevant to the price-exaction charge), notes that **Power
and James Gordon each gave in a paper**, and opens **Power's protestation**. Right
page (folio **22**) completes the protestation and sets out **Power's appellation
from the presbytery to the Provincial Assembly (Synod) of Galloway** — his central
plea of an unfair process. The accuser's forename is now legible: **Uchtred McDowell,
younger of French**.

### Modern English
**William McDowell**, asked about victual (grain) prices in 1621–1623, says that to
his memory the **fiars** (the official annual grain price) was 18 pounds, the price of
victual in two of those years 12 pounds; the cheapest he bought meal at Michaelmas
for 16 (the cheapest at Wigton), and it was no cheaper at any time in 1621–1622.
*Subscribed: William McDowell.*

**Mr Gilbert Power and James Gordon each gave in a paper.** Power's is his
**protestation**: forasmuch as he has been accused of divers heinous crimes — tending
both to a **criminal suit** and to **deprivation from his ministry** — by **Uchtred
McDowell of French and his adherents**, which accusations have never been set out
particularly, with the circumstances needed to make a formal libel (so that he could
give particular answers, or elide/clear them), nor is he present when objections about
their relevancy are made; and though he gave in a desire to that effect he has had no
satisfactory answer, yet the process has proceeded to the admission and examination
of witnesses. Therefore he, **Mr Gilbert Power, minister at Stoneykirk, defendant,
protests** that before any advisement or sentence, the **full extract of the process**
(interrogatories, depositions, and all) be given to him at his own charges, that
particular answers…

### Modern English
[Power continues:] that answers, replies and disputation may be made on the
**relevancy**, clearing and eliding of the accusations before any advisement, as is
just — protesting always that whatever has been or shall be done be no way prejudicial
to him till his demands are granted, nor to his supplications, answers and objections;
but that all, with the rest of the process, be **read, heard, seen, considered, and
enlarged as often as needed** — and that this protest not prejudice his appellations
underwritten.

**Mr Gilbert Power's appellation from the presbytery of Stranraer to the Provincial
Assembly of Galloway** (to meet at Stranraer, the third Tuesday of April 1643):
Whereas it was the mind of the Provincial Assembly, and the presbytery's own offer,
that **before any censure**, brethren and ruling elders from the presbyteries of
**Wigtown and Kirkcudbright** should be sent for to have a voice in it, and brethren
from the presbytery of **Ayr** written to for their concurrence — yet, contrary to
this, **this presbytery has condemned and suspended him from his ministry**;
therefore he **appeals to the Provincial Assembly**. His grounds: **his own
adversaries are admitted as his judges and witnesses**, and certain ruling elders
"continued of purpose for this action" are known to [be his enemies]…

## Notes & context
- **Accuser named: Uchtred McDowell, younger of French.** The forename that read as
  a faint "[B.]" for many folios is legible here as **Uchtred** — an old Galloway
  MacDowall forename. So the prosecutor throughout is **Uchtred McDowell, younger of
  French**. (Worth propagating back through the people index at Stage 3.)
- **Power's defence turns procedural.** His **protestation + appellation** are a
  natural-justice argument: (1) no proper *formal libel* with particulars, so he
  cannot make particular answers; (2) he is denied the **full extract** of the
  process; (3) the presbytery suspended him **without the outside brethren/elders**
  (from Wigtown, Kirkcudbright, Ayr) the Synod had required; (4) **his accusers sit
  as his judges and witnesses**. He therefore **appeals over the presbytery's head to
  the Synod of Galloway** — which explains why the presbytery keeps deferring "till
  they advise with the Synod."
- **Dating the Synod:** the **Provincial Assembly of Galloway** is to meet at Stranraer
  the **third Tuesday of April 1643** — a fixed point for the case's next stage.
- **Grain-price evidence:** William McDowell's deposition on the **fiars** (18 pounds)
  and market meal prices (12, 16) of 1621–23 is being used to test the price-exaction
  / usury charge against Power.
- **New/clarified:** **James Gordon** (gives in a paper — the Stoneykirk relief
  distributor of img_0021, perhaps a Power ally); the three neighbouring presbyteries
  (**Wigtown, Kirkcudbright, Ayr**) and the **Synod of Galloway**.
- **Palaeography applied:** `eliding` / `cleiring` = defeating / clearing a charge
  (Scots legal); `relevancy` = legal sufficiency of a charge; `advisement` =
  deliberation to sentence; `fiars` = the annually-fixed county grain prices; `pairties
  ... admitted to be my Judges` = the accusers also sitting in judgment. Dates "1642"
  Old Style. `y`+superscript → *th-*.

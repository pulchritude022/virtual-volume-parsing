# CH2/341/1 — Image 11 (folios 10v–11), Nov 1642 (Power case, continued)

Left page (folio **10a**) is **Power's further supplication** — four more demands
that French be forced to instance/prove his charges (the Synod-book articles, a
second perjury before the late Mr John Watson, the Inch-visitation confession, and
the drunkenness story) — then the presbytery **defers** answering until the
process nears its close. Right page (folio **11**) routes two depositions, then
**French gives in a paper asking that Mr James Blair be removed from the
presbytery** as a partisan of Power, narrating a bitter teind-valuation dispute.
Faded, esp. the lower right — treat as tentative.

### Modern English (paraphrase — low confidence)
Power asks that his answers be put in better form if needful, then makes four
further demands: **(1)** that French be urged to prove the first seven articles of
his libel by producing the Synod book he referred to — or at least to give
particular instances for each article, so Power can answer them; **(2)** that,
having averred Power guilty of a *second perjury* allegedly committed before the
late **Mr John Watson**, French should qualify (substantiate) it; **(3)** that,
having averred at the last visitation of the kirk of Inch that he heard Power
confess [oppression] "in [warandice?]", French make it good (both these points
being *criminal*), and bind himself to pursue Power for them by the civil law;
**(4)** that, having averred he would prove Power "so drunk till I spewed
(vomited) in Knaggis's [enclosed] house, and that the said **Annabel** and **James
[Gibson?]** carried me to my bed," French verify it. Power also asks that the
depositions of **[Charles] Campbell, Baillie of [Carrick?]**, and **Mr Joseph
Cleland, minister at Kirkcowan**, be used in the process. *Subscribed: Mr Gilbert
Power.* The presbytery, as to these four, thinks it not fitting to delay the
process by granting them, but **defers** answering until the process nears its
close.

### Modern English (paraphrase — low confidence)
As to the last two points (Charles Campbell's and Mr Joseph Cleland's
depositions), the presbytery ordains **Mr Hugh Hutcheson** to warn Charles
Campbell to be present the next day, and **Mr John Dick** to receive **Mr Joseph
Cleland's** deposition.

**Mr French then gives in a paper:** he humbly craves that **Mr James Blair be
removed from the presbytery** — first, because from the beginning of this
accusation Blair has pleaded as a *party* on behalf of Mr Gilbert, his passion
often wronging both himself and the action in hand; and secondly, because Blair
[acting as arbiter] once wronged French. The narrative: Mr Gilbert, maliciously
set against French, would not ease him regarding his [teind] payments as he did his
other parishioners; after long contest the difference was **referred to the lairds
of Logan and Kilaster**, with **Mr James [Blair]** [as go-between]; they agreed on
15 merks for a "100 land," yet French gets only four merks for all the teind lands
in the parish. The dispute not being finally settled, a written *minute* was drawn;
French, trusting Blair as he would his own friends, entrusted the minute to him —
but Blair "far outran my expectation": when he saw his good-father (father-in-law?)
could not prove his case, he sided with Mr James [Gilbert?]. And so, by the
entreaty of **my Lord of Airds** — for neither the lord, nor the Sheriff, nor
Garthland, nor any friend could get the minute produced — French was driven to
great pains and expense, and at last forced to **ride to Edinburgh and bring home
letters of exhibition** (a court order compelling production), which are extant
still.

## Notes & context
- **The case widens into presbytery politics.** Beyond answering the libel, both
  sides now fight over *who may sit in judgment*: Power earlier tried to disqualify
  the ruling elders Garthland and Charles Campbell (img_0006); now **French moves to
  remove Mr James Blair** (a minister-member) as Power's open partisan. The court is
  visibly factional.
- **New evidentiary colour:**
  - A **second perjury** allegedly sworn before the late **Mr John Watson**.
  - The **drunkenness** charge gets its instance: Power "so drunk till I spewed in
    **Knaggis's** house," carried to bed by **Annabel** and **James [Gibson?]**.
  - A **teind-valuation dispute** ("15 merks for a 100 land" vs. "four merks")
    referred to the lairds of **Logan** and **Kilaster**, with a contested written
    *minute* Blair allegedly withheld — forcing French to **ride to Edinburgh for
    letters of exhibition**. This is the concrete grievance underlying the feud.
- **New people/offices:** **Mr James Blair** (minister; Power's partisan), **Mr
  Joseph Cleland** now identified as **minister at Kirkcowan**, **[Charles] Campbell,
  Baillie of [Carrick?]**, **my Lord of Airds**, the lairds of **Logan** and
  **Kilaster** (arbiters).
- **Palaeography applied:** `qualifie` = to substantiate/prove (Scots legal);
  `letters of exhibition` = a court warrant compelling production of a writ;
  `minute` = a written memorandum of agreement; `teind` = tithe; `100 land` = a
  land unit rated at £100 of "old extent"; `goodfather` = father-in-law; `regrated`
  here reads as "sided with/favoured"; `spewed` = vomited. `y`+superscript → *th-*.

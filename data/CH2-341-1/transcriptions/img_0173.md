## Notes & context
★★ **The Commission of the Kirk formally reproves the delay (letter of 6 June 1649).** The heart of this opening is the **verbatim letter from the Commission of the Kirk** ordering the presbytery to **close James Ross of Balneil's malignancy process "speedily … or at least prepared against the General Assembly, so it be no longer prejudiced."** The Commission frames the delay itself as a wrong to Balneil ("whether he be guilty or not … it is a great prejudice to him that the process is not closed"). This is the img 162 referral coming home to roost: having bounced the case up to the Commission, the presbytery is now being told to finish it.

★★ **Why it won't close — the witnesses fail.** The presbytery's candid answer: **"we could get nothing done in the process because of the difficulty that occurs there,"** despite spending two full presbytery days on it. The witness-gathering (from imgs 168, 171) collapses: of **Park [Bennoch], [Holmes], Robert Craik, and John [Murchie]**, only Craik and Murchie compear, and they depose only that they **"neither concealed his being put into, nor his coming out of, prison"** — nothing to the malignancy charge. And Balneil has **legally blocked the whole method** by protesting, under the Act [of Classes], **that no witness be received against whom he might justly except.** So the case is procedurally stuck.

★ **The refused subscription.** A concrete new charge surfaces: at a shire committee, an anti-Engagement supplication to Parliament was subscribed by all present **except Balneil**, who alone was absent — with the certification that non-subscribers **"should be held disaffected to the cause of God."** Referred to **Mr John Crosse's declaration** (Crosse having chaired). This keeps the case alive even as the witness route dies.

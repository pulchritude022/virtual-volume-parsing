# CH2/341/1 — Image 141 (folios 140v–141)

Left page (foliated **140a** = folio 140 verso) finishes the **23 August 1648**
GA-report and carries the ★★ **General Assembly's resolution of the Ardwell
appeal**: the commissioners, having taken the whole Assembly's advice, report that
the GA **appointed the laird of Ardwell either to confess, or to purge himself by
his oath, before the congregation of Stranraer — and thereafter he shall have
liberty to present his bill against the Lady Freugh**. Also from the GA: ministers
are to **draw up the "enormities of the army,"** and — because of the widespread
guilt in the late Engagement — the Assembly has ★ **discharged all ministers from
giving the communion in their congregations until further order**. Then: the
**widows-and-orphans roll** is pressed on the other two presbyteries against the
Synod; and ★ a **letter from Ireland** warns that **James McBryde, guilty of
"hostility," has fled from [Ballymoney] in Ireland to Galloway** — the brethren to
search and, if found, remit him to the magistrate to be **sent back to Ireland**.
The right page (folio 141) has the routine **13 and 27 September 1648** sittings:
exercise/preaching, the **national fast kept on 10 September**, and **Mr James
Lowrie's disputation theses "de latitudine salutis"** (on the extent of
salvation), explicated and disputed in Latin.

### Modern English
[GA report, continued:] ...declared a copy of the General Assembly's Declaration
against the Engagement. It was recommended to the brethren, with all possible
speed, to give copies of the said papers and read them in their several churches;
also that the Assembly had appointed all ministers to **draw up the enormities of
the army**; and that the Assembly, considering that great multitudes are involved
in the late Engagement, had **discharged all ministers from giving the communion in
their congregations until further order**. As also the commissioners reported
that, according to the directions given them by the presbytery, they had taken the
advice of the whole Assembly concerning **Ardwell's business**, and that ★ **the
General Assembly appointed the laird of Ardwell either to confess, or to purge
himself by his oath, before the congregation of Stranraer; and thereafter he
should have liberty to present his bill against the Lady Freugh**.

The brethren who stayed at home reported they had preached in the vacant
congregations as appointed. Mr John Dick reported that Robert McIlhaffie and
Isobell McMillan were ordered to give signs of repentance for their adultery.

The presbytery, considering that the **widows and orphans** are too long wanting
their relief, appoint the Moderator to write to the other two presbyteries to have
the names of the widows and orphans in readiness against the Synod, that some solid
and unanimous course may be taken for their relief.

The same day, the kirk being advertised by letters from Ireland that ★ **one James
McBryde, guilty of hostility, has fled from [Ballymoney] in Ireland to this
country**, it was seriously recommended to the brethren to make diligent search in
their parishes for the said James McBryde, and if found, to remit him to the
magistrate, that so he may be **sent over to Ireland**.

The next meeting is appointed this day fortnight, before communion, at the **New
kirk**, and the morrow thereafter at the **old kirk of Glenluce**, for a visitation
of that kirk. — *Mr John Craig, clerk to the kirk.*

## Notes & context
- ★★ **General Assembly resolves the Ardwell appeal.** The July 1648 Assembly,
  hearing Ardwell's appeal (imgs 137–138), rules: he must **confess or purge
  himself by oath before the congregation of Stranraer, and only thereafter may
  present his counter-bill against Lady Freugh**. So the Assembly upholds the
  presbytery's core demand (purgation first) while granting Ardwell his slander
  suit in sequence — the decisive turn in the multi-year arc.
- ★★ **Communion discharged after the Engagement.** The Assembly **suspended the
  Lord's Supper in all congregations** because "great multitudes" were guilty in
  the late Engagement — a striking penitential-discipline measure in the immediate
  aftermath of Preston (Aug 1648). Ministers are also to compile the **"enormities
  of the army."**
- ★ **Cross-border fugitive: James McBryde.** A man "guilty of hostility" (i.e.
  implicated in the Irish war/rebellion) flees from **[Ballymoney]** (Co. Antrim)
  to Galloway; the presbytery is to hunt him and hand him to the magistrate for
  **extradition to Ireland** — another North-Channel policing episode.
- ★ **National fast kept 10 September 1648** (the fast appointed earlier, imgs
  134–135, in the day of danger) — a datable liturgical event.
- **Mr James Lowrie's ordination trials** continue: his Latin disputation **"de
  latitudine salutis"** (on the extent of salvation) on 27 September — theological
  colour on a candidate's examination.
- **Visitation of the old kirk of Glenluce** appointed (with a communion at the New
  kirk) — the physical follow-through on the Glenluce plantation.

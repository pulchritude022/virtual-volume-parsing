### Modern English
Mr John Scott again appointed to speak [the Bailie of Glenluce] about the robbery.

**The brethren exhorted to see that their own poor are maintained within their several parishes** (as some had begun). **It is recommended to the Sheriff to end the vagrant, masterless beggars, and to move at the next Committee that constables be appointed to furnish the same.** [Andrew of] Arisemman would not keep the meeting; Scott to require some to appear, else cite by the kirk-officer.

Marg. McLurie (fornication) cited a third time. Andrew Gyle (disobedience to Kirkcolm) cited a third time. **Patrick McKie compeared, required to subscribe the petition against the Engagement and to declare whether he voted in arms to oppose the well-affected who rose for their own defence; the judgment being against the Engagement, he is appointed to make public confession, and on satisfaction to be received to the Covenant.** Scott to require **James Ross of Balneil to appear before the Commission of the Kirk, taking the extract of the process.** **William Baillie (a Lieutenant of horse in the Engagement) compeared, confessed his guilt, and is appointed to make public confession at the kirk of Luce**, then to the Covenant.

## Notes & context
★★★ **The 1650 Scottish Metrical Psalter adopted.** A crisp national-history anchor: **"the brethren, in obedience to the act of the General Assembly, have begun to practise the new Psalter"** — the presbytery introducing the **1650 Scots Metrical Psalms** (authorised by the 1650 General Assembly, and still the psalter of the Church of Scotland) in the Rhins pulpits, within weeks of its adoption.

★★ **The 1650 witch-hunt running.** Confirmation that the parish-by-parish **witchcraft enquiry** ordered at img 189 is underway ("the brethren are found to be using diligence anent the trial of witchcraft").

★ **Parish administration.** The **widows-and-orphans money distributed per parish**; the **Portpatrick/Stoneykirk land-annexation revisited** with an on-site perambulation (imgs 182–183); the **Inch parishioners summoned** (only Patrick Agnew of Sheuchan appearing) for a "grievanced matter"; poor relief pressed onto parishes and the Sheriff asked to appoint **constables against masterless beggars**. A **28 May visitation at Portmontgomery (Portpatrick)** opens (elders: **Vans of French** for Stoneykirk, **Patrick McDowall of Logan** for Kirkmaiden).

★ **The Engagement penitents continue:** **Patrick McKie** put to public confession (examined whether he "voted in arms to oppose the well-affected who rose for their own defence"); **William Baillie, a Lieutenant of horse,** to confess at the kirk of Luce; **Balneil** to appear before the Commission with the process extract.

- **Lowest-confidence readings (Dad):**
  - **"[Wm] Vans of French"** (Stoneykirk elder) — the Vans-of-Barnbarroch / laird-of-French family; forename tentative. **Patrick McDowall of Logan** (Kirkmaiden elder) is clearer.
  - The 28 May venue read as "[Portmontgomeris]" (Portpatrick) — the heading is faint.
  - "Arisemman" (who "would not keep the meeting") = Andrew Kennedy of Arisemman/Airieman (img 189).
  - Patrick McKie's examination clause ("voted in arms to oppose the well-affected who rose for their own defence") — the phrasing is the standard 1650 test for Engagement guilt; wording partly reconstructed.

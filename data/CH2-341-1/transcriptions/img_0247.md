# CH2/341/1 — Image 247 (folios 246a–247)

## Folio 246a (left page)  *(confidence: low)*

*A **supplication by Alexr McDowall complaining of the Stranraer session's procedure against him** in his adultery process: that the session, "by summons," took depositions against him — notwithstanding his objection that the witnesses (naming **Andro McBryde**) bore "malice" and were "known to be his pursuers" — and admitted them to depose "he not being called to see if he had anything to object against them." The session's sentence enjoined him to appear before the congregation this Sabbath; he "was not able to be present himself that day, being sick." **The presbytery finds the session of Stranraer's procedure orderly, and appoints Mr John Dick and Mr John Park to confer with Alexr McDowall at Stranraer and settle it before the next meeting.** Reasonably legible. Side-margin captions: "McDowall / supplication".*

## Notes & context
★★★ **The Resolutioner–Protester rupture inside Stranraer presbytery — Turnbull dissents on the late General Assembly.** The most consequential entry of the batch. After weeks of deferral and a special conference at Stoneykirk (imgs 244–245), the presbytery finally gives its **judgment on the lawfulness of "the late General Assembly"** (the 1651 Dundee Assembly, whose validity the Protesters denied). **Most of the members "resolved affirmatively"** — the **Resolutioner** line, approving the Assembly. But **Mr Alexr Turnbull "did dissent and protested, in his own name and in the name of all [that were] absent," reasons to be given the next day — and Mr James [Bell] likewise dissented and protested.** Here is the great national schism of the Kirk crystallising into a formal, minuted split within one small presbytery, with the veteran **Turnbull (a former moderator) taking the Protester side.** A vivid local instance of the divide that would fracture the Church of Scotland for a decade. *(Cross-refs the Protester thread via Mr James Guthrie, img 225, and the Commission's Resolutioner act, img 234.)*

★★ **The session-procedure complaint: Alexr McDowall vs his accusers.** A revealing due-process dispute: **Alexr McDowall supplicates that the Stranraer session took depositions against him (in a suspected-adultery process, with Cristian McDowall) from witnesses he says were "his pursuers" bearing "malice" (naming Andro McBryde), and admitted them "he not being called to see if he had anything to object" — and enjoined him to the stool this Sabbath though he was "sick."** The presbytery upholds the session as having "proceeded orderly," but appoints **Dick and Park to confer** — the ordinary tension between a defendant's objections and kirk-session summary procedure. (The adultery-proof connects to the John Morison / Gilbert Neilson deposition, img 246; whether the accused man is Alexr McDowall or Neilson, and Cristian McDowall the woman, is tangled — verify.)

★ **John McDowall's obstinacy.** Alongside, **John McDowall** (the Stranraer neighbour-quarrel man, img 245) **satisfies the ordinance**, but his earlier remissness leaves him under warning of a first public admonition from **Mr John Park.**

## Notes & context
★★ **The Inch settlement stalls on a legal catch — "no minister settled."** A telling procedural knot: the presbytery has been pressing the **Sheriff to give Mr John Dick possession of the Inch glebe** (imgs 209–217), but the Sheriff now **"would give no answer till there were a minister settled at the kirk of Inch"** — the very thing the glebe possession is meant to complete. The planting of Dick at Inch (in train since img 199) is caught in a chicken-and-egg between the civil and kirk processes.

★★ **The Ireland-malignancy cases and "my Lord of Ardes."** **John Campbell "is not yet to be found in this kingdom"** (still fled, cf. imgs 212–218), while **Robert Campbell** now reports he is doing diligence to get a **testimonial of his Ireland carriage "conform to my Lord of Ardes"** — i.e. certified through **the Viscount Montgomery of the Great Ards**, the leading Scots magnate of Ulster. This pins the presbytery's vetting of returned soldiers to the Montgomery interest in the Ards (cf. the Donaghadee / George Munro material, imgs 204–216).

★ **Discipline in the New Year.** The 1 January 1651 sitting keeps the docket moving: **George Adestoun** at last compears, acknowledges his long disobedience (imgs 205–217), and is referred to the Stoneykirk session to satisfy — notably also **"for his slandering of James [Corsbie]."** **Dinnaggot** is cited anew; and Dick's report on the **Gilbert Kennedy of Aviolen adultery** (Margaret Morion / Janet Williamson) runs on into the next opening.

Minister of **Inch**, presented by patron the **Earl of Cassillis** to an ex-bishop's kirk,
and — alongside his namesake (but distinct) colleague **Mr George Dick** — one of the two
"Dicks" whose separate careers can be easy to conflate in this register.

## A steady administrative hand

Dick moderates repeatedly across the decades (e.g. May 1643 [img_0034]) and is the
presbytery's regular choice for delivering awkward messages and presiding over milestones:
he notifies James Ross of Balneil about Glenluce's referral to the General Assembly
[img_94], and he is the one who preaches and formally receives Mr John Scott as minister of
old Glenluce, walking the whole presbytery out to Glenluce Abbey afterward to give him
possession [img_126–127].

## The Islandmagee transportation petition

Late in the 1640s a petition surfaces to transport Dick himself to an Irish parish —
eventually identified as **Islandmagee**, Co. Antrim (the clerk's "Ilmagie") — which is
appealed to the General Assembly alongside the unrelated Ardwell appeal, then lapses when
its commissioner fails to appear [img_132, img_136, img_138–139]. Dick stays at Inch.

## Distinguishing the two Dicks

Because "Mr George Dick" and "Mr John Dick" appear so often in the same sittings, the
surest way to tell them apart in this register is by parish and role: John Dick is
consistently tied to **Inch** and to Glenluce-planting ceremony; George Dick is the one sent
to Edinburgh on Glenluce's mortification business during the Power case (see his page).

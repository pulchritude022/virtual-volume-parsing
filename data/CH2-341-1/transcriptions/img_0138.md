# CH2/341/1 — Image 138 (folios 137v–138)

The close of the **Ardwell** matter for now, at the **Stranraer sitting of 7 June
1648**. Left page (foliated **137a** = folio 137 verso) finishes Ardwell's
supplication: he complains that at the **Provincial Synod (Wigtown, 19 October
1647)** the presbytery grounded an act on the information of one member rather
than on the process before it; and he formally ★ **appeals from the presbytery of
Stranraer to the next General Assembly (to be held at Edinburgh, the second
Wednesday of July next [1648])**, summoning the presbytery to appear there with
the whole process. The right page (folio 138): the presbytery finds the appeal's
narrative "false" and full of reproaches, but — the General Assembly being near —
**resolves not to proceed further in the process until it takes the Assembly's
advice**; and appoints **Mr Alexander Turnbull** to give in his disputation
theses. Then ★ the **transportation petition for Mr John Dick revives**: the
commissioner **John [Kennedy?] of [Barsell?]** compears and gives in a supplication
(subscribed by the parishioners of **Islandmagee** in Ireland, with a letter from
the **presbytery in Ireland** and a roll) craving that **Mr John Dick, present
minister at Inch**, be transported to Islandmagee — which the presbytery takes to
mature deliberation.

### Modern English *(sense)*
[Ardwell's supplication, continued:] "...I should be delayed and [wronged] by
them, as may now be remembered, that I would not conform so to do. [I] went to that

would do anything but that for which they had good grounds... But seeing I find it
otherwise, and [that they have] no mind but to keep me under the said scandal
unless I do that which is contrary to order — a very dangerous preparative in the
land — which forced me to make my address to another judicatory... **Therefore,
seeing the General Assembly of this nation duly judges between me and the
Provincial [Synod] of this province, I the said alleged do hereby appeal from your
wisdoms of the kirk of Stranraer unto the next ensuing General Assembly, to be
holden at Edinburgh the second Wednesday of July next to come**; and that for many
relevant reasons, which are to be given..."

## Notes & context
- ★★ **Ardwell appeals to the General Assembly.** Rather than swear or confess,
  Ardwell **appeals from the presbytery of Stranraer to the General Assembly**
  (Edinburgh, second Wednesday of July 1648), summoning the presbytery to appear
  there with the whole process. The presbytery, judging the appeal false and
  abusive but wary so close to the Assembly, **suspends the process pending the
  Assembly's advice**. This is the procedural cliff-hanger of the Ardwell arc —
  the case now goes up to the national church court.
- ✦ **Place-name resolution:** the Irish parish petitioning for Mr John Dick,
  rendered "[the Maghis]" on imgs 132/136, is here written "[Islandmagie]" — i.e.
  **Islandmagee (Island Magee), Co. Antrim**, an Ulster peninsula parish with
  Scottish planter congregations. Adopting **Islandmagee**.
- ✦ **Mr John Dick is minister of Inch.** This opening states twice that Dick is
  "present minister at Inch" (and would be transported "from the said kirk of
  Inch") — clarifying his charge (earlier openings had left his parish uncertain /
  guessed Kirkcowan). This also fits his glebe dispute with **Lt Col James Agnew**,
  whose land adjoins the Inch kirk (img 136).
- **Mr Alexander Turnbull** to give in his **disputation theses** — a routine
  ministerial exercise ("contradiction") the presbytery is catching up on.

## Notes & context
★★★ **The 1651 General Assembly reaches the Rhins — "the late General Assembly holden at Dundee."** The great Resolutioner Assembly of 1651 (St Andrews, adjourned to **Dundee**, July–August 1651) sends its warning down the presbytery chain: **Wigton's commissioners Mr William Maxwell and Mr John Vaus carry it to Stranraer "for keeping correspondence,"** delivering **a printed warning and "several articles" against ministers and others "who shall oppose or not acknowledge" the public affairs** — i.e. the Public Resolutions (cf. the Commission's act, img 234) — **and appointing a solemn national fast.** Stranraer at once orders the fast-causes read in every congregation and kept the next Lord's day. The Kirk's national machinery — Assembly → neighbouring presbytery → local pulpits — captured in one entry.

★★ **Discipline: John McKerrow's perjured slander, and John Campbell again.** **John McKerrow** of old Glenluce is sharply rebuked and set to public repentance for **slandering Alexr Milhorn "with perjury"** and defying both presbytery and session. And the perennial **John Campbell** case advances another notch — **Mr John Park to give him a second public admonition** if his contumacy continues (from imgs 232, 234–236). A parallel Stoneykirk disobedience is remitted to Mr James Lawrie and referred to the laird for civil punishment.

★ **The Glenluce kirk-and-school business carried forward.** The committee from the img 237 visitation reports back "at some length concerning the kirk and the school," and the presbytery names **Blair, Dick, and Park to meet the parish and settle the fabric and schooling** — the parochial-improvement thread continuing.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 105 (folios 104v–105), January 1647 — Livingstone to keep a Glenluce session; a poor-relief mortification pursued from Cathcart of Genoch; Ireland letters

Left page (folio **104a**): (tail of 30 Dec) **Mr James Bell appointed to preach at Glenluce the 13 January**;
**it is appointed that Mr John Livingstone repair to Glenluce on the 13 January and preach, and after
preaching keep a session with the elders**; **★ Mr Alexander Turnbull reported he had spoken the minister
of Girvan concerning Margaret [Mc—], the suspect adulteress, and the minister of Girvan reported the said
Margaret was [weak] and not able to travel, that she might [give] satisfaction at Kirkmaiden**; **Mr
Alexander Turnbull reported he had written to Mr David Buttle in Ireland to discover where [Margaret
McFadden?], adulteress, fugitive, did reside, that she might be returned; and had received an answer from
Mr David Buttle that the presbytery could not get any trial of her**; **★ the which day compeared Margaret
Henderson [in sackcloth], and confessed her fall in adultery with James Gordon in [—], and was enjoined to
repair to [the place] where she committed the fact, there to give signs of repentance, and to return a
testificate of her satisfaction**; and **John Mc[—], disobedient to the session of [Inch], appointed to be
summoned to the next presbytery**. Right page (folio **105**): the said John Mc[—] to be **summoned from
the pulpit, with warning**; the next meeting appointed [in] January (Mr Alexander Turnbull to make, Mr
John Dick to add); then **Presbytery at Stranraer, [—] January 1647** — absent Mr James Blair and the
elders of Stoneykirk and Portmontgomery; an exercise (Mr Alexander Turnbull made, Mr John [Dick] added,
1 Thess. 2:17–18, both approved); the Kirkmaiden elder's absence the day before excused; **Mr James Bell
reported he preached at Glenluce as ordained**; **Mr John Livingstone reported his diligence at Glenluce,
approved**; and **★ the presbytery ordained Mr John Livingstone (moderator) and Mr [McDowell] of
[Crocha?], ruling elder, to pursue in name of the presbytery, before any competent judicature, John
Cathcart of Genoch — anent [the money/mortification] in his hands, disponed by Mr [Porterfield?] for the
relief of the poor**.

## Notes & context
- **★ A poor-relief mortification pursued at law.** The presbytery **empowers Livingstone and a ruling
  elder to pursue John Cathcart of Genoch "before any competent judicature"** for a **sum/mortification in
  his hands "disponed… for the relief of the poor"** — apparently an endowment (possibly disponed by the
  late **Mr [John] Porterfield**, the deceased Kirkcolm minister) that Cathcart is withholding. A second
  instance (after the Glenluce Abbey teinds, imgs 0100–0101) of the presbytery going to the civil courts
  to secure kirk/poor funds. A good social-history node (poor relief; mortifications; church finance in
  the courts). Confirm **"Cathcart of Genoch"** and the disponer.
- **★ Advancing the Glenluce settlement.** With the call to Crichton pending (img 0102), **Livingstone is
  sent to Glenluce to preach and keep a session with the elders** (13 Jan), **Bell also preaching there**,
  and **Livingstone's "diligence at Glenluce" reported and approved** — the presbytery actively nursing
  the parish toward a settled ministry.
- **★ The cross-channel and cross-parish pursuits stall.** Two offenders elude the discipline: **Margaret
  [Mc—], "the suspect adulteress," is too weak to travel** (to satisfy at Kirkmaiden, per the Girvan
  minister), and **[Margaret McFadden?], a fugitive adulteress in Ireland, cannot be traced** (Mr David
  Buttle's reply: "the presbytery could get no trial of her"). Vivid of the limits of kirk reach across
  the North Channel and the Ayrshire border. Meanwhile **Margaret Henderson** confesses adultery with
  **James Gordon** and is sent to do penance where the fact was committed.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `to pursue in name of ye presbyterie… before any judicature
  competent` = to litigate on the presbytery's behalf in the appropriate court; `disponed… for relief of
  ye poor` = conveyed/mortified as a poor-relief endowment; `after preaching to keep a session With the
  elders` = to hold a kirk-session sitting; `summond fra pulpit, giving Warning` = cited by public
  proclamation; `could not get any tryall of her` = could obtain no examination of the fugitive; `returne
  ane testificate of her satisfactioun` = bring back a certificate that penance was performed. **Both pages
  heavily water-stained** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1647**.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 69 (folios 68v–69), Jan–Mar 1645 — Power will not absolutely consent; the helper referred upward; a fugitive to be apprehended

Left page (folio **68a**): **the conclusion of Mr Gilbert Power's third letter** — he **cannot give
an absolute answer/consent** to a helper ("the sense of former practices makes me… not knowing what
they [intend]"), asks that whatever is done in his business be sent to him **under the clerk's
hand**, and **humbly craves the brethren's help amid his infirmity**, signing "**Mr Gilbert
Power**." The presbytery, reading this as **in effect "an absolute refusal of a helper,"** **refers
the whole matter to the Provincial Assembly / General Assembly for decision.** Then a **criminal
discipline case** opens (John Ferguson, a "heinous fact"; witness Jonet Kennedy). Right page (folio
**69**): the case continued — the presbytery, unable to try it till **John Ferguson is
apprehended**, **recommends a man (James Dow[nell]?) in whose bounds the fact was committed to
apprehend and detain him for "ecclesiastical or civil trial,"** promising to interpose for a civil
warrant ("he shall do good service to God and the country"); two papers delayed; then **Presbytery
at Stranraer, 5 March 1645** (Bell & Blair exercise, Col. 4).

### Modern English (partial — page trimmed)
[The Ferguson case:] witnesses testify (Jonet Kennedy affirms she had seen the said **John
Ferguson**, etc.). **The presbytery refused to delay trying the matter till John [Ferguson] be
first apprehended.** In consideration of **the heinous fact committed by John Ferguson** (with [—]

bounds the fact was committed and where the man usually resides, to take, apprehend, and detain
John Ferguson in firm ward, that he may be made forthcoming for ecclesiastical or civil trial** —
and, failing that, that [others] apprehend him — the presbytery declaring he shall **"do good

Blair, Col. 4).

## Notes & context
- **★ Power will not "absolutely consent" — the helper goes to the Assembly.** The moving close of
  Power's third letter: **he cannot give absolute consent to a helper**, because **"the sense of
  former practices"** (his years of mistreatment) makes him wary of what is intended; he asks only
  that **any decision in his business be sent to him under the clerk's hand**, and **humbly craves
  the brethren's help in his infirmity.** The presbytery reads this as **"in effect an absolute
  refusal"** and **refers the whole helper question up to the General Assembly.** So the collegiate
  arrangement stalls on **Power's justified caution** — a psychologically acute detail: even
  restored and cared-for, he does not fully trust the men who once deposed him. A key note for his
  person-page (his guardedness) and for the timeline (the helper matter unresolved, remitted to the
  Assembly, early 1645). See [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]].
- **A fugitive and the kirk–state interface.** The **John Ferguson** case shows the **limits of
  church discipline**: unable to proceed till the man is **apprehended**, the presbytery **enlists a
  local landholder (James Dow[nell]?) to seize and hold him "in firm ward" for ecclesiastical or
  civil trial**, and undertakes to secure a **civil warrant** — "good service to God and the
  country." A vivid glimpse of how kirk and civil magistrate cooperated to pursue serious offenders
  (the "heinous fact" is not spelled out here — a sexual crime, given the witnesses; handle with
  care). New names: **John Ferguson, Jonet Kennedy, [—] Agnew, John Kennedy, James Dow[nell].**
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `no absolute consent` = no unconditional agreement; `former
  practizes` = the earlier (hostile) proceedings against him; `extract… under your clerk's hand` =
  an authenticated copy of the minute; `remit/refer to the assembly` = send the cause up to the
  higher court; `heynous fact` = a grievous crime; `firm ward` = secure custody; `made
  forthcuming` = produced for trial; `interpone… for a warrant` = intervene to obtain civil
  authority. **Both pages stained/trimmed** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1645.

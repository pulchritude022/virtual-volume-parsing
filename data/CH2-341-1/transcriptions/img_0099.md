## Notes & context
- **★★ The Torrane adultery comes to trial — and the accused's careful denial.** After the son's sworn
  deposition (img 0097), **James Torrane elder is now examined in person** on the suspected adultery with
  **Marion Mure in Inch**. His answer is a study in guarded confession: he **admits she was with him
  "several times… in the chamber, the door locked"** but **denies "any carnal dealing"** — so the
  presbytery, unable to prove the act, **discharges him from her company "in any place that might give
  occasion of suspicion,"** the standard remedy for scandalous (but unproven) association. A second
  witness, **Margaret Mean**, corroborates only the appearance ("standing near the door… once"). A
  textbook example of how the kirk handled *fama* (scandalous report) short of proof. The alleged partner
  is now read as **Marion Mure** (cf. the stained "Margaret [—]" of img 0097 — likely the same woman,
  Marion Mure). A rich, well-documented discipline case for the wiki, with named witnesses.
- **★ The Argyll collection delivered; repentances beginning.** The **collection for the distress of
  Argyll** (imgs 0096–0097) is **handed to the moderator**; and the **Glenluce penitents** — **Thomas
  Adair** and **Margaret Mc[Cristine?]** — have **begun their public repentance/satisfaction** (Bell
  reporting after preaching there). The threads of the last openings closing out.
- **★ The Irish correspondence continues.** **Turnbull wrote to Mr David Buttle, a minister in Ireland,
  "but received no answer"** — the Stranraer presbytery's habitual reach across the North Channel to
  pursue offenders and business (cf. the fugitives sent back "to satisfy"). Also a letter to the
  **minister of [Girvan?]** about a woman — cross-presbytery discipline.
- **★ Into the visitation round — Leswalt (26 Aug), Kirkcolm (27 Aug).** The presbytery continues its
  **parish visitations**, opening **Leswalt on 26 August 1646** with its own minister **Mr Hew Hutcheson**
  preaching (Col. 2:12) and being tried by his eldership — the same standard form as the Kirkmaiden and
  Portmontgomery visitations (img 0098).
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `suspect for adulterie… being accused Whether he did frequent or
  keep company… discharged… of her company in any place yt might give occasioun of suspicioun` = the
  remedy for scandalous association where adultery is not proven; `she Was With him in the chamber, ye
  Door locked… bot he Denyed… any carnall dealing` = admitted circumstance, denied the act; `being sworne,
  Deponed yt she never saw any evill appeirance… only once` = a corroborating witness's limited testimony;
  `the contributioun… Delyvered to ye moderator` = the collection handed over; `visitatioun` = a parish
  inspection. **Both pages faded, the right heavily stained** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*.
  Year **1646**.

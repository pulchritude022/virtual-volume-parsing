# CH2/341/1 — Image 23 (folios 22v–23), 6 April 1643 — Power's six appeal reasons

Both pages set out **Mr Gilbert Power's numbered reasons of appeal** to the
Provincial Assembly (Synod) of Galloway. They are a sustained **due-process /
natural-justice** argument: the elder **Charles Campbell** leaked a witness's
deposition to the accuser; **women "of the base sort," some deranged**, were
summoned from the pulpit under pain of excommunication; his supplications are
ignored while the accuser's are granted; and the prosecution is **malicious**,
designed to weary him "in continual process."

### Modern English
[Reason 2, cont.:] …[certain elders] are favourites to his accuser and have shown
partial dealing — particularly **Charles Campbell**: appointed by the presbytery to
go with **Mr John Porterfield and Mr Hugh Hutcheson** to receive the deposition of
**Findlay Campbell, baillie of Corse**, the two ministers delivered the written
deposition to Charles at his earnest request (he claiming **Mr John Dick**, then
clerk, had directed him to receive and bring it) — yet Charles, against all equity,
**gave it to the pursuer**, as both confessed before the presbytery; and, questioned,
Charles contradicted himself — he had avowed to the ministers that Dick authorised
him (as they testified), but Dick denied it, and Charles then acknowledged he had no
warrant and "did it of himself." Therefore Power appeals.

**Reason 3:** contrary to the Synod's advice, **women were summoned publicly from the
pulpit under pain of excommunication**, and again summoned to appear under the like
pain, to witness against him in **criminal and capital** matters — women "of the base
sort," **some known to be distracted (deranged)** — whose depositions the presbytery
had not even received; and warrants issued to summon **the pursuer's own tenants**.

```
[of] new witnesses; and so, by all appearance, thinks,
[by] wearying of me, to obteine his desyre[,] whereby [...]
```

### Modern English
[Reason 3, cont.:] …the poor, deprived, beggars, and others who were **informers and
plotters** against him; and his own depositions, however valid against them,
disregarded — against all equity; therefore he appeals. **Reason 4:** some of the
judges are plainly set against him and act as *parties* — they have, without warrant
of presbytery or session, **cited witnesses publicly from the pulpit under pain of
excommunication**, to disgrace him among his parishioners, show their power, and
strike terror into witnesses so none dare testify [for him]; this ought to be
referred to the Synod. **Reason 5:** his just desires and supplications are denied or
slighted — never answered, only delayed — while the pursuer's requests are mostly
granted; therefore he appeals. **Reason 6:** it may appear the pursuit is
**malicious** — the accuser is assured that even if he fails to prove his charges he
can, through his favourites, **keep Power in continual process**: whereas custom is to
keep an ordinary number of diets for bringing in witnesses, or to name the whole
witnesses at first, here he holds **new diets day by day for new witnesses**,
purposing "**by wearying of me, to obtain his desire.**"

## Notes & context
- **Power's due-process case, complete.** This is the heart of his appeal to the
  Synod: a Covenanter minister arguing **natural justice** against his own presbytery —
  (2) elders are his accuser's favourites and one (**Charles Campbell**) **leaked a
  deposition** to the prosecutor; (3) **women** — "base," some "distracted" — were
  **coerced from the pulpit under excommunication** to give criminal/capital evidence;
  (4) witnesses cited publicly to terrorise them; (5) his petitions ignored while the
  accuser's are granted; (6) the prosecution is **malicious**, using endless diets to
  **wear him down**. It is a strikingly modern-sounding fair-trial argument.
- **Evidence-tampering named:** the **Charles Campbell** episode (leaking **Findlay
  Campbell of Corse's** deposition to French, then lying about his authority, exposed
  when **Mr John Dick** denied directing him) is concrete misconduct by a ruling
  elder — and explains why Power fought Campbell's commission back in img_0006.
- **New people:** **Findlay Campbell, baillie of Corse** (a witness), and the roles of
  **Charles Campbell** / **Mr John Dick** / **Porterfield** / **Hutcheson** in the
  leaked-deposition affair.
- **Palaeography applied:** `kythed` = shown/made manifest; `posed` = questioned;
  `distracted` = insane/deranged; `dyet(s)` = court sitting(s); `pro nor contra` =
  neither for nor against; `voiced in the back of Scotland` (idiom) = decided behind
  his back; `containe me in continuall processe` = keep me perpetually under trial.
  Dates "1642" Old Style. `y`+superscript → *th-*.

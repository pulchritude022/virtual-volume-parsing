# CH2/341/1 — Image 88 (folios 87v–88), Jan–Feb 1646 — collecting "the work of God"; the search for malignants; a second fugitive to Ireland

Left page (folio **87a**): (still 28 Jan) **★ the presbytery ordains Mr John Livingstone and Mr
Alexander Turnbull to collect "the remarkable passages of the late work of God in this kingdom," per
the desire of the Commission of the General Assembly**; a **sackcloth penitent ([—] Fennell/Adair?)
confesses adultery [with] John Pirie in Inch and is remitted to the Inch session**; **Mr John Dick
ordained to write to the presbytery in Ireland to try whether John Pirie — "fugitive out of this
kingdom" — is residing in their bounds, and if so that he may be sent back**; next meeting 18
February. Then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 18 February 1646** — an exercise (Mr Hew Hutcheson made, Mr
James Bell added, 1 Thess. 1:6–8, approved); **Mr Alexander Turnbull absent, excused for infirmity and
sickness.** Right page (folio **88**): the brethren report **the fast kept (4 Feb) per the Commission's
letter**; on **searching for malignants** they **could get notice of none yet**, so the presbytery
**orders a more narrow trial to find any guilty of malignancy**; **Mr John Dick has not yet written to
Ireland** (re Pirie) but hopes shortly; and a **letter, a declaration of the Commission, and an act of
Parliament are to be published in every church on the Lord's day, after the forenoon sermon, before
the blessing** — the brethren reporting they had published them as appointed, and every minister to
report at the next presbytery.

### Modern English (partial — page faded)
**The whole brethren reported the fast was kept per the Commission's letter** [4 Feb 1646]. **On being
asked whether there were any malignants within their charges, the brethren could get notice of none.**
**The presbytery ordained that a more narrow (searching) trial be used to find any guilty of
malignancy.** **Mr John Dick reported he had not yet written to the presbytery in Ireland** [re Pirie]
but hoped shortly to get occasion. **A letter, a declaration of the Commission of the Assembly, and an
act of Parliament were presented, to be published speedily in every church and parish within the
bounds, on the Lord's day after the forenoon sermon before the blessing** — the papers having been
sent beforehand to every parish by the moderator; the brethren report they had published them as
appointed. Every minister to be ready to report at the next presbytery.

## Notes & context
- **★ "The remarkable passages of the work of God" — collecting providences (1646).** A notable task:
  **Livingstone and Turnbull are ordered to collect "the remarkable passages of the late work of God
  in this kingdom"** for the Commission of the Assembly — the Covenanting habit of recording
  **providential deliverances and mercies** (victories, escapes, judgments) as evidence of God's hand
  in the war. Fitting that **John Livingstone** (famous for his memoirs and "memorable
  characteristics") is named. A good theme node (providence-collecting) and a biographical touch for
  Livingstone.
- **★ The search for malignants intensifies.** The **process against malignants** (img 0087) advances:
  the brethren **found no malignants at first**, so the presbytery orders a **"more narrow trial,"** and
  a **declaration of the Commission and an act of Parliament are to be read from every pulpit** on the
  Lord's day. The machinery of **parish-level political purging after Philiphaugh** is now in full
  operation across the Rhins — every minister deputed to hunt out royalist sympathisers. Central to the
  1646 political-religious theme.
- **★ A second fugitive to Ireland — John Pirie.** Distinct from the murder-suspect **[Mr Colbie?]**
  (imgs 0085–0087, Livingstone's letter), here **John Pirie, a fugitive adulterer, has fled to Ireland**,
  and **Mr John Dick is to write to the Irish presbytery to have him sent back** — a second cross-channel
  discipline pursuit running in parallel. (This helps disambiguate the earlier "fugitive to Ireland"
  entries: there are **two** — Colbie/murder and Pirie/adultery.) A named parishioner (the sackcloth
  penitent) and Pirie for the Inch page.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `the remarkable passages of the late Worke of god in this
  kingdome` = notable providences of the recent war; `malignants… malignancie` = royalist
  sympathisers and their guilt; `more narrow tryall` = a stricter investigation; `Declaration of the
  commissioune… act of parliament… published… after the forenoons sermon, before blessing` = official
  papers read from the pulpit at the set point in worship; `fugitive out of this kingdome… sent back
  againe` = an offender who fled the realm, to be extradited via the church courts. **Both pages
  faded** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1646.

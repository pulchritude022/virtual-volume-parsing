# CH2/341/1 — Image 24 (folios 23v–24), 6 April 1643 — appeal reasons 7–9

Both pages continue **Power's numbered appeal reasons** (7–9): the charges were kept
**general and obscure** so he could not answer particulars; **witnesses were led only
on what "conduced to the accuser's ends,"** and his requests to re-examine them and
to put his own interrogatories were **refused**; and the presbytery pressed him to
answer **without sight of the process**, aiming to catch him in contradiction with
the deponents by exploiting "the frailty of memories." (The right page's inner margin
runs into the binding — several line-openings are reconstructed.)

### Modern English
[Reason 6/7, cont.:] …so long as he can expostulate and there is any witness in the
world to summon — there being no end of process, and he never helped by the
presbytery — he appeals. **Reason 7:** all accusations ought to be *particular* in
matter and manner, so a defendant can answer; but his accusation is *general*, and
though he supplicated that **Uchtred younger** be put to a formal **condescendance of
particulars**, they accepted from him a paper "not so general, yet as obscure," to
which no answer can be made; so his answers, though true, are met by depositions
resting "on conjectures and presumptions," and some of the particulars are **"old
allegeance" done before the libel**. It should be referred to the Synod. **Reason 8:**
witnesses were received on particulars **not condescended to Power** — so he could
not make the particulars clear with their circumstances, nor know what was omitted so
the witnesses might be interrogated and the truth found — they being examined only on
the [accuser's interrogatories].

### Modern English (paraphrase — low confidence)
[Reason 8, cont.:] the witnesses were **inquired of nothing but what conduced to the
accuser's ends, and the truth wrested thereby**; and when Power asked for **re-
examination** of some witnesses, and that **his own interrogatories** be put to them
with their answers required, this was **slighted and refused** — contrary to all
judicial practice, civil or ecclesiastical; therefore he appeals. **Reason 9:** having
been accused on generals and having demanded particulars, and the witnesses received
on the general libel and interrogated on particulars first by the **accuser's**
interrogatories (a form never used in any court), he hoped the presbytery — after
examining the witnesses for its own information — would **put him to particulars** so
he could answer, and for this asked the **extract of the proceedings** at his own
charges; yet they pressed him with their process, that he answer **without seeing it**,
to whatever the accuser gave in — thereby "making advantage of odious circumstances of
depositions," intending to **bring him into contradiction with the deponents** (hard to
escape, given the frailty of men's memories). Nevertheless, still willing to answer
despite his hazard (lest they pretend disobedience or presume on his unwillingness),
he has given in his answers.

## Notes & context
- **A complete natural-justice brief.** With reasons 7–9 this opening finishes Power's
  appeal (imgs 0022–0024): the charges are **too general/obscure to answer**; witnesses
  were **led on the accuser's interrogatories only**, with re-examination and his own
  cross-interrogatories **refused**; he was denied the **process extract**; and the
  whole design was to **trap him in contradictions** and **wear him down**. It reads
  as an early, articulate demand for the rights we would call *specific charges,
  disclosure, and cross-examination* — remarkable in a 1643 kirk court.
- **"Auld allegeance … since the incidencie of the said libell"** (reason 7): Power
  again objects that some particulars are events *after* the original libel (cf. his
  "posterior" argument in img_0008) — they cannot prove the original charge.
- **Where the case stands:** Power has formally **appealed to the Synod of Galloway**
  (to meet at Stranraer, 3rd Tuesday of April 1643, per img_0022). The presbytery's
  earlier decision to "let the papers lie till they advise with the Synod" now makes
  sense — the venue is shifting upward.
- **Palaeography applied:** neat main-clerk hand, but the **inner (bound) margin of the
  right page is lost**, so line-openings in `[ ]` are reconstructed; `condiscendance
  of particulars` = a formal specification of the charges; `interrogators` =
  interrogatories (written questions to witnesses); `auld allegeance` = old
  matters/claims; `per` = by/according to. Dates "1642" Old Style. `y`+superscript →
  *th-*.

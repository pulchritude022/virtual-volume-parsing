# CH2/341/1 — Image 104 (folios 103v–104), 2–30 December 1646 — a charity for a minister's wife; a Glenluce kirk-moneys visitation; Marion Mure's mutual-denial examination; the year closes

Left page (folio **103a**): (tail of 1 Nov) the Alexander McDowell case recommended to the next
presbytery; the next meeting appointed **2 December** (Mr James Bell to make, Mr Hew [Hutcheson] to add).
Then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 2 December 1646** — absent Mr Alexander Turnbull; an exercise (**Mr James
Bell made, and Mr James Blair added**, 1 Thess. 2:13–14, approved); the **elders of Kirkcolm,
Portmontgomery and Stoneykirk, who had been absent from the Synod and preceding presbytery, gave their
reasons and were excused**; **★ the brethren (Mr James Blair, Mr John Dick, Mr John Livingstone, Mr James
Bell, Mr Hew Hutcheson), whereof one gave a dollar, [collected] for the succour of Mr [—] Lindsay's
wife**; **the presbytery ordained Mr John Livingstone, Mr James Blair, [the laird of] Craigcaffie [and
others] their commissioners to repair to Glenluce on Monday next, and there to take trial touching the
kirk moneys and [business] of the said kirk**; and **Mr John Dick reported that Thomas Morton had begun to
give signs of repentance at Glenluce**. Right page (folio **104**): **★ Marion Mure, being examined,
confessed: (1) that she said she would not [part] from him if he had been able; (2) that she had been
often with him in a chamber, the door locked; (3) [she confessed] he [dallied] with her often — but
denied he ever had carnal dealing with her; the presbytery continued this matter till further trial**;
**★ Jean[?] McDowell in Stoneykirk compeared in sackcloth, confessing adultery with Alexander McDowell,
and was enjoined per order**; the next meeting appointed **30 December**; then **Presbytery at Stranraer,
30 December 1646** — absent but one elder of Stoneykirk and Kirkmaiden; an exercise (Mr James Blair made,
Mr Alexander Turnbull added, 1 Thess. 2:15–16, approved); Mr Alexander Turnbull's former absence excused;
and **the commissioners appointed for the [Glenluce kirk] business reported their diligence** [continued
overleaf].

## Notes & context
- **★ Marion Mure's examination — mutual denial in the Torrane case.** Now the *woman* in the **Torrane
  adultery** (imgs 0094–0103) is examined and, like James Torrane elder (img 0099), gives a **damning-yet-
  guarded confession**: she **admits she was "often with him in a chamber, the door locked"** and even
  (line 1) makes a compromising statement — **but denies "carnal dealing."** With both parties admitting
  the *circumstances* but denying the *act*, and two witnesses (James Torrane younger, Margaret Mean), the
  presbytery **continues the matter for further trial** rather than convicting — a fine illustration of
  the evidentiary threshold for adultery (strong *fama*, no proof). One of the volume's best-documented
  discipline cases.
- **★ The Stoneykirk adultery — Jean & Alexander McDowell.** The **Alexander McDowell** who had appeared
  in sackcloth (imgs 0102–0103) is now paired with **Jean[?] McDowell**, who confesses **adultery with
  him**; both do penance. (The McDowell surname saturates the Rhins — no assumed kinship to French/
  Garthland.)
- **★ Ministerial charity — a dollar for Mr Lindsay's wife.** A humane vignette: **the brethren take up a
  collection among themselves — "one gave a dollar" — for the succour of Mr [—] Lindsay's wife**, a
  distressed minister's family. A small but telling record of clerical mutual aid (and a link to the
  earlier "labour given to Mr [John] Lindsay," img 0102).
- **★ Glenluce kirk moneys under scrutiny.** As the planting of Glenluce advances (the call to Crichton,
  img 0102), a commission (**Livingstone, Blair, Craigcaffie**) goes to Glenluce to **audit the kirk
  moneys** — tightening the financial house before a minister is settled. **Thomas Morton** meanwhile has
  **begun his repentance** there.
- **⚠ Bell and Blair are distinct men (confirmed).** This opening names **"Mr James Bell made, and Mr
  James Blair added"** in the *same* exercise — settling that **Bell ≠ Blair** in this register. That
  sharpens (does not solve) the Kirkcolm question: the minister who preached at the 27 Aug "Kirkcolm"
  visitation (img 0100) really was **Bell**, not Blair — so either Bell serves Kirkcolm, or the visited
  parish/heading needs re-checking. Flagged for Dad.
- **The year closes.** The register reaches **30 December 1646** — completing the 1646 sederunts. 1647
  opens on the next opening.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `being examined, confest… bot Denyed yt he ever had carnall
  dealing… continued… till further tryall` = admitted circumstance, denied the act, case held over;
  `Whereof one Did give a Dollar, for succor of… Lindsay's Wyff` = a voluntary collection for a needy
  minister's wife (a *dollar* = a rix-dollar/thaler coin); `to take tryall touching ye kirk moneyes` = to
  audit the parish funds; `former absence… excused` = attendance accountability. **Both pages heavily
  water-stained** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1646**.

  2. **Jean[?] McDowell** confessing adultery with **Alexander McDowell** (Stoneykirk).
  3. **The dollar for Mr [—] Lindsay's wife**; the **Glenluce kirk-moneys commission** (Livingstone,
     Blair, Craigcaffie).
  4. The **2 Dec & 30 Dec 1646** dates; **Bell vs Blair** as two men; **Thomas Morton** begun repentance.

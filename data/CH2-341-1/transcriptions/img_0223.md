## Notes & context
★★★ **Robert Campbell's testimonial — Mr Andrew Stewart of Donaghadee.** The great hold-out finally submits documentary proof: **Robert Campbell** (the "What! malignancy in me!" defier, imgs 210–222), having promised under his hand, **compears and produces a letter of testimony from Mr Andrew Stewart, minister of Donaghadee** — the noted Ulster-Scots minister (later author of a history of the Church in Ireland). The testimony is markedly **qualified**: it acknowledges Campbell's involvement in "the malignant courses" and his carriage, "notwithstanding plain proof and representations to the contrary," yet stops short of affirming the gravest charge (that he hindered those fleeing to Scotland for relief). This pins the whole Ireland-malignancy dossier back to **Donaghadee** (cf. imgs 209–210) and shows the presbytery weighing a Scots-Ulster minister's careful, hedged certificate.

★ **Garthland, Arisemman, and the New-Year discipline.** The **Laird of Garthland**'s public satisfaction at **Stoneykirk** is delayed by some hindrance, the ordinance renewed (his Stranraer appearance already done, img 222). **Arisemman (Andrew Kennedy)**, at last **recovered of his long sickness** (imgs 213–220), is set to public trial; **Margaret McClounie** faces her **third** admonition; and the **Kennedy adultery** (Morion/Williamson) confrontation is renewed. The sitting of **20 March 1651** keeps the exercise (Scott, Crosse, Dick).

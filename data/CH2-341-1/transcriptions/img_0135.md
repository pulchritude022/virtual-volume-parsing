# CH2/341/1 — Image 135 (folios 134v–135)

Left page (foliated **134a** = folio 134 verso) finishes the **28 April 1648
Commission of the Kirk letter** — subscribed **"Mr Ro[bert] Douglas, moderator"**
(of the Commission of the General Assembly) — and copies its **postscript (dated
Edinburgh, 1 May 1648, also subscribed Robert Douglas)** appointing ★ a **national
fast to be kept in all congregations on the last Sabbath of May 1648**, for
entreating the Lord's mercy in the day of danger. The right page (folio 135)
records the presbytery's obedience (the fast to be intimated next Lord's day and
kept the Sabbath thereafter), then copies ★★ a **further Commission letter (dated
Edinburgh, 5 May 1648)** charging the presbytery to **give no assistance or
countenance to the Engagement** — to send none of their number to attend the
**levies raised for it**, to read or allow no orders or papers advancing it, and
to **preach against its unlawfulness** — all which the brethren "most readily and
willingly promised." The page ends with two local discipline cases from the
**Kirkinner** session: the adultery of **Jonet McGie** and **Marion McWilliam**,
both confessing in sackcloth and enjoined penance.

### Modern English
[Commission letter, concluded:] "...as fellow-labourers, with a single eye with
you in the former work of the Lord, we give you warning of the danger and stir you
up all to be earnest with God in prayer and supplications for your own
stedfastness, and to give a faithful warning unto all with whom you have to do,
against all the snares and temptations of these times; that you may so carry
yourselves in all things that, when the Lord shall be pleased to bring you together
in a General Assembly, all of you may come furnished with the spirit of [meekness],
unity and zeal, that the boasting of adversaries may be put to silence... So [prays]
your affectionate brethren, the Commission of the General Assembly. — **Mr Robert
Douglas, moderator.**"

There was also added to the former letter a **postscript, dated Edinburgh, 1 May
1648**: *"It is evident from the premises how great and many difficulties the kirk
of God and the cause is brought under, by reason of the guilt and [defection] of
the people, and their revolting from the cause of God to which many are come; and
the cause we have to fear [the loss] of all that God has done for us — [therefore
we think fit], that a **fast be kept in all the congregations of the land, the last
Sabbath of May**, for entreating the Lord for mercy in the day of our danger... We
doubt not but you will be careful that [some] of your number keep the next general
meeting [of] the commissioners, the last [—] day of May."* — Subscribed **Mr
Robert Douglas, moderator.**

### Modern English
The presbytery, having taken their letter to consideration, were humbly glad that
the Lord did so work on the hearts of [the commissioners], giving them wisdom and
courage to go on notwithstanding the many difficulties in their way; and heartily
recommended it to the several brethren to be earnest with God on their behalf; and
in obedience to the postscript, appoint **the fast to be intimated the next Lord's
day** in the several congregations of the presbytery, and kept the Lord's day
thereafter.

There was **another letter from the Commissioners of the Kirk, dated Edinburgh, 5
May 1648**, presented to the presbytery, desiring and charging them — every one of
the brethren — to **withdraw themselves from giving any assistance or countenance
in any way to the proceedings in the Engagement** and its levies; and desiring the
presbytery to **send none of their number to attend the [levies] raised for the
Engagement**, and that none read or allow any to read any order or papers
concerning the advancement of the present Engagement... The presbytery strongly
recommended to the brethren that they should walk according to the directions of
the Commissioners of the Kirk, and be so far from any way countenancing the present
Engagement that, on the contrary, they would be free both in preaching and
[otherwise] to show the unlawfulness of the present Engagement; which all the
brethren most readily and willingly promised to do. The informations were
delivered by the Moderator to the several brethren, and appointed to be read the
next Sabbath.

The same day, **Jonet McGie**, guilty of adultery with **Marion McWilliam**,
referred by the session of **Kirkinner**, was called; she confessed her adultery
with the said Marion McWilliam, was formerly rebuked for the same, and was
appointed to give signs of repentance in sackcloth in the kirk of Kirkinner until
the session of that kirk be satisfied.

**Marion McWilliam**, being called, compeared in sackcloth and confessed her
adultery [in the same matter]; she was formerly rebuked and appointed to give
signs of repentance at **[Kirkmadrine?], Portmontgomery, Leswalt**, and [Kirkcolm?]
until the session of the said kirk be satisfied concerning her repentance.

## Notes & context
- ★★ **The Kirk's non-cooperation with the Engagement, at parish level.** The 5
  May 1648 Commission letter is the operative order: ministers are **not to attend
  the military levies** raised for the Engagement, **not to read or countenance any
  Engagement papers**, and are to **preach its unlawfulness**. The Stranraer
  presbytery's brethren "most readily and willingly" promise. This is the local
  enactment of the Kirk–Engager split that culminated at Preston (Aug 1648).
- ★ **National fast, last Sabbath of May 1648** (postscript of 1 May), for mercy
  "in the day of our danger" — a datable liturgical event to anchor in the wiki
  timeline.
- ★ **Robert Douglas** confirmed as moderator of the Commission of the General
  Assembly, subscribing both the letter and its postscript.
- ★ **Discipline cases (Kirkinner):** **Jonet McGie** and **Marion McWilliam**,
  both "guilty of adultery" and processed together (the shared male partner is not
  named in this act); Jonet's penance at Kirkinner, Marion's spread across several
  Rhins parishes ([Kirkmadrine?], Portmontgomery, Leswalt, [Kirkcolm?]), implying a
  notorious/multi-parish scandal.
- ✦ **Place-name resolution:** the recurring Rhins kirk rendered "[Clanrie?]" /
  "[Chirmarin?]" in imgs 130–133 appears here in a clean parish list beside
  Portmontgomery, Leswalt and Kirkcolm, and now reads more plausibly as
  **Kirkmadrine** (the Rhins parish adjacent to Kirkmaiden). Provisionally adopting
  "[Kirkmadrine?]"; still to be confirmed.

- Lowest-confidence readings (Dad):

     male partner (the text has "hir adulterie" for both, suggesting two women).
  2. **"[Kirkmadrine?]"** — the recurring Rhins kirk (see resolution note above);
     and the parish list for Marion's penance.

     Engagement" (faded).

     meeting of the commissioners).

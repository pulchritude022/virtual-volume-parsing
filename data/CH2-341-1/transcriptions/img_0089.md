# CH2/341/1 — Image 89 (folios 88v–89), February 1646 — the witch processes handed to Garthland; the Glenluce planting negotiated; Stoneykirk's ruinous kirk

Left page (folio **88a**): the **malignants** business — commissioners to put **the acts of Parliament
against malignants in execution** and report their diligence; the **parishioners of Glenluce petition
again for "some able minister to break them bread" for their desolate congregation**, so the
presbytery appoints **the laird of Garthland, Mr John Livingstone, and Mr James Blair to treat with the
Glenluce gentry about planting the kirk** (and the stipend valuation); **★ the moderator is ordained
to deliver the processes against the witches to the laird of Garthland** (touching the particular
action of **[—] Cochran**), that it may be prosecuted; and **John Cloud in Kirkmaiden compeared in
sackcloth, confessing adultery.** Right page (folio **89**): **John Cloud sentenced to stand half a
year in sackcloth in every one of the parish churches, barefoot and bareheaded at the most open church
door** (Turnbull to proceed toward excommunication if he fails); **John McDowell and Effie Adair in
Inch each confess mutual adultery** and are enjoined per the former order; **a contribution to be
uplifted for Mr Robert McClellan**; **★ the presbytery, considering the ruinous/decayed state of the
kirk of Stoneykirk, appoints Livingstone and Mr John McDowell of Brochtoun to speak the Stoneykirk
parishioners about repairing it**; and the **next meeting appointed for 18 March.**

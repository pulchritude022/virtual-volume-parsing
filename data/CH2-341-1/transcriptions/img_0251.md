## Notes & context
★★ **Turnbull's Protester dissent renewed — and the presbytery reacts to "a declaration emitted by the English."** Two nationally significant threads run through the badly-damaged left folio. First, the **Resolutioner–Protester split** (imgs 247–248) is minuted again: the presbytery's **ordinance approving the late General Assembly is renewed, and Mr Alexr Turnbull once more "dissents and protests, in his own name and in the name of the absent brethren"** — consideration delayed to the next meeting. Second, and new, the brethren respond to **"a declaration emitted by the English concerning the kingdom," finding it of "great prejudice" to the ministry, and appoint the ministers to warn their people** — almost certainly the **Cromwellian incorporating union / Tender of Union** pressed on Scotland in early 1652 (the English commissioners at Dalkeith). A rare glimpse of the Rhins clergy reacting to the English conquest and union directly.

★ **The Dunbar-prisoners contribution wound up; a new sitting under Bell.** The **David Kennedie / Dunbar relief** accounting (imgs 243, 249–250) reaches a committee stage — with **Captain David Kennedie** appearing where the earlier commissioner "David" is absent, and Dick/Park/Lawrie appointed to settle it. **Robert Campbell has satisfied.** Then a fresh sitting opens (**8 [March] 1652, under moderator Mr James Bell**, img 242), with the note "no doctrine that day, it not being ordinary," and business about the **commissioner of the Laird of [Dalveen?]** — but the right folio is among the worst-bled in the volume and mostly conjectural below its first third.

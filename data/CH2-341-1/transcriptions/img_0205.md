## Notes & context
★★★ **A thousand merks for the ministry's Regiment of Horse — with a receipt, verbatim.** The financial spine of the 1650 mobilisation surfaces in hard cash: **the Presbytery of Stranraer advanced a thousand merks Scots toward "the outputting of the Regiment of Horse willingly offered by the ministry of the kingdom,"** and the **discharge (receipt) is copied into the register** — "Received … a thousand merks Scots for the proportion of the levy of a horse regiment agreed upon by the General Assembly," tied to **Colonel [Archibald] Strachan's horse-regiment** (the officer who had beaten Montrose at Carbisdale), witnessed by the three ministers **Dick, Park, and Crosse** and **subscribed by Mr James Hamilton at Edinburgh, 29 July 1650**. A remarkably concrete document of the Kirk actually *funding* a cavalry regiment for the campaign against Cromwell. **Mr John Scott is "at the army"** (the chaplaincy of img 204), and another minister-Engager (**Lt-Col McDowall**) renews his Covenant on a sickbed.

★★ **"Assured … to lift victual for George Munro's army" — a malignancy supplication.** Building on the General Assembly's line about "malignants lately from Ireland" (img 204), **Alexr Parker supplicates**, pleading that he had been **"assured [given protection], through the favour of certain forces, to lift victual off the country for furnishing the army under the command of George Munro in the north parts of Scotland"** — a defence of collaboration with **General George Munro's (Engagement/royalist) forces**. A vivid glimpse of how ordinary men were caught between armies. (Continues img 206.)

★ **The duel and the adultery.** **William Broune, back from Ireland, confesses carrying the challenge (cartel) from George Montgomerie to Gilbert Kennedy** — set to confess at Stranraer (closing the duel case of imgs 185–196). And the **new adultery scandal against Gilbert Kennedy of Aviolen** (img 203) is worked up: **Margaret Wilson** "followed Gilbert to Edinburgh" then vanished, and **Mr John Dick is to try the [other] women "bruited" with Kennedy in Inch** — the duellist's reputation collapsing into a sexual scandal.

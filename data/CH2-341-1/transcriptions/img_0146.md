# CH2/341/1 — Image 146 (folios 145v–146)

The heart of the **post-Engagement purge**: a detailed, numbered **register of named
local men reported to the Committee as malignants/Engagers**, which the presbytery's
commissioners (Turnbull, Lowrie, Dick, Crosse) laid before the shire Committee at
Stranraer. Because the parchment is faded (left page especially), the readings are
partly for sense — but the **roll of names is legible and historically valuable**:
★★ **Ardwell** (a committee-man who "dealt for a place" in the Engagement, giving
**money to Major McDougall** for office); **[Gordon of] Earlston**, **[MacDowall of]
Garthland**, and **Sir John Hamilton**; **my Lord Galloway** and an **Agnew**
colonel; ★ **James [—]** who took great sums of money from the tenants for the
Engagement *notwithstanding the Earl of Cassillis wrote to him not to raise a man*;
a man who took a **protection from Duke Hamilton**; ★ **Craiglaw**, who took a
**protection from [the Earl of] Callendar** and dealt with **Garthland to be his
lieutenant-colonel**; and ★ **Thomas Stewart**, who was in all the committees last
summer and **hired men for the Engagement** in the towns of Wigtown and others. The
commissioners are appointed to represent all these particulars to the Committee, to
desire it to try and censure them, and the presbytery to censure ecclesiastically
per the Commission of the Kirk.

## Notes & context
- ★★ **A local register of the 1648 Engagers.** This opening is one of the most
  historically valuable in the volume: a concrete, name-by-name account of how the
  **Engagement was raised in Wigtownshire** and who was implicated — a grassroots
  companion to the national Act of Classes (1649). It names **Alexander McCulloch of
  Ardwell** (buying office via **Major McDougall**), the Covenanting-turned-suspect
  house of **Gordon of Earlston**, **MacDowall of Garthland**, **Gordon of
  Craiglaw**, **Sir John Hamilton**, **Thomas Stewart**, and **James [—]** — set
  against the anti-Engagement magnates **the Earl of Cassillis** and (as informant)
  **the Earl of Galloway**.
- ★★ **Ardwell as an active Engager.** Beyond the adultery scandal that has run since
  img 128, Ardwell is here accused of a **political** offence: sitting on the
  committee, administering its acts, and **buying a military "place" with money paid
  to Major McDougall**. This adds a whole political dimension to his person-page and
  helps explain the presbytery's persistence against him.
- ★ **"Protections" from Engagement leaders.** Two men are reported to have taken
  **protections** — one from **Duke Hamilton** (James Hamilton, 1st Duke, commander of
  the Engager army destroyed at Preston), **Craiglaw** from **the Earl of Callendar**
  (the Engager lieutenant-general). A "protection" was a safe-conduct/immunity, so
  taking one was itself evidence of siding with the Engagement.
- ★ **Cassillis vs the local raisers.** **James [—]** raised men and money for the
  Engagement from his tenants **despite a direct letter from the Earl of Cassillis**
  (a leading Kirk-party Covenanter) forbidding it — a sharp illustration of how the
  Engagement split even the Covenanting interest at the local level.
- **Procedure:** the presbytery hands the roll to the **shire Committee** for civil
  trial/censure and reserves **ecclesiastical censure** to itself under the Commission
  of the Kirk — the twin-track (civil + church) purge in action.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 123 (folios 122v–123), 10 November 1647 — the Old-Glenluce gentry supplicate for a clean presentation to Mr John Scott; the presbytery answers each article; and the state of the kirk of Stranraer

Left page (folio **122a**): (Commission letter, continued) **as for the first three particulars of their
desire, the moderator entreated the brethren that they would be mindful of the same; as for the last, there
is none known within the bounds of this presbytery to be Malignants or [compliers], as hath been [shown] to
the Assembly and Commission before; the papers delivered to the moderator were given to the brethren,
appointed to make use of the same according to the directions set down in the said papers; as for the fast
and thanksgiving, it had been already [kept]**; **★ the said day a letter was presented by Mr John
Livingstone from the presbytery of Ireland, and read; unto which Mr Alexander [Turnbull] was appointed to
write an answer, many particulars of the letter being related to and written by Mr Alexander in the
presbytery's name, the copy whereof was in his custody**; **★ the same day the Laird of Garthland declared
that he had been [acquainted] with Mr James Lowrie, a very gracious young man, [wishing him] to come to [the
bounds], and desired that the presbytery would [join] with [him] in anything to him; it is appointed that
the moderator shall write to the foresaid Mr James for the effect foresaid**; then **★★ the same day there
was a supplication given in [by] many of the gentry of Glenluce, the tenor whereof follows: "The humble
supplication of the gentlemen and gentry of the OLD parish of Glenluce, unto the Reverend Moderator and
remanent Brethren of the presbytery of Stranraer — it is our humble desire, if it would please your wisdoms,
to grant and subscribe our presentation to Mr John Scott, of the right of the gift of mortification of a
thousand merks, with the Abbey for his manse and glebe; and that without any provisos or restrictions any
way prejudicial to the settling and confirming of the said stipend to the said Mr John Scott and to his
successors serving the cure at the said kirk, after his [decease]…"**. Right page (folio **123**):
(supplication, continued) **"…3. it is our humble desire that it would please you to write a letter to the
said Mr John Scott, desiring him to be [content], and to come and accept the presentation; 4. it is our
[humble] desire, seeing our said Old kirk of Glenluce [is destitute], to cause supply [it, a minister] to
preach once a fortnight at least at the said kirk, till God [provide]…"**; then **★ to their supplication the
presbytery answered as followeth — To the first article: that there were no restrictions prejudicial to Mr
John Scott in that presentation, [but] that they did offer to give…; and as there were many weighty
considerations moving the presbytery to put in the act of the General Assembly holden at Edinburgh, June
1646, bearing date June 17 (which was also ratified in Parliament), [so that] the supplicants saw no
prejudice in the restrictions; … [to the second article,] the presbytery did answer that seeing Mr John
Scott had already [approved] of that presentation… they shall [draw] up and subscribe [it]; … [to the third
article,] they appoint the moderator to write to the said Mr John; and for answer to the fourth [article],
they appoint Mr Hew Hutcheson to preach at [Glenluce] till the next Sabbath**; then **★ the said day the
presbytery, taking to their consideration the condition of the kirk of Stranraer — that it hath not a
settled [stipend], the said Mr John Livingstone['s charge] being only by transaction bestowed by the Earl of
Cassillis and the town of Stranraer… and taking also to their consideration that act by which [James Ross of
Balneil] did already oblige himself… for obtaining their arrears…** [continued overleaf].

## Notes & context
- **★★ The gentry push back for a clean presentation.** A revealing sequel to Scott's protestation (img
  0120): **the gentlemen and gentry of the OLD parish of Glenluce petition the presbytery, in an engrossed
  supplication, to grant and subscribe the presentation to Mr John Scott "without any provisos or
  restrictions any way prejudicial to the settling and confirming of the stipend"** — i.e. the parish's
  landowners side with their prospective minister and want the contested clauses dropped, so his living (the
  1,000-merk mortification + the Abbey for manse and glebe) is secure for him and his successors. The
  supplication runs to four articles (subscribe the presentation; secure it against restrictions; write to
  Scott to come and accept; supply the vacant kirk meanwhile). This is the community itself intervening to
  land its minister — good colour for the Glenluce and Scott nodes.
- **★ The presbytery holds the line — but softens.** The **article-by-article answer** defends the
  restrictions as flowing from the binding **GA act of 17 June 1646 (ratified in Parliament)** — the court
  cannot simply strike them — yet it **agrees to write to Scott urging him to accept, and appoints Hutcheson
  to supply the vacant kirk.** A neat picture of a church court balancing legal constraint against pastoral
  pragmatism.
- **★ "None known to be Malignants."** In answering the Commission's remonstrance (img 0122), the presbytery
  reports **"there is none known within the bounds of this presbytery to be Malignants or compliers"** — the
  Rhins declaring itself clean of royalist sympathisers in the 1647 purge climate. Notable for the political
  temperature of the district.
- **★ A candidate on the horizon.** **The Laird of Garthland recommends "Mr James Lowrie, a very gracious
  young man,"** and the moderator is to write to him — a probable future minister entering the record (worth
  a placeholder person-node).
- **The kirk of Stranraer unsettled.** The court turns to **the condition of the kirk of Stranraer itself:
  it "hath not a settled stipend," Livingstone's charge resting only on a transaction "bestowed by the Earl
  of Cassillis and the town of Stranraer"** — the temporal insecurity of even the presbytery seat's own
  ministry (and the backdrop to the Glasgow call for Livingstone, img 0113). James Ross of Balneil recurs re
  the arrears.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `grant and subscryve our presentatioun to Mr Johne Scott… Without any
  provisoes or restrictiones… prejudiciall to ye settling… of ye stipend` = a parish petition for an
  unencumbered presentation; `none knowen… to be Malignants or compliers` = declaring the district free of
  royalist sympathisers; `ratified in parliament… ye act of ye generall assemblie… bearing Date Junii 17
  [1646]` = the binding statutory basis of the division; `hath not a settled stipend… only by transactioun
  bestowed by ye Earl of Cassillis and toune of Stranrawer` = a ministry funded by private arrangement
  rather than a fixed stipend. Firm hand on the left, faded on the right — the supplication's later clauses,
  the Stranraer paragraph, and Lowrie's name are provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1647**.

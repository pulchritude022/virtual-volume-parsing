## Notes & context
★★★ **Three national fasts — "for the sins of the king's family" and "for the sins of the whole land."** The presbytery relays the Commission of the Kirk's appointment of **three solemn fasts**: one for "our contempt of the Gospel," and two intimated a fortnight later — **"for the sins of the king's family"** and **"for the sins of the whole land."** In the spring of 1651, with **Charles II** crowned at Scone (1 Jan 1651) and the Kirk uneasily allied to a king whose court it distrusted, a public fast for the *sins of the king's family* is a pointed Covenanter act. The brethren are also to read **the Act against compliance with the Sectarians** — the "Sectarians" being Cromwell's Independent army now occupying the south-west — squarely dating the page to the occupation.

★★ **Inch fights the transportation of Mr John Dick — a formal remonstrance.** Following img 227–228, the **heritors and parishioners of Inch "again renew their supplication and remonstrance, that notwithstanding the act of transportation"** they might keep **Mr John Dick** as their minister; the presbytery sends them to confer with Dick directly. A congregation actively resisting the removal of its minister — the case is now a live tug-of-war between Inch and whatever charge is calling Dick away.

★ **Discipline under the occupation.** The 21 May sitting keeps the exercise (Dick, Lawrie, Park), proceeds against a **Campbell** to the "first step of process," examines **Mr John Baillie of Dunragit** as a witness (probably in the long-running Thomas Baillie robbery, imgs 194–217), and notes **Margaret Morion** "sometime forborne to compear."

- **Lowest-confidence readings (Dad):**
  - **The lower half of folio 229 is badly water-stained and largely illegible** — the Campbell, Dunragit, and Margaret Morion items are given only in outline; verify against the original.
  - **"[John] Campbell … first step of process"** — the margin reads "Jo: Camp:", but John Campbell had been "fled the kingdom" (imgs 212–222); either he has returned or this is a different Campbell — verify.
  - **The three fasts and "the Act against compliance with the Sectarians"** are the priority readings on folio 228a and moderately secure, though stained.
  - The 21 May **sederunt** names (ruling elders, commissioners for the new kirk of Glenluce, "Wilson messenger") are heavily faded and largely conjectural.

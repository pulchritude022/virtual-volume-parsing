# CH2/341/1 — Image 161 (folios 160v–161)

The **ministers' own testimony closes the Balneil case in his favour.** Left page
(foliated **160a** = folio 160 verso): the presbytery, noting that on art. 2 Balneil
had referred himself "to the act of the ministry, with whom he had been most present
and intire," asks the brethren to declare what they know of his affection or
disaffection to the Engagement. ★★ **Mr James Blair deposes: "The first day the
gentlemen of the shire were bound at Glenluce, by a motion of Argyll's, I saw Balneil
subscribe the National Covenant"** — and recalls that at ★ **the marriage of Balneil's
daughter to Major McDougall**, walking in the garden after dinner, **Balneil affirmed
to him that the Engagement was unlawful**. The right page (folio 161): ★ **Mr John
Crosse and Mr John Scott corroborate** — Balneil "expressed his dislike of the
Engagement," acted (so far as he conformed) "for fear of the shire" and "not out of
any good affection" to it; and on art. 9 the minister **will not say Balneil was a
spreader of civil news**. Taken with the session, the committee, and the lay witnesses
(imgs 155–160), Balneil is now cleared on every front.

## Notes & context
- ★★ **The ministers vouch for Balneil.** The decisive corroboration: **Blair** saw Balneil
  **subscribe the National Covenant** and heard him call the Engagement **unlawful** in
  private; **Crosse** heard him **express dislike of the Engagement** ("not the glory of God
  nor the good of religion"); **Scott** says any compliance was **"for fear of the shire,
  not out of any good affection,"** and refuses to call him a spreader of civil news. After
  the session ("ingenuously declared the truth"), the committee ("nothing to say against
  him"), and the lay witnesses (img 160), the clergy's own testimony completes Balneil's
  exoneration — a fully documented acquittal.
- ★★ **"The gentlemen of the shire were bound at Glenluce, by a motion of Argyll's."** A
  concrete political datum: a **subscription/binding of the Wigtownshire gentry to the
  National Covenant at Glenluce, prompted by the Marquess of Argyll** — the Kirk party's
  loyalty test in the county, with Balneil signing. Worth an event/reference note.
- ★ **Genealogy: Balneil's daughter married Major McDougall.** A family link surfaces in
  passing — **James Ross of Balneil's daughter wed Major McDougall** (the Engager officer of
  imgs 146/160). A prime fact for both person-pages and exactly the kind of biographical
  detail the project is meant to consolidate.
- ★ **"For fear of the shire, not out of good affection."** Scott's phrase crystallises the
  defence that recurs across these examinations — men complying under social/military
  pressure rather than conviction — and is a useful lens on the whole county purge.

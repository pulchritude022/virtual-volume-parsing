# CH2/341/1 — Image 155 (folios 154v–155)

The **close of the Balneil dossier**. Left page (foliated **154a** = folio 154
verso), badly faded: Balneil finishes ★ **article 7** (the only "civil novel" he owns
is talk of the English army) and answers ★★ **article 8 (disaffection to the cause of
God)** by protesting his forwardness in "all the former engagements" — describing the
★ **Engagement rendezvous at Craignarget for the soldiers of Glenluce and Mochrum**,
and how **Colonel Agnew / Airieman** pressed one of his tenants to be a soldier; and
begins ★ **article 9** (the alleged "miner" he is charged with prosecuting). The right
page (folio 155) finishes article 9 — the man offered himself as a tenant to the
**laird of Craiglaw** and went to the ★ **rendezvous at Portmontgomery**; Balneil says
he acted **only for his tenants and himself, not to advance the Engagement** — and
records the ★★ **Session's verdict**: having heard his answers and removed him, the
elders, each particularly enquired, declared that **so far as they knew he had
ingenuously declared the truth**.

## Notes & context
- ★★ **The Session finds for Balneil (in part).** After the full charge (imgs 152–153)
  and answer (imgs 153–155), the elders' verdict is notably lenient: **so far as they
  know, he "ingenuously declared the truth."** The whole Balneil sequence thus documents
  a heritor substantially clearing himself at the parish level — a counterweight to the
  register of committed Engagers (imgs 146–147) and a key outcome for his person-page.
- ★ **Engagement musters localised.** Two named rendezvous surface: **Craignarget** (for
  the soldiers of Glenluce and Mochrum) and **Portmontgomery** — concrete geography of
  how the 1648 levy was raised in the Rhins/Machars, useful for place-pages.
- ★ **Balneil's exculpatory narrative.** His defence is that he delivered only a few men,
  protected his (and my Lord Galloway's) tenants from being pressed, and even referred a
  reluctant man to the Wigtown authorities — arguing his conduct proves *dis*affection to
  the Engagement. Whether sincere or self-serving, it is a rich first-person account of a
  laird navigating the levy.
- **Cast cross-links:** **Colonel Agnew / Airieman** (pressing a tenant), the **laird of
  Craiglaw** (taking a man as tenant), and the **Earl of Galloway** (whose tenants Balneil
  shields) — the same names as the Engager register, now seen from Balneil's side.

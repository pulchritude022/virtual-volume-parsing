# CH2/341/1 — Image 67 (folios 66v–67), 30 December 1644 — **Power's own terms for a colleague; the heritors' answer**

A rare, detailed pair of documents: **Mr Gilbert Power's own written conditions for accepting a
colleague/helper in his old age**, and **the heritors of Stoneykirk's answer** — together a full
picture of how a collegiate charge was negotiated for an aged, restored minister, down to the
merks and the glebe.

Left page (folio **66a**): the presbytery's plan (the heritors to secure **£/500 merks yearly**
for Power's lifetime, a helper provided from the stipend; **Livingstone to deal with the heritors,
Blair with Power**; a diet 30 Dec); then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 30 December 1644**, where
**Blair presents a paper in Power's name** — **Power's conditions**: (1) that he be **paid all his
restand [arrears] stipend and secured**; (2) that the **parishioners rejoin him, repair the decayed
kirk, and assist in discipline**; (3) that he **peaceably dwell out his years unmolested**; (4)
that provision be **secured after his death** [for wife/heirs] from the annual. Right page (folio
**67**): Power's further conditions (5–6: the heritors to **live in love** with him; **till a helper
is provided he keeps his whole stipend**) and his **generous offer** — to assign **300 merks yearly
plus the glebe of Kirkmadrine** toward the helper, **keeping 500 merks** for himself (paid yearly
at two terms); then **the heritors of Stoneykirk's answer paper**, agreeing point by point (pay
arrears, join with him, repair the kirk, secure the glebe and annual, provide the 500 merks).

### Modern English
[Power's paper, cont.:] *"(5) That the heritors will live in love and deal with me as becomes
parishioners. (6) That, till a helper be provided, I may enjoy my whole stipend."* **Upon these
conditions Power liberally offers**, without conditional entreaty: **to assign 300 merks of his
stipend yearly, together with the glebe of Kirkmadrine, toward the helper's maintenance**, and to
pay the minister's teind; and, before his decease, on sufficient security, to advise; **granting to
assign his whole stipend for 500 merks, to be paid him yearly at two terms.**

**That day the heritors of Stoneykirk gave in a paper in answer**, point by point: **(1)** none of
the parishioners indebted to him but shall gladly pay him (or their bailies), and for their own
land shall bear between them the annual payment; **(2)** when he is enabled to discharge the
ministry [with a helper], they will join with him in everything requisite, and **repair the kirk**;
**(3)** he shall have [security] for his own payment; **(4)** what can be demanded of them in law
or equity, touching **securing the glebe and the annual, they shall not be deficient**, so far as
to content him for **500 merks**…

## Notes & context
- **★ Power's own terms — a second first-person Power document (his old age).** After his letter to
  Garthland (imgs 0050–0051), this is Power's **second sustained first-person text**, and the most
  humane: an **aged minister's conditions for accepting a colleague** — *pay my arrears and secure
  me; let the people rejoin me, repair the kirk, and assist my discipline; let me dwell out my years
  unmolested; provide after my death; let the heritors live in love with me; till the helper comes,
  leave me my whole stipend.* His tone is generous and conciliatory ("for the love I owe to my Lord
  and the brethren… a helper that will not govern me, but have a respect for the flock and me"). This
  is **the emotional and biographical capstone of the Power story**: vindicated, restored, and now
  arranging the end of his ministry with dignity. Quote at length on his person-page. See
  [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]].
- **The money, in detail.** A concrete Scottish-stipend picture: **Power keeps 500 merks a year
  (paid at two terms)**, and **assigns 300 merks + the glebe of Kirkmadrine** toward the helper's
  maintenance; the **heritors** undertake his arrears, the kirk repair, and securing **the glebe and
  the annualrent**. Useful for the economics of a **collegiate charge** and for a glossary node on
  **stipend / merks / glebe / annualrent / teind.**
- **The glebe of Kirkmadrine.** The **glebe of Kirkmadrine** — Kirkmadrine in the Rhins (Stoneykirk
  parish) is famous for its **early-Christian inscribed stones** (5th–6th c.), among the oldest
  Christian monuments in Scotland. Its appearance here as **glebe land of Stoneykirk** is a lovely
  deep-time detail; a place node worth enriching. (Confirm the reading "Kirkmadrine.")
- **The parish reconciled to Power.** The **heritors' answer** — agreeing to pay, join, repair, and
  secure — shows Stoneykirk **reconciling with the minister it once helped depose.** The breach that
  needed the Earl of Cassilis to mediate (img 0062) is closing; the parish now provides for its old
  minister. A satisfying resolution to the parish-vs-minister conflict.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `restand stipend` = arrears of stipend (still owing); `outred`
  = provided/furnished; `gleib` = the glebe (the minister's parcel of land); `annual`/`annualrent` =
  an annual payment/interest charge; `teind` = tithe; `merks` = the merk (2/3 of a pound Scots);
  `at tuo termes` = paid at the two term-days (Whitsun and Martinmas); `heritors` = the landowners.
  Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1644.

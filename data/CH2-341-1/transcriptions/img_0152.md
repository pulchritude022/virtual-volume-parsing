# CH2/341/1 — Image 152 (folios 151v–152)

The **Session of old Glenluce's roll of examined heritors continues** (the paper of
12 December 1648, read to the presbytery on the 15th) — a remarkable **parish-by-name
audit of who did what in the 1648 Engagement**. Though faded, the roster is legible in
outline: a **Colonel [—]** (his tenants put forth a man, a letter written to get a man
to go "for £40"); ★ **Michael Dinragot** (put forth men and goods "as others did," and
wanted the Parliament's observations read as well as the Assembly's declarations);
**How [Hugh] Ronald(s) of Airieman** (dealt with **Colonel James Agnew**, offered to be
his lieutenant-colonel, paid his proportions); ★ **Patrick McKie** (put forth no men
but **paid £15 for them**, and was employed by **Garthland** to collect the money);
**Alexander Hanna** (put forth the men; a **James McConochie** thread); ★ **Litle
Dinragot** (wished to be a captain in **Garthland's regiment** but **Mr John Dick
forbade him**); and finally ★★ **James Ross of Balneil**, charged by the minister with
several **points of malignancy** — beginning with taking a **commission at Michaelmas
to the Parliament**. Names recur from the Engager-register of imgs 146–147 (Agnew,
Garthland, Balneil, Airieman), now anchored to the parish that examined them.

## Notes & context
- ★★ **A named parish roll of Engagement participation.** This is the fullest local
  prosopography of the whole purge sequence: the Glenluce session's declarations of
  each heritor's role — putting forth men and "proportions," collecting money, seeking
  captaincies in **Garthland's regiment**, dealing with **Colonel James Agnew**. For
  Stage 3/4, nearly every line here is a person-page fact (**Patrick McKie** paid £15;
  **Litle Dinragot** blocked from a captaincy by **Mr John Dick**; **Airieman**'s
  lieutenant-colonelcy offer under Agnew; **Balneil**'s Michaelmas commission).
- ★ **Colonel James Agnew surfaces by name.** The "Agnew colonel" of img 146 is here
  confirmed as **Colonel James Agnew**, with **Airieman** offering to serve as his
  lieutenant-colonel — tying the register (146–147) to the parish examinations (151–152).
- ★ **Garthland's regiment as the local Engager unit.** Multiple men are placed in
  relation to **Garthland's regiment** (McKie collecting its money; Dinragot seeking a
  captaincy) — establishing Garthland (MacDowall) as the county's Engager colonel.
- ★ **Balneil charged at last.** After his due-process supplication (img 148), **James
  Ross of Balneil** is now formally **charged by the minister** with points of
  malignancy (a Michaelmas commission to Parliament first) — the charges likely
  continue on the next opening.
- **Money detail:** concrete sums — **£15** (McKie for men), **£40** (to get a man to
  go), "a mark" (to the captains) — useful economic texture.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 62 (folios 61v–62), Sept 1644 — the reposition act concluded; Leswalt visitation; a public-penance case

Left page (folio **61a**): the **conclusion of the General Assembly's act reponing Power** (from
img 0061) — it orders that the deposition sentence be treated **"as if it had never been given,"**
appoints **Mr James Bonar (minister at Maybole?), with the Clerk of Stranraer, to make public
intimation** of the sentence and reposition, **earnestly desires the Earl of Cassilis to help
make peace** between Power and his parishioners, and **ordains the act inserted in the books of
the Synod of Galloway and the presbytery of Stranraer** and extracted from the Assembly's books;
**subscribed "A. Ker"** (the Assembly clerk). Right page (folio **62**): the **Visitation of the
kirk of Leswalt, 4 September 1644** — **Mr Hew Hutcheson (minister of Leswalt) approved** (elders
and minister examined; family exercise urged); **Mr James Bell admonished for absence**; and a
**public-penance case** — **Helen Bertoun(?) of Stranraer**, disobedient to the session, ordered
to **abstain from Anna [—]'s company under pain of malediction** and to make **public repentance
in sackcloth** until true signs of repentance appear; a Leswalt disobedience case cited to 5 Sept.

## Notes & context
- **The reposition finalised — and peace to be brokered by the Earl of Cassilis.** The tail of
  the **General Assembly's act reponing Power** (img 0061) is pure vindication: the deposition is
  to be treated **"as if it had never been given"**; **public intimation** of Power's restoration
  is ordered (by **Mr James Bonar, minister at Maybole?**, with the Stranraer clerk); the act is
  to be **registered in the Synod-of-Galloway and Stranraer-presbytery books**; and — strikingly —
  the Assembly **"earnestly desires the Earl of Cassilis to make true peace between Mr Gilbert and
  his parishioners."** The **Earl of Cassilis** (John Kennedy, 6th Earl, a leading Covenanter
  magnate) is brought in as **peacemaker** at Stoneykirk — a measure of how bitter and public the
  breach was. Subscribed by the Assembly clerk **A. Ker.** This seals the reframing: **Power won,
  and the national church actively worked to reinstate and reconcile him.** See
  [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]].
- **Hew Hutcheson = minister of Leswalt.** The 4-Sept visitation examines and approves **Mr Hew
  Hutcheson as minister of Leswalt** (his own parish) — another firm parish-minister
  identification (cf. Livingstone→Stranraer, Blair→Kirkcolm). Same visitation form: minister
  preaches, withdraws, elders approve "unanimously," minister then examined on the elders; the
  recurring plea is for **family exercise (household worship).**
- **A public-penance case — the kirk's discipline at ground level.** **Helen Bertoun(?) of
  Stranraer**, for a scandal involving one **Anna [—]**, is ordered to **abstain from her company
  "under pain of God's malediction"** and to **stand in sackcloth in the kirk making public
  repentance** until "true signs of repentance appear." A vivid, human instance of Reformed
  discipline (the sackcloth/public-repentance ritual) — and genealogically useful (two named
  Stranraer women). The exact nature of the scandal is faded; flag.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `repositioun` = reposition/restoration; `as if… had never
  been given` = the void-*ab-initio* effect; `publick intimatioun` = the pulpit announcement of
  the restoration; `Sackcloath` = the penitential garb worn during public repentance; `under the
  pain of Gods maledictioun` = under threat of God's curse (the sanction for defying discipline);
  `family exercise` = household worship; `conversatioun` = manner of life. Capital **W** for
  *w/wh*. Register 1644; act 1642.

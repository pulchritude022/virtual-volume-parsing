### Modern English
… [it was] desired in council that [he apply] himself to serve for the good of the public, according to the said [—]. The committee report follows.

> **At Stranraer, 7 November 1650.** The said day, the presbytery, having received a letter from some of our reverend brethren with the associate forces requiring some of our number to consult — for which, in counsel with the rest of the brethren, they appointed **our brother Mr John Crosse to repair with diligence to Glasgow** for that end, before the end of this instant, and to inform himself of the estate of affairs, and **to represent the grievance** of that [bounds]. And considering that at our appointed meeting with the Presbytery of Wigton no quorum went concerning members to attend, the committee, on the 20th, required the same of us again.

**Presbytery met at Stranraer, 20 November 1650.** All the brethren present except Mr John Crosse; the ruling elders present except the dwellers of the old kirk of Glenluce. Exercise by Mr John Dick on 2 Thess 2:10, Turnbull adding; tried and approven. Mr Thomas Hutcheson to exercise next day (2 Thess 2:9), Mr James Bell second speaker. **James Kennedy excused for absence from the last meeting** …

## Notes & context
★★ **The Western Association chaplaincy pinned down — Mr John Crosse to Glasgow.** The 7 Nov business is entered as a formal report: on a letter from "our reverend brethren with the **associate forces**," the presbytery sends **Mr John Crosse to repair to Glasgow** (the Western Association's centre), **"to inform himself of the estate of affairs and to represent the grievance"** of the bounds. This nails the Rhins presbytery's tie to the **Western Association** (the army behind the October 1650 Western Remonstrance) — ministers dispatched to Glasgow, the committee pressing "again" for men. (Cf. imgs 204, 213.)

★★ **The Ireland-malignancy cases grind toward censure.** The vetting of Ireland-returned men reaches teeth: **John Campbell** (accused of beating Knockeghinnoch to conscript him, imgs 207–212) **"is not yet in this kingdom"** (still fled), and the presbytery **renews the act to enter Maryport's [Adair's] sworn deposition** against him. **Robert Campbell** (the "What! malignancy in me!" defier, img 210) is given one last chance to fetch a testimonial from Ireland, **"failing which … to be suspended from the communion of the Kirk"** for his former disobedience — the first move to actual sentence in these Ulster cases.

★ **The Inch glebe secured.** The **Sheriff has undertaken to ratify Mr John Dick's possession of the glebe**, and Dick is to meet him and **"take instrument on the possession"** — the legal completion of the Inch settlement (imgs 199–213). Also: **George Adestoun** still won't compear; **the Laird of Airieman** (the Arisemman/Airieman line — cf. Andrew Kennedy of Arisemman) appears with a desire (continues next opening).

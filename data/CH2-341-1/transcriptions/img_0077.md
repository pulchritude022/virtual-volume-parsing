# CH2/341/1 — Image 77 (folios 76v–77), late June–July 1645 — ★ the Directory for Public Worship arrives; a relief collection for Dundee; a thanksgiving for victory

Left page (folio **76a**): discipline from the previous sitting — an **incestuous-adultery case**
(a man confesses lying with **Agnes** first in **Lambert McKelvie's** house, another night in [—]
McCulloch's, and two or three nights in her father's house; the brethren find him **guilty of
incestuous adultery** and order both to **sackcloth penance through all the kirks of the presbytery,
beginning at Glenluce**; the woman, **now in England, to be summoned on her return**); and the case
of **Helen Bruton**, found to have **misbehaved to the prejudice of the presbytery and the Stranraer
session** and **remitted to the Stranraer session for censure**, and **ordained not to cohabit with
Anna Dalrymple**; a **visitation of Inch appointed for the 20th.** Right page (folio **77**):
**Presbytery at Stranraer, [2] July 1645** — the arrival of **★ a letter from the commissioners of
the Assembly with a copy of the Directory [for Public Worship] and 8 copies of the acts of the late
Assembly (dated 22 January 1645) enjoining the practice of the Directory — which the presbytery
ordains to be practised**; a **letter and act of estate ordaining a collection for the supply of the
town of Dundee for its losses by the enemy** (every brother to uplift it); and a **letter from the
moderator of the commissioners of the Assembly appointing a thanksgiving for a victory obtained
against the enemy, fought the 29 of June last** — which the brethren report keeping on the Lord's day
immediately before.

### Modern English
**Presbytery at Stranraer, [2] July 1645.** **★ There was presented a letter from the commissioners

## Notes & context
- **★★ The Directory for Public Worship reaches Stranraer (July 1645).** A genuine landmark of
  Scottish church history recorded at parish level: the presbytery receives **"a copy of the Directory
  and 8 copies of the acts of the late Assembly, dated 22 January 1645, enjoining the practice of the
  Directory," and "ordains the practice of the Directory."** This is the **Westminster Directory for
  the Public Worship of God** — approved by the General Assembly on **3 February 1645** and by
  Parliament, replacing John Knox's *Book of Common Order* — being adopted in the Rhins of Galloway.
  A headline glossary/theme node (**the Directory for Public Worship**) and a datable moment of the
  Covenanting reformation of worship touching every kirk in the presbytery.
- **★ Relief for war-torn Dundee.** A **national collection "for the supply of the town of Dundee for
  its losses by the enemy"** — Dundee had been **stormed and plundered by Montrose in April 1645**;
  the kirk organised relief for the sacked burgh across the country, including this corner of Galloway.
  A concrete link between the Rhins and the wider war.
- **★ A thanksgiving for victory (late June 1645).** The Committee of Estates and the commissioners of
  the Assembly appoint **a thanksgiving for a victory "fought the 29 of June last."** In late June
  1645 the Covenanters' good news was **the surrender of Carlisle to the Scottish army (28 June
  1645)** — very likely the victory meant (alternatively news of **Naseby**, 14 June, reaching
  Galloway). Either way, a datable public thanksgiving observed in the parishes — flag the exact
  identification for review.
- **An incestuous-adultery case.** The grim discipline entry — a man **"guilty of incestuous
  adultery"** with **Agnes** (the "incestuous" tag signalling they were within forbidden degrees) —
  sentenced to **sackcloth through every kirk of the presbytery**; the woman pursued **on her return
  from England.** Sober material (handle names with care); note **Lambert McKelvie** and the McCulloch
  house as settings.
- **Helen Bruton & Anna Dalrymple.** **Helen Bruton** remitted to the Stranraer session for repeated
  misbehaviour and **forbidden to cohabit with Anna Dalrymple** — with the earlier entry (img 0070,
  a man ordered to abstain from Anna Dalrymple's company), this hints **Anna Dalrymple** was a woman
  of notorious reputation in Stranraer. Two named women for the Stranraer page (treat with discretion).
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `the Directorie` = the Directory for the Public Worship of
  God (1645); `acts of the late Assemblie, date 1645 Januar 22… Injoyning the practise of the
  Directorie` = the General Assembly's enactment adopting it; `act of estate` = an act of the
  Committee of Estates; `collectioun for the supplie of the towne of Dundie` = a war-relief
  collection; `thanksgiving for the victorie… foughten the 29 of Junii last` = an appointed day of
  public thanksgiving; `incestuous adulterie` = adultery within the forbidden degrees; `stand in
  sackcloth in all the kirks` = perform public penance in every parish. **Left page stained** —
  provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1645.

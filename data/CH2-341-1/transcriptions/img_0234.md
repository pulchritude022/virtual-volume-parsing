### Modern English
> **[Perth], 24 of May, 1651.** The Commissioners of the General Assembly, understanding that there are diverse [disorderly] persons, of the ministry or members [of presbyteries], who — notwithstanding the public warnings and declarations given out for their information — do yet **disobey and oppose the public resolutions**, which have been given for the good of religion, king, and kingdom (now in eminent hazard and danger, through the prevalence of that army of Sectaries within the kingdom): therefore **they ordain all presbyteries to make diligent enrolment, if there be any such of their number; and if any such be found, to confer with them; and, after other conference, upon their wilful disobedience and opposition to the public resolutions, that they summon them to the next General Assembly, to answer for the same.**

## Notes & context
★★★ **The Resolutioner–Protester rupture, entered verbatim — the Public Resolutions act of the Commission (Perth, 24 May 1651).** This is the single most politically important document in the batch. The **Commission of the General Assembly** (sitting at Perth), acting for the **Resolutioner** majority, orders every presbytery to **identify, confer with, and — if obstinate — summon to the next General Assembly any minister or member who "disobeys and opposes the public resolutions."** The **Public Resolutions** (Dec 1650–1651) admitted former Engagers and "malignants" back into the army and public offices to resist Cromwell's occupation ("that army of Sectaries within the kingdom"); those who refused were the **Protesters**. That Stranraer presbytery copies this act into its register — days after being ordered to read "the Act against compliance with the Sectarians" (imgs 229, 233) — shows the great national schism of the Kirk arriving as an administrative instruction in the Rhins. Cross-refs the Protester thread already visible via **Mr James Guthrie** (img 225) and the fasts "for the sins of the king's family" (img 229).

★★ **John Campbell's contempt hardens.** Ordered to redo his defective public repentance (img 232), **John Campbell instead "gives a token of contempt to the presbytery"** and stays away — his defiance now a settled pattern, driving the case toward admonition and referral upward. Meanwhile the Synod resolves the **Gilbert Kennedy of Aviolen** adultery by putting **Margaret Morion to a purgation-oath at the fast** and remitting **Marion Auld** to old Glenluce (closing the thread of imgs 229a–230).

★ **The Inch minister crisis escalated.** All the **heritors of Inch** are summoned to attend the presbytery on **8 July** "anent the minister's business" — the presbytery forcing a decision on the resisted transportation of **Mr John Dick** (imgs 227–232).

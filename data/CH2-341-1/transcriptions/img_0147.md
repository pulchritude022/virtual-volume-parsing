# CH2/341/1 — Image 147 (folios 146v–147)

The purge continues and — crucially — gives us **the accused men's own answers**.
Left page (foliated **146a** = folio 146 verso) finishes the 29 November business:
the presbytery holds ★ **the Engagers' answers to the committee "not satisfactory,"**
and appoints Turnbull (with another) to tell the committee so — **especially the
answers of the Sheriff-younger, Sir James Ronald(s), Craiglaw, and Thomas Stewart**;
then ★ **John Ronald(s)** is reported to have **withheld the money for relief of the
poor**, and is to be called to account (Blair to warn him); two ruling elders are
excused for missing the October Synod; and a new **sitting of 30 November** writes
to Kirkcudbright and Wigtown about the **widows and orphans**, notes a **laird
[of Mochrum?] who had a child baptized irregularly** (by Andrew Cowin), and reports
that the Sheriff, Craiglaw and James Ronald(s) are **willing to compear**. The right
page (folio 147) records their ★★ **depositions/defences**: the **Sheriff-younger**
(dealt with the Earl of Galloway, but denied counselling to seize money); the
**Sheriff** (did not deal earnestly with Garthland, rather desired him to raise a
regiment of foot); **Craiglaw** (hired men but "not for the Engagement merely," took
a protection but did not procure it, never truly intended to be Garthland's
lieutenant-colonel — "Garthland was in earnest, not he"); and **James Ronald(s)**
(familiar with the officers, but for the good of the Earl of Cassillis; convoyed a
gentleman recommended to him; denied taking money from the tenants).

## Notes & context
- ★★ **The Engagers answer for themselves.** Read with img 146, this opening gives
  both halves of the purge: the accusation (146) and the **defence** (147). The
  pattern of the defences is historically telling — each man minimises: "not for the
  Engagement *merely*," "before the Earl's declaration," "Garthland was in earnest,
  not I," "for the good of [the anti-Engager] Cassillis." A vivid record of how
  local gentry navigated the sudden reversal after Preston.
- ★ **Named again:** the **Sheriff** and **Sheriff-younger** of Wigtown, **Sir James
  Ronald(s)**, **[Gordon of] Craiglaw**, **Thomas Stewart**, and **[MacDowall of]
  Garthland** — with the anti-Engagement magnates **Cassillis** and **Galloway** as
  the poles the accused try to align themselves with. Good person-page material.
- ★ **Withheld poor-relief money: John Ronald(s).** A discrete accountability case —
  money collected for the relief of the poor allegedly not applied to that use — to
  be produced before the presbytery.
- ★ **Irregular baptism by "Andrew Cowin."** A laird [of Mochrum?] had a child
  baptized outside the ordinary channel (by one Andrew Cowin — a suspended or
  irregular minister?); the presbytery moves to summon him. A discipline thread worth
  following.
- **Procedure:** the presbytery defers to the **Commission of the Kirk** for
  direction, and coordinates with the civil committee — again the twin-track purge.

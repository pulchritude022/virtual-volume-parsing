## Notes & context
- **★★ The Glenluce division completed — the Old kirk gets its minister.** This is the counterpart to
  Crichton's admission at New Luce (img 0115): the **Old kirk of Glenluce is now planted with Mr John Scott**,
  "minister at [—]," whom the **1647 General Assembly transported** to the charge. The heritors petition for
  his presentation; **Livingstone draws it up; Scott preaches (Psalm 23) and is formally presented to "the
  old kirk of Glenluce" with its stipend "furth of the Abbey and teinds," beginning crop-year 1647.** With
  both halves of the June-1646 GA division (img 0106) now filled — **New Luce/Crichton** and **Old
  Glenluce/Scott** — the multi-year planting saga reaches its resolution. A headline node for the wiki.
- **★ The mortification passes the Great Seal.** A concrete legal milestone: **James Ross of Barneill
  delivers to the presbytery "the gift of mortification to the kirk of Glenluce, past the Great Seal"** — the
  endowment (the long-disputed mortification, imgs 0101–0111) has now been formalised as a royal grant under
  the **Great Seal of Scotland**, giving the funding a firm legal footing. James Ross of Barneill remains the
  presbytery's key lay agent throughout.
- **★ The heritors split again — ecclesiastical vs temporal.** A precise legal nuance: the heritors (**Thomas
  Hay of [—], Hew Kennedy of [—], William [Gordon] of [—]**) **will assent "to what was ecclesiastical in
  the act of the General Assembly, but not to what was temporal"** — i.e. they accept the church's planting
  of the kirks but resist the financial/property burden — whereas **James Ross of Barneill and [McDowell] of
  [—] assent wholly.** This crystallises the whole heritor-resistance thread (imgs 0107–0111): the lairds'
  objection was always to the *money*, not the *ministry*.
- **The bridge of Glenluce again.** The **Whitsunday term of the stipend is earmarked "for repairing the
  bridge of Glenluce"** — echoing img 0110's diversion of vacant stipend to the bridge of Luce; the
  community infrastructure funded out of the parish's teind income.
- **The Ardwell case grinds on.** The persons summoned at img 0118 are now revealed as **witnesses "for
  finding out the truth of the alleged adultery of the Laird of Ardwell with Jean Fyfe"** — the presbytery
  gathering evidence after the compurgation/appeal impasse. And a penitent (**John Mc[—]**) is set to do
  **sackcloth penance in the New kirk of Glenluce** (the first recorded discipline in Crichton's new
  parish).
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `gift of mortificatioun to ye kirk of Glenluce, past ye Great Seale` =
  the endowment confirmed by royal charter under the Great Seal; `assent to What Was ecclesiastick… but not
  to yt Whilk Was temporall` = distinguishing the church-planting from its financial obligations; `present
  Mr John Scott to ye old kirk of Glenluce, and stipend… furth of ye Abbey and teinds` = presentation to a
  benefice with its teind stipend; `entry to ye uplifting of ye stipend… crop and yeir of God 1647… first
  termes payment at Witsonday` = the start date of stipend collection; `sackcloth in ye New kirk of
  Glenluce` = public penance. **Both pages heavily faded/torn** — the heritor surnames, Scott's former
  charge, and the exact September days are provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1647**.

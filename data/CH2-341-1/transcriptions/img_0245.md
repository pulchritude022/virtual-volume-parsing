# CH2/341/1 — Image 245 (folios 244a–245)

## Folio 244a (left page)  *(confidence: low)*

*Closing the 3 December 1651 sitting. A committee (**Mr John Dick, Mr James Lawrie, Mr John Park**) is appointed to confer with [Robert Campbell] before the next presbytery and report. **John McDowall, summoned for disobedience to the Stranraer session, compears and gives in a supplication complaining of being wronged by Andrew McDowall in Stranraer — the presbytery, finding the session had enjoined both men to public confession, orders John McDowall to satisfy per the session's ordinance** (a neighbour-quarrel). Then **the moderator reports on the special conference-day held at Stoneykirk about the late General Assembly business — kept per ordinance, the conference also held the day after — and the presbytery delays further consideration till the conference's outcome is known.** Next meeting appointed. Very badly water-stained. Side-margin captions faded.*

## Folio 245 (right page)  *(confidence: medium)*

*The **24 December 1651 sitting.** All brethren present; ruling elders all absent except Stranraer and Portmontgomery (the rest "lawfully absent at the former meeting"). Exercise by **Mr John Crookes** (1 Tim 1.4), doctrine tried and approven. Then **Mr John Dick and Mr James Lawrie report on their conference with George Adestoun — he gave "no great satisfaction," remaining insensible of the scandal arising on one of his Sabbaths, and, being cited, did not compear; so the presbytery orders Mr James Lawrie to appoint him a public satisfaction within the parish of Portmontgomery, and to compear next day under pain — otherwise "they will go on with the process of excommunication."** Finally, **the repairing of the kirk of old Glenluce: Mr John Crosse reports the heritors, spoken to, are "unwilling for the repairing of the buildings" and would give no answer.** Marginal "245" catchword; subscribed "Mr John Crosse."*

## Notes & context
★★ **George Adestoun to the brink of excommunication.** The insolent, long-defiant **George Adestoun** (= Alexstoune/Adeston, disruptive since imgs 192, 242) is dealt with by a conference of **Mr John Dick and Mr James Lawrie**, but gives "no great satisfaction" and is "not sensible of the scandal"; refusing to compear, he is set a **last public-satisfaction ultimatum in Portmontgomery — appear next day under pain, "otherwise they will go on with the process of excommunication."** The disciplinary machine grinding toward its ultimate censure.

★ **A neighbour-quarrel: John vs Andrew McDowall.** A very human parish dispute: **John McDowall and Andrew McDowall of Stranraer**, both enjoined public confession by the session, fell out "the next day after the ordinances," each "displeased with one another"; **John is ordered to satisfy per the session, Andrew to be called next meeting.** And the **old Glenluce kirk-repair** (imgs 237–239) hits the same wall — **the heritors "unwilling" and giving no answer** — while the **late-General-Assembly conference at Stoneykirk** (img 244) is reported held, its outcome awaited before the presbytery will judge. The **24 December 1651** stated meeting is briskly conducted with most elders excused.

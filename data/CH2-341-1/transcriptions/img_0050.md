# CH2/341/1 — Image 50 (folios 49v–50) — Kirkcolm patronage (May 1643); a re-copy of the 1642 process

Left page (folio **49a**, faded): the **patronage dispute over vacant Kirkcolm** — a
**presentation (of Mr James Smith) is rejected** because the presbytery claims the
**patronage of Kirkcolm as its own**, and the **Earl of Galloway** counter-claims it; **Mr
David Dick preaches at the Kirkcolm visitation (16 May 1643)**, the presbytery carefully
noting his preaching is **only a presbyterial exercise, prejudging no one's patronage
right.** Right page (folio **50**) is a **re-copy/extract of the 1642 Power process** —
the **bribe depositions** (Turnbull and George Dick each **deny taking a bribe**), the
**order for the 15 June 1642 process-revision meeting** with neighbouring presbyteries,
the **summons of Power to hear sentence**, and (new) **Power's own letter to the laird of
Garthland.** *(This right page duplicates content at imgs 0036/0039/0041 — evidently a
formal process-extract.)*

### Modern English (partial — page faded)
A **presentation of Mr James Smith to the kirk of Kirkcolm** which **the presbytery could
not accept, because it conceives the right of patronage to that kirk belongs to the
presbytery.** The presbytery met at **Kirkcolm on Tuesday 16 May [1643] for a visitation**;
**Mr David Dick** obliged to preach there. **At Kirkcolm, 16 May 1643:** the members met to
visit the parish; **Mr David Dick ready to preach** per the preceding presbytery's
ordinance. **The Earl of Galloway, presenting himself to the presbytery** and **claiming
the right of patronage of the kirk**, warned that **Dick's preaching should not prejudice
the right of presentation**; the presbytery declared to the Earl that **Dick's preaching
that day should in no way prejudice the Earl's right (if he had any), nor strengthen the
presbytery's — that Dick has no presentation, and his preaching is not a trial for the
ministry but simply a presbyterial exercise at a visitation.** In regard the presbytery is
informed the **patronage belongs to it**, [the matter is reserved]: **neither Mr David nor
any other to be presented or preach there until the right of patronage be disputed and
cleared.**

### Modern English
[Process-extract:] **Ardnell** [deposed]; a "**null**" from Ardnell "having more voices than
one." **Mr Alexander [Turnbull] declared before the presbytery that he received no bribe to
be Power's enemy; and Mr George Dick declared he did not get his "horse-grass" by way of a
bribe** to be Power's enemy. The presbytery ordered **the laird of Garthland to exhibit the
letter sent to him by Mr Gilbert.** The presbytery **ordained a meeting at Stranraer on 15
June 1642 for revising the process against Power**, the other two presbyteries to be
acquainted so they may **send commissioners to advise the final determination and
sentence.** That day the presbytery ordered **Mr John Dick and Mr Hugh Hutcheson to summon
Power to appear at Stranraer on 15 June, "now to run with continuation of days," to hear
sentence pronounced against him** on the points accused by **Uchtred McDowell of French and
James Gordon in Culgroat.** Dick and Hutcheson returned, declaring they had summoned him.

  and carefully **reserves the question**, ruling that **Mr David Dick's visitation-sermon
  prejudges nothing.** A glossary/event node on **patronage** and the 1640s tension between
  heritors' presentation and presbyterial "planting." New names: **the Earl of Galloway**
  (patron), **Mr David Dick** (preacher — surname uncertain).

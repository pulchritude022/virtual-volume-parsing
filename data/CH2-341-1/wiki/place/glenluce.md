The parish whose vacancy, funding, and eventual split into two charges (the old kirk of
Glenluce and the new kirk at Balneil/New Luce) is the volume's longest-running piece of
unfinished business. See
[The division and planting of Glenluce](entity:event/the-division-and-planting-of-glenluce)
for the full narrative.

## The Abbey as financial engine

**Glenluce Abbey**, ruined since the Reformation, is not just a monument in this record but
the literal source of the mortification that eventually funds the old kirk's stipend — its
teinds tacked out, its fabric surveyed room by room during the long vacancy [img_101,
img_129]. When Mr John Scott is finally admitted minister of the old kirk, the whole
presbytery walks out to the Abbey together to give him formal possession [img_126–127].

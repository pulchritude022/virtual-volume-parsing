# CH2/341/1 — Image 145 (folios 144v–145)

The presbytery's engagement with the **civil Committee sitting at Stranraer** in the
purge that followed the 1648 Engagement. Left page (foliated **144a** = folio 144
verso) finishes the 14 November business and opens the **Stranraer sitting of 29
November 1648**: the exercise; ★ **Mr James Blair's report** that the other two
presbyteries had not their **widows-and-orphans roll** ready, so a new meeting is
set at Wigtown for **13 December** to bring in the names of the **stewartry** and of
Wigtown presbytery; disputation appointments (Turnbull on the common head, ★ **Mr
John Crosse on the contradiction "de statu primi hominis"** — on the state of the
first man/Adam); and — the headline — ★★ **the Sheriff [younger] desires the
presbytery to correspond with the Committee then convened at Stranraer**, so the
presbytery sends **Mr Alexander Turnbull and Mr James Lowrie** with a set of
**desires: purge the judicatories of malignants, deal impartially, give no ground
of complaint, and take speedy course** for relief. The right page (folio 145) has
★ **the commissioners' report of the Committee's answer** (it is "about to purge"
its judicatories and asks the presbytery for information against malignant members;
promises to write to the stewartry committee about the widows' money) and ★★ a
**numbered bill/classification the presbytery gave in concerning degrees of
complicity in the Engagement** — badly faded and here rendered only for sense.

(2) to deal impartially with all men; (3) to provide [against complaint], so that
there be no just ground of complaint against any employed in the administration of
their acts; (4) to take some speedy course to get the [poor] relieved, and to write

## Notes & context
- ★★ **Presbytery ↔ Committee at Stranraer over purging malignants (Nov 1648).**
  With a **civil Committee sitting at Stranraer**, the Sheriff-younger invites the
  presbytery to correspond; the presbytery sends **Turnbull and Lowrie** with formal
  **desires to purge the judicatories of malignants, act impartially, and relieve the
  poor**, and the Committee answers that it is itself "about to purge" and asks the
  presbytery for information against malignant members. A vivid picture of the
  church–state machinery of the post-Engagement purge operating at the local Galloway
  level (the same purge that would be codified nationally in the 1649 Act of Classes).
- ★★ **A numbered classification of Engagement complicity.** The presbytery's bill
  enumerates degrees of guilt — furthering the Engagement as priest/clerk/voter;
  agreeing "for money only" before the act; seizing the goods of those who fled; the
  conduct of soldiers; those who did/did not absent themselves against the army in
  September, etc. Even in its faded state this is important evidence of how the
  categories of "malignant" were being drawn on the ground. **Read at low confidence.**
- ★ **Widows-and-orphans money pressed (Wigtown, 13 Dec).** The relief fund for the
  widows and orphans (of ministers/soldiers?) is chased across the three presbyteries
  of the Synod; a roll-gathering meeting is set at Wigtown for 13 December, naming the
  **stewartry** (of Kirkcudbright) and **Wigtown** presbytery.
- ★ **Disputation "de statu primi hominis."** A second Latin thesis-topic in the
  trials (after "de latitudine salutis," img 141), here assigned to **Mr John
  Crosse** — theological colour, and confirmation that **Crosse** is a
  trials-candidate/brother alongside Lowrie.

  2. **"Mr John Crosse"** — recurs here as exercise-partner and disputant; confirm

  3. **"the Sheriff [younger]"** — the civil officer who invited the correspondence;
     name/identity not given.
  4. **Exercise text "[1 Thess.] 5:6"** and the **Latin "de statu primi hominis."**
  5. Whether the committee at Stranraer is the **Committee of War / of Estates for
     the shire of Wigtown** (cf. imgs 136–137) — likely, but not named here.

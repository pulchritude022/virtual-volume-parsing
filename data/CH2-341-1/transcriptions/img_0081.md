# CH2/341/1 — Image 81 (folios 80v–81), August 1645 — the catalogue of ministerial sins concluded, and the Assembly's "Remedies"

Both pages continue the engrossed Assembly document (imgs 0079–0080). Left page (folio **80a**)
finishes the list of ministerial faults, items **7–12**: (7) want of zeal and love to the charge of
souls, not weighed with the woe of not winning sinners; (8) self-seeking in preaching, affecting wit
and skill rather than the wisdom and power of God; (9) rashness in preaching, not studying to be
furnished, and neglecting to apply the doctrine to the hearers; (10) indiscreet carriage whereby
"godliness has got a deep wound and profaneness lifted up the head" (referring to a grievance/act set
forth by the General Assembly at Edinburgh); (11) little care to foresee our danger abroad or at home,
[not weighing] the causes of our calamity; (12) ministers negligent to wrestle in prayer for a
blessing on their labours. Then a new head, **"Remedia" (Remedies)** — (1) fill vacant charges with
godly, able men and admit no unqualified helper. Right page (folio **81**) continues the remedies,
items **2–8**: (2) brethren to admonish one another lovingly, and whoever neglects presbytery or Synod
after admonition to be censured; (3) careful visitation of kirks (each elder deponing about the
minister, and one about another); (4) care to divide the labour and to have family worship among
neighbours; (5) **every minister to be humbled for his former failings and make his peace with God,
"that he may the more effectually preach repentance to others, and stand in the gap to turn away the
wrath, weeping between the porch and the altar for all the abominations of the land"**; (6) special
care that ministers seek the good, mind God's worship, and draw daily from Christ the fountain of
life; (7) care for conferences in presbyteries; (8) every minister to be faithful in bringing home
the Word of God to the heart and affections.

## Notes & context
- **★★ The Assembly's "Remedies" — a ministerial reformation programme (1645).** The counterpart to
  the catalogue of sins (imgs 0079–0080): a numbered set of **remedies** the General Assembly
  prescribed to presbyteries — fill vacancies with able godly men, mutual brotherly admonition,
  faithful **visitation of kirks** (the deponing procedure spelled out), family worship, and above all
  **personal humiliation of the minister** ("stand in the gap to turn away the wrath, weeping between
  the porch and the altar for all the abominations of the land" — echoing Joel 2:17 and Ezekiel 22:30).
  Together imgs 0079–0081 preserve, at parish level, the **Covenanting Kirk's programme of ministerial
  self-reform in the disastrous summer of 1645** — a coherent, quotable document worth featuring on
  the site (theme: "causes of wrath" + "remedies"; the reforming ideal of the godly minister).
- **A cross-reference to an earlier Assembly act.** Item 10 cites a grievance "set forth by the
  General Assembly at Edinburgh [1641?]" — flag the year/act for identification (an earlier act
  against the growth of profaneness).
- **Value despite fading.** As with img 0080, both pages are faded and the wording is provisional; the
  numbered structure is secure. Worth a careful re-reading against the manuscript for a clean edition
  of this set-piece.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `Remedia` = remedies (the Latin head); `stand in the gap… weeping
  between the porch and the altar` = biblical imagery (Ezek. 22:30; Joel 2:17) for intercessory
  repentance; `visitatioun of kirks… called in to Depone about the minister` = the formal parish
  visitation, elders testifying about the minister and each other; `the great sheepherd` = Christ (Heb.
  13:20); `helper or servant to another` = an assistant minister admitted under a senior (cf. the Power
  helper negotiations, imgs 0066–0069). **Both pages faded** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*.
  Date 1645.

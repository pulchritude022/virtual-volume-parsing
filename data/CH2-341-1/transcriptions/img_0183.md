**Presbytery at Stranraer, 13 February 1650.** Absent: Dick, Scott, [Crosse], and Mr James Currie (at Edinburgh); ruling elders of Inch, [Leswalt], Stoneykirk, Kirkmaiden. Currie made and Park added (1 Thess 1:4), approven. Turnbull excused.

**The presbytery ordains Mr John Park to represent the abuses done [to the town touching the] Covenant; and appoints him and the Provost of Stranraer to represent to the Committee of War that certain malignant persons afflict the town of Stranraer, and to desire that some course be taken for restraining them.**

## Notes & context
★★ **The 2-merk-land moved from Stoneykirk to Portpatrick — and "Portmontgomery" identified.** This opening records the **formal presbyterial act annexing the 2-merk-land from the parish of Stoneykirk to the parish of Portpatrick**, on the perambulators' finding (img 182) that the land lay far from Stoneykirk kirk and near Portpatrick, "in foul weather" hard to reach. Crucially, Portpatrick is here consistently called **"Portmontgomery" (Port of Montgomery, Portpatrick's older name)** — which **resolves the long-standing "Portmontgomery" puzzle** from earlier folios (where the "kirk/Session of Portmontgomery" was uncertainly flagged as a possible alias of Kirkcolm): it is Portpatrick. The act is recommended on to the commission for plantation of kirks.

★★ **Malignants afflicting Stranraer.** A striking political item: the presbytery **appoints Mr John Park (newly its minister) and the Provost of Stranraer to represent to the Committee of War that certain malignant persons are "afflicting" the town of Stranraer, and to seek a course for restraining them** — the post-Engagement factional tension spilling into everyday harassment in the burgh, and the kirk turning to the military committee for protection.

★ **The Engagement satisfactions continue:** **Major John McDowall** (the Engagement "Major" who came forward at img 182) is to confer with Park and Dick about how far he engaged; **George Montgomerie** to be summoned; and the brethren to report their **poor-relief diligence**. Year is now 1650, on the eve of the Cromwellian invasion.

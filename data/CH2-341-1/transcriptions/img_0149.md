# CH2/341/1 — Image 149 (folios 148v–149)

A run of estate, discipline and infrastructure business from the **30 November 1648**
sitting. Left page (foliated **148a** = folio 148 verso): ★ **Mr John Dick (minister
of Inch) wants a glebe and manse** — Blair to deal with the Sheriff of Galloway,
whose land it is; **Mr James Lowrie** is to be given **possession of the glebes of
Toskerton and Clashquhat** (Stoneykirk); the **paternity case of Marion** is to be
summoned again; and ★★ **Thomas Hay of Park presents a supplication** (full tenor
entered) against **How [Hugh] Ronald(s) of Airieman**, who gave in "an infamous
bill" to the committee charging Hay with malignancy — chiefly **procuring a
protection from Duke Hamilton** to be free of the Engager army's quartering. The
right page (folio 149) finishes Hay's supplication (asking to be tried and
vindicated; the presbytery summons Airieman) and turns to Glenluce parish works: ★
**planting a school in the two Glenluce parishes** (the gentry offer 200 marks) and ★
**repairing the ruinous bridge of Glenluce** by a rate levied across the presbytery's
parishes (contributions listed).

## Notes & context
- ★★ **Thomas Hay of Park vs How Ronald of Airieman.** A second gentry supplication
  (after Balneil, img 148): **Thomas Hay of Park** answers the malignancy bill that
  **How [Hugh] Ronald(s) of Airieman** lodged against him — the core charge being his
  **protection from Duke Hamilton** to escape the Engager army's quartering (this is
  almost certainly the unnamed man of img 146, item 5). He counter-alleges the bill is
  a private vendetta ("oppressing… raising of him and his tenants") and asks the
  presbytery to try the matter and vindicate him — so the presbytery summons Airieman.
  A rich two-sided local feud playing out through the purge machinery.
- ★ **Glebes and manses.** The plantation's material tail continues: **Mr John Dick
  (Inch)** still lacks a glebe and manse (to be designed out of the **Sheriff of
  Galloway's** land), while **Mr James Lowrie** is put in possession of the
  **Toskerton and Clashquhat** glebes at Stoneykirk — a concrete step in his newly
  begun ministry there.
- ★ **A school for Glenluce.** Following the visitation injunction (img 144) to plant
  a school, the gentry pledge **200 marks**; the presbytery works out a split between
  the old and new kirks. Good social-history detail.
- ★ **The ruinous bridge of Glenluce.** A presbytery-wide **rate to rebuild the
  bridge** — Kirkmaiden, Stoneykirk, Inch, Kirkcolm, Stranraer, Portmontgomery,
  [Leswalt] each assessed — one of the clearest "public works" entries in the volume,
  and a useful list of the constituent parishes with relative valuations.
- **Paternity case:** Marion is to be summoned yet again — the case grinds on.

## Notes & context
★★ **Boundary settled — and both ministers agree.** The Stoneykirk/Portmontgomery dispute (Garthland's supplication, imgs 231–232) is closed with a precise surveyor's fact: the lands of **Kidork and Barnsalloch** lie "two-thirds of a mile" nearer Stoneykirk than Portmontgomery, per **the perambulation approved 20 November 1650** — so they remain with Stoneykirk. Notably **both sides consent**: the Stoneykirk minister and heritors, and **Mr James Blair, minister at Portmontgomery**, each profess the arrangement prejudices no one's stipend. A rare amicable close to a parochial teind fight.

★★ **The fasts kept and "the Act against compliance with the Sectarians" read.** Following the appointment at img 229, the brethren **report back that they kept the appointed fasts and read the Commission's warning and the Act against compliance with the Sectarians** — the anti-Cromwellian discipline duly executed across the bounds. A contribution for "the poor sufferers in England" is also handled (cf. imgs 216, 96).

★ **Church government at the exercise; a Leswalt adultery.** Mr John Park's common head is **"de regimine in genere"** — church government — a pointed theme in 1651 with the Resolutioner/Protester rift live. And **Mary McCrakan**, adulteress with **Archibald Kennedy** of Leswalt, confesses "with unwillingness" and is remitted to her session (a distinct Leswalt case from Crosbie/Gib, img 226).

# CH2/341/1 — Image 25 (folios 24v–25), 6 April 1643 — appeal ends; a third accuser

Left page (folio **24a**) finishes Power's appeal reasons (9–10): the suspension
"closely bears a sentence, as of things proven," the accuser had "assurance of the
judgement of the judges" beforehand, and the presbytery sentenced on the accuser's
and witnesses' desires while **never mentioning Power's answers**. Right page (folio
**25**) closes and subscribes the **appellation**, then a **third accuser — James
Gordon** — gives in his own five-point paper, near-identical to French's and
Ellaster's. (The right page's inner margin runs into the binding.)

## Folio 24v (left page, marked "24a")  *(confidence: medium)*  — appeal reasons 9–10

```
Whilk answeares (if they had beene tryed and found [true])
[wold] have not only hindered the receaving of farther witnessing,

10[.] To witnesse that the accuser is more favoured then I am,
it may clairly appeir by the desyre given in, craving my sus-

(as it is informall) [they] hes receaved, as satisfactory, and
[given] ane informall sentence, as [both] appeir by
the act whereon they ordaine[.] they have considered
the desyre of the persewer, and advysed with the desyre
of the witnesse, and therfor given sentence, and never
maks mentioun of my answeares and supplications given in,
that they have ather seene or considered them[.] Whilk, of
all, ought (before any sentence) not only [to have] beene con-
sidered, but tryed, before they had made any procedure[.]
nather wold the presb. grant me libertie to cleir
my innocencie in that particular for the whilk I wes
sentenced, by bringing in of elders to witnesse the
trueth[.] allse I wes desyred by the moderator to bring
them[.] and therfor it ought to be referred, as above is ———
        Having ———
```

### Modern English
[Reason 9, cont.:] They went on to receive further witnesses; whereas Power's answers,
if tried and found true, would have **hindered further witnessing and invalidated the
accusation**. Not content, they proceeded (on the accuser's desire) to an **act of
suspension**, which "closely bears a sentence, as of things proven," implying further
punishment — grounded on particulars of which he is "most innocent" and offered to
clear; the act prejudices his name and calling, against equity. **Reason 10:** the
accuser is more favoured — his very paper craving the suspension shows he had
knowledge of the depositions **and "some assurance of the judgement of the judges,"**
even prescribing them an order; the presbytery received this informal paper as
satisfactory and **gave sentence on the accuser's and witnesses' desires, never once
mentioning Power's own answers and supplications** — which ought to have been
considered and tried first; nor would they let him bring in elders to clear his
innocence, though the moderator had asked him to bring them.

### Modern English
[Power's appellation concludes:] he had asked that the process led against him — "now
a whole year" — be **ratified and inserted in the process, subscribed by the whole
members**, and that a **just copy and extract** be given him under the clerk's hand at
his own charges; this **was refused**; therefore he **appeals to the Synod**,
protesting liberty to enlarge his reasons and add more. *Subscribed: Mr Gilbert
Power.*

**James Gordon's paper follows** — a **third accusation**: notwithstanding French's
libel (perjury, usury, cloaking adultery, and many more crimes), since it was given in
Power has committed the following: **(1)** Sabbath-breaking, in September [1641/1642],
**drinking in an alehouse with the Portpatrick/Portspittal tenants**, reckoning old
debts of their lands, and settling with them; confessed before the Stoneykirk Session,
saying "he had neither pen nor ink to give them discharges"; **(2)** sowing sedition
between the lairds of **Garthland and French** (his letter extant); **(3)** ordinary
lying — telling **Mr Alexander Turnbull, minister at Kirkmaiden**, that "he took
bribes from the lairds of **Ardnell and Ellaster** to be his enemy," and telling **John
Baillie of Little Dunragit** the presbytery would refer French's whole accusation to
Power's own oath; **(4)** falsifying the Session book; **(5)** contempt — preaching
every Sabbath since his suspension, and on **20 March**…

## Notes & context
- **Three coordinated accusers.** With **James Gordon's** paper, Power now faces **three
  near-identical post-suspension accusations** — from **Uchtred McDowell of French**
  (img_0007), **Andrew McDowell of Ellaster/Killaster** (img_0019), and now **James
  Gordon** (img_0025). The word-for-word overlap (Sabbath-drinking with the Portpatrick
  tenants; sedition between Garthland and French; the same two lying-instances; the
  falsified Session book) shows an **organised prosecution faction**, which strengthens
  the credibility of Power's "malicious pursuit" appeal.
- **Reversal on James Gordon.** Earlier (img_0021–0022) James Gordon appeared merely as
  a Stoneykirk relief distributor who "gave in a paper"; that paper is now revealed to
  be an **accusation**, not support — so Gordon is on French's side, not Power's. (Note
  for the people index: Gordon = accuser.)
- **Date to reconcile:** Gordon dates the Sabbath-drinking to **"September 1641"**,
  whereas French/Ellaster place it in **September 1642** (imgs 0015, 0019). One is
  likely a slip; flag for review.
- **Confirmed offices/relations:** **Mr Alexander Turnbull = minister at Kirkmaiden**;
  the lying-instances recur verbatim (Turnbull; John Baillie of Little Dunragit).
- **Power's appeal, subscribed.** The full 10+-reason appellation to the Synod is now
  complete and signed — the case's centre of gravity has shifted to the **Provincial
  Assembly of Galloway**.
- **Palaeography applied:** the **inner (bound) margin of the right page is largely
  lost**, so most line-openings are reconstructed (low confidence); `comptit` = counted/
  settled (accounts); `buds` = bribes; `ratified and insert in the proces` = formally
  entered in the record; `ordinarly Sabbothlie` = every Sabbath. Dates "1642" Old Style.
  `y`+superscript → *th-*.

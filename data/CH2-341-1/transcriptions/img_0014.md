# CH2/341/1 — Image 14 (folios 13v–14), Dec 1642 (Power case → parish business)

Left page (folio **13a**) concludes **Power's Tarbret supplication** (the Session
judged the £5 "too much," advised against re-pillorying Tarbret to spare **Fergus
Cochran's** household, and Power recorded the sum in the Session book) and begs to
be restored; the presbytery defers. Right page (folio **14**) turns to general
business: a new sederunt — **Stranraer, 29 December 1642** — a **thanksgiving** for
9 Jan 1643, and a **petition to divide Glenluce parish and build a new kirk in the
moor**.

## Folio 13v (left page, marked "13a")  *(confidence: medium)*  — Power's Tarbret plea concluded

### Diplomatic transcription
```
being at the Session, I acquainted the Elders with what
had passed betwixt Ardnell & John & me, anent the
said Robert[,] & told them that Ardnell had delyvered to me
fyve [punds], & that in regard Robert Tarbret had no direction
ather from the Session or me to go to the pillar, I spoke
nothing to him till I had thair advyce[.] Who all in one
accord answered that it was too much[.] In respect he denyed
the fact, offered still his purgatioun, nather could it be [cleared][;]
& they desyred me also not to put him to the pillar again
at all, since it was a mater that could not be proven, &
might be a means to skaill [= break up] Fergus Cochrans house, with
whose wyfe he was slandered[.] The fyve [punds] which was
received, I did write in the Session booke in presence
of them all[.]  All which I am able to prove by Elders
presentlie in the town[.] & if your ws will grant a
warrant to summon the rest of the elders now absent,
who were acquainted with the whole proces, I referr
the same to their declaration[.] And what the said Robert
gave afterhand (whither it was 3[s] or what else, I am not
in memorie), it was for the kirkofficer that cited him[.]
In consideration wherof, seing it weights me exceedingly
to have my mouth closed, I humblie entreat your ws
(out of your brotherlie love & compassion) would
bee pleased to repone [= restore] me to the libertie of the exercise
of my ministrie till the closing of the proces[.] & your
ws answer humblie I require[.]  Sic subscribitur   Mr
Gilbert power ———
Severall of the members being absent, the presbiterie delayes
the consideration of these papers given in by French & Mr
Gilbert till the next presbiterie day ———
The next meeting is appoynted this day fourteen dayes[.]
The clerke is appoynted to write to [Machtoun?] to bee
here the next presbiterie day ———
```

### Modern English
Power continues: at the Session he told the Elders what had passed between Ardnell,
John, and himself about Robert Tarbret, and that Ardnell had delivered him £5; since
Tarbret had no direction from Session or Power to go to the pillar (stool of
repentance), Power said nothing to him till he had the Elders' advice. They all
answered it was "too much"; since Tarbret denied the fact and still offered his
purgation, and it could not be cleared, they desired Power not to put him to the
pillar again — the matter could not be proven and might "**skaill** (break up)
**Fergus Cochran's house**," with whose wife Tarbret was slandered. Power recorded
the £5 in the Session book before them all, and can prove it by Elders now in town.
Whatever Tarbret gave afterward (perhaps 3s) was for the kirk officer who cited him.
Since being silenced "weights him exceedingly," he entreats to be **restored to the
exercise of his ministry** till the process closes. *Subscribed: Mr Gilbert Power.*

Several members being absent, the presbytery **defers** these papers till next day;
the next meeting is this day fortnight; the clerk to write to [Machtoun?] to attend.

### Modern English
French is permitted to bring in **other witnesses** (not formerly named) with their
particulars against the next day, to replace any lawfully repelled — but not
otherwise. The letter from **Mr James Bower and Mr James Hamilton** was read.

**Presbyterial meeting at Stranraer, Wednesday 29 December 1642.** Mr Hugh Hutcheson
treated the controversy *De personis Mediatoris* (on the person of the Mediator —
a theological disputation); approved for the most part, admonished in a few things.
Absent: Mr Alexander Turnbull and the Ruling Elders of Stranraer, Stoneykirk,
Glenluce, Leswalt, Kirkmaiden, Kirkcolm. A **letter from the commissioners of the
General Assembly attending the Parliament** was read, ordering a **thanksgiving on
9 January 1643**.

**A petition by James Rose of Balneil[?]** and other heritors of the **moor of
Glenluce**, in their own name and the rest of the moor's inhabitants, desiring —
because of the great distance from the parish kirk — that the presbytery declare the
necessity of **dividing the parish and erecting a new kirk in the moor**, and set a
day to view the bounds. The presbytery, having considered it and heard **Mr George
Park, minister at Glenluce**, and some elders — the division being unanimously
agreed by all the parish elders in Session — declares the **division necessary and
expedient**, and resolves to meet [to view the bounds].

## Notes & context
- **The Power case pauses; parish life resumes.** After the suspension (img_0012),
  this opening shows the presbytery's *other* work: a **thanksgiving** ordered by
  the General Assembly's commissioners attending Parliament (a marker of the
  1642–43 Covenanter church-state machinery), and a substantial **parish-division
  petition** for **Glenluce** — the moor inhabitants want their own kirk because the
  parish kirk is too distant. This is a church-planting/boundary matter, useful for
  the parish/geography strand of the wiki.
- **The Tarbret adultery clarified:** the scandal is **Robert Tarbret with Fergus
  Cochran's wife**; Power's whole defence is that he acted as reluctant intermediary
  (the £5 recorded openly in the Session book) and shielded Cochran's household from
  an unprovable public accusation.
- **New people:** **Fergus Cochran** (the wronged husband), **Mr James Bower** &
  **Mr James Hamilton** (correspondents), **James Rose of Balneil[?]** (Glenluce
  heritor/petitioner), **Mr George Park** (minister at Glenluce). Offices: the
  **General Assembly commissioners** attending Parliament.
- **Palaeography applied:** `skaill` = to scatter/break up (a household); `pillar` =
  the pillar/stool of public repentance; `repone` = to restore (to office);
  `De personis Mediatoris` (Latin) = "on the person(s) of the Mediator" — a set
  theological controversy for the exercise; `heritors` = landowners liable for kirk
  upkeep; `condescend on a day` = fix a date. `y`+superscript → *th-*.

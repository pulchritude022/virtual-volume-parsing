## Notes & context
★★★ **The Earl of Galloway asks the presbytery for a regimental chaplain.** In the anxious summer of 1651 — Cromwell's army in Scotland, the Public Resolutions raising forces (imgs 234–235) — **a letter from my Lord of Galloway to moderator Mr James Blair requests the presbytery "to appoint one of their number for attending his regiment."** The presbytery will not act alone: because the **Presbytery of Wigton is "equally interested,"** it defers, and directs Blair to consult Wigton and to write back to the Earl. A vivid glimpse of the wartime demand on the ministry — a nobleman raising a regiment and seeking a chaplain from among the parish clergy — and of inter-presbytery coordination within the Synod of Galloway.

★★ **John Campbell escapes his rebuke — by the first speaker's forgetfulness.** The long-running discipline of the returned Ireland-malignant (imgs 216–235) stalls on a human slip: **the first speaker "having forgotten this day to call John Campbell before the congregation and to rebuke him,"** the presbytery simply **renews the former ordinance.** The machinery of public repentance shown missing a beat.

★ **Slander, a visitation, and the exercise.** **Mr John Scott gives John McCristie, "slanderer of his neighbour," the first public admonition.** The presbytery sets a **visitation of old Glenluce** for the following Tuesday (carried out at the 1 Aug/[—] Aug sitting — the minister preaching on his ordinary and the elders examined). **Mr John Dick supplies the exercise for the absent Mr Ferguson**, keeping the weekly homiletic rota going.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 140 (folios 139v–140)

Left page (foliated **139a** = folio 139 verso) continues the **28 June 1648**
sitting. ★ A **Commission of the General Assembly act (Edinburgh, 9 June 1648)** is
read requiring presbyteries to try every minister's firmness against the
Engagement — the presbytery takes "short trial" and finds **none guilty** (all
firm). It then ★ **names its commissioners to the next General Assembly**: **Mr
John Levingstoun, Mr John Dick, Mr Alexander Turnbull**, and (for a ruling elder)
the **Sheriff of Galloway younger** or, failing him, **Patrick Agnew of Sheuchan**.
The presbytery officer **James Murray's fee** (20 shillings from each brother,
since the 1647 Assembly) is uplifted, and the brethren are to press the gentlemen
to fulfil the "condescendments" made to **Mr James Lowrie**. The right page (folio
140): preaching appointments to **supply the vacant kirks** while the commissioners
are away; the next meeting left open pending their return; then the **Stranraer
sitting of 23 August 1648** — the commissioners returned from the Assembly report
their diligence and present ★ the **General Assembly's Declaration against the
unlawful Engagement** (printed), the **act for regulating ministers in their
preaching**, and the **Assembly's answer to the Committee of Estates (28 June)**.

### Modern English
The same day, an act of the Commissioners of the General Assembly (dated
Edinburgh, 9 June [1648]) was presented, the tenor whereof follows: *"The
Commissioners of the General Assembly seriously recommend to presbyteries to take
special notice and trial of every brother who is [forward] in the public business;
that if any be found who does not declare himself against the present Engagement,
nor join with the brethren in their common protestations against it, nor give out
all informations to the brethren of the unlawfulness of it, they may be referred to
the next General Assembly; and if any of them who have already declared for it,
that they be presently censured."* Which act being read, the presbytery took short
trial of every brother, and after trial found **none of the number guilty**; all
were exhorted to constancy, and to declare the mind of God concerning the present
course to the people.

The same day, the kirk nominated and appointed **Mr John Levingstoun, Mr John Dick,
and Mr Alexander Turnbull**, ministers, and the **Sheriff of Galloway younger** —
or, in case they cannot attend, **Patrick Agnew of Sheuchan**, ruling elder — their
**commissioners to the next General Assembly**.

The same day, the Moderator Mr Alexander Turnbull received from every one of the
brethren **20 shillings for James Murray's fee**, since the Assembly of 1647 until
this next ensuing Assembly of 1648.

The same day, the kirk seriously recommended to the laird of [Killaser younger], a
ruling elder for [Stoneykirk], that he deal with the gentlemen that they really
perform the **condescendments made to Mr James Lowrie**...

## Notes & context
- ★★ **The 1648 General Assembly reaches the Rhins.** This opening brackets the
  July 1648 Assembly: on 28 June the presbytery tries its ministers for firmness
  against the Engagement (finding all sound) and sends **Livingstone, Dick and
  Turnbull** (plus an Agnew ruling elder) as commissioners; by the **23 August
  1648** sitting they are back, reporting and entering the Assembly's headline
  papers — the **printed Declaration against the unlawful Engagement**, the **act
  regulating ministers' preaching**, and the **answer to the Committee of Estates**.
  The 23 August sitting falls days after the Engager army's destruction at
  **Preston (17–19 Aug 1648)** — the register is capturing the Kirk party's moment
  of vindication.
- ★ **The Agnews as ruling elders/commissioners.** The **Sheriff of Galloway
  younger** (the hereditary sheriffship was the Agnews of Lochnaw) and **Patrick
  Agnew of Sheuchan** appear as the presbytery's lay commissioners — reinforcing how
  central the Agnew family is to this presbytery (cf. Lt Col James Agnew, the Dick
  glebe dispute, img 136; Lady Freugh née Agnew, img 133).
- ★ **James Murray, presbytery officer/agent**, paid a **fee of 20 shillings per
  brother** — a rare glimpse of the court's paid administration.
- **Mr James Lowrie**: the presbytery presses the Stonykirk gentlemen to honour the
  "condescendments" (financial/stipend promises) made to him, and to have the
  commissioners see to his return to his charge.
- **Vacant-kirk supply**: a practical roster of who preaches where while the
  commissioners are at the Assembly — a useful list of the presbytery's kirks
  (Kirkmaiden, [Stoneykirk], [Stranraer], Inch, [Kirkmadrine]).

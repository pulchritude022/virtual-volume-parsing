# CH2/341/1 — Image 27 (folios 26v–27), April 1643 — the presbytery answers the appeal

Both pages record the **presbytery of Stranraer's formal reply** to Mr Gilbert
Power's appellation. Having "stated the question — whether the reasons of Power's
appellation were valid or not — after due consideration and reasoning they were
**thought invalid**," and the presbytery sets down its rebuttal, first to the
**protestation** prefacing the appeal (which it says imputes injustice to the
court), then point by point: **To the 1st reason… To the 2nd reason… To the 3rd
reason…** It is the mirror image of img_0023–0025 — the court defending its
process to the Synod.

### Modern English
[Following a decision "not delayed till a more fit time":] the question being put —
**whether the reasons of Mr Gilbert Power's appellation were valid or not** — after
due consideration the presbytery **judged them invalid**, for the reasons following.

**Reasons why the presbytery of Stranraer judged Power's protestation and appellation
invalid.** As to the **protestation** that preceded the appeal — which "contained
somewhat that seems to argue injustice in the presbytery" — although the court is
"abundantly satisfied of the uprightness of our carriage in the whole business," yet,
for the satisfaction of the Provincial Assembly and its own clearing (and
notwithstanding that Power's "reiterated assertion" makes no impression on the
assembly, his very boldness having been encouraged by the "**lenity**" of the last
Provincial Assembly to bring the matter "**upon the stage**" again), the presbytery
will lay these considerations before **the next ensuing Synod**. And in general,
whatever Power complains of in his protestation, the presbytery is assured he "can
never prove," each point having received an answer that will satisfy the Synod — an
answer they think not fitting to repeat here, since it will appear "**when our
presbytery books shall be revised.**" **To the 1st reason of the appeal** they answer:
that what was afterward the determination of the [presbytery…]

### Modern English
[Answer to reason 1, concl.:] whatever was afterward the determination of the
presbytery before the last Synod — or its "offer" to the Synod — is in no way
infringed by the presbytery, since "our resolution was only to **crave the Synod's
concurrence and advice** toward a full and final sentence, which is yet to come; and
whatever our offer was, we still resolve to perform it."

**To the 2nd reason of the appeal:** those Power "alleged to be his parties, and are
admitted to be his judges and witnesses" — his objections against them "**were fully
heard and answered by the presbytery and approved by the Synod**," notwithstanding all
he could object. To the second part of that reason — that "**inlaik [supplied/co-opted]
elders**" were kept in the court "of purpose for this action" — they answer that they
admitted no inlaik elders but such as had **lawful written commissions from Sessions**
(which are ready to exhibit); and the continuing of elders through "his long and
tedious process" actually helped move his business along — "whereof we have
experience," for some elders were changed and, not being acquainted with the business
"**ab initio**" (from the start), "their [verdicts?] were **non liquet**" (not proven /
undecided). And as for the **Charles Campbell** episode — even if true, since it
concerns "**but one member, it cannot be a relevant reason of appeal from the whole**";
and what Campbell did, "he acknowledged to be done in simplicity, and was admonished
for it by the presbytery."

**To the 3rd reason of the appeal:** although they "think it not inexpedient, in any
matter of scandal, to admit women as witnesses," yet — being observant of the Synod's
advice — "**we have received the deposition of no women as yet**," and the mere
**summoning** of them is no way contrary to the Synod's advice. As for the rest of "the
general assertions and forged allegiance" in his third reason, they "think not
ourselves bound to answer until such [time…]"

## Notes & context
- **The court answers back.** After imgs 0022–0025 gave Power's full appellation, this
  opening is **the presbytery's formal rebuttal**, drawn up for the Synod. It "**stated
  the question**" and judged the appeal **invalid**, then rebuts the protestation and
  reasons 1–3 in order (the answers to the remaining reasons presumably run onto the
  next opening). This is the pivot from *accusation/defence* to *the court defending its
  own procedure on appeal*.
- **Key procedural admissions.** The presbytery concedes the **Charles Campbell**
  leak — but minimises it: "concerning but one member… cannot be a relevant reason of
  appeal from the whole," and Campbell "acknowledged it done in **simplicity** and was
  admonished." It also concedes it has "**received the deposition of no women as yet**"
  (answering reason 3's excommunication-summons complaint) — i.e. women were *summoned*
  but not yet *deponed*.
- **"Inlaik elders."** The rebuttal to reason 2 turns on **inlaik elders** — elders
  brought in / co-opted to make up the court. The presbytery insists all sat on **lawful
  written commissions from their Kirk Sessions**. This is a useful gloss on how the
  presbytery's membership was constituted.
- **The Synod is the destination.** The presbytery repeatedly defers to "**the next
  ensuing Synod**" and says its full answers will appear "when our presbytery books
  shall be **revised**" — the Provincial Assembly of Galloway will review the presbytery
  register. Confirms the case is being escalated, not closed.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `ab initio` = from the beginning; `non liquet` =
  "it is not clear" (the classical verdict of *not proven / undecided*); `lenitie` =
  leniency; `inlaik` = a supply/deficiency made up (co-opted); `upon the stage` = back
  into public contention; `simplicitie` = innocent good faith, without malice. Capital
  **W** for *w/wh* throughout this clerk's hand. Dates "1642" Old Style. `y`+superscript
  → *th-*.

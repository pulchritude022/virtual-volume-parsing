## Notes & context
Two sittings of the **Stranraer presbytery** (24 Jan and early Feb 1649), mostly ordinary post-Engagement housekeeping in the shadow of the Covenant-renewal drive. This opening's sitting-header — "Presbyterie holden at **Stranraer**" — retrospectively fixes the recurring venue mis-read as "Chalmerain" on img 162; corrected there.

Threads carried over:
- ★ **The Covenant-renewal machinery in action.** The Commission of the Kirk sends a letter enclosing an **act of the late General Assembly**, several instruments, and **three psalm books** to be published by the brethren; a second letter warns the presbytery **not to be too hasty in admitting anyone to the Covenant "with an under-promise"** (a qualified subscription) — the presbytery is to hold out for unqualified adherence. The brethren are tasked to **draw up "the conditions of the Kirk"** (the terms/form of subscription) to return to the Commission. This is the same subscription-administration business begun on img 162.
- ★ **Patrick McKie** (the Garthland-regiment paymaster named as an Engagement complier back on img 152) resurfaces: rather than a public process, the presbytery, on the Commissioners' advice, holds that if his subsequent carriage has not been scandalous or openly disaffected he may be **received on repentance**; his formal process is meanwhile delayed. A softening, case-by-case line toward lesser compliers.
- The **Andrew Cowan** irregular-baptism matter is pushed back down to the **session of Stranraer** to be examined and determined there.
- A woman "who had shown signs of repentance" is **received** back per the kirk's directions (routine penance).
- Weekly exercise: **Hosea 5:9–10** (Crosse opening, Currie adding). Preaching roster rotates John Dick / John Crosse / James Currie across Stranraer, Old Glenluce, and Kirkmaiden.

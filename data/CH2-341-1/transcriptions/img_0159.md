# CH2/341/1 — Image 159 (folios 158v–159)

Left page (foliated **158a** = folio 158 verso): ★ **How Ronald of Airieman gives a
confession** of his fault (the wrong to Park), but — reports **Mr John Crosse** — "not
so full and ingenuously as one would have wished," so he is appointed to **confess
before the congregation** next Sabbath, the minister to decide whether to receive him
to the Covenant; more **fast-breakers** are cited (with **Major McDougall, George
Hann** and others); then the ★ **presbytery of 3 January 1649** resolves ★★ **to
proceed with Balneil's process** — **Mr James Blair dissenting that "he thought it not
ripe enough," his dissent and reasons entered in the register** — and Balneil is called
and questioned anew. The right page (folio 159) is the ★★ **formal re-examination of
James Ross of Balneil on the ten articles**, put to him one by one in the presbytery;
to each he **refers himself to his written answers** ("the first page," "the second
page," etc.) — confirming the article structure (the Michaelmas commission; refusing the
supplication; the **committee at [Auchrowan]** and the Earl of Galloway naming his own
officers; advising **Major McDougall**; the protection; the Estates' observations;
spreading civil news; accompanying the "miner").

## Notes & context
- ★★ **A recorded dissent — Mr James Blair.** A striking procedural detail: when the
  presbytery resolves to proceed against Balneil, **Blair formally dissents** that the
  process is "not ripe enough," and his dissent and reasons are **minuted in the register**
  at his own request. Evidence of genuine disagreement within the court over how hard to
  press the purge — and a rare glimpse of a named minister protecting due process.
- ★★ **Balneil stands on his written answers.** Put the ten articles again, viva voce,
  Balneil simply **refers to his signed answers** (imgs 153–155) — "the first page," "the
  second page." This confirms the article numbering and shows a defendant holding a
  consistent line. The one fresh nuance: on art. 5 he insists the **Covenant "was not
  minded that day nor several days after,"** dating his conformity.
- ★ **Airieman's grudging confession.** His acknowledgment of wronging **Park** is judged
  **not full or ingenuous enough**; he must **satisfy before the congregation**, with his
  Covenant admission left to the minister's discretion — the slander case (imgs 156–157)
  moving to public penance.
- ★ **More fast-breakers, including gentry.** The fast-breaking citations now sweep in
  **Major McDougall** and others — the same discipline reaching a named Engager officer.
- Confirms the committee meeting-place **"[Auchrowan]"** and the article that the **Earl of
  Galloway should name his own officers** (cf. img 153).

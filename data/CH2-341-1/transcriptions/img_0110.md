# CH2/341/1 — Image 110 (folios 109v–110), 24 February–17 March 1647 — the Glenluce arrears settlement (teinds 1642–46; the bridge of Luce); Crichton's next trial; a Kirkcolm contumacy

Left page (folio **109a**): (the 24 Feb Glenluce settlement, continued) the heritors to **make payment of
the arrears — the teinds for the years 1642, 1643, 1644, 1645, and out of the vacant stipend of 1646** —
and **the bridge of Luce being [ruinous] to be repaired out of the [vacant stipend]**, the presbytery to
use its endeavour thereto; **but if they shall not do it, seeing the work is hindered by their delay, the
presbytery engages itself to concur with James Ross of Balneil in any legal way against the heritors of
Glenluce till payment be made**; and **hereby declared that if any do not consent before the next presbytery
day (putting the presbytery to the trouble of a legal course), the presbytery shall not consent that they
get any relief of their part of the foresaid 380 merks of the vacant stipend 1646, but that it shall come
to some pious use of the poor**; of all which agreements the presbytery **gave intimation to all the
heritors present, and [ordained] John McDowell of [Cardoch?] to give notice to those not present**. The
next meeting appointed **17 March** (Mr Hew Hutcheson to give in the common head *De causa impulsiva
electionis*; Mr John Crichton to make, Mr John Livingstone to add). Then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 17
March 1647** — absent Mr Alexander Turnbull and the elders of Leswalt, Portmontgomery, Inch [and Kirkcolm];
**★ an exercise (Mr John Crichton made, Mr John Livingstone added, 1 Thess. 3:1–2, both approved)**
[Crichton's next trial]; **John Mc[—] compeared, the presbytery having…** Right page (folio **110**): …**found
him faulty in divers points — disobedient to the minister and session of Kirkcolm, and for setting up a [—]
in Kirkcolm without licence — [so] he was enjoined to give signs of repentance for the public [scandal],
first at Stranraer, next at Kirkcolm; and if he give satisfaction, to be [received] the first day he stands
at Kirkcolm; and if he give not satisfaction, that he be excommunicated [and summoned] before the presbytery
that he may be further censured**; **all the contributions of the presbytery [for the poor] delivered to
William [Baillie?], bailie [of Stranraer]**; **Mr Hew Hutcheson reported he preached at Stranraer last
Sunday (his right day); Mr John Dick reported he preached at Glenluce last Sunday**; **Mr James Bell
appointed to preach at Stranraer the next Sunday, Mr Alexander Turnbull the eight days after**; **Gilbert
Mc[—] in Kirkcolm to be summoned to the next presbytery**; **★ the which day James Adair in Portmontgomery
compeared in sackcloth and confessed his fall in adultery with [Jonet —], and was enjoined per the order**;
and **the which day, in consideration of former acts anent the settling of the parish of Glenluce, the
gentlemen and heritors of Glenluce coming…** [continued on the next opening].

## Notes & context
- **★ The Glenluce arrears itemised — and the bridge of Luce.** The settlement (imgs 0107–0109) is now
  spelled out: the heritors owe **the teind arrears of 1642–1645 plus the vacant stipend of 1646**, and —
  a lovely local-infrastructure detail — **"the bridge of Luce," being ruinous, is to be repaired out of
  the vacant stipend**. The presbytery's threat is sharpened: pay, or (a) the presbytery **litigates
  alongside James Ross of Balneil**, and (b) the dissenters **forfeit their share of the 380 merks to "some
  pious use of the poor."** A concrete picture of how a parish's stipend, arrears, and even a bridge were
  bound together in the "planting."
- **★ Crichton's trials continue.** On 17 March **Crichton "makes" the exercise (1 Thess. 3:1–2), approved**
  — a further ordination trial passed — while **Hutcheson is set the common head *De causa impulsiva
  electionis*** ("the impelling cause of election," a predestination topic). The New Luce planting grinding
  forward.
- **★ A Kirkcolm contumacy escalated toward excommunication.** **John Mc[—]**, disobedient to the Kirkcolm
  minister and session (and for "setting up [something] without licence"), faces **graded penance at
  Stranraer and Kirkcolm, on pain of excommunication** — discipline against obstinacy, with the parish and
  presbytery seat both used as places of public shame.
- **Everyday texture.** The **poor-box contributions handed to a burgh bailie (William Baillie?)**, the
  **preaching-supply rota** (Hutcheson, Dick, Bell, Turnbull), and **James Adair of Portmontgomery's
  adultery penance** — the ordinary grain of the record (note yet another **Adair** — Thomas at Glenluce,
  James at Portmontgomery).
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `ye teinds for ye yeeres 1642… and… ye vacant stipend 1646` = the
  schedule of stipend/teind arrears; `ye bridge of Luce… repaired out of ye vacant stipend` = a bridge
  funded from the unpaid stipend fund; `concur With James Ross… in any legall Way… till payment be made` =
  joint litigation to compel payment; `come to some pious use of ye poor` = diverted to poor relief; `De
  causa impulsiva electionis` = the doctrinal common head on the cause of (divine) election; `setting up a
  [—] Without licence… excommunicated… further censured` = graded discipline for contumacy. **Both pages
  heavily water-stained/torn** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1647**.

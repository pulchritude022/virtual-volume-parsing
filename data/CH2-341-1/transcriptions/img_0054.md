## Notes & context
- **Blair's admission to Kirkcolm is underway.** The vacant **Kirkcolm** (empty since
  Porterfield's death) is being filled by **Mr James Blair**: he does his **common head** and
  **popular sermon** as trials, an **edict** is served, a **public diet** set for **23 December
  1643** and a session diet for **3 January 1644**, and a **dimission is referred to the Synod
  of Galloway** — the full ordination/admission machinery. A clean example of "planting a
  kirk" and a milestone for **Blair's biography** (he moves from a floating exercised minister,
  and earlier Ireland-supply, to a settled charge). See [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]].
- **Letters from Ireland — the Covenant across the North Channel.** Two letters from **the
  commissioners of the Assembly at [Dublin?], 9 Nov 1643**, about requiring **dwellers and
  passengers to subscribe** [the Solemn League and Covenant] — the Stranraer presbytery again
  a hinge in the **Scotland–Ulster** relationship (cf. Livingston's and Blair's Irish supply,
  imgs 0047–0048). The place-name "Dublin" is uncertain (could be another body) — flag.
- **The parish catalogue and "revolted passengers."** The order that **each session compile a
  catalogue of its parishioners** — to detect the absent and the **"passengers that
  revolted"** — is a striking population-control measure, tied to the war/Covenant mobilisation
  and the flux of people crossing to and from Ulster. Good social-history colour.
- **Power still surfacing.** Even here, at the foot of the right page, **Mr Gilbert Power**
  reappears — consistent with his unresolved status (imgs 0049–0052). The exact sense is lost
  to the ghosting; flag for re-imaging.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `common head` = the set theological commonplace for a
  trial; `dimissioun` = a demission/transference paper (or a formal dismissal to another
  court); `edict` = the public citation served before an admission, that any objector may
  appear; `Notarie` = the notary who authenticates the edict's service; `parochiners` =
  parishioners; `passingers` = people crossing (to/from Ireland); `revolted` = fell away /
  deserted (the cause). **Top-left corner water-eaten; right page heavily ghosted** — treat the
  whole opening as provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1643–44.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 136 (folios 135v–136)

Left page (foliated **135a** = folio 135 verso) finishes the **Inch 17 May 1648**
sitting and opens an extraordinary **Glenluce sitting of 25 May 1648**. The Irish
**petition to transport Mr John Dick to Ireland lapses** (the commissioner **John
[Kennedy?] of [Barsell?]** and no one in his name compeared). Then ★ the
**arbitration of Mr John Dick's manse and glebe**: the heritor is **Lieutenant
Colonel James Agnew**, whose land adjoins the kirk and who claims in law he need
not provide a glebe; the matter is referred to two lawyers — **Mr Thomas
Nicolson** (for the presbytery and Dick) and **Mr David [Garriet?]** (for Agnew,
nominated by "Bonqin," Agnew's brother) — with **Lord [—] or Lord Craighall** as
oversman if they disagree, Agnew and Dick to repair to Edinburgh. At the **Glenluce
sitting (25 May)**, ★ **Mr John Levingstoun and Mr James Blair are both absent,
"gone to Ireland,"** and the presbytery is extraordinarily convened on special
business: it resolves that ★★ the **Committee of War of the Shire of Wigtown** be
petitioned to supplicate Parliament against the Engagement levy, and appoints two
brethren to draft the petition. The right page (folio 136) copies that **petition
verbatim** — the presbytery declaring it **cannot in conscience countenance the
intended levy for the Engagement**, nor satisfy its flocks of its lawfulness, and
beseeching that the Engagement be carried no further until the Kirk's desires are
satisfied.

### Modern English
[...the Committees of War of both kingdoms had petitioned the Parliament to give a
satisfactory answer to the just and necessary desires of the Commissioners of the
Kirk, and that a course might be taken to satisfy the doubts and fears of godly and
well-affected men concerning the present Engagement before it should be any further

*"The humble petition of the presbytery of Stranraer, unto the right honourable the
Committee of War within the Sheriffdom of Wigtown, humbly sheweth: That whereas we
understand there is a **levy intended toward an Engagement in war** [in a doubtful
cause], we do protest in the sight of God that, so far as we can know ourselves —
neither of our own knowledge nor by report — we are no ways satisfied in our
conscience, neither are we able to satisfy the doubts and fears of the flock
committed to our care, concerning the lawfulness of the same; which threatens
present and imminent danger to religion and to all the good of the cause; and

## Notes & context
- ★★ **The presbytery's anti-Engagement petition to the Committee of War of
  Wigtown (25 May 1648).** This is a concrete, datable act of ecclesiastical
  resistance to the Engagement, entered verbatim in the register: the ministers
  refuse to countenance the levy, protest "in the sight of God" that they cannot in
  conscience support it, and petition to have it stopped. It is the local execution
  of the Commission's 5 May charge (img 135) and situates the Rhins directly in the
  1648 crisis that ended at Preston.
- ★ **John Livingstone and James Blair "gone to Ireland" (May 1648).** Note that
  Livingstone — simultaneously being called to Ancrum (img 130) — is here recorded
  absent in Ireland; useful for reconstructing his 1648 movements before his
  translation.
- ★ **Mr John Dick's glebe → a legal arbitration.** The dispute with **Lieutenant
  Colonel James Agnew** (heritor, land adjoining the kirk) is put to two named
  lawyers (**Mr Thomas Nicolson**; **Mr David [Garriet?]**) with a Lord of Session
  (**Lord Craighall** = Sir John Hope of Craighall, or "Lord [Transdome?]") as
  oversman — a glimpse of how glebe-designation disputes escalated to Edinburgh
  lawyers. Ties "Bonqin" to the Agnews (brother of Lt Col James Agnew).
- The **Irish transportation petition for Dick lapses** by non-appearance — so Dick
  stays (for now) in the Stranraer presbytery.

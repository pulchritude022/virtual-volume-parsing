# CH2/341/1 — Image 95 (folios 94v–95), 27 May–24 June 1646 — Garthland made commissioner; a poor-box misappropriation; the deposition against James Torrane elder

Left page (folio **94a**): (tail of 27 May) **McDowell of Garthland made commissioner to attend the next
General Assembly, and to act according to the commission given, which is appointed to be delivered to
them**; next presbyterial meeting appointed **24 June** (Mr John Livingstone to make, Mr Hew Hutcheson
to add). Then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 24 June 1646** — an exercise (Mr John Livingstone made, Mr Hew
Hutcheson added, 1 Thess. 2:5[?], approved); absent Mr Alexander Turnbull and the elders of Kirkmaiden;
**the absence of Mr James Blair the two preceding presbytery days excused, and the absence of the elder
of Portmontgomery the former days excused**; **Mr John Dick reports [touching the judges/magistrates for
punishing offenders]**; **the rest of the brethren reported they had used diligence for dealing with the
magistrates, per the act of Parliament** [against malignants]; and **the matter about Stoneykirk
deferred**. Right page (folio **95**): **the brethren could get no word where Marion Torrane resides**;
**★ John Mc[—] in Inch to be summoned to the next presbytery, under pain of the censure of the kirk, for
intromitting with / troubling the poor's silver [and applying it to his own particular use]**; a
matter about the poor referred to Mr Alexander Turnbull, and **continued till Turnbull returns out of
Edinburgh**; **the brethren of [Colmonell] to cause summon Thomas Adair in Glenluce by letter and bring
back answer**; **Agnes McColbie continued**; **Mr John Dick reported he had dealt with James Ross [of
Barneill] as ordained**; and **★★ the deposition in the suspected adultery of James Torrane elder in
Inch** — a witness (a woman), being called and interrogated on what she knew, **deposed that she had
known and seen them in bed together in the very act of uncleanness, and further declared she rebuked the
same both to James Torrane and to the woman**; then **James Torrane younger, being called, appeared**
(continued on the next opening).

## Notes & context
- **★★ The Torrane adultery — an eyewitness deposition.** The suspected adultery flagged at img 0094
  now comes to proof: a woman witness deposes on oath that she **"knew and saw them in bed together, in
  the very act of uncleanness"** — and that she had **rebuked both James Torrane and the woman** at the
  time. This is a striking, unusually graphic piece of kirk-session evidence-taking: named parties
  (**James Torrane elder**, the suspected adulterer; **James Torrane younger**, called next — kin?; the
  woman, possibly the **Marion Torrane** whose residence the brethren cannot trace), a sworn eyewitness,
  and a formal deposition. A strong set-piece and genealogical node for the wiki (confirm the surnames
  and the woman's identity).
- **★ A poor-box scandal.** **John Mc[—] in Inch** is summoned under pain of church censure **for
  "intromitting with / troubling the poor's silver"** — i.e. meddling with, or misapplying, the kirk's
  poor-box funds. A rare glimpse of financial malfeasance in parish poor-relief, distinct from the usual
  sexual discipline; a good glossary/theme node (the poor's silver; intromission; kirk finance).
- **★ Garthland commissioner to the General Assembly.** Completing img 0094's election, **the laird of
  Garthland (McDowell of Garthland)** is named a **commissioner to the 1646 General Assembly** (alongside
  Livingstone and Turnbull) — the leading Stoneykirk heritor now back from Ireland and representing the
  presbytery at the supreme court. A firm biographical datum for Garthland.
- **The ordinary machinery.** The **24 June sederunt** (Livingstone moderating the exercise, Hutcheson
  clerk), **absences excused** (Blair, the Portmontgomery elder), the continuing **magistrates/malignants
  diligence** per the act of Parliament, the **Stoneykirk repair deferred** again, **Agnes McColbie
  continued**, and **Thomas Adair cited by letter** — the settled weekly grind, with Turnbull away **"out
  of Edinburgh"** (perhaps at the Assembly/commission business).
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `intromitting With… the poores silver` = meddling with poor-box
  money (embezzlement/misapplication); `commissioner to attend ye… generall assembly… according to yt
  commissioun given` = an elected representative acting under a formal commission; `Deposed… in bed
  together, in ye verie act of uncleannesse` = sworn eyewitness testimony of adultery; `summond
  literatorie` = cited by letter/writ; `out of Edinburgh` = away in Edinburgh. **Both pages faded, the
  right heavily ghosted** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1646**.

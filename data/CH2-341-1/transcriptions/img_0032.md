# CH2/341/1 — Image 32 (folios 31v–32), May 1642 — the kirk-seats act; John McCulloch of Marton's deposition

Left page (folio **31a**) finishes the **Glenluce kirk-seating act** (a detailed
re-allocation of pews around the old "bishop's seat," protested by **John and James
Rosse**). Then the pivotal document begins: **John McCulloch of Marton's written
deposition**, "exhibited under his hand" and ordered inserted — his sworn eyewitness
account of **how Mr Gilbert Power allegedly falsified umquhile Peter McCulloch's
deathbed testament (c.1628).** Right page (folio **32**) gives the deposition in full:
Power wrote a first testament making **Marton's son** executor, then secretly wrote a
**second** naming Peter's **two brothers** — drawn up when Peter "was out of his perfect
memory." This is the factual root of French's central "perjury/false-testament" charge.

### Modern English
"**In the year 1628, or thereabouts:** it is true that I, the said **John McCulloch,
and my son**, being in the house where **umquhile Peter McCulloch** lay **near the point
of death**, and **Mr Gilbert Power** being there — the said Mr Gilbert **wrote a
testament at Peter's desire**, wherein he made **[McCulloch] my son executor to all
Peter's part of the whole goods and gear** between him and **Margaret Moore his spouse**
(which was **the half**, there being **no children** between them); and Peter presently
**subscribed** it.

"And I, going home that night, the said **Mr Gilbert made another testament** — or [by]
day — and **put in Peter's two brothers, both, as executors**, and drew it up **in a
chamber where Peter did not lie** (as I was informed), and when **Peter was out of his
perfect memory**; he came and presented it to him, which Peter **subscribed**, and
[Power] **kept it close until Peter had died.** And when I and my son, after the burial,
came to his house to enter into possession of his goods, **the two brothers and Mr
Gilbert said I and my son had nothing to do there, because there was a later testament
wherein they were executors.** I challenged both them and Mr Gilbert; and after some
reasoning, Mr Gilbert and I **fell into a great dispute** about it. And I, minded to
complain to those who would help me of the wrong done to me and my son, **the two
brothers and I agreed that I should have the half, and they the other half**; and Mr
Gilbert **delivered both testaments to me, both subscribed by Peter.** But it is true the
**first testament, wherein my son was executor, was filled up in [certain] parts with
other ink**…"

## Notes & context
- **The heart of the "false testament" charge — from an eyewitness.** French's libel
  (img_0007-ff) accused Power of forging **umquhile Peter McCulloch's testament**. Here
  is the underlying story, sworn by **John McCulloch of Marton (Myrton)** himself: Power
  wrote a **first** deathbed testament making **Marton's son** executor (to Peter's half,
  no children by **Margaret Moore**); then, that same night, wrote a **second** naming
  **Peter's two brothers** as executors — drawn up **out of Peter's sight and when Peter
  was "out of his perfect memory,"** and **concealed until Peter died.** A dispute, then
  a compromise (half and half), then Power handed both signed testaments to Marton — who
  now alleges the **first was altered "with other ink."** This is the concrete factual
  spine of the whole prosecution.
- **Major genealogy yield (the McCulloch focus).** New, precisely-related people:
  - **John McCulloch of Marton** (= **Myrton**, the Galloway seat near Monreith) — the
    laird, deponent, and a **McCulloch**;
  - **his son** (unnamed — "my sone") — the intended executor;
  - **umquhile Peter McCulloch** — the deceased (d. **c.1628**), **childless**, with a
    half-share of goods;
  - **Margaret Moore** — Peter's **spouse**;
  - **Peter's two brothers** — the rival executors (names not given here).
  This ties the "Peter McCulloch false testament" thread (see
  [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]], [[mccollough-genealogy-project]]) to a named,
  landed McCulloch family (Myrton) and dates Peter's death to **c.1628**.
- **The Glenluce "bishop's seat."** The seating act reorganises pews around the old
  **bishop's seat** (a relic of pre-1638 episcopacy) — the same "bishop's seat" a
  minister supplicated for at img_0029. **John and James Rosse** protest again (cf.
  img_0031, James Rosse's grudge against the minister). Kirk-seating disputes were about
  status; worth a wiki note.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `umquhile` = late/deceased; `geir` = goods,
  movable property; `the halfe` = the deceased's half of the marital goods (Scots law:
  goods split between spouses, and the "dead's part"); `out of his perfyt memorie` =
  not of sound mind; `bavidure` = a violent quarrel/altercation (uncertain word);
  `apud acta`; `filled up… With other inke` = the interlineation/alteration alleged.
  Left page badly blotted. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates Old Style.

     given name; check if it appears later.
  2. `bavidure` (the "great [dispute]") — an unusual Scots word; confirm.
  3. The final line: `Was filled up in [ther] peter all pairts, With other inke` — the
     exact allegation of alteration; re-read (it continues onto the next opening).
  4. Left-page seating names: `Crongs`, `Cullessick`, `Balmirreis`, `[James?]`, and
     `William Baillie of Meikle Dinragit` — the pew-holders.
  5. `anno 1628` — confirm Peter McCulloch's year of death.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 85 (folios 84v–85), Oct–Nov 1645 — the mortification goes to the Synod; new moderator; the fugitive Ferguson traced toward Ireland

Left page (folio **84a**): the Glenluce visitation concludes — **James Ross of Barneill will not
deliver the mortification to the presbytery until he sees a minister provided for the kirk**, so the
presbytery **refers the mortification matter to the Synod**; a **privy trial** (ministry and elders
approved); the next meeting to be at the Synod (**Hutcheson, clerk**). Then a new heading, **Presbytery
at Stranraer, 13 November 1645** — an exercise (Mr John Dick made, Mr John Livingstone added, 1 Thess.
1:1–3, approved); the elders of Leswalt and Inch admitted; **Mr John Livingstone chosen moderator and
Mr Hew Hutcheson chosen clerk** (the half-yearly election); and the elders of Stranraer, [Kirkcolm?],
Portmontgomery, and [Kirkmaiden?] challenged for absence. Right page (folio **85**): those elders'
**excuses found relevant**; a **Glenluce transgressor enjoined to sackcloth penance on Sunday 17
November**; **★ Mr John Dick ordained to summon John Ferguson from the pulpit a second time — "who is
fugitive out of the kingdom" — and to write a letter to the presbytery in Ireland to try whether he is
residing in their bounds**; the **vacant charge of Glenluce to be supplied by three brethren in turn
(Turnbull, Hutcheson, Blair)**; and the **next meeting appointed for 3 December** (Livingstone to make,
John Dick to add).

## Notes & context
- **★ The fugitive Ferguson chased across the North Channel.** The **John Ferguson** case (imgs
  0069–0070, 0073–0074, 0076) takes a striking turn: he is now declared **"fugitive out of the
  kingdom,"** so besides a second pulpit summons the presbytery has **Mr John Dick write to "the
  presbytery in Ireland" to discover whether he is residing in their bounds** — the Stranraer court
  using its Ulster church links (Blair, Livingstone) to pursue an offender who has fled to Ireland. A
  vivid instance of **cross-channel church cooperation in criminal discipline**, and the reason the
  earlier excommunication (img 0076) had stalled — the man had simply left the country. Note for the
  Ferguson timeline and the Scotland–Ireland thread.
- **The Glenluce mortification punted upward.** With **James Ross of Barneill refusing to release the
  mortification until a minister is actually settled**, the presbytery **refers the whole finance
  question to the Synod** — the vacancy/endowment deadlock (img 0084) escalating to the higher court.
  And meanwhile the **pulpit is supplied by rotation (Turnbull, Hutcheson, Blair).**
- **The half-yearly election.** At the post-Synod sederunt (13 Nov 1645) the presbytery **chose Mr
  John Livingstone moderator and Mr Hew Hutcheson clerk** — the regular rotation of office (Livingstone,
  min. of Stranraer, evidently now home from Ireland and back in the chair).
- **Ordinary discipline.** A **Glenluce penitent** set to sackcloth on 17 Nov; absent elders' excuses
  accepted — the normal give-and-take of the court.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `referred… to the Synode` = remitted to the provincial assembly;
  `privie, severalie tryed` = the privy trial, each examined apart; `chosen moderator… chosen clerk` =
  the periodic election of officers; `fugitive out of the kingdome` = fled the realm (to Ireland);
  `Writt a letter to the presbytery in Ireland` = correspond with an Ulster presbytery to trace him;
  `supplie the pulpite… by turnes` = provide supply preaching in rotation; `make… add` = deliver and
  second the weekly exercise. **Left page stained** — provisional; right page fairly legible. Capital
  **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1645.

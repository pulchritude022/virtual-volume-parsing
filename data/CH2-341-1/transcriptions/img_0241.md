## Folio 241 (right page)  *(confidence: medium)*

## Notes & context
★★★ **John Campbell driven to a THIRD public admonition — "insolent expressions and behaviour even this day before them."** The long serial of the returned Ireland-malignant (imgs 216–240) reaches its sharpest point: Campbell **hands in yet another paper to defer his process, and behaves "insolently" to the presbytery's face.** They respond by **ordering the process to proceed to a third public admonition** unless his own minister finds him "very penitent"; **Mr James Lawrie, having already found him "very obstinate" at the second admonition, is set to the third.** The disciplinary escalation — first, second, now third admonition — laid out step by step, a textbook of Presbyterian censure meeting an immovable subject.

★★ **The excommunicate John Stewart, chased across the bounds.** The scandalous person flagged to Wigton (img 240) has a name here — **John Stewart** — and the machinery of inter-presbytery citation is visible: **Stranraer asks Wigton to summon him; he does not appear; so Mr William Maxwell is to be written to again to cite him "against the next meeting."** Cross-bounds discipline in slow, dogged motion.

★ **A levy census and a vacancy supplication.** Two small state-of-the-times items: the brethren are told **to bring "the names of all the [—] soldiers within the bounds"** — the presbytery being made to register men for the war (cf. the "outputting of their men," img 240) — and **Mr Alexr Forbes supplicates for a [translation/supply] under the Commission of the General Assembly's recommendation**, with a **contribution for Mr William Forbes** noted. Church and army administration interleaved as usual in autumn 1651. The **8 October 1651** stated meeting opens.

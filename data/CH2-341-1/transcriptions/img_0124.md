# CH2/341/1 — Image 124 (folios 123v–124), 10 November–1 December 1647 — Livingstone sent to Edinburgh for a Stranraer stipend out of the bishopric of Galloway; the Glenluce arrears and Scott's presentation; poor Walter Wilson's relief

Left page (folio **123a**): (Stranraer/Glenluce matter, continued) whereas the gentry of Glenluce **detain
the [arrears] of the mortification, to the no small prejudice of the New kirk of Glenluce**, the presbytery
**★ this day appoints Mr John Livingstone, with all speed convenient, to go to Edinburgh** — in their name
and for himself — **to compear before the Lords and others, Commissioners of Parliament for the plantation
of kirks, to supplicate that a competent stipend may be provided for the kirk of Stranraer out of the rents
of the Bishopric of Galloway (or such other ways as their Lordships shall think fit), and to give reasons
why the same ought to be done… in a cause so [urgent]; 2. that he would deal with the Gentlemen that they
would be pleased to pay to James Ross the arrears of the mortification, and if they be unwilling, [that he
shall] with Balneil pursue the same by [law]; 3. that he shall take with him the presentation subscribed by
the moderator and clerk, and deliver the same to Mr John Scott if he will accept thereof, and, if need be,
to advise with my Lord Advocate anent the form of the presentation**; **Mr James Bell appointed to pray at
Stranraer on Sabbath forenoon days**; then **★ the said day there was a supplication presented to the
presbytery by a poor man called Walter Wilson, [parishioner] of Stranraer, [desiring] the presbytery to
consider his poor condition and that he might be supplied and helped by the several sessions of the
presbytery; which the presbytery, having to their consideration, did recommend him to the several
sessions**; the next meeting appointed **this day twenty days, being the first of December** (Mr Alexander
[Turnbull] to make, Mr John Dick to add). Then **Presbytery at Stranraer, December 1, 1647** — absent Mr John
Livingstone, Mr John Crichton, and the ruling elder of Inch. Right page (folio **124**): **Mr Alexander
Turnbull made and Mr John Dick added the exercise (1 Thess. 4) and both were approved**; the ruling elder of
Leswalt admonished for absence; **the moderator declared that he had written a letter to Ireland concerning
John Bain(?) and Alexander McIlwrick, the answer whereof is not yet come**; **[the moderator, Garthland?]
declared that he had written a letter to Master James Lowrie, and another to Mr John Scott, conform to the
act of the former presbytery**; **★ the moderator engaged the brethren whether the act against disobedients
had been read according to the act of the presbytery — who answered it was read**; **Mr Hew Hutcheson
reported that he had preached at Glenluce according to the directions of the presbytery**; **Mr James Bell
reported that he had preached at Stranraer**; **★ Mr Alexander Turnbull reported that he had written an
answer to the letter presented last day from the presbytery in Ireland, the copy whereof he read in
presbytery and was approved**; **Mr James Blair and Mr John Dick appointed to pray at Stranraer** (forenoon
and afternoon Sabbaths); **Mr James Blair gave in the common head *De redemptione***; the next meeting
appointed at Stranraer **this day month, being the twenty-[ninth] of December** (Mr James Blair to handle the
common head).

### Modern English (partial)
[Whereas the gentry of Glenluce detain the arrears of the mortification, to the no small prejudice of the
New kirk of Glenluce,] **★ the presbytery this day appoints Mr John Livingstone to go with all speed to
Edinburgh — in their name and for himself — to compear before the Lords and others, Commissioners of
Parliament for the plantation of kirks, to supplicate that a competent stipend be provided for the kirk of
Stranraer out of the rents of the Bishopric of Galloway (or as their Lordships think fit), and give reasons
why; 2. to deal with the Gentlemen to pay James Ross the arrears of the mortification, and if unwilling, to
pursue them by law with Balneil; 3. to carry the presentation subscribed by the moderator and clerk and
deliver it to Mr John Scott if he will accept it, advising with my Lord Advocate on its form if need be.**
Mr James Bell to pray at Stranraer on Sabbath forenoons. **★ A supplication by a poor man, Walter Wilson,
parishioner of Stranraer, desiring the presbytery to consider his poor condition and be supplied by the
several sessions — recommended to the several sessions.** Next meeting the first of December (Turnbull to
make, Dick to add). **Presbytery at Stranraer, December 1, 1647.** Absent Livingstone, Crichton, and the
Inch elder.

## Notes & context
- **★★ Livingstone to Edinburgh — a stipend for Stranraer from the bishopric of Galloway.** The presbytery's
  most consequential act here: it **commissions Livingstone to go to Edinburgh and petition the Commissioners
  of Parliament for the Plantation of Kirks for a competent, settled stipend for the kirk of Stranraer "out
  of the rents of the Bishopric of Galloway."** In the Covenanter period the abolished bishops' temporalities
  were a fund for planting parishes, and here the presbytery reaches for the **Galloway bishopric rents** to
  fix Stranraer's chronic under-funding (img 0123). His commission is threefold: **the Stranraer stipend, the
  Glenluce arrears (to be paid to James Ross or pursued at law with Balneil), and delivering Scott's
  presentation** (advising with the Lord Advocate on its form). A rich node linking the presbytery to central
  government and church finance.
- **★ Poor relief — Walter Wilson.** A small but human item: **a poor man, Walter Wilson of Stranraer,
  supplicates the presbytery for maintenance, and is "recommended to the several sessions"** — the mechanism
  by which the presbytery spread the burden of an individual's relief across all its parishes' poor-boxes.
  Good for the "poor relief" glossary node and a person-entry.
- **The Ireland channel again.** The moderator has **written to the presbytery of Ireland concerning John
  Bain(?) and Alexander McIlwrick** (the fled New-Luce adulterer, img 0122) — answer awaited — and Turnbull's
  formal **answer to the Irish presbytery's letter** is read and approved: the two courts in steady
  correspondence across the North Channel.
- **Housekeeping confirmed.** The **discipline act was read in the parishes** (as ordered, img 0122); letters
  written to **Lowrie and Scott** (imgs 0122–0123); **Hutcheson supplied Glenluce and Bell supplied
  Stranraer** (as appointed, img 0123); the **common head *De redemptione*** (Blair) — the doctrine of
  redemption, another Calvinist staple; and supply prayers arranged at Stranraer.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `compeir befor ye Lords and oyers, Commissioners of parliament for
  plantatioun of kirks… out of ye rents of ye Bishoprick of Galloway` = petitioning the parliamentary
  plantation commission for a stipend from the (abolished) bishopric's revenues; `require Wt Balneill to
  pursue ye same by law` = enforcing the arrears through the courts; `a poor man… supplied and helped by ye
  severall sessiones… recommend him to ye severall sessiones` = spreading poor-relief across the sessions;
  `common head De redemptione` = the doctrinal topic of redemption. Firm hand; the Ireland names (John Bain?)
  and the December day are provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1647**.

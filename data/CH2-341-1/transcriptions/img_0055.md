## Notes & context
- **Kirkcolm is planted — Mr James Blair admitted (3 Jan 1644).** This is the **payoff of the
  long Kirkcolm vacancy** (empty since Porterfield's death, imgs 0045–0048; the patronage
  dispute with the Earl of Galloway, img 0050; Blair's presentation and trials, imgs
  0053–0054): on **3 January 1644 the presbytery, meeting at Kirkcolm, admits Mr James Blair
  as minister** — Mr George Dick preaching, the edict served and subscribed (23 Dec), the
  congregation formally asked for objections (none), and Blair received. A clean
  **ordination/admission** set-piece and a firm date for **Blair's charge at Kirkcolm** — key
  for his person-page. See [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]].
- **Glenluce is the next vacancy to fill.** With Kirkcolm settled, the presbytery turns to
  **planting Glenluce** (Turnbull / George Dick to treat with the parishioners) — the Glenluce
  thread (Porterfield's visits, George Park, the Ross/mortification sub-plot) moves toward its
  own settlement. Details are lost to damage; flag.
- **The admission liturgy.** Note the shape: a **preaching moderator**, the **edict served and
  subscribed** in advance, and the **public "objection" question to the congregation** before
  reception — the standard Reformed **admission of a minister**. A glossary/method node on
  "admission / planting a kirk" can cite this and img_0054's trial sequence together.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `Dyet` = an appointed court/church meeting (here the
  "diet of admission"); `edict` = the public citation served beforehand; `intimat` = announced
  from the pulpit; `parochines/parochiners` = the parish/parishioners; `reasoners` (uncertain)
  = those with an interest to be heard; `made [the exercise]` = delivered the set sermon.

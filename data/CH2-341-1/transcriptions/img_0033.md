# CH2/341/1 — Image 33 (folios 32v–33), May 1642 — John McCulloch of Marton's deposition (articles 1–6)

The **written deposition of John McCulloch of Marton** continues (from img_0032),
answering French's interrogatories article by article — a devastating catalogue against
**Mr Gilbert Power**: (1) the altered testament added bequests **in Power's favour** (a
500-merk bond discharge, a beast to Power's wife, Power's Bible); (2) Power **evicted
the tenant Alison McFadzin** with "a great number of men," holding the house eight days;
(3) usury/annualrent taken in meal and bere from **David McCulloch** (Marton pleads
ignorance); (4) Power **melted and sold the great kirk bell** without parish consent
("as he confessed"); (5) Power extracted **corn/teind by decree** ("£10 per boll"); (6)
the earlier accusation before the bishop. A key evidentiary document for the whole case.

### Modern English (partial — left page faded)
[Article 1, concl.:] …[the additions were written] in Power's own hand and in Power's
favour, **which was not done at the writing of the [first] testament**, for Marton was
present himself. The altered testament had **Peter discharge a bond of 500 merks to Mr
Gilbert** (which Peter had lent to **Andrew McDowell of Lochnall**, borrowing "in Mr
Gilbert's name"); **secondly, leave a bull worth a calf, or a stirk, to Mr Gilbert's
wife; thirdly, leave Mr Gilbert his Bible.** Marton, receiving both testaments,
"**confirmed the last, and not this**" — "which was **altered** in this kind."

**Article 2 — the oppression of Alison McFadzin:** Marton got possession of the lands of
**Kirkland** from **[Uchtred] McDowell of Glenluce** and the laird of Marton, of which
**Alison McFadzin was tenant**; Mr Gilbert held a tack in that land, had sub-set it, and
a tangled tack-dispute followed (the tack "miscarried and put by hand," left with agent
**Thomas Dunlop**); Mr Gilbert **took a decree** after being duly paid two years and
more, and **poinded** Marton "in a great storm of frost and snow." [continues →]

### Modern English
[Article 2, concl.:] **Mr Gilbert came with a great number of men and put out the said
Alison [McFadzin], his wife, and all his goods** (or most), and with his people **kept
the house eight days** till he forced Alison to submit and pay him duty; some two years
later Marton got the tack back, obtained a **null decree against Alison**, put him out,
and installed **another tenant of his own who pays sure rent.**

**Article 3 — usury (taking meal or bere for annualrent, severally from David
McCulloch):** Marton "**knows nothing of it to my memory**," nor what Power got from
David McCulloch.

**Article 4 — putting away the kirk bell:** there was "**a little land-bell and a
great (meikle) kirk-bell**," and Marton thinks **Mr Gilbert himself, as he confessed,
put away [melted/sold] the great bell**; **no one of the parish consented**, none got
any of the price, nor does Marton know what price he got.

**Article 5 — taking a peck of corn from the [mill]-land by the strong hand:** true,
Power got a peck of corn out of the land, "but I know him to have no right to it"; Marton
does not know him to take any by force, "**but in a tack of my vicarage set by him**":
Marton owed **two bolls for his teind-land**, and, delaying payment in **1629, 1630,
1631**, Power **took a decree for the two bolls for the three years and made him pay £10
per boll.**

**Article 6 — the accusation before the bishop:** Marton has "already declared I know
nothing" of it.

## Notes & context
- **A six-count indictment, sworn by a McCulloch laird.** John McCulloch of Marton's
  deposition maps almost one-to-one onto French's libel: (1) **falsified testament**,
  (2) **violent eviction** of a tenant, (3) **usury/annualrent** in kind, (4) **selling
  the kirk bell**, (5) **rigorous teind-decrees**, (6) the earlier episcopal accusation.
  Where he has first-hand knowledge (1, 2, 4, 5) he is damning; where he does not (3, 6)
  he honestly pleads ignorance — which makes the deposition more, not less, credible.
- **The altered bequests all favour Power.** The interlineations Marton says were added
  "in Mr Gilbert's own hand… in Mr Gilbert's favour": a **500-merk bond discharged to
  Power**, a **beast (bull/stirk) to Power's wife**, and **Power's Bible** — i.e. the
  forgery enriched the minister. Concrete and quotable.
- **"Mr Gilbert… put away the meikle kirk bell — as he confessed."** A vivid charge: the
  minister **melted down or sold the great kirk bell** of (Stoneykirk?) without parish
  consent and pocketed the price. Great colour for the wiki and a serious canonical
  offence (alienating kirk goods).
- **The kirk bell / bell metal.** Distinguishes a **"little land-bell"** (a hand-bell)
  from the **"meikle kirk-bell"** (the tower bell). Worth a glossary entry.
- **Genealogy / people.** Confirms **David McCulloch** in the usury/annualrent thread
  (cf. memory: David paid Power's annualrent in bere 1639–40); adds **Alison McFadzin**
  (an evicted **male** tenant, with a wife — not the "widow" of earlier notes), **Andrew
  McDowell of Lochnall** (a 500-merk borrower), **Thomas Dunlop** (agent), and
  **Uchtred McDowell of Glenluce** as a prior landholder of **Kirkland**. Dates Peter
  McCulloch's estate/testament to **c.1628** and Marton's teind dispute to **1629–31**.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `allay putting` = putting away / doing away with
  (here melting or selling the bell); `meikel` = great/large; `annuell` = annualrent
  (interest on a loan, the usury charge); `boll` = a dry measure of grain; `ilk` = each;
  `strak out the tak` = struck out/cancelled the tack; `poinded` = distrained goods for
  debt; `null decreet` = a decree of nullity (voiding Alison's tack). Left page heavily
  blotted. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates Old Style.

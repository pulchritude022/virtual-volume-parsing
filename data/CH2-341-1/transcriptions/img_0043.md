# CH2/341/1 — Image 43 (folios 42v–43), June–July 1642 — visitations; Marion Power summoned; GA commissioners chosen

Left page (folio **42a**) finishes the **Kirkmaiden visitation (23 June)** — **Mr
Alexander Turnbull** preached (Rom 11:8–9), the minister and elders mutually approved —
then the striking item: **Garthland produces a Stoneykirk-elders' protestation against
Marion Power** (Gilbert Power's daughter) for a **Lord's-day disturbance "within and
without the kirk" against Mr Alexander Turnbull** on **19 June**; she is summoned to
Portpatrick for **6 July**. **James Rosse** dodges again. Right page (folio **43**): the
**Portmontgomerie (Portpatrick) visitation, 6 July** (**Mr James Blair** preached Heb
13:12–14), and the election of **commissioners to the General Assembly at St Andrews** —
**Mr George Dick** and **Mr John Livingston** (in Ireland; **Mr Alexander Turnbull** to go
in his stead), with **Garthland** (or failing him, **the laird of Logan**) as ruling
elder. **This anchors the whole section to 1642** (the GA met at St Andrews in 1642).

### Modern English
**Kirkmaiden visitation (23 June):** **Mr Alexander Turnbull preached (Rom 11:8–9)** and
was approved; the **Session book** revised and approved; the minister, asked if he had
anything against the elders, "**was sorry for their slackness**" [in visiting/providing];
the elders **approved their minister** in doctrine, discipline, and life.

That day **Garthland produced a protestation from the elders of Stoneykirk for the trial
and censure of Marion Power** — for [a disturbance] "**both within the kirk and without
the kirk, against Mr Alexander Turnbull and others, upon the Lord's day, 19 June
1642.**" The presbytery **ordained Marion Power to be summoned to Portpatrick for 6
July.** **James Rosse of Balneil**, summoned *apud acta*, was called, **compeared not**,
and was **summoned a second time (pro 2do)** to Portpatrick, 6 July.

That day **Mr John Livingston was chosen commissioner to the General Assembly to be held
at St Andrews**; the clerk to send **testimonials and an excuse for absence**, he **being
in Ireland for the time.**

### Modern English
**Presbytery at Portmontgomerie (Portpatrick) for visitation, 6 July 1642.** **Mr James
Blair, minister there, preached (Heb 13:12–14)** and was approved (absent: Mr John Dick;
the elders of [Inch] and Stranraer). The elders **approved their minister** in doctrine,
discipline, life, and conversation; the minister asked that the elders **bring the people
of their quarters to catechising more frequently**, and he would **help in catechising**
himself.

**Mr George Dick was chosen commissioner to the General Assembly, together with Mr John
Livingston; and, failing Livingston's return from Ireland, Mr Alexander Turnbull is
chosen to go in his stead.** **Garthland was chosen [ruling-elder] commissioner; failing
Garthland, the laird of Logan.** The commissioners **received their commission with
promise of fidelity.** The **minister of Glenluce craved liberty to go to Edinburgh
before the General Assembly** on his own urgent affairs (and the parish business of
**Balneil** being at Edinburgh, that business to be delayed till he returns) — granted.
**This being the presbytery day immediately preceding the Assembly, the appointment of a
new diet is delayed till the commissioners return.**

## Notes & context
- **CHRONOLOGY CONFIRMED — this section is 1642, not 1643.** The presbytery elects
  commissioners to **"the General Assembly to be held at St Andrews"** and notes "**this
  being the presbytery day immediately preceding the Assembly.**" The General Assembly met
  at **St Andrews in July 1642** (the 1643 GA was at Edinburgh). Together with **Livingston
  being in Ireland** (Monro's Ulster army landed April 1642) and the MS's own **"1642"**
  dates, this **fixes the deposition (16 July 1642) and the whole live-register stretch
  (imgs 0036–0043) to 1642.** **IMPORTANT CORRECTION:** my transcriptions of **imgs
  0036–0042 that read/dated "1643" are wrong — they are 1642** (I misread the final digit;
  the MS writes 1642). Power's Synod appeal (front folios ~22–28) is the genuinely **1643**
  material — Power appealing his **1642 deposition**. See [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]].
  The frontmatter `year` of imgs 0036–0042 is being corrected to 1642.
- **Marion Power strikes back.** Gilbert Power's daughter **Marion Power** (cf. memory:
  m. Alexander McDowell) causes a **Lord's-day disturbance "within and without the kirk"
  against Mr Alexander Turnbull** — one of the ministers who deposed her father — days
  before/after the deposition. A vivid family-loyalty episode; she is summoned for trial
  (6 July, Portpatrick). Track as its own mini-event and a Power-family node.
- **The presbytery's GA delegation (1642).** Ministers: **Mr George Dick** + **Mr John
  Livingston** (or **Mr Alexander Turnbull** if Livingston stays in Ireland). Ruling elder:
  **Garthland** (or **the laird of Logan**). Useful for placing these men in the wider
  Covenanter church at the 1642 St Andrews Assembly.
- **Routine parish life continues:** Kirkmaiden and Portpatrick visitations (mutual
  approval of ministers/elders), the push for **catechising by parish quarters**, and the
  **Glenluce minister's** Edinburgh errand (tied to the **Balneil** parish-division
  business, cf. the new kirk sited at Balneil, memory).
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `apud acta` = summoned in open court; `pro 2do` =
  a second citation; `catechising… in their quarters` = house-to-house catechism by parish
  district; `promise of fidelitie` = the commissioners' oath of faithful representation;
  `coming ober` = coming over (back from Ireland). Capital **W** for *w/wh*. **Dates 1642.**

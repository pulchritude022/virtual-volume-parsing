### Modern English
**Mr Hugh Hutcheson and Mr John Park reported that Alexr Agnew (who bore office as Lieutenant-Colonel in the late unlawful wars) had confessed his guilt in acceding to those unlawful wars, and was received to the Covenant.** **William Broune to be cited to the next meeting at Stranraer for his accession to the abovenamed combat.** **Mr Alexr Turnbull reported that his letter to Garthland about sending for George Montgomerie (who challenged Gilbert Kennedy to the combat) will be of no purpose until the presbytery have more freedom of meeting; and therefore forbore "till the Lord shall be pleased to give more freedom to the meetings of this kirk."** **Mr John Park reported he had spoken James Kennedy and got assurance that George Adestoun should not be molested by him until required to appear before the presbytery.** **Mr John Scott reported that he heard the Commission of the Kirk was risen and could get no further advice from it about foreign malignants; whereupon the presbytery appointed that the commissioners who repair to the General Assembly should consult it thereanent.** **Mr Hugh Hutcheson to see the Sheriff about dealing with the Committee of War, that some common course be taken for restraining masterless wandering beggars.** **Mr John Scott reported that Arisemman was very sore sick and unable to attend; to be required to attend next meeting if his health serve.**

## Notes & context
★★ **The Engagement reconciliation essentially completed.** In one sitting the presbytery **closes out its principal Engagement penitents: Lt-Col Alexr Agnew, Patrick McKie, and William Baillie are all "received to the Covenant,"** and **James Ross of Balneil's process** is finally delivered into his own hands to carry to the **Commission of the Kirk** (which he keeps just missing — it "was risen"). This is the practical end of the great 1648-Engagement reckoning that has run since img 149.

★★ **"Till the Lord give more freedom to the meetings of this kirk."** A quietly telling line: **Turnbull forbears to press the summons of George Montgomerie (the duellist) "until the presbytery have more freedom of meeting"** — a hint of the disruption of church business in the anxious summer of 1650 (between Montrose's execution and Cromwell's July invasion). The **Commission of the Kirk "risen"** and the foreign-malignants question deferred to the **General Assembly** (which met July 1650) point the same way.

★ **Social-order housekeeping.** The **Sheriff and Committee of War** to be dealt with for "restraining masterless wandering beggars"; the **Portpatrick/Argrenan perambulation** committee reconstituted (Dick, Turnbull, Park, and the Sheriff); routine discipline (Margaret McLourie's fornication, the stubborn Andrew Gyle). And **George Adestoun**, having finally made his public confession, **has fled to Ireland** — the ordinance held over against his return.

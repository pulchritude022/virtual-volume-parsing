# CH2/341/1 — Image 78 (folios 77v–78), July–Aug 1645 — mustering the presbytery's forces; a solemn humiliation; an August kirk visitation

Left page (folio **77a**): the 2 July sitting continues — **a letter from the General Assembly
[Commission], directed at Perth 18 July 1645, requiring the whole presbytery to stir up their people
to be in the readiness appointed by Parliament and to send forward the presbytery's forces**, and
appointing **a solemn humiliation on 26 July for the great distress of the land**; the brethren
**undertake to stir up their people, and appoint Mr George Dick to attend the forces to be levied out
of the presbytery of Stranraer**; **the fast to be intimated for Saturday 26 July and the Lord's day
following**; **Mr John Livingstone appointed to repair to the Assembly at Perth**; and a **visitation
of Inch appointed for the 20th** — then a new heading, **a presbytery for the visitation [of Inch],
[13] August 1645** (Mr John Dick preaching, approved; Turnbull and others absent). Right page (folio
**78**): the **appointed visitation was not kept, in respect of the small attendance of the
parishioners**; a **standard visitation examination** is nonetheless made (the elder interrogated —
the people carry themselves orderly, the session concurs in discipline; the minister removed and
examined, approved); and **Patrick [—], for gross contempt and slandering of the [kirk] session, is
enjoined to make satisfaction to the session under pain of excommunication.**

## Notes & context
- **★ The presbytery mustered for war (July 1645).** A vivid snapshot of the Kirk mobilising against
  Montrose: the **General Assembly's Commission (at Perth, 18 July 1645) orders the presbytery to
  "stir up their people" to the military readiness appointed by Parliament and to send forward its
  forces**; the brethren comply and appoint **Mr George Dick to attend the forces levied out of the
  presbytery of Stranraer** — the parish clergy acting as recruiting agents in the crisis summer of
  1645 (Montrose rampant in the north). A strong theme node (the 1645 levy) tying Galloway to the
  national war.
- **A solemn humiliation "for the great distress of the land."** A **fast/humiliation appointed for
  Saturday 26 July and the following Lord's day** — the spiritual counterpart to the levy, in a year
  of plague, defeat, and Montrose's victories.
- **Livingstone to the Assembly at Perth.** **Mr John Livingstone appointed to repair to the Assembly
  at Perth** — implying he was **back from his spring Ireland mission** (img 0070) by mid-July 1645;
  a datum for his movements (the 1645 General Assembly/Commission met partly at Perth to avoid
  plague-struck Edinburgh).
- **A routine kirk visitation, deferred.** The **Inch visitation (13 Aug 1645) could not be held for
  low attendance**, but the standard visitation catechism was still run (people orderly, session
  concurs in discipline, minister approved) — a good template of how a parish visitation was
  conducted. And **Patrick [—]'s slander of the session**, punished with the threat of
  excommunication, shows the session defending its authority.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `readines appointed be the parliament… send forward the forces`
  = the military levy/mobilisation ordered by Parliament; `solemne humiliatioun… for the greate
  Distresse of the land` = an appointed fast of national repentance; `Committee for the… Assemblie`
  = the Commission of the General Assembly; `repair to the Assemblie at Perth` = go as commissioner
  to the Assembly (held at Perth in 1645); `carryed them selves orderlie` = behaved in good order;
  `did concur in administratioun of Discipline` = cooperated in exercising kirk discipline; `under the
  paine of excommunicatioun` = on penalty of excommunication. **Both pages faded** — provisional.
  Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1645.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 231 (folios 230a–231)

## Folio 230a (left page)  *(confidence: low)*

*Still 21 May 1651. Thomas Myrton and Mary McNish of old Glenluce — who had formerly fallen in adultery, and (since the death of Myrton's wife) resumed an "uncomely carriage" — both confess, are sharply rebuked, and remitted to their session. Then the Laird of Garthland gives in a **supplication** (copied verbatim) touching a process before the presbytery, at the instance of Mr John Blair and inhabitants of the lands of [Spittal] within the parish of Stoneykirk, over the **dismembering of certain lands from Stoneykirk**. Water-stained. Side-margin captions: "Thos Myrton", "Garthland supplicatioun", "for dividing".*

## Notes & context
★★ **Garthland's teind-dispute supplication — a parish-boundary fight in Stoneykirk.** Fresh from his Engagement rehabilitation (imgs 220–225), **James McDowall, Laird of Garthland**, appears here in a wholly secular capacity: as **heritable tacksman of the teinds of Stoneykirk** (out of which he pays "a great proportion of stipend to the ministers"), he petitions against a process — brought by **Mr John Blair and the inhabitants of the lands of [Spittal]** — to **dismember certain lands from Stoneykirk and annex them to Portmontgomery** (i.e. Portpatrick). He argues the change would derogate the teinds he pays, void his tack, and prejudice the Stoneykirk minister's stipend. This revives the long-running **Portpatrick / Stoneykirk boundary controversy** (imgs 182–183, 192), now framed as a teind-and-stipend money question, with **the Laird of Freugh speaking for the parish** on the other side and the presbytery sending the parties to confer "without scandal of the people."

★ **Adultery resumed after a spouse's death.** **Thomas Myrton and Mary McNish** of old Glenluce, who had "formerly fallen in adultery together," resume their "uncomely carriage" once Myrton is widowed; both confess and are remitted to the session — a compact instance of the presbytery tracking a relapsed relationship across a change in marital status.

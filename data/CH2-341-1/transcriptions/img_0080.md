# CH2/341/1 — Image 80 (folios 79v–80), August 1645 — the Assembly's engrossed "catalogue of sins": enormities in the ministry and among the people

Both pages engross **the catalogue of "enormities and corruptions" enclosed with the General
Assembly's circular of 5 August 1645** (imgs 0078–0079), which the presbytery ordered written into its
book. Left page (folio **79a**) heads it **"Enormities and corruptions in the ministry and the
remnant thereof, recommended to the several presbyteries,"** followed by a numbered list of
ministers' failings — walking in a natural way without employing Christ; (1) frequenting and complying
with sinners of all sorts; (2) great worldly-mindedness, forgetting "the main"; (3) slighting God's
worship in families; (4) want of gravity in carriage and apparel, dissoluteness of life, lightness in
the apparel of wives and children; (5) excessive drinking in taverns and alehouses; (6)
discountenancing the godly and speaking ill of the brethren; (7) the Sabbath not sanctified after
sermon ("as if the Sabbath ended with the sermon"); (8) swearing and oaths; (9) sluggardness in duty.
Right page (folio **80**) opens a second head, **"To all the people of God — In our calling,"** with a
further numbered list touching the ministers' duty in the **public cause**: (1) following the old
courses God forsook; (2) treating the ministry as an unsanctified worldly calling rather than a
spiritual one; (3) partiality in dealing with the scandalous and forgetting the common good; (4)
silence in the public cause and coldness in bearing public burdens (some so gross that in public fasts
"little or nothing" is done); (5) ambiguous speaking and pleading for "the wicked cause," turning
people's hearts from obeying God's instruments in this work; (6) idleness — seldom preaching, or
preaching without study, reading, and meditation.

## Notes & context
- **★★ The 1645 "catalogue of sins" — a self-examination of the ministry.** This is the enclosed paper
  of the General Assembly's circular (imgs 0078–0079): a remarkable **numbered examination of the
  "enormities and corruptions in the ministry," recommended to every presbytery**, and a parallel list
  on the duties of ministers "in the public cause." It reads as the Covenanting Kirk turning its
  reforming zeal inward in the crisis of 1645 — indicting ministers for worldliness, neglected family
  worship, tavern-drinking, lightness in dress, Sabbath laxity, cold preaching, and (most tellingly)
  **lukewarmness or double-dealing in "the public cause"** (the Covenant/war): ambiguous speech,
  pleading "the wicked cause," turning people from "God's instruments in this work." A superb primary
  text for the wiki — quotable, and revealing of what the Covenanters thought a faithful minister and
  people should be. Theme/glossary nodes: **"causes of wrath" / national self-examination**, and
  **"the public cause."**
- **Value despite the fading.** Both pages are badly faded, so the wording above is provisional — but
  the *shape* (two headed, numbered lists of ministerial/popular sins) is clear and worth flagging for
  a careful re-reading against the original, since the full text would make an excellent set-piece for
  the site.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `Enormities and corruptiones in the ministrie… recommended to
  the severall presbyteries` = a checklist of clerical failings circulated for self-examination;
  `Walking in a naturall Way without imploying Christ` = living by nature, not by faith; `Worldlie
  mindednes… forgetting the Maine` = worldliness, neglecting the chief thing; `the Sabbath… ended With
  the Sermon` = treating the sermon as the whole of Sabbath duty; `the publick cause` = the Covenant
  and the war effort; `Gods Instruments in this Worke` = the leaders of the Covenanting cause. **Both
  pages faded** — provisional throughout. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Date 1645.

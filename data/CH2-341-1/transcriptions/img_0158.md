# CH2/341/1 — Image 158 (folios 157v–158)

The record crosses into **1649**. Left page (foliated **157a** = folio 157 verso)
finishes the 27 December business: ★ **Blair and Lowrie report back from Wigtown** —
they spoke to **my Lord Galloway** about the **Sheriff-younger**, and Galloway's answer
was that he was **not to arrest the Sheriff of anything, but if challenged would answer
in writing**; **How Hutcheson** is to receive [the Sheriff?] to the Covenant on his
repentance; ★ several Stranraer men — **Cowan (elder), Alexander McBryd, Alexander
McMurrie, George McRalstoun, William Gordon** — are referred by the session **for
breaking the fast** and summoned; and commissioners are appointed to press the committee
again for a sight of Balneil's process. Then opens the ★ **presbytery at Stranraer, 2
January 1649** (extraordinary, no exercise; Blair moderator *pro tempore*). The right
page (folio 158): the ★ **glebe-and-manse contract for the old and new kirks of Glenluce
is presented and read**; ★★ **Crosse and Crookes report the committee would not sit at
Glenluce, but the gentlemen said they had nothing to say against Balneil and the
presbytery might proceed without them**; a **letter from the absent moderator** asks the
brethren to meet the morrow; and **Balneil's process is again delayed** (to 3 January),
the presbytery noting he answered "wisely" to several points.

### Modern English
[widows/orphans money] ...would be delivered to the church upon demand.

**Mr James Blair and Mr James Lowrie reported** they went to Wigtown to keep
correspondence, and had spoken to **my Lord Galloway concerning the Sheriff-younger**;
his answer was that ★ **he was not to arrest the Sheriff of anything, but if the Sheriff
challenged him about any breach, he desired it in writing, and he would answer**. The
church appoints **Mr How Hutcheson to receive [the Sheriff] to the Covenant on
confession of his repentance** for his dealing with my Lord Galloway, next Sabbath.

The same day, the session of Stranraer referred ★ **Cowan (elder), Alexander McBryd,
Alexander McMurrie, George McRalstoun and William Gordon for breaking the fast on
Thursday last**; called, they compeared not, and are ordained to be summoned. The
presbytery appoints Tuesday for trying Balneil's process, and (sitting Saturday) appoints
**Mr James Dick, Mr John Crosse and Mr John Crookes** to keep correspondence with [the
committee] and earnestly entreat a **copy — or at least a sight — of Balneil's process**.

**Presbytery held at Stranraer, 2 January 1649.** No exercise, the presbytery being
extraordinarily convened. Absent: Mr John Dick, Mr Alexander Turnbull, Mr James Lowrie;
the ruling elders of Kirkmaiden, Stoneykirk, Portmontgomery, [Colvend], [Airieholland].
**Mr James Blair appointed moderator pro tempore.**

### Modern English
The **contract of the glebe and manse at the old kirk of Glenluce (with one at the new
kirk) is presented and read**, appointed to be [engrossed] against the next presbytery,
that it may be judged.

**Mr John Crosse and Mr John Crookes reported** they had attended the committee at
Glenluce on Saturday, but ★ **the committee did not sit**; so they spoke to some of the
gentlemen of the committee, showing that the presbytery had appointed them to desire a
sight of Balneil's process. The gentlemen answered that if the presbytery had a sight of
the process of the committee held at Stranraer, they doubted not the presbytery knew
enough of what was in it. Being asked whether they would give the presbytery any sight of
Balneil's [dealings] — whether he furnished any hand or took anything for raising the
Engagement — they answered that ★★ **Balneil had declared himself before them that he had
not done it; and that they had nothing to say against Balneil, but that the presbytery
might proceed without them.**

A **letter from the absent moderator** was presented, desiring the brethren to meet on the
morrow at Stranraer. The presbytery, several brethren being absent, **delay
consideration of James Ross of Balneil's process until the morrow** (he summoned *apud
acta*), considering that **he had answered wisely to several of the points**...

## Notes & context
- ★★ **The committee has "nothing to say against Balneil."** The decisive external
  verdict in the Balneil case: the civil committee's own gentlemen, when pressed, admit
  they have **nothing against him** and that the presbytery may proceed without them. Read
  with the session's "ingenuously declared the truth" (img 155), this leaves Balneil
  effectively cleared — a notable outcome given the nine-article indictment.
- ★ **Galloway vs the Sheriff-younger, defused.** My Lord Galloway declines to press the
  charge, offering only a written answer if challenged — the county magnates closing ranks
  and letting the matter drop, while the church still requires the Sheriff's Covenant-
  repentance.
- ★ **Breaking the fast — named offenders.** Five Stranraer men (**Cowan the elder,
  Alexander McBryd, Alexander McMurrie, George McRalstoun, William Gordon**) are cited for
  **breaking the fast** kept for the Covenant renewal — ordinary parishioners caught by
  the same discipline as the gentry, useful for the burgh's prosopography.
- ★ **Glebe/manse contract engrossed.** The Glenluce settlement (img 157) is now a written
  **contract** for both kirks, presented for judicial approval — the plantation's material
  problem nearly closed.
- **New year:** the register turns to **1649**; the Balneil process rolls from 27 Dec →
  2 Jan → 3 Jan, tracked across sittings.

## Folio 225a (left page)  *(confidence: low)*

*Still the 9 April 1651 sitting. Robert Campbell at last compears and, the charges laid to him, purges himself by oath on the first article but confesses the second (his compliance with malignants and adhesion to the malignant course in Ireland under my Lord of Ardes) — so the presbytery appoints him to rise before the congregation and evidence his repentance publicly. Then a string of "no report" entries deferred for the absence of Scott and Dick (Dinnaggot; Morion/Marchbanks). Heavily water-stained. ## Notes & context
★★ **Robert Campbell splits the charge — oath on the one, confession on the other.** The long Robert-Campbell duel (imgs 210–224) reaches its hinge: **he purges himself by oath** of the graver allegation — that in "gathering the distressed" out of Ireland he **wronged honest people** or hindered those "escaping to Scotland for conscience sake" — but **cannot deny, and confesses, "his compliance with malignants and his adhesion to the malignant course,"** having "continued to lift the distressed … from my Lord of Ardes" (**Viscount Montgomery of the Great Ards**, cf. imgs 219, 223) in a course "against the rebels." For that (and his "contumacy and rude carriage") the presbytery **orders him to rise before the congregation after the sermon and evidence his repentance publicly** — the defiant "What! malignancy in me!" man finally set to the stool.

★ **A presbytery thinned to a standstill.** Fully **six items are deferred "no report"** for the absence of **Mr John Scott** and **Mr John Dick** (the two most active investigators) — Dinnaggot, the Morion/Marchbanks case, Margaret McClounie, Arisemman, and John McChristie's registration; Dick "who should have brought in the legal procedure in writing" is absent. The sitting closes noting the presbytery **"not being frequent"** (poorly attended) and delays the rest — another trace of the wartime disruption (cf. imgs 222, 225).

★ **A new adultery referral — Patrick Crosbie and Janet Gib.** From the parish of **[Cassnall]** the session refers up **Patrick Crosbie**, who **compears and confesses adultery** with **Janet Gib**; warned of his danger, he is **remitted to his own session** for censure "according to the Act of the General Assembly," while Janet Gib (absent) is cited a second time — a textbook presbytery-to-session remit.

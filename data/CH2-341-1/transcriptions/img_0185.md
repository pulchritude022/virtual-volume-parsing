### Modern English
… [who] did rob the poor Englishman.

**Mr John Park reported they met with Major John McDougall, who professed grief for his engaging in the Last unlawful Engagement;** he is to meet again with Currie, Park, and Dick, that they may report what obedience and repentance they find in him.

**Mr Alexr Turnbull reported that the Laird of Garthland is minded to go and subscribe by the last of May.** Dick, Currie, and Turnbull are to warn him that **if he does not go to the Commission of the Kirk by its next meeting, the presbytery will be forced to go on with the process against him.**

**The presbytery ordains Lt-Col [Alexr Stewart] to give publicly a brief acknowledgment of his sorrow for engaging in the Last unlawful Engagement, in his parish kirk**; the minister to report his obedience and signs of repentance. **Lieutenant [Agnew] in [Ardmenoch] to confer with Currie and Park about his signs of repentance for engaging** in the unlawful Engagement.

**Mr John Park reported that he had summoned George Montgomerie and Cochrane of Chappell over a combat between them.** Chappell did not compear; **William Broune, one of the witnesses, deposed about the combat between Chappell and George Montgomerie.**

### Modern English
**Unto the Reverend the moderator and the remnant of the presbytery of Stranraer.** I, your servant **William Broune**, being desired to appear before your presbytery to testify concerning a combat between **Capt George Montgomerie and Gilbert Cochrane of Chappell** — these are to testify that I, **being in my bed about ten [o'clock], was desired to carry a note to the said Gilbert Cochrane, having been told it was for an appointment to combat. So, to hinder it, I told Gilbert Cochrane that Capt George Montgomerie had it in mind, and desired him to forbear. He answered that he had not forborne, for he never called for the note … and refused; and thereupon they [went]. But the combat of their swords was not seen by me; so I went again to my own bed, and what further was done I never saw nor concealed.** This for my conscience, as I shall answer to God, averring I am innocent and would be loath to do offence to any, so far as the Lord shall direct. **Sic subscribitur, William Broune.**

**Robert Campbell and his son appeared this day, referred to next presbytery. Capt [John] Stewart appeared, referred to next presbytery.** The moderator satisfied about the Declaration of the last Assembly [read]. Mr John Crosse to preach next presbytery day at Stranraer. **A perambulation of Stoneykirk to be at the first meeting after the Synod.** Mr James Boyd to appear to give signs of repentance for his fall in fornication and his disobedience.

## Notes & context
★★ **A gentlemen's duel, and the kirk's eyewitness.** The Montgomerie/Chappell "combat" of imgs 183–184 gets its evidence: **William Broune's signed deposition**, a rare first-person vignette of a near-duel — he was roused from bed "about ten o'clock" and asked to carry a note arranging a combat between **Capt George Montgomerie and Gilbert Cochrane of Chappell**; he tried to head it off by telling Cochrane to forbear, but Cochrane refused; "the combat of their swords was not seen by me," and he went back to bed. A vivid slice of quarrelsome Rhins gentry society, and of the kirk-session policing private violence.

★★ **The Engagement satisfactions escalate up the officer class.** A concentrated run of military penitents: **Major John McDougall** "professed grief for his engaging"; a **Lt-Colonel** ordered to make a public acknowledgment in his parish kirk; **Lieutenant Agnew** to confer about his repentance; and — pointedly — **the Laird of Garthland put under ultimatum: subscribe/appear at the Commission of the Kirk by its next meeting, or the presbytery "will be forced to go on with the process against him."** Even the presbytery's greatest patron is being held to the same discipline (cf. his supplication, img 180).

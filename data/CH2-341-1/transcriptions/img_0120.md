## Notes & context
- **★★ The financial anatomy of the Glenluce division — in full.** This opening is the single clearest
  statement of how the June-1646 General Assembly split Glenluce (cf. imgs 0106, 0119): **the OLD kirk
  (Scott) gets the mortified 1,000 merks + the Abbey and teind + manse and glebe; the NEW kirk (Crichton)
  gets the 620 merks of local stipend + the surplus + the arrears of the mortification.** Both the **gift of
  mortification** and the **GA act of 17 June 1646** are noted as **ratified in Parliament** — the funding
  now doubly secured, in church and civil law. A definitive source for the two Glenluce person/place nodes.
- **★ A minister protests his own presentation.** Remarkably, **Scott does not simply accept — he gives in a
  signed protestation with four legal objections**, arguing the stipend is "debatable" (i.e. contestable at
  law rather than firmly secured from the heritors), that it runs against Assembly acts, that it "defrauds"
  the old kirk of its old locality, and that it is "very prejudicial." He still subscribes ("Mr John
  Scott"), and the presbytery replies it **cannot alter the tenor of the presentation** given the binding GA
  act. A vivid instance of a settled minister negotiating hard over the security of his living even as he is
  planted — good colour for Scott's biography and for a glossary note on *presentation / stipend security*.
- **The death-or-removal clause.** A precise legal safeguard: at Scott's death or removal, neither he nor
  his heirs may claim beyond **the half-year's rent then current** — standard protection of the benefice's
  continuity.
- **Housekeeping.** The next exercise (Bell to make, Blair to add) and the **6 October** Stranraer meeting
  close the opening.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `offered… a presentatioun to ye old kirk of Glenluce, and to ye
  mortified stipend of a thousand merks… for manse and gleib` = presenting a minister to a benefice with its
  endowment; `gift of mortification ratified in parliament… ye act of g. assemblie… also ratified in
  parliament` = the endowment and division confirmed by statute; `a debatable stipend, bot not secured furth
  of ye heritors… attendance at ye plea in law` = a stipend exposed to litigation rather than firmly charged
  on the lands; `ye presbyterie could not alter ye tenor of ye presentatioun` = the local court bound by the
  Assembly's terms. **Both pages heavily faded/torn** — the New-kirk minister's name (read as **Crichton**),
  the Latin tag *quod tribuo*, and several figures are provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1647**.

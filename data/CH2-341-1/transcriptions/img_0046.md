# CH2/341/1 — Image 46 (folios 45v–46), Dec 1642 – Jan 1643 — the Stoneykirk tumult; Power still protesting

Left page (folio **45a**): **Presbytery at Stranraer, 14 December 1642** — cross-presbytery
**commissioners for the Stoneykirk business** arrive (**Mr George Galloway & Mr Andrew
Adair** from Wigtown; **Mr James Inglis & the Goodman of Girvanmill** from Ayr), and **Mr
Gilbert Power — though deposed — protests against all the presbytery's elections and
acts**, the moderator answering. Right page (folio **46**): the account of the **tumult at
Stoneykirk** — **women "made a great deal of tumultuous stir in the kirkyard and kirk,"
obstructing Mr James Turner, a commissioner from the General Assembly** — with **Power and
Blair examined on who was present** (a named list). Then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 11
January 1643** (the register crosses into 1643). *(Both pages badly browned.)*

### Modern English (partial — page faded)
**Presbytery at Stranraer, 14 December 1642.** Exercise: **Mr Hugh Hutcheson made it, Mr
James Blair added, on Colossians 3:12** — approved. Absent: Mr George Dick; the ruling
elders of Inch, Glenluce, Stoneykirk. **Mr John McClellane** [reported] on the Kirkcudbright
business. **Commissioners to keep correspondence in the Stoneykirk business:** **Mr George
Galloway and Mr Andrew Adair** from the **presbytery of Wigtown**; **Mr James Inglis and
the Goodman of Girvanmill** from the **presbytery of Ayr**. **Mr Gilbert Power protested
against all the elections and acts of the presbytery** — the **moderator answering that
what Power disowns "was done by the whole presbytery in their lawful elections."** Various
men (Mr James Blair, James Kennedy, Alexander Innes, Mr Andrew Adair, Josua McKay) were
severally desired to give their **suffrage**.

### Modern English (partial — page faded)
[The tumult at Stoneykirk:] **the women there made "a great deal of tumultuous stir, both
in the kirkyard and kirk,"** and even in the kirk **confessed speaking and stirring on the
Lord's day**, obstructing **Mr James Turner, a commissioner from the General Assembly**, and
putting him to trouble within the kirk; and thereupon they "**geared [uttered] sundry
importunate and injurious [outcries]**," so the speaker could not be heard. **Mr Gilbert
Power and Mr James Blair, examined on who was present at the Sabbath tumult**, named:
**Christian of [Miluel], the Lady of French[?] and [others] with their daughters, the
Goodman of Barlockhart, James Cathcart, [—] Dunbar, James Grey, Robert McClearnan, James
McDowell of [—]field**, and others. **The moderator asked the votes on how to proceed**;
it was resolved (one voice) to **deal first with the [Stoneykirk] Session**; and the
**Stoneykirk elders to come before the presbytery**. **Mr James Blair to make, and Mr
Alexander Turnbull to add, the exercise next day, 11 January 1643.**

**Presbytery at Stranraer, 11 January 1643.** Mr James Blair made, and Mr Alexander
Turnbull added, the exercise **on Colossians 3:13–14** — approved. Mr George Dick and Mr
John Dick absent…

## Notes & context
- **The Stoneykirk tumult — the deposition backfires.** A vivid scene: after Power's
  deposition, **the women of Stoneykirk riot "in the kirkyard and kirk,"** disrupting a
  **General Assembly commissioner (Mr James Turner)** on the Lord's Day with "importunate
  and injurious" outcries. **Power and Blair are examined on who took part** — yielding a
  **named crowd** (the Goodman of Barlockhart, James Cathcart, James Grey, Robert
  McClearnan, James McDowell, and women with their daughters). This is **popular resistance
  to the deposition** — a discrete, colourful event for the wiki, and a cluster of
  **pro-Power parishioners** to link. (The presence of "**the Lady of French**" is
  surprising given French is the prosecutor — the reading is uncertain; flag.)
- **Power will not accept the deposition.** Even after being deposed, **Power keeps
  protesting "against all the elections and acts of the presbytery"** (14 Dec) — the
  moderator insisting the acts were "**done by the whole presbytery in their lawful
  elections.**" His resistance continues into 1643 (and connects to his Synod/GA appeal,
  the front folios). Key for his biography: he did not go quietly.
- **A cross-presbytery apparatus for Stoneykirk.** Commissioners from **Wigtown (George
  Galloway, Andrew Adair)** and **Ayr (James Inglis, Goodman of Girvanmill)**, plus the
  **Kirkcudbright** business (**Mr John McClellane**) and the **Synod of Ayr & Galloway** —
  the whole region is managing the Stoneykirk aftermath. New people/place nodes.
- **CALENDAR CORRECTION (important for the whole project).** The register crosses from
  **December 1642 to "11 January 1643"** written *as 1643*. **Scotland moved New Year's Day
  to 1 January in 1600** — so **Scottish records of the 1640s use 1 January year-start, and
  need NO "Old Style" Jan–March adjustment.** My earlier notes that glossed "1642"
  Jan–March dates as "= 1643" are therefore **wrong for Scotland** and should be dropped in
  the refining pass: **dates are as written** ("20 March 1642" = March 1642; "January 1643"
  = January 1643). This tightens the timeline: the deposition was **July 1642**, and the
  register runs forward into **1643** from here. See [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]].
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `tumultuous stirre` = a riot/disturbance; `geard`
  (uncertain) = made/uttered; `misbearing` = misbehaving; `voyces` = votes; `suffrage` =
  vote; `Goodman of [place]` = the tenant-farmer/laird of that farm. **Both pages badly
  browned** — much is conjectural. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates **as written (Scots New
  Style)**.

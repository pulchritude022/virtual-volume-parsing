Left page (folio **99a**): (tail of the **Leswalt visitation**, 26 Aug) the minister [Mr Hew Hutcheson]
being interrogated what concurrence he had of the elders in discipline, answered **they did concur
according to their power, and a good part of them used family worship according to their abilities**;
**the session book being visited was approved**; **John Vans[?], summoned pro 2 and not appearing, to be
summoned pro 3**; **Thomas Morton appointed to be summoned pro [3]**. Then **★ the visitation at Kirkcolm,
27 August 1646** — absent Mr Alexander Turnbull and the elders of Kirkmaiden, Colmonell, Inch and
Portpatrick; **Master James Bell preached on his ordinary text (Matt. 23:44[?]), approved**; **the
minister and elders being removed, the elders were called in one by one and interrogated whether they
knew anything of the minister in respect of his doctrine or discipline for which they would have him
admonished — they answered they knew nothing, he gave them satisfaction**; and **the minister being
interrogated what concurrence he had of the elders** [continued on the right page]. Right page (folio
**100**): the minister answered **the elders did concur in discipline, [and kept] family worship**; **the
session book being visited was approved**; **★ Mr John Dick appointed to write a letter to the presbytery
in Ireland, that Agnes McColbie be sent over for further trial of her suspected [fault]**; **James Torrane
continued**; **Marion Mc[Chesant?] continued, in respect of Mr Alexander Turnbull's absence**; **Thomas
Morton continued, in respect of the ruling elder of Glenluce's [absence]**; the **next presbyterial
meeting appointed at Stranraer 10 September, Mr John Livingstone to handle the common head**; then **★

## Notes & context
- **★ The visitation round completed — Leswalt & Kirkcolm.** This opening finishes the **Leswalt
  visitation** (26 Aug; Hutcheson's eldership found to "concur… and a good part used family worship") and
  records the **Kirkcolm visitation** (27 Aug) in the same form. A useful datum on the **spread of family
  worship** in the Rhins ("a good part of them used family worship according to their abilities").
- **⚠ Name/parish puzzle (for Dad).** The minister who preached at the **27 August visitation** reads
  clearly as **"Master James Bell"** (Matt. 23:44) — yet img 0099 appointed the 27 Aug visitation "at
  **Kirkcolm**," whose minister has hitherto been **Mr James Blair** (admitted 1644), and img 0098's
  **Portmontgomery** visitation (30 July) had **Mr James Blair** preaching. In this register **Bell and
  Blair appear as two distinct men** (Bell repeatedly preaches at Glenluce, imgs 0097, 0099). So either
  (a) **Bell**, not Blair, now serves **Kirkcolm** (Blair having moved to Portmontgomery/elsewhere), or
  (b) the torn header parish is not Kirkcolm, or (c) the clerk writes the same man's name two ways. This
  bears directly on the recurring **Kirkcolm ⟷ Portmontgomery** question — please resolve at review.
- **★ Agnes McColbie to be fetched from Ireland.** The **bed-scandal case of Agnes McColbie** (imgs
  0094–0099), repeatedly "continued" because she is **dwelling in Ireland**, is now pressed: **John Dick
  is to write to the Irish presbytery that she be sent over for further trial** — the standard
  cross-channel extradition of an offender "to satisfy," exactly as with the earlier fugitives.
- **★ Livingstone to Edinburgh over an Abbey removal.** The presbytery **sends Livingstone (with Dick and
  Turnbull as procurators) to Edinburgh to enforce a decreet for the removal of Patrick Mc[—] out of "the
  Abbey of [Soulseat?]"** — a temporal/property action over former monastic lands (Soulseat Abbey lay in
  Inch parish, near Stranraer; possibly Glenluce Abbey). Evidence of the presbytery pursuing kirk/teind
  property interests in the civil courts. (Confirm the abbey and the person.) NB this is a *separate*
  Edinburgh errand from the July mission to oppose Livingstone's transportation (img 0096).
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `Did concur… a good part of them used family Worship according to
  their abilities` = the eldership's concurrence and the practice of household devotion; `Writt… to ye
  presbyterie in Ireland, that [she] be sent over for further tryall` = a request to the Irish presbytery
  to return an offender; `commissioner… to prosecute ye Decreet… against… his removeall out of ye Abbey`
  = enforcing an eviction decree over abbey lands; `occasionally met` = a specially-convened (not
  ordinary) session; `common head` = the set theological topic for the exercise. **Both pages heavily
  water-stained** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1646**.
- **Lowest-confidence readings (Dad):**
  1. **"Master James Bell" at the Kirkcolm (27 Aug) visitation** — the Bell/Blair + Kirkcolm/Portmontgomery
     tangle (see the ⚠ note).
  2. **Agnes McColbie to be sent over from Ireland** for further trial (John Dick's letter).
  3. **Livingstone (with Dick & Turnbull) to Edinburgh** to enforce the removal of **Patrick Mc[—]** out
     of **the Abbey of [Soulseat?]**.
  4. The **Leswalt "family worship" datum**; the continued cases (**James Torrane**, **Marion
     Mc[Chesant?]**, **Thomas Morton**); the **10 Sept 1646** date.

Left page (folio **93a**): (tail of 13 May) **★ Agnes McColbie, being returned out of Ireland,
compeared and confessed that she had lain several [nights] in bed with a man named [—], but denied she

next meeting appointed 27 May (Mr John Dick to make, Mr John Livingstone to add). Then **Presbytery at

## Notes & context
- **★ Agnes McColbie — a cross-channel bed-scandal, and a pregnancy defers her trial.** A returning
  migrant from Ireland, **Agnes McColbie confesses she lay several nights in bed with a man but denies
  any "carnal dealing"** — the classic scandal of *suspected* fornication/adultery (sharing a bed being
  itself a censurable occasion of sin, even absent proven intercourse). Her further trial is then
  **deferred because she is "great with child" and "for the present dwelling in Ireland"** — a vivid
  glimpse of the constant Galloway↔Ulster traffic (cf. Margaret Cobland, img 0092) and of how pregnancy
  and cross-channel residence stalled the kirk's discipline. A good human/genealogical node (confirm the
  surname **McColbie**).
- **★★ Glenluce referred up to the General Assembly.** The long-running **Glenluce planting/stipend
  deadlock** (imgs 0084–0092) — repeatedly stalled by James Ross of Barneill's absence and by
  Garthland/Livingstone's absence in Ireland — is now **escalated by the presbytery to the General
  Assembly**, with John Dick (moderator) charged to give formal notice to Ross of Barneill. This is the
  local court conceding it cannot resolve the finance deadlock and pushing it to the supreme court of the
  Kirk. Ties directly to the img 0092 note that the point was first referred to the Synod.
- **★ Commissioners to the General Assembly chosen.** The presbytery elects **Mr John Livingstone, Mr
  Alexander Turnbull, and James [—]** (a minister and elder, name continued overleaf) as its
  commissioners to the 1646 General Assembly — the settled machinery of representation, and a firm
  biographical datum for Livingstone (Stranraer) and Turnbull.
- **★ The Torrane adultery suspicion (Inch).** A **suspected adultery of "James Torrane elder in
  Inch,"** with **James Torrane younger and one John [—]** to be summoned as parties/witnesses to the
  trial — an intra-family discipline case worth tracking (elder vs younger of the same name). Confirm
  the surname **Torrane** and the relationships.
- **Ordinary rhythm.** The **post-Synod moderator role** (John Dick), the **rebuke of absent elders**
  (Kirkmaiden, Leswalt), the recurring chase after **summoned-but-absent offenders** (Thomas Adair of
  Glenluce, the Kirkmaiden adulteress), and the **cross-presbytery letters** (Turnbull to write about a
  woman's adultery) — the weekly discipline grind of 1646.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `did lie by [severall nights] in bed… bot denyed… any carnall
  dealing` = confessed the scandalous circumstance but denied the act itself; `great With child… dwelling
  in Ireland` = pregnant and resident across the water (case deferred); `referred… to ye Generall
  assemblie` = escalated to the supreme court of the Kirk; `did choose… commissioners` = elected
  representatives to the Assembly; `summonded pro 1… pro 2` = successive citations. **Both pages faded**
  — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1646**.

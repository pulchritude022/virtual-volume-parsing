## Notes & context
★★ **A parish-boundary / annexation survey — Rogele and Barnsalloch measured between Stoneykirk and Portmontgomery.** The bulk of folio 214a is a **perambulation-and-annexation matter**: two "honest men out of every parish" are appointed to **measure the distances of Rogele and Barnsalloch** as between **Stoneykirk** and **Portmontgomery**, to settle which parish they should belong to (Barnsalloch found "nearer to Stoneykirk"). This is the same species of boundary business as the **Argrenan perambulation** (imgs 191–211) — the post-Reformation reshaping of parish bounds in the Rhins, here driven by an earlier annexation of a kirk to Portmontgomery.

★★ **The Gilbert Kennedy of Aviolen scandal — the letter to Stirling forborne.** Following img 213 (where Dick was to write to Stirling that the fugitive **Gilbert Kennedy** clear himself), Mr John Park now reports that, **finding "no effectual way to bring that forward," they did forbear writing to Stirling** — and the presbytery approves the omission. Another woman, **Marion [—]**, is added to the adultery dragnet around Kennedy (cf. Margaret Morion and Janet Williamson, img 212) and cited in writing.

★ **A *pro re nata* sitting of 2 December 1650, and the chaplaincy still out.** The opening closes into an emergency (*pro re nata*) meeting of **2 December 1650** — notable because **Mr John Crosse "is not yet returned from the associate forces"** (the Western Association army he was sent to at Glasgow, imgs 213–214), confirming the chaplaincy was a sustained absence. The meeting is convened to read **two letters from the Commission of the Kirk** (continues img 216). Also rolling on: **Margaret McClounie**'s public repentance (first admonition given, second to follow); **Arisemman (Andrew Kennedy)** still sick (cf. img 213); the **Thomas McDowall** (Garthland's brother) satisfaction and the **Maryport deposition** against John Campbell.

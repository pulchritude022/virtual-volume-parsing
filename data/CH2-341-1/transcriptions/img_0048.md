### Modern English (partial — page faded)
**Presbytery at Stranraer, 29 March 1643.** Mr George Dick made, and Mr John Dick added,
the exercise **on Colossians 3:16** — approved. Absent: Mr James Blair (and his ruling
elder); the elders of Inch and Stoneykirk. **Mr George Dick** argued his correspondence
with **Kirkcudbright in Mr John Man's trial**, by reason of that minister's attendance on
a considerable portion of his parish. **Mr Alexander Turnbull reported that Patrick
McKelling, the adulterer, had fled into the parish of Kirkcowan**; but, on intelligence,
he should be apprehended (toward Portpatrick or **Cardrona**); whereupon the presbytery
ordered **the Abbey minister to make public intimation**, that if he can be found out, he
be apprehended next Sabbath and **put to Ballantrae[?]**. The moderator reported writing to
the **Synod and the Earl of Galloway about planting Kirkcolm**; a letter to **Mr David
[Bullo], minister**; and the **declaration to be read in all the kirks** of the presbytery.
**Turnbull, Clerk.** **Mr George Dick to preach at Kirkcolm.**

## Notes & context
- **The register settles into routine 1643 governance.** With the Power case decided, the
  minutes are now the ordinary work of a presbytery: **inter-presbytery correspondence**
  (the **Mr John Man** trial with Kirkcudbright — Man possibly = minister of **Anwoth**,
  the famous parish of Samuel Rutherford; confirm), the **planting (settling a minister) of
  vacant Kirkcolm** (via the **Synod** and the **Earl of Galloway** as patron), the
  **weekly Colossians exercise**, a **public fast and General Assembly declaration** (the
  1643 national mobilisation), and **Irish-refugee relief** accounting. Good "ordinary
  life" context to balance the Power drama.
- **A fugitive adulterer.** **Patrick McKelling, "the adulterer," has fled into Kirkcowan**;
  the presbytery mobilises to **apprehend him** and "put [him] to Ballantrae[?]." A discrete
  discipline case and a glimpse of how kirk-sessions pursued offenders across parish lines.
- **The Stoneykirk tumult, still unresolved.** A minister is ordained **to deal again with
  the women who made the tumult** (img_0046) — the deposition's aftermath drags on into
  spring 1643.
- **Kirkcolm's vacancy being filled.** Following **Porterfield's death** (img_0045), the
  presbytery works with the **Synod and the Earl of Galloway (patron)** to plant Kirkcolm;
  **Livingston and George Dick supply the pulpit** meanwhile. Ties the Kirkcolm-vacancy
  thread forward.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `planting [a] kirk` = settling/installing a minister
  in a vacant parish; `publict fast` = an ordered day of fasting and humiliation; `passingers
  out of Ireland` = refugees crossing from Ulster; `make publict intimatione` = announce
  from the pulpit; `Abbie minister` = the minister of the (former) abbey parish (Glenluce?
  Soulseat?). **Both pages browned** — many readings provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*.

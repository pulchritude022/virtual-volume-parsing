# CH2/341/1 — Image 84 (folios 83v–84), October 1645 — the visitation of Glenluce; pressing James Ross for the mortification

Left page (folio **83a**): a **visitation of Glenluce appointed for Monday 20 October** (Mr John Dick,
moderator, to preach); then the heading **Presbytery at Glenluce, 20 October 1645, for the visitation
of the said kirk** — **Mr John Dick preached (Col. 4:15)**; **absent the ruling elders for
Portmontgomery, Leswalt, Stranraer, and Stoneykirk** (those absent both days to be summoned and, if
they have no relevant excuse, censured); **Patrick [—] reported to have begun to give satisfaction**;
and an elder/offender **[Alexander] Mc[—], not appearing, to be summoned a second time.** Right page
(folio **84**): **Mr James Blair to charge the Glenluce preacher to summon [the offender] publicly
from the pulpit**; **Mr John Dick reports he preached at Glenluce on 20 October per the presbytery's
order**; the **Glenluce session book could not be visited**; the **parishioners of Glenluce desire the
presbytery to find them a minister**; and the presbytery presses **James Ross of Barneill to deliver
over the Glenluce mortification for the kirk** — Ross promising to stand by a **sufficient stipend**
but declining to hand over the mortification [until it is secured].

### Modern English (partial — page faded)
**Mr James Blair ordered to charge the Glenluce preacher to summon [Alexander] Mc[—] publicly from
the pulpit to appear before the presbytery.** **Mr John Dick reported he preached at Glenluce on 20

visited.** **The parishioners of Glenluce desired the presbytery to take care to provide a minister
for the kirk of Glenluce, and meanwhile to supply it, till the Lord furnish a minister.** The
presbytery, **considering that if the parishioners would secure a sufficient stipend it would do its

## Notes & context
- **The Glenluce planting drags on — and the money is the sticking point.** The long **Glenluce
  vacancy** (imgs 0044–0047, 0071, 0083) is examined at a formal **visitation (20 Oct 1645)**: the
  **parishioners plead for a minister**, and the whole question turns on **finance** — the presbytery
  presses **James Ross of Barneill to deliver over the Glenluce mortification** (the endowment fund he
  has held since the abbey-linked 1000-merk bequest, imgs 0044–0045), while Ross **agrees to stand by
  a "sufficient stipend" but refuses to hand over the mortification until it is secured.** A concrete
  church-finance stand-off: no settled minister without a secured stipend, and no secured stipend
  without Ross releasing the endowment. Central for the Glenluce parish page and the
  planting/mortification timeline; person node **James Ross of Barneill.**
- **A model visitation record.** The Glenluce visitation shows the routine machinery — the moderator
  preaching (Col. 4:15), the session book called for (here uninspectable), absent elders (Portmontgomery,
  Leswalt, Stranraer, Stoneykirk) marked for censure, and offenders summoned (with escalation **from
  a second citation to a public summons "from the pulpit"** when an offender ignores the court).
- **"Portmontgomery" again.** The recurring elder-district **"Portmontgomery"** appears once more in the
  absentee list — consistent with the standing puzzle (likely an alias/quarter within Blair's Kirkcolm
  charge, not a separate parish; flagged across earlier files).
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `visitatioune of the said kirk` = the formal parish visitation;
  `Session buke… culd not be visited` = the session's minute-book was unavailable for inspection;
  `provyde/furnish a minister` = call and settle a minister; `supplie… till the Lord furnish a
  minister` = provide pulpit supply during the vacancy; `mortificatioun` = an endowment/bequest in
  mortmain for the kirk; `secure… sufficient stipend` = guarantee an adequate stipend; `summond
  publicklie out of pulpit` = cited by proclamation from the pulpit. **Both pages faded** — provisional.
  Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Date 1645.

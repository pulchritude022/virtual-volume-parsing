# CH2/341/1 — Image 150 (folios 149v–150)

The disciplinary machinery of the purge shifts to the **renewal of the Covenant and
the debarring of Engagers**. Left page (foliated **149a** = folio 149 verso): the
next meeting is set for **27 December**; then, at an **extraordinary December
sitting**, ★★ **a letter and a packet of papers arrive from the Commission of the
Kirk** — the **Covenant, the Solemn Acknowledgment, the Large and Short
Declarations, and answers to observations on the Assembly's declarations** — divided
among the brethren; **How [Hugh] Ronald(s) of Airieman** answers that the particulars
he is challenged on are contained in his own bill to the committee against Thomas Hay
of Park (the matter delayed); and **Mr John Dick** is sent to **Mr George Richeson**
to learn the Commission's mind. The right page (folio 150): **Mr John Crookes** is to
keep correspondence with Wigtown presbytery about ★ **the persons to be debarred from
the Covenant**; the presbytery reads ★★ **two acts of the Commission — renewing the
Solemn League and Covenant, and ordaining every presbytery and minister to search
their sessions for compliers with the late unlawful Engagement** and declare who is
admissible; and at the ★ **sitting of 15 December** Dick reports back from Richeson
with the Commission's direction on debarment.

### Modern English
The next meeting is appointed at Stranraer, **27 December**, being Wednesday come a
fortnight. **Presbytery at Stranraer, [7] December.** No exercise, the presbytery
being extraordinarily convened. The same day, ★★ **a letter was presented from the
Commission of the Kirk, together with the papers of the Covenant, the Solemn
Acknowledgment, the Large Declarations, the Short Declarations, and answers to the
observations upon the Assembly's declarations**, which were divided among the
brethren.

**How [Hugh] Ronald(s) of Airieman**, being called, compeared; his answer is that the
particulars for which he is challenged are contained in a bill given in by himself to
the committee against **Thomas Hay of Park**, which he intends to follow before the
committee; and the presbytery, considering that the committee sits before the next
church meeting (where he undertakes to prove the bill), thinks fit to **delay** and
lay the whole business before the committee, and thereafter he is to answer before
the church at Stranraer, **27 December**.

The church, having read the acts of the Commission concerning the **taking of the
Covenant** and what proceeds from it, thinks fit — for further clearing and
satisfaction — to **send to Wigtown and keep correspondence, and to get some copies
thereof**. **Mr John Dick** is appointed to go to **Mr George Richeson** to learn the
progress, and the mind of the Commission, from whom he is lately returned.

### Modern English
**Mr John Crookes** is appointed to keep correspondence with the presbytery of Wigtown
about the business, and to learn their judgment concerning ★ **the persons to be
debarred from the Covenant**; and, at his desire, the presbytery appoint **Mr James
Blair and Mr John Crosse** to write out and subscribe the copies the presbytery holds.
The moderator enquired whether any brother had received an answer to the former letter
to Wigtown presbytery concerning the **Sheriff's business**; the brethren answered
none had; it is recommended to Mr John Crookes to see what is done.

The same day, the church, having read two acts of the Commission of the Kirk — ★★ **one
concerning the renewing of the Solemn League and Covenant, the other ordaining all
presbyteries and ministers within this kirk, in their several bounds, with all rigour,
to take cognisance and declare persons [in]admissible [in relation to] the act of the
unlawful Engagement** — in obedience thereto, the church earnestly recommends the
several brethren within their bounds **to make diligent search and trial in their
sessions** [for] compliance in relation to the late unlawful Engagement, and to
**declare all such as shall be found guilty of compliance** before the presbytery to be
holden at Stranraer the foresaid day of December.

**Presbytery held at Stranraer, 15 December.** The church being extraordinarily
convened, no exercise. Absent: Mr Alexander Turnbull; the ruling elders of Old
Glenluce, Stranraer, Stoneykirk, Kirkmaiden, Portmontgomery, Leswalt. **Mr John Dick
reported that he had written to Mr George Richeson** and showed a letter from him
concerning the **mind of the Commission about the persons to be debarred from the
Covenant**; which was read, and Mr [Dick] approved for his diligence.

## Notes & context
- ★★ **The Covenant-renewal papers arrive.** The Commission of the Kirk sends the
  whole documentary apparatus of the post-Engagement settlement — the **Solemn League
  and Covenant**, the **Solemn Acknowledgment [and Engagement to Duties]**, the
  **Large** and **Short Declarations**, and answers to observations on the Assembly's
  declarations — to be divided among the brethren and copied. A datable local receipt
  of the Kirk-party's programme in the winter of 1648–49.
- ★★ **Debarring Engagers from the Covenant.** The Commission's two acts order (a) the
  **renewing of the Covenant** and (b) a **search of every session** for compliers with
  the Engagement, to declare who is admissible. This is the parish-level face of the
  purge that the Act of Classes will nationalise weeks later — and it dovetails with
  the suspension of the Glenluce session (img 144) and the gentry supplications (imgs
  148–149).
- ★ **How Ronald of Airieman defers to the committee.** In the Park↔Airieman feud
  (img 149), Airieman says his charges are in his own committee bill and undertakes to
  prove them there; the presbytery delays and routes it through the civil committee,
  with an answer due 27 December.
- ★ **New links: Mr George Richeson.** An external figure (a Commission member or
  agent?) whom **Mr John Dick** consults for "the mind of the Commission" — worth a
  reference note. And **Mr John Crookes** recurs (cf. img 143) as the brother handling
  Wigtown correspondence.

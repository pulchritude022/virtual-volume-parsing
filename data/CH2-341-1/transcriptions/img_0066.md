# CH2/341/1 — Image 66 (folios 65v–66), December 1644 — **the aged Power: Stoneykirk petitions for a colleague**

A humane and important opening. Restored to his charge, **Mr Gilbert Power is now old and infirm**,
and **his own kirk session petitions the presbytery for "an able and qualified man" to be joined
to him as a helper/colleague** — with the presbytery insisting that **Power first be paid all his
arrears.** A poignant late chapter of the Power story.

Left page (folio **65a**): **Presbytery at Stranraer, 6 December 1644** — exercise (Livingstone /
Hutcheson, Col. 4:7–9); **thanksgiving kept in the kirks "for the delivery of Newcastle"** (the
Scots army's capture of Newcastle, 19 Oct 1644); a **letter to Mr Gilbert Power** about supplying
Stoneykirk (**Mr James Bell to preach there next Sabbath**); John Dick to baptize a child; then a
heading **Presbytery at Stranraer, 27 December 1644 — thin "upon the occasion of correspondence
for the [trial] of the witches."** Right page (folio **66**): the **General Assembly commissioners
chosen** (Livingstone, Blair, and Uchtred McDowell of Garthland) for the **GA at Edinburgh, Jan
1645**; then the **Stoneykirk session's petition** — the parish "in a manner desolate by reason of
the great infirmity of Mr Gilbert Power, who partly for age and partly for infirmity is not able
to discharge the laborious function of the ministry" — **craving a helper**; and the presbytery's
**advice** (via Livingstone): a brother to deal with Power and the heritors to bring in a helper,
**Power first to be paid all arrears.**

### Modern English
**That day the presbytery chose Mr John Livingstone, Mr James Blair, and Uchtred McDowell of
Garthland to go to the ensuing General Assembly at Edinburgh (Jan 1645).** **That day the greater
part of the Session of Stoneykirk gave in a petition**, whose tenor follows: *"We humbly complain
that, whereas the parish of Stoneykirk is in a manner desolate, by reason of the great infirmity
of Mr Gilbert Power — who, partly for age and partly for infirmity, is not able to discharge the
laborious function of the ministry — [and considering] the necessity of that parish, unless some
able and qualified man were provided as a helper to him in the Lord's work; therefore we humbly
supplicate your Wisdoms to take the condition of that poor people to your consideration, and find
out, by your godly care, that there may be added to him some faithful and qualified man for the
setting forward of the work of God in that place; and we shall not be wanting in acknowledgement.
So subscribed. 18 December."* **The presbytery, taking the petition to consideration, Mr John
Livingstone gave in this advice: that a brother of the presbytery be appointed to deal with Mr
Gilbert and the heritors about bringing in a helper — on condition, first, that Mr Gilbert be paid
all his arrears before any new preaching arrangement.**

## Notes & context
- **★ The aged Power — Stoneykirk seeks a colleague.** A moving, decisive turn for the Power
  story: restored by the General Assembly (imgs 0061–0063), **Power is now old and infirm** — his
  **own kirk session** describes the parish as **"in a manner desolate, by reason of the great
  infirmity of Mr Gilbert Power, who partly for age and partly for infirmity is not able to
  discharge the laborious function of the ministry,"** and petitions for **"an able and qualified
  man… as a helper to him in the Lord's work."** This dates his decline to **Dec 1644** and frames
  the **last phase of his ministry** — vindicated, restored, but aged and needing a colleague. It
  also explains the **Stoneykirk pulpit-supply** on the left page (Bell preaching). Prime material
  for Power's person-page (his old age, the parish's care for him). See
  [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]].
- **His arrears must be paid first.** The presbytery's advice is telling: a helper only **"on
  condition, first, that Mr Gilbert be paid all his arrears."** After years of being frozen out
  (unpaid while "not approven"?), the restored Power's **stipend arrears** are to be settled before
  any collegiate arrangement — a small justice, and a tie to the wider stipend/teind theme
  (Porterfield, the Earl of Galloway).
- **The delivery of Newcastle — a national thanksgiving.** The kirks kept **thanksgiving "for the
  delivery of Newcastle to the army"** — i.e. the **Scots Covenanter army's capture of
  Newcastle-upon-Tyne, 19 October 1644**, a major event of the Civil War. A clean datable
  national-events node (Dec 1644 thanksgiving).
- **Commissioners to the 1645 General Assembly.** **Livingstone, Blair, and Uchtred McDowell (of
  Garthland?)** chosen for the **GA at Edinburgh, January 1645.** (Note: "of Garthland" — earlier
  the pursuer was **Uchtred McDowell of French**; confirm whether this elder-commissioner is the
  same man or the laird of Garthland.)
- **The witch business rumbles under everything.** The 27-Dec presbytery is **thin "upon the
  occasion of correspondence for the [trial] of the witches"** — the Privy-Council-commission
  effort (img 0065) is drawing members away. A grim continuity.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `in a maner Desolate` = effectively without a functioning
  ministry; `the Laborious functioun of the ministry` = the demanding work of the pastoral office;
  `helper` = a colleague/assistant minister (a "second charge"); `heritors` = the landowners
  liable for stipend/fabric; `arrears` = unpaid stipend owed; `delivery of Newcastle` = the town's
  surrender/capture; `commissioners of the kirk` = the standing commission of the General Assembly.
  Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1644 (into Jan 1645).

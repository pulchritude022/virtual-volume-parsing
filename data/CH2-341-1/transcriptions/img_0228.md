## Notes & context
★★★ **"The clerk hid the session book for fear of the enemy" — the occupation in a single line.** The most vivid war-detail yet: a whole adultery process (the long-running **Gilbert Kennedy of Aviolen** case) stalls because **the session clerk had physically hidden the session book "for fear of the enemy,"** so no extract can be produced. Paired with **Arisemman's excuse** — that he stayed away because he "knew not that the enemy was removed from Stranraer" and has since been out of the country — these lines show **Cromwellian troops physically occupying Stranraer in the spring of 1651** and the parish records and delinquents scattering before them (cf. "the trouble of the time," imgs 222, 225–227).

★★ **The Inch congregation resists losing Mr John Dick — by staying home.** Img 227's supplication to retain Dick is answered here: **"this day there came none from the parish of Inch to concur in some ecclesiastical course for keeping still with them their minister Mr John Dick."** The threatened *transportation* of Dick (planted at Inch, imgs 193, 209) is live, and the parish's own commissioners fail to appear — whether from the disruption or from division is unclear.

★ **Routine discipline grinds on under the occupation.** Patrick Crosbie's parish is now fixed as **Leswalt** (resolving img 226's "[Cassnall]"), and he offers to make public repentance; his partner **Janet Gib** dodges a third citation. Two **Inch** men (John Adair, David Hutcheson) are summoned for Sabbath-breaking and drinking, and **Janet Blain** of New Glenluce, sharply rebuked for fornication, is remitted to her session.

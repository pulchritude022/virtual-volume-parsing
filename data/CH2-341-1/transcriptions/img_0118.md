## Notes & context
- **★★ The Ardwell case escalates to the General Assembly — and is sent back.** The adultery-slander cause
  (McCulloch of Ardwell vs the Lady Freugh, imgs 0113–0117) becomes a jurisdictional set-piece: the
  presbytery **refuses to entertain McCulloch's counter-complaint against the Lady Freugh** and requires
  **him to confess or purge himself**; he **appeals**; the presbytery, finding "no just reason for his
  appeal" but stymied by the **non-appearance of the key witness** (stuck in Ireland, img 0117), **carries
  the whole process up to the General Assembly** via its commissioners — and by the September sitting the
  GA has **"remitted it back to the presbytery."** A clean illustration of how a local scandal could ricochet
  up and down the Presbyterian court hierarchy.
- **★ The 1647 General Assembly delegation.** The presbytery **elects its commissioners to the General
  Assembly**: the three ministers **Livingstone, Blair, and Turnbull**, plus the ruling elder **the Laird of
  Garthland (McDowall of Garthland)** — the recurring lay leader (cf. GA commissioner at img 0095). Useful
  for dating and for the person-pages.
- **The fugitives cold.** The brethren **"could get no word of James Adair or James Mc[Credie]"** — the
  Adair adultery thread (imgs 0114–0116) hits a dead end for now; the men have vanished.
- **Continuity of worship during the GA.** A practical note: **Dick is to supply preaching each sabbath
  "till the return of the commissioners from the General Assembly,"** and the next meeting is left to the
  moderator to set on their return — the court winding down for the Assembly season (the 1647 GA sat at
  Edinburgh in August).
- **Standing business.** The **common head *De reprobatione*** (Bell, on Rom. 9 — the Covenanters'
  Calvinist staple); the perennial **widows/orphans list**; the **Ireland correspondence**; a penitent
  (**John Mc[—]**) satisfied; and a fresh **batch of citations** (Michael Mc[—], John Tarbet, Marion [—],
  Humphrey, Jean Hutcheson, Morison) whose cause is not yet stated.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `either to confess or purge himself` = the alternatives put to an
  accused (compurgation); `ordained ye commissioners to take out ye Whole process to ye generall assemblie`
  = referring a cause upward with the full record; `Ardwells business Was remitted to ye presbyterie` = the
  superior court sending it back down; `common head De reprobatione, Rom. 9` = the doctrine of reprobation;
  `preach… sabbathlie till ye returne of ye commissioners` = pulpit supply during the Assembly. **Both pages
  heavily faded/torn** — the July day, the September month, and several surnames are provisional. Capital
  **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1647**.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 61 (folios 60v–61), Aug 1644 — **THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY REPONES POWER**: the deposition declared null

**★ MAJOR DOCUMENT.** Folio 61 registers the **General Assembly's act reponing (restoring) Mr
Gilbert Power** — declaring the presbytery's sentence of deposition **null, and null from the
beginning** — which **overturns the outcome the earlier register implied.** Power himself
exhibits the act and has it entered in the presbytery book (Aug 1644).

Left page (folio **60a**): the **Visitation of the kirk of Stranraer, 22 August 1644** —
mirroring img 0060: **Mr John Livingstone (minister of Stranraer) preaches (John 19:30), is
approved**, withdraws while the elders approve him "in all," then is examined about the elders
(he desires the **worship of God set up in families** and the **suppression of idleness**);
George and John Dick excused their absence. Right page (folio **61**): **Mr Gilbert Power
exhibits "the act of his reposition, obtained from the General Assembly," and has it registered**
— the act (**St Andrews, 4 August 1642**) recites the parties (Power, minister of Stoneykirk,
vs George Dick, John McClellan, Hew Henderson [Synod of Galloway moderator], Uchtred McDowell of
French and others), and, on full review of the process and depositions, **declares the sentence
of deposition null and null from the beginning, and ordains Power reponed to the full and free
exercise of his ministry.**

### Modern English
**That day Mr Gilbert Power exhibited and produced "the act of his reposition, obtained from
the General Assembly," desiring it be entered in the presbytery's register, per the Assembly's
own direction; which the presbytery thought reasonable and directed to be inserted. The tenor
whereof follows:**

**"At St Andrews, 4 August 164[2], Session [—].** [Act of Mr Gilbert's reposition.] Forasmuch
as the **supplication presented at the instance of Mr Gilbert Power, minister of Stoneykirk,
against Mr Jo[hn —] and George Dick** [and the moderator and elders of the **presbytery of
Stranraer**], and **Mr John McClellan and Mr Hew Henderson (moderator) and the rest of the Synod
of Galloway**, and **Uchtred McDowell of French**, and divers others concerned — complaining of
**a grounded sentence of deposition pronounced against the said Mr Gilbert most wrongfully** …
The matter being called, the said **Mr Gilbert present in person**, and **Mr George Dick, Mr
John McClellan, and Uchtred McDowell also present in person, for themselves and in the name of
the remanent defenders**; and the whole matter, with the process upon the said sentence of
deposition, the depositions of witnesses, and the full process of the cause, being fully heard
and ripely advised: **the Assembly, finding that the grounds whereupon the said sentence
proceeded did not relevantly infer deposition, nor were lawfully proven, therefore declares the
said sentence to be null, and to have been from the beginning null in itself; and ordains the
said Mr Gilbert to be reponed against the same, and to have the full and free exercise of his
ministry at the said [kirk]."**

## Notes & context
- **★ THE OUTCOME REVERSES: Power was reponed by the General Assembly.** This act — which Power
  himself produces and gets registered — **declares the presbytery's deposition of him null (and
  "null from the beginning"), and restores ("repones") him to the full and free exercise of his
  ministry at Stoneykirk.** So the deposition (16 July 1642, img 0041) was **not the end**: Power
  **appealed to the General Assembly then sitting at St Andrews, which on 4 August 1642 overturned
  it.** The whole 1643–44 register — the **fresh supplications *against* Power** (img 0049, 6 Apr
  1643), his being **"not approven" / "not desyred" at every privy trial** (imgs 0052, 0057), and
  his being **challenged for absenting the Synod** (img 0059) — now reads as **the local heritors
  and Synod refusing to honour the Assembly's reposition**, with Power finally **forcing the act
  into the presbytery's own book (Aug 1644)** to compel his standing. **This materially reframes
  the case and needs to ripple back through earlier files (which cast the deposition as the
  result).** See [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]] — update the spine.
- **The parties named — the anti-Power coalition, on record.** The defenders who appeared to
  uphold the deposition are **Mr George Dick, Mr John McClellan, and Uchtred McDowell of French**
  (with **Mr Hew Henderson**, moderator of the **Synod of Galloway**, and the **presbytery of
  Stranraer**). **Uchtred McDowell of French** is the chief lay pursuer (the "instant" of the
  1642 process, img 0050); **George Dick** the leading ministerial opponent. A clean roster for
  the people-pages and the event node "Power's deposition & reposition."
- **⚠ Date to confirm — but the sequence coheres.** The act is headed **"St Andrews, 4 August
  164[2]."** St Andrews = the **1642** General Assembly (which sat from late July 1642), so the
  reposition falls just **~3 weeks after the 16 July 1642 deposition** — a tight, coherent appeal.
  (If the final digit were "4," it would instead be a recent 1644 restoration cleanly ending the
  limbo; the 1644 GA, however, met at Edinburgh, not St Andrews — which favours **1642**.) Confirm
  the year; either way the **substance (Power reponed, deposition null) is the headline.**
- **Livingstone confirmed minister of Stranraer.** The left-page visitation examines and
  approves **Mr John Livingstone as minister of Stranraer** (his own kirk) — the famous
  Covenanter preacher, minister of Stranraer 1638–48. Earlier files loosely associated him with
  the Stoneykirk affair; he was in fact **Stranraer's** minister (a neighbour to Power's
  Stoneykirk). Note for his person-page.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `repositioun` = **reposition** — formal restoration to
  ministerial office; `reponed against the same` = restored notwithstanding the sentence;
  `grounded sentence` = the reasoned sentence (of deposition); `Null in the self` = null in
  itself, void *ab initio*; `to inferre depositioun` = to warrant deposition; `in specie` =
  the process itself, in full; `remanent defenders` = the remaining respondents; `Forsameikle
  as` = "Forasmuch as" (the preamble). **Right page legible in patches** — the operative
  negative ("did **not** relevantly infer… nor were lawfully proven") is the crux; verify.
  Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Act dated 1642; register 1644.

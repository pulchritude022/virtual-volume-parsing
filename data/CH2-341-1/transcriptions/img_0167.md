## Notes & context
The 7 May 1649 sitting is dominated by the **stalled Balneil case**, and adds vivid biographical colour:
- ★★ **Balneil is "out of the country," and has the process papers himself.** The civil Committee had promised (via Mr James Dalgleish, img 166) to send Balneil's process to the presbytery "with one of their members," but it never came — because **Balneil is abroad and "had it in custody"** (i.e. holds his own dossier). Mr James Blair — already the Synod's man for the widows' and orphans' money — is deputed to prise it out of the Committee. Consistent with the volume's picture of Balneil as a mobile, well-connected laird (earlier: Border/Galloway movements, Ireland links).
- ★★ **Balneil "would not join with the old parish of Glenluce"** — he is refusing to reintegrate into the parish/kirk-session life while his eldership question hangs unresolved; the presbytery orders Mr John Scott (minister of old Glenluce) to exhort him again and summon him.
- The **referral loop restated**: everything (via both presbyteries and the Synod) keeps bouncing **back to the Commission of the Kirk**, which wants the process "read and cleared" first — so the brethren are told to pin down **exactly which article they think is not yet cleared** and instance it. The bureaucratic deadlock is now explicit.

Church-life housekeeping: **Thomas Adair** has finally completed his satisfaction at old Glenluce and is received; **Margaret Dunn** (his fornication partner, img 166) summoned; **Margaret [Ladner?]** does sackcloth. Common-head rota (John Scott, *de ecclesia visibili et invisibili*); mutual **correspondence with the Wigtown presbytery** formalised; **James Murray**'s arrears — money owed him **by the laird of Balneil** — pressed again (Balneil's debts and his discipline are entangled).

**Cast confirmed active May 1649:** Mr John Scott (min. old Glenluce), Mr James Blair, Mr Alexander Turnbull, Mr James Dalgleish, Mr [John] Hutcheson. Ruling elder **James Murray** (old Glenluce) is Balneil's creditor.

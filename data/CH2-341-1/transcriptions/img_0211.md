## Notes & context
★★★ **Section boundary — the Synod's revision (Wigtown, 15 Oct 1650) — and the after-shock of Dunbar.** This opening closes a section of the register: the clerk **Mr John Park** signs off, and the **Synod of Galloway's revision-attestation** follows in another hand — **"At Wigtown, 15 October 1650, this book revised and approven … Robert Fergusson, Clerk of the Synod"** (the same Robert Fergusson who attested in April 1650, img 188). The business it closes is dominated by the fallout from **Dunbar**: the presbytery drafts a formal remonstrance to the war Committee that **"their forces are so long and so much detained from the bounds,"** and — remarkably — lays the blame for **"the slipping off of George Monro and Dalyell['s regiments]"** (the melting-away/desertion of those units) squarely on **"the Committee of that bounds."** It appoints a **solemn humiliation "in regard of the Lord's displeasure testified in the breaking of their army,"** per the **Commission of the Kirk's declaration** — the Kirk processing the catastrophe of 3 September through fasting and self-examination.

★★ **Gilbert Kennedy flees his scandal into the army.** The adultery-plagued **Gilbert Kennedy of Aviolen** (imgs 203–210) has **"slipped off to be a quarter-master"** — enlisting to escape discipline — and the presbytery formally asks the **Committee of Association** to **send him back** so his "gross scandal" can be dealt with. A telling instance of the war offering an escape hatch from kirk censure.

★ **Local business.** The **Argrenan perambulation** (the Stoneykirk/Portpatrick boundary, imgs 191–203) is finally measured and to be reported under the measurers' subscription; the **privy censures** (the brethren's mutual review) are held and "approven," while the **ruling elders are censured for negligent attendance.**

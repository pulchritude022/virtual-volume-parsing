## Notes & context
★★★ **A synodal landmark — "this book revised and approven by the Synod," Wigton, 11 June 1651.** The register itself records its own audit: the completed section is **subscribed by the presbytery clerk, Mr James Lawrie ("Clks presbyterii")**, and then attested by the **Synod of Galloway** at its Wigton meeting of **11 June 1651**, signed by **R. Ferguson, clerk of the synod ("Clks Synodi")**. This is the presbytery-book being carried up to the superior court for review and approbation — a rare glimpse of the routine oversight machinery, and a firm dating anchor for the whole 1650–51 stretch.

★★ **A congregation petitions to keep its minister — the Dick "transportation" question.** The parishioners of [a Rhins parish] present a **supplication craving that the ministry of Mr John Dick "be continued among them,"** and the presbytery, rather than deciding, **resolves to take the matter to the Synod.** The phrasing implies a pending *transportation* — a call to move Dick elsewhere that his own people are resisting; the higher court will decide. (Dick is the presbytery's clerk and busiest investigator, imgs 213–226.)

★ **The annual re-election and the ordinary form.** The 29 April 1651 sitting **re-chooses Mr James Blair as moderator "anew" and continues Mr James Lawrie as clerk** — the yearly reconstitution of the presbytery's officers — and keeps the exercise on 2 Thessalonians 3 (Crosse, Dick). Marginal captions on the faded lower folio ("Mr John Crosse," "commission of ruling elders," "John Campbell") show business continuing, including the still-unresolved **John Campbell** malignancy matter.

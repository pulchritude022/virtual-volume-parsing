## Notes & context
★★ **The Balneil case comes full circle — and stalls.** After the presbytery referred James Ross of Balneil's process up to the Commission of the Kirk (img 162, because the Commission's own act was "not fully plain" on whether taking a parliamentary commission debars a man from the eldership), the **Commission has sent it straight back** — "not being satisfied," it desired the process be remitted to the presbytery again. Balneil, aggrieved at being "so long kept from the eldership… looked upon as suspect, as disaffected to the cause," now **petitions the presbytery directly** (his supplication copied verbatim into the register) to take up his process, clear any doubt, and **repone (restore) him**. The presbytery promises to consider it "as soon as possible" but — needing to see papers that Mr Alexander Turnbull (its commissioner to the Commission) had lodged, which Balneil is asked to produce — **defers the reading to the next day.** This is the procedural knot at the heart of the volume's 1648–49 arc: the machinery for purging Engagement-compliers has no clean rule for a man the witnesses cleared but who did take the parliamentary commission, so he ping-pongs between presbytery and Commission. Prime material for the James Ross of Balneil person-page — his own voice ("to the great grief and prejudice of your supplicant").

★ **Stranraer ↔ Ireland correspondence revived:** Mr John Dick appointed to write to "the kirk of Ireland" — the recurring Ulster link (Islandmagee / the Irish presbytery) that runs through this volume.

Routine: exercise on **[Isaiah?] 5:16–18** (Bell opening, Currie adding); James Currie back from Edinburgh reports his diligence with the "conditions of the Kirk" errand (img 164); James Murray named ruling elder for old Glenluce; Thomas Adair continues sackcloth; Patrick McKie's process again delayed (now pending the Synod's advice); fast-collection logistics coordinated with the kirk of Wigtown.

## Notes & context
★★★ **The registered deposition against John Campbell — an eyewitness of malignant violence in Ireland.** This opening copies *in extenso* the **sworn deposition** (taken 28 Nov 1650 before a session **at Girvanmains in Carrick**, under the session clerk's hand, and produced by Mr Alexr Turnbull) that the presbytery has been chasing since imgs 207–215. **The goodwife of Maryport** testifies that, about the time **George Munro** crossed the water (Munro's Ulster forces, cf. imgs 204–211), armed men — **John Campbell among them** — came to the house of **Maryport at Knockeghinnoch**, and that in a quarrel over whose the house/goods were, **Campbell gripped and struck a man on the head, threatened him with a pistol, and twice swore "By God, you lie."** This is the documentary heart of the "malignant carriage in Ireland" charge — a rare first-person parish record of the violence of the 1649–50 Ulster service.

★★ **The Commission of the Kirk's three-fold letter, and the chaplaincy sustained.** The 2 Dec *pro re nata* sitting reads two letters from the **Commission of the Kirk**: a **collection for the distressed [Protestants] in England**, a call for a **frequent meeting of Commission members**, and a **warning to be read from the pulpits**. Notably the Commission specifically **desires Mr John Crosse — "a member of the Commission" — still at Glasgow with the forces** — to attend, and the presbytery writes to him there: confirming both Crosse's standing in the national Kirk courts and the continuing absence of the Rhins' chaplain with the Western Association host (cf. imgs 213–215).

★ **The routine resumes.** A stated meeting follows (**11 December 1650**) with the ordinary exercise (Hutcheson and Bell on 2 Thess 2), the elders again absent about the Portmontgomery business (cf. the annexation survey, img 215).

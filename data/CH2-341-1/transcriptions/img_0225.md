## Notes & context
★★★ **The Resolutioner–Protester split reaches the Rhins — via James Guthrie.** A remarkable political trace: the presbytery requires the Laird of Garthland (freshly satisfied for the Engagement, imgs 220–224) to **subscribe the General Assembly's declaration**, and his inserted written report reveals he was **at Edinburgh in May [1650], where — the Commission not sitting — he joined the ministers of Edinburgh in subscribing "both a declaration and petition, to be presented by Mr James Guthrie to the next meeting of the Commission."** **Mr James Guthrie** was the foremost of the **Protesters** — this places Garthland's rehabilitation squarely inside the bitter **Protester/Resolutioner** controversy of 1650–51, and his subscription "Garthland" pledges him "faithful to that Cause and Covenant." A striking instance of a reformed Engager pledged to the strict-Covenanting party.

★ **The war still thinning the presbytery.** The sitting of **9 April 1651** holds **no exercise** — the brethren absent and [the congregation/court] "removed from this place" — a further sign of the disruption under the Cromwellian occupation (cf. the "trouble of the time," img 222); only a handful of points are treated. A **John [McGhie]** of Old Glenluce is cited a second time for disobedience.

  - **"Mr James [Guthrie]"** — the name of the presenter is a strong but not certain reading; the political weight makes it worth verifying.
  - The 9 April "no exercise / removed from this place / nine points" clause is faint and partly conjectural.
  - **"John [McGhie]"** of Old Glenluce — surname uncertain.

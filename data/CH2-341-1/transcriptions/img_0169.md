## Notes & context
★ **Annual visitation of Stoneykirk, 23 May 1649.** A textbook presbyterial visitation of the parish where the deposed-then-reponed **Mr Gilbert Power** long served (Power is now dead; the minister here is **Mr James Lawrie**, admitted to Stoneykirk in 1648). The form is the standard one: the minister preaches (Isa 2:3, approven), the session-book is inspected, then the minister is removed while the parishioners testify to his doctrine, life, catechising, and visiting of the sick — all favourable — and then the elders are removed while their carriage is examined. The concrete parish business: they are **setting up schools and engaging a schoolmaster** (the heritors have given a bond for his maintenance) and **repairing the kirk and manse**.

★★ **The Balneil process, still stuck.** The top of the left page finishes the previous day's witchcraft/Wigtown-correspondence item, and the right page returns to **James Ross of Balneil**: **Mr James Blair reports that the presbytery of the shire (Wigtown) refused to hand over an extract of Balneil's process under their clerk's hand**, offering only to let the Stranraer clerk see and copy it. The **laird of Logan**, appointed by the Committee to bring the process, did not produce it. Balneil himself is to appear at Stranraer on **5 June**. This continues the img 162–168 referral loop: everyone holds a piece of the process and no one will surrender the original — the case cannot close.

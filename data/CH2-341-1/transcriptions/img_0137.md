# CH2/341/1 — Image 137 (folios 136v–137)

Three sittings. Left page (foliated **136a** = folio 136 verso): the anti-Engagement
supplication (img 136) is to be presented by **Mr John Dick and Mr John Craig** to
the **Committee of War at Wigtown on 26 May**; then at an extraordinary **Glenluce
sitting (31 May 1648)** — Livingstone and Blair still in Ireland — they report ★
that the **Committee of War of Wigtown, in answer, drew up and subscribed its own
petition to Parliament** to the same effect, and that when the Committee was found
sitting at Glenluce "to draw up some things tending toward the Engagement," the
Moderator **Mr Alexander Turnbull** was sent to desire it to do nothing toward the
Engagement until Parliament answered — **to which the Committee condescended**.
Then the **Stranraer sitting (7 June 1648)**: Blair's exercise (Titus?/4:14–15)
approved; Livingstone and Blair back from Ireland; the paternity-witness woman
summoned **pro tertio** (third time); penance follow-ups on the McMillan/[McCulloch]
Sabbath scandal and the McGie/McWilliam adultery. The right page (folio 137) copies
★ a **fuller Ardwell supplication** rehearsing the whole history: the "false and
frivolous scandal of adultery with **Jean Fyfe**" raised by **[Agnes] Agnew, Lady
Freugh**; his bill of complaint against her (referred to the presbytery of
Stranraer); his refusal to swear as "contrary to order"; and his **appeal to the
last General Assembly (held at Edinburgh, opened 4 August [1647])**, which declared
his oath should not be taken but upon **pregnant presumptions of the fact**.

### Modern English
The same day, the presbytery nominate and appoint **Mr John Dick and Mr John
Craig** to present this supplication unto the Committee of War within the Shire,
which is to sit at Wigtown the morrow, being 26 May, and to wait for their answer.

**Presbytery at Glenluce, 31 May 1648.** The kirk being extraordinarily convened,
there was no exercise; absent Mr John Levingstoun and Mr James Blair, and all the
ruling elders. Mr John Dick and Mr John Craig reported that they had gone to
Wigtown as appointed and presented the supplication in the name of the kirk unto
the Committee of War at Wigtown; and that ★ the **Committee, in answer, had drawn
up and subscribed a petition to the Parliament** for the effect contained in the
said supplication — which was approved.

The same day, the kirk being informed that the Committee of the Shire was then
presently sitting at Glenluce to draw up some things tending toward the Engagement,
appointed the Moderator **Mr Alexander Turnbull**, in their names, to desire that
the Committee do nothing tending toward an Engagement, seeing they had not yet
received an answer to the supplications sent from the Committee to Parliament;
which desire, the Moderator reported, **was condescended to**.

**Presbytery holden at Stranraer, 7 June 1648.** Mr James Blair exercised on
[Titus?] 4:14–15, and was approved. Present Mr John Levingstoun, and the ruling
elders of Old Glenluce, Portmontgomery, Leswalt. Mr James Blair and the ruling
elders formerly absent — their excuses were approved. The woman present with Marion
[in the paternity case], and [—] of her soldiers, being called, compeared not, and
are to be summoned a third time (pro tertio).

### Modern English
**Mr Alexander Turnbull** reported that he had preached at Glenluce and rebuked
**John [McCulloch?] of [Airieholland?]** as appointed (the Sabbath-night scandal,
img 132). **Mr James Blair** reported that **Jonet McGie**, adulteress with **Marion
McWilliam**, was ordered to give signs of repentance.

The same day, **the laird of Ardwell**, being called, compeared; and being desired,
according to the act of the General Assembly, the advice and act of the Synod of
Galloway, and several acts of this presbytery, either to yield to purging himself
of the scandal so long since put to him, or to confess, refused to do either, but
gave in a supplication, the tenor whereof follows:

*"[Whereas] I, the alleged McCulloch of Ardwell, being put upon, under a **false
and frivolous scandal of adultery with Jean Fyfe** (this long time past), which was
raised and affirmed by **[Agnes] Agnew, Lady Freugh, without any just grounds** —
whereupon I gave in a **bill of complaint against her** to the session of [the]
ministry, who referred the same to the presbytery of Stranraer... I presented the
said complaint to the said presbytery, who refused it; and thereupon [they demanded]
that I would purge myself by oath of the said scandal, and in respect it was
contrary to order I refused; whereupon they made an act to urge me to do the same,
without my just reasons. But upon informations they had... they summoned me again
to do conformably to the order; which forced me to **appeal to the last General
Assembly, held at Edinburgh, [opened] the 4 of August [1647]**; which venerable
Assembly took my appeal to consideration and declared by act that **my oath should
not be taken but upon pregnant presumptions of the fact**, as itself will testify;
and the presbytery was ordered thereafter to advise concerning the said scandal...
I, the said alleged, getting information that the presbytery had taken trial
accordingly by examining many witnesses for proving presumptions of the said
scandal — rather, I might say, the naked fact itself — [resolved]... by lying under

## Notes & context
- ★★ **The Committee of War of Wigtown sides with the Kirk.** A striking outcome:
  the presbytery's anti-Engagement supplication (img 136) is not just received —
  the **Committee of War itself draws up and subscribes a petition to Parliament**
  for the same effect, and agrees (when found sitting at Glenluce) to **do nothing
  toward the Engagement until Parliament answers**. This shows the anti-Engager
  alignment of the local shire authority in Galloway, spring 1648.
- ★ **Ardwell's procedural history (self-narrated).** The supplication is the
  fullest account yet of the case's course: scandal of adultery with **Jean Fyfe**,
  raised by **Lady Freugh ([Agnes] Agnew)**; Ardwell's **counter-complaint** against
  her; his refusal to swear as "contrary to order"; his **appeal to the General
  Assembly (Edinburgh, 4 August 1647)**; and the Assembly's ruling that his oath be
  taken only on **pregnant presumptions of the fact**. Excellent primary material
  for the Ardwell/Lady Freugh person-and-event pages.
- **Livingstone and Blair** return from Ireland by 7 June 1648 (absent in Ireland
  25–31 May; present 7 June) — refines the Livingstone timeline alongside the Ancrum
  call.
- Penance follow-ups close two earlier cases (the Sabbath-scandal man rebuked at
  Glenluce; McGie/McWilliam ordered to signs of repentance).

### Modern English
**Presbytery at Stranraer, 30 October 1650.** All the brethren present except Mr James Blair; all the ruling elders absent, at a committee that convened at Stoneykirk and Inch. This meeting was appointed by the brethren. Exercise by Mr John Crosse, Mr John Park adding on 2 Thess 1:6–7, doctrine tried and approven. **This day Mr John Park was chosen moderator, and Mr James Lawrie clerk, to continue till the next Synod.** **Mr Alexr Turnbull appointed to take Maryport['s Adair's] deposition upon oath, and bring it under the session clerk's hand.** **John Campbell — brought before us for being in the malignant courses in Ireland — is removed out of the country.** **Robert Campbell, referred back by the Synod to the presbytery, to be summoned against next day.** **Mr James Blair and Mr John Park to speak the Sheriff, that he put Mr John Dick in real possession of his glebe according to law.**

### Modern English
**George Adestoun** (formerly cited before the Session of Stoneykirk, and being disobedient, thrice cited to the presbytery) was this day called and did not compear; Mr James Lawrie to give him public citation. **Thomas Baillie in the old parish of Glenluce, being cited, this day compeared; and, in regard the Committee of War had directed him cited to answer for the alleged act of robbery committed by him (he being under the scandal thereof), the presbytery enquired at him anent the said robbery; and although he denied it, the presbytery showed that David [Kennedy] and William Baillie of Curragie were there at the time.** The allegation was remitted; **Mr John Scott and Mr John Dick to require the witnesses to keep next meeting, that they may bear witness.** **Mr James Lawrie reported anent Mr Thomas McDowall, brother to the Laird of Garthland; and in regard he confessed his guilt in his accession to the unlawful Engagement, both here and in Ireland, the presbytery appoints Lawrie to confer, and him to give sign of repentance, and to go on to give public sign of his obedience and repentance for the same.** **Margaret Morion and Janet Williamson (under the scandal of adultery with Gilbert Kennedy of Aviolen) are to be tried by our brother Mr John Dick, and report made to the presbytery next meeting.**

## Notes & context
★★ **A new moderator, and the Ireland/Engagement docket rolling on.** The 30 Oct 1650 sitting installs **Mr John Park as moderator and Mr James Lawrie as clerk** ("till the next Synod"). The malignancy-from-Ireland cases persist: **John Campbell** (the alleged forced-conscriptor of Knockeghinnoch, imgs 207–210) **has "removed out of the country"** to evade the process; **Robert Campbell** (the defiant "What! malignancy in me!" man, img 210) is **referred back down to the presbytery by the Synod** and re-summoned; and — a notable family link — **Mr Thomas McDowall, brother to the Laird of Garthland**, confesses his accession to the Engagement **"both here and in Ireland,"** and is set to public repentance, tying the leading local Engager family (Garthland, imgs 180–195) to the Irish service.

★ **The robber named again; the adultery still spreading.** **Thomas Baillie** of Old Glenluce (the robber of the "poor Englishman," imgs 184–205) is finally examined face-to-face; he denies it, but the presbytery names witnesses (**David [Kennedy] and William Baillie of Curragie**) who "were there at the time," and the case goes to proof. And the **Gilbert Kennedy of Aviolen adultery** dragnet adds two more women — **Margaret Morion and Janet Williamson** — to be tried by Mr John Dick (cf. imgs 205–211). Also: **George Adestoun** still defies citation; and the Sheriff is again pressed to give **Mr John Dick real possession of his Inch glebe**.

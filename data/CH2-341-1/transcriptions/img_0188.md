### Modern English
**At Wigtown, [16 April 1650]: this Synod, having revised this book, approves the same. Robert Fergusson, Clerk of the Synod.**

**Presbytery meeting at Stranraer, 8 May 1650.** Exercise by Mr James Bell (first speaker) and Mr James Blair (second), on 2 Thess 1:6–8, approven. All the brethren present except Mr John Scott. **Elders present: for Stranraer, Andrew [—] a bailie; for Inch, Alexr McBlain; for Portmontgomery [Portpatrick], John [Cochrane]; for Kirkcolm, Charles Campbell; for Glenluce, Alexr McMirchen** — the rest absent. **Mr Alexr Turnbull elected moderator, Mr John Park clerk.** The presbytery books delivered to the new clerk, none wanting.

**Mr John Scott to speak the bailie about the robbery committed upon an Englishman by [—] Thomas Baillie, parishioner in old Glenluce, formerly cited before the Committee of War.** [So the robber of the "poor Englishman" (imgs 184, 186) is named: Thomas Baillie.]

**Report was made that John McDowall — who bore office as Lieutenant-Colonel in the Last unlawful Engagement — publicly acknowledged in the kirk of Stranraer the unlawfulness of the Engagement and his guilt in acceding to it, and, upon satisfaction given, [is] to be received to the Covenant.** **Alexr Agnew, who bore office as Lieut-Colonel in the Engagement, [is absent] from the presbytery meeting** …

### Modern English
Mr Hew Hutcheson to cause cite him to the next presbytery. **Mr Hew Hutcheson had forgotten to take the confession of Alexr Agnew (who bore office as Lieutenant-Colonel in the unlawful Engagement); ordained again to take his confession of his guilt in engaging next Sabbath**, and report.

**The Session of Stranraer reports that its witnesses in the matter of the intended combat between George Montgomerie and Gilbert Kennedy of [Aviolan] deposed on oath that [William Broune] read a challenge sent in writing by George and averred by Gilbert; and that the said Gilbert bared the [scabbard] of his sword and was on the way to the appointed place** — the session's register bearing it at more length. **The presbytery officer could not find Gilbert Kennedy nor William Young** (both sworn parties to the combat) at their ordinary haunts; to be cited to next meeting. **William Broune (who carried the challenge)** compeared, acquainted [the presbytery] with his part in delivering the letter and challenge, and undertook to appear again when called.

**George Alexstoune, disobedient to the Session of Stoneykirk**, not compearing, Mr James Lawrie to cite him publicly [by higher-stage process]. **Mr John Scott, being a member of the Commission of the General Assembly, to consult the Commission about foreign malignants residing within the bounds.**

## Notes & context
★★ **Section boundary — Synod attestation and post-Synod election (May 1650).** The opening is anchored by the **Synod of Galloway's revision-attestation ("At Wigtown, [16 April 1650] … the Synod, having revised this book, approves the same, Robert Fergusson, Clerk of the Synod")** and the consequent **post-Synod presbytery election: Mr Alexr Turnbull moderator, Mr John Park clerk.** The sederunt names the ruling elders parish-by-parish (a useful prosopographical snapshot: **Alexr McBlain** for Inch, **John [Cochrane]** for Portpatrick, **Charles Campbell** for Kirkcolm, **Alexr McMirchen** for Glenluce). This is on the eve of the Cromwellian invasion of Scotland (July 1650).

★★ **The Engagement reckoning all but completed.** The satisfactions climax: **Lt-Col John McDowall has publicly acknowledged the unlawfulness of the Engagement in the kirk of Stranraer and is to be received to the Covenant**; and **Lt-Col Alexr Agnew's confession — which the clerk Hutcheson had simply "forgotten to take" — is rescheduled**. So both Lieutenant-Colonels of the local Engagement forces are brought to public repentance. **Mr John Scott, as a member of the Commission of the General Assembly, is to consult it about "foreign malignants residing within the bounds"** — the vigilance widening to incomers.

★ **The duel, and the robber named.** The Montgomerie combat gets fuller evidence: the Stranraer session's witnesses depose that **a written challenge passed between George Montgomerie and Gilbert Kennedy [of Aviolan], that Gilbert bared his sword and was already "on the way to the appointed place,"** with **William Broune** (the challenge-carrier, img 185) confirming his part. And the **robbery of the "poor Englishman"** (imgs 184, 186) is pinned on a named man: **Thomas Baillie, parishioner in old Glenluce**, already cited before the Committee of War.

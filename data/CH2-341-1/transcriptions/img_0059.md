## Notes & context
- **Power challenged for Synod absence — still on the rolls in July 1644.** Two years after his
  deposition-process (1642) and eighteen months into the appeal, **Mr Gilbert Power is still
  treated as a member of presbytery** — indeed **challenged (with Hew Hutcheson) for absenting
  himself from the Synod.** This is the clearest sign yet that, in the running register, **Power's
  status remained formally unresolved**: subject to the same attendance discipline as any
  minister, neither restored nor finally cast out. A key data-point for his person-page timeline
  (deposed by process, yet an active/absentee member into 1644). See
  [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]].
- **Livingstone moderator.** **Mr John Livingstone chosen moderator** (July 1644, till the next
  Synod) — the famous Covenanter preacher of Stoneykirk now heads the presbytery. Hew Hutcheson
  continues clerk. Office rotation as usual.
- **The Porterfield satisfaction rumbles on.** A **second letter to the Earl of Galloway about
  "satisfaction"** (to Mr John Porterfield, imgs 0057–0058) — the money/patronage dispute over
  Kirkcolm is still unresolved and the presbytery keeps pressing the patron.
- **Kirk reparation and a national fast.** The **reparation of the kirk** (Livingstone, George
  Dick, **the laird of Garthland**, and others) and a **fast appointed by the Assembly, kept** —
  the fabric of the parish and the rhythm of the 1644 national mobilisation, side by side.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `challenged for [their] absence` = formally called to
  account for non-attendance; `reported to the Synod` = made the required return/attendance;
  `reparatioun` = repair of the church fabric; `satisfactioun` = payment/compensation (here to
  Porterfield); `fast appointed in the assembly` = a General-Assembly-ordered day of
  humiliation; `commissions… admitted` = elders' credentials accepted. **Folio 59 is very
  faint** — treat as provisional; re-image. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1644.

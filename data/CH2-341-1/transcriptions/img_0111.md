# CH2/341/1 — Image 111 (folios 110v–111), 17 March–7 April 1647 — the Glenluce heritors default, so the presbytery goes to law; a divinity-bursary conference; penance for interrupting the pulpit

Left page (folio **110a**): (17 March, continued) **★ the same day, it being reported by a letter from
James Ross of Barneill that none of [the heritors] at all had made payment or given surety to him for the
diligence appointed for payment of the arrears of the mortification — whereby it appears that they intend
not to obey the act of the General Assembly, but by delays to hinder the settling and planting of the
kirks of Glenluce — whereupon the presbytery ordained that this act, and all former acts anent the business
of Glenluce, be [extracted] by the clerk and given to James Ross of Barneill; and ordained the moderator,
in name of the presbytery, to write to the Commission of the Kirk, and to the advocate and agent of the
kirk, or any other to whom it is needful, to concur with the said James Ross in petitioning the Parliament
or Lords of Session, or any judicatory competent, that the act of the General Assembly may be put in
execution**; the exercise was disputed; the next meeting appointed **7 April** (Mr Hew Hutcheson to handle
the common head *De causa impulsiva electionis*). Then **Presbytery at Stranraer, [7] April 1647** — absent
Mr John Dick, Mr Alexander Turnbull, and the elders of Inch and Kirkmaiden. Right page (folio **111**): the
Leswalt elder's absence the last day excused; **John Mc[—] to be summoned to the presbytery to be further
censured for his disobedience, per the act**; **Mr James Blair reported that James [—] had begun to give
signs of repentance**; **★ the presbytery thought fit to have a conference at the Synod with the presbytery
of Wigtown about the maintenance of the bursar at the college of Divinity**; **the moderator and clerk
reported that all they were ordained about the business of Glenluce, they had done**; **★ Gilbert Mc[—] in
Kirkcolm compeared, and was ordained to give signs of repentance at Kirkcolm the next Sabbath for his
miscarriage in speaking to the minister while he was in the pulpit, before the pronouncing of the
blessing**; **the brethren reported the fast was kept in the several parishes last Sunday**; and **the
presbytery ordained the moderator and Mr [McDowell], ruling elder, to give [notice] to Patrick Mc[—]…**
[continued overleaf].

## Notes & context
- **★★ The Glenluce heritors default — and the presbytery goes to court.** The climax of the arrears
  stand-off (imgs 0107–0110): **James Ross of Barneill's letter reports that not one heritor paid or gave
  surety**, revealing they mean to **"by delays hinder the settling and planting of the kirks of
  Glenluce."** So the presbytery **extracts all the Glenluce acts to James Ross and directs the moderator
  to enlist the Commission of the Kirk and the kirk's advocate/agent to sue "before the Parliament or Lords
  of Session, or any competent judicatory," to have the General Assembly's act put in execution.** The
  planting of New Luce is now to be forced through the civil courts — a striking case study in how kirk
  and state law combined to fund a new parish against resistant landowners.
- **★ A divinity bursary — investing in the next generation.** A quietly important item: the presbytery
  will **confer with the presbytery of Wigtown at the Synod "about the maintenance of the bursar at the
  college of Divinity"** — the two presbyteries jointly supporting a divinity student (bursar) at
  university. Evidence of the Covenanter Kirk's investment in ministerial education. (Which college is not
  named — likely Glasgow or Edinburgh.)
- **★ Penance for interrupting the pulpit.** A vivid worship-discipline vignette: **Gilbert Mc[—] of
  Kirkcolm must do public penance "for his miscarriage in speaking to the minister while he was in the
  pulpit, before the pronouncing of the blessing"** — a rare, human glimpse of decorum enforced in the
  Sunday service (and of how seriously the interruption of worship was treated).
- **Ordinary business.** The **common head** *De causa impulsiva electionis*, the **fast kept** in every
  parish, and **John Mc[—]'s** continuing contumacy — the settled rhythm around the Glenluce drama.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `none… had made payment nor given suretie… intend not to obey ye act…
  Bot by Delayes hinder ye setling and planting` = the heritors' passive resistance; `all former acts…
  extracted be ye clerk… to concur… in petitioning ye parliament or Lords of Session… that ye act… may be
  put in executioun` = escalating to enforce the Assembly's act at civil law; `maintenance of ye bursar at
  ye colledge of Divinitie` = supporting a divinity student's bursary; `miscarriage in speaking to ye
  minister While… in pulpite… before ye pronouncing of ye blessing` = disrupting public worship; `De causa
  impulsiva electionis` = the doctrinal topic on the impelling cause of election. **Both pages heavily
  water-stained/torn** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1647**.

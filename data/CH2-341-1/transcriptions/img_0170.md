## Notes & context
★★ **The hunt for corroborating "speeches" widens (continues img 168).** This opening carries forward the Balneil probation: **Mr James Lawrie is deputed to keep correspondence with the presbytery of Wigton** and to run down the hearsay of malignant **speeches allegedly spoken at "the Earl of [—]'s table in [Blaketoune?]"** first flagged at img 168. On the return leg (his report, right page), the Wigton brethren confirm some of them were "at the table and heard it," promise to examine it privately, and a **Mr Andrew [Kennedy?]** surfaces as a potential witness. So the presbytery is reaching outside its own bounds to build the case — the same difficulty as img 169 (nobody will surrender the original process; corroboration is thin).

★ **Presbytery at Stranraer, 6 June 1649 — washed out.** A vivid weather note: **"there was no exercise this day … by reason of the greatness of the waters"** — flooding kept the brethren away until afternoon (compare img 74's flooded Water of Luce in 1645). Routine: Mr John Scott ordained to try delinquents and a scandal of adultery in his bounds.

★ **Left page (169a):** an elder is to be **put out of the office of eldership** unless he gives satisfaction — Mr John Scott (minister over Balneil) sent to deal with him; and a note that a commission for the **trial of witches** had to be **remitted because it touched the "unjust Engagement" men** and its bearer had to attend Parliament. Also a scandal/adultery case (a woman, a chamber overnight) referred to Scott to try.

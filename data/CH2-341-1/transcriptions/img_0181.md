**The minister being removed, the elders were severally examined on his carriage** — his life and conversation (free of scandal?), doctrine (edifying?), catechising, visiting the sick, family worship, and exercise of discipline; **and specifically his carriage during the unlawful Engagement — whether he preached against it and did not assent to it — [to which] they answered he was free of all guilt of the said Engagement.** The minister, called in, was examined about his elders' carriage, whether the books were regular, and whether they were not slack in keeping sessions and censuring sin. The elders who acted as officers in the Balneil cause were admonished [about admissions requiring the lairds' consent]; and Garthland to address himself to the Commission of the Kirk with all diligence.

## Notes & context
★★ **Balneil's public satisfaction is certified.** The Balneil arc effectively closes here: **Mr John Scott reports that James Ross of Balneil did publicly confess his charged lapses before the congregation, "expressing his sorrow,"** and the **old-Glenluce session's formal testimonial is copied in, "extracted by me, Joannes [Ford], clerk of the session of Glenluce."** He is to appear once more before the presbytery. From malignancy prosecution (1648) → GA remit to satisfaction (img 175) → articles of confession (img 177) → public repentance certified (here): the whole case resolves as reconciliation.

★★ **Garthland routed to the Commission of the Kirk.** The presbytery finds that **a heritor of Garthland's rank must make his satisfaction to the Commission of the Kirk, not the presbytery** (per the GA act), and the moderator is to write on his behalf — noting pointedly that **"there are no more Engagers that have offered themselves to the presbytery as yet"** (a candid measure of how few came forward voluntarily). So the two big penitents diverge: Balneil satisfies locally, Garthland at national level.

★ **Visitation of Portpatrick (26 Dec 1649)**, with the now-standard **Engagement-loyalty question built into the visitation** ("whether he preached against it … free of all guilt of the Engagement") — showing the anti-Engagement purge routinised into ordinary kirk visitation. Also: **Marion Mc[—], a vagrant relapse, to be excommunicated**; commissioners consider providing the **Communion vessels**; Scott and Lawrie take up the plantation commission.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 37 (folios 36v–37) — the live 1643 register: Livingston to Ireland; James Blair's supplication

The current register runs forward through **several 1643 sederunts**. Left page (folio
**36a**, badly faded): **19 May** (the moderator and **Mr James Blair** to deal with
**Mr Gilbert Power**; **Mr John Livingston** appointed to preach 21 May); a meeting
appointed **14 June**; **Presbytery 15 June 1643** — Livingston **too sick to preach**,
the moderator absent sick, a **letter from the General Assembly's commissioners (10 & 14
June)**, and **Livingston receives direction from the Assembly to go to Ireland**;
**Presbytery 26 July 1643**. Right page (folio **37**): Session commissions and the
**Leswalt** visitation report; **James Rosse** fails to compear (summoned again); and
**Mr James Blair's supplication** — he asks the presbytery to **clear him of French's
defamation** (branding him an unjust, "unconscionable" dealer under the same scandal as
Power).

### Modern English
That day **Robert Bryson produced a commission from the Session of Stranraer**, which was
read. That day **the members of the Session of Leswalt**, called in and asked about
**their minister's life, doctrine, and discipline, approved him.** That day **James
Rosse[, of Balneil?]**, having been appointed to appear before the presbytery **at
Leswalt on 8 June**, being called, **compeared not**; he was ordained **to be summoned a
second time (*pro 2do*)** to appear at Stranraer **15 June**, with certification as
effeirs.

**Mr James Blair had given in a supplication on 6 April, which was kept out of the
register till now** (the business being "upon a stay," and he out of the way, so the
parties could not be prevailed with); Blair desired his supplication be heard and
inserted, as follows: "**Right reverend and worshipful brethren, I, your brother Mr
James Blair, minister at Portmontgomerie [Portpatrick]** — having occasion sometimes to
be abroad in my ordinary charge, and by necessary affairs in other parts of the country
— **my ears have always been [filled], at various removes, with the complaint given in
against me to you by the laird of French**; some affirming that I was hardly **branded as
an injurious and wicked dealer, a doer of injustice, and of most unconscionable and
dishonest dealing**; which reports spread both at home and abroad; and, moreover, some
affirming **I lay under the same scandal** [as Power], unable to purge myself — or else
[French] was able to justify it. **This caused me great grief of mind and distraction of
thoughts**, wherefore I have been desirous to consider the frame of his…"

## Notes & context
- **Livingston's Irish commission, dated.** The live register nails down the **John
  Livingston-to-Ireland** thread we first saw at img_0026: on **15 June 1643** Livingston
  **received direction from the General Assembly's commissioners (letters of 10 & 14
  June) to go to Ireland**, and by **26 July** he preached "because of his going." This is
  precise biographical dating for the historical Covenanter minister of Stranraer.
- **The dispute widens to Mr James Blair.** French's litigation is not confined to Power:
  he has **defamed Mr James Blair (minister of Portpatrick)** — Power's known partisan —
  as an "injurious, wicked, unconscionable dealer," even hinting Blair "lies under the
  same scandal." Blair's **supplication for vindication** (dated 6 April, suppressed till
  now) shows the **faction fight spreading across the presbytery's ministers.** Key for
  Blair's biography and for the French/Power conflict map.
- **Register mechanics laid bare.** Blair's supplication "**was always kept out of the
  register till now**" — an explicit admission that the clerk **held documents back** and
  entered them selectively. Corroborates how contested the record itself was (cf. the
  back-fill order, img_0029).
- **Ongoing threads:** **James Rosse** (the Glenluce dissenter, img_0031) still dodges the
  presbytery — failing to appear at Leswalt (8 June), summoned **pro 2do** to Stranraer
  (15 June). **Leswalt's** minister passed his visitation. **Robert Bryson** brings a
  Stranraer Session commission.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `pro 2do` = for the second time (a second citation);
  `With certificatioun as effeires` = under the appropriate legal warning; `portmontgomerie`
  = Portpatrick (the Montgomery-held port); `purge my selfe` = clear myself by oath/proof;
  `frame of his [mind/complaint]` (trailing). **Left page ink badly browned** — most of
  its readings are conjectural. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates as written (1643, after
  25 March).

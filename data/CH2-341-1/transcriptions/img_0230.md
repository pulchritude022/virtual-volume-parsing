# CH2/341/1 — Image 230 (folios 229a–230)

## Folio 229a (left page)  *(confidence: low)*

*Still the 21 May 1651 sitting, heavily stained. A **sworn examination** of Margaret [Morion] in the Gilbert Kennedy of Aviolen adultery case — numbered interrogatories on whether she was ever privately with him in a chamber or stable "of the Cuiles"; she denies and offers her oath, and a witness (Mr McLannan, servant at the Cuiles) is present. Then Margaret McClounie (Glenluce), relapsed, has undertaken to the public place; and **Arisemman at last compears and voluntarily purges himself by solemn oath.** Side-margin captions: "1 / 2 / 3" (interrogatories), "Marg: McClunie", "Arisemman".*

## Notes & context
★★★ **Arisemman purges himself by oath — the long paternity case sent up to the Synod.** After years of illness, evasion, and citation (imgs 189, 213–228), **Andrew Kennedy of Arisemman** finally **compears and "voluntarily purges himself on his solemn oath that he was free of all carnal dealing with that woman, Marion Auld"** — the mother in his long-running paternity/scandal case (Marion first appears img 189). His oath of purgation shifts the burden: **the presbytery "refers Marion Auld and her business to the Synod."** The classic Reformed resolution of an unprovable charge — compurgation by the accused's oath, the woman's counter-claim escalated.

★★ **A verbatim adultery interrogation — Margaret and Gilbert Kennedy "at the Cuiles."** Folio 229a preserves the rare **numbered interrogatories** put to Margaret [Morion] in the **Gilbert Kennedy of Aviolen** adultery case: whether she was ever "privately with him in a chamber or the stable of the Cuiles." She denies flatly, offering her oath — with a telling near-admission ("if she was seen in the house, she was in the said chamber in his company") — while a servant of the Cuiles, **Mr McLannan**, stands as witness. A vivid, procedurally exact fragment of session-level sexual discipline.

★ **Sabbath-breaking at Inch, and the routine.** **The Vans brothers (John, Patrick, and a third) of Inch** enter to satisfy for "disorderly walking in Sabbath-breaking, drinking, fighting" (cf. img 228's Adair/Hutcheson); **Margaret McClounie** of Glenluce undertakes public repentance at last; **Patrick Crosbie** of Leswalt makes his; and a faded item touches a Boyd summoned before Stranraer session under the General Assembly's act.

- **Lowest-confidence readings (Dad):**
  - **Both folios are heavily water-stained; the lower half of folio 230 is largely illegible** (the Crosbie and Boyd items given only in outline).
  - **"Marion Auld"** as the woman in Arisemman's purgation — a strong reading and it ties to the img 189 paternity case; verify the surname.
  - **The three numbered interrogatories** on folio 229a are the priority reading and moderately secure, but the connective wording and "the Cuiles" (a place, perhaps Culhorn or a farm of Aviolen's) are conjectural.
  - **"the Vans brothers … in the Inch"** and **"Mr McLannan, servant in the Cuiles"** — the by-names are best readings.

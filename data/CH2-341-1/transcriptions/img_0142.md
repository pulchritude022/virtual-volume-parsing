# CH2/341/1 — Image 142 (folios 141v–142)

Two headline events at the **Stranraer sittings of 4 and 11 October 1648**. Left
page (foliated **141a** = folio 141 verso): the routine trials — **Mr James
Lowrie** is examined on the common head, languages, chronology and places of
Scripture — and (★) Lowrie is appointed **to deal with Ardwell** so he may satisfy
the General Assembly's ordinance about the scandal of adultery he lies under. The
right page (folio 142): ★★ **Mr James Lowrie is admitted minister of the kirk of
Stonykirk** "according to the usual form used in the kirk of Scotland"; and ★★
**Ardwell, being called and desired to confess or purge himself, declares he is
most willing to purge himself by his oath** — the presbytery appoints him to do it
the next kirk day before the congregation. Also: the paternity-case witnesses
**Cathrin and Jonet McBryd** (present at Marion's delivery) fail to compear; a new
case — **Thomas Adair**, cohabiting with **Margaret Hannay** (frequent fornication,
plus a new fornication with another woman) — is referred to **Mr John Scott** at
Glenluce; and penance/fugitive follow-ups (the Engagement-fugitive **James McBryde**
still not found).

### Modern English
[Lowrie's trial:] everything being done and he removed, the brethren were desired
to declare their judgments, who all declared they thought him **most fit for the
ministry in that place**. Mr Alexander Turnbull was appointed to preach; and ★
**the same day Mr James Lowrie was admitted minister of the kirk of Stonykirk,
according to the usual form used in the kirk of Scotland in the admission of
ministers.**

**Session 2.** **Cathrin McBryd and Jonet McBryd**, who were present with Marion
[Catt] at the time of her delivery, being called, compeared not; Mr James Blair is
appointed to process them.

★ **[Ardwell], being called, was desired — conform to the ordinance of the General
Assembly — either to confess or purge himself by his oath; he declared that he
would be most willing to purge himself by his oath; and the kirk appointed him to
do it the next kirk day before the session of their [own] congregation.**

The same day, **Mr John Scott** declared that **Thomas Adair** did still cohabit
with **Margaret Hannay**, and would not satisfy the kirk for his frequent and
continued fornications with her, and moreover was **fallen in a new fornication
with another woman**; the kirk appointed Mr John [Scott] to process him.

Mr James Blair reported that **Jonet McGie** and **Marion McWilliam** are going on
to show signs of repentance for their adultery. Mr John Dick reported that **Robert
McIlhaffie** and **Isobell McMillan** are continuing to show signs of repentance.
Mr Alexander Turnbull reported he had written to the kirk of **Wigtown** concerning
the widows and orphans. The brethren reported they had enquired after **James
McBryde** but could not find him. Mr Alexander Turnbull gave in his theses. Mr John
Dick reported he had preached at Stranraer. The brethren, being removed and each
particularly enquired [privy trial], were approved. — *Mr John [Craig], clerk.*

## Notes & context
- ★★ **Mr James Lowrie admitted to Stonykirk (11 October 1648).** The young
  candidate whose Stonykirk call and trials have run since img 130 is now formally
  **admitted minister of Stonykirk** — a completed ordination arc and a headline
  biographical fact for his person-page.
- ★★ **Ardwell agrees to purge himself by oath.** After years of resistance, and
  following the General Assembly's ruling (img 141), Ardwell **declares himself
  willing to purge by oath**, appointed for the next kirk day before his own
  congregation. This is the effective resolution of the long Ardwell/Lady Freugh
  arc (watch the next openings for the oath actually taken and his counter-suit).
- ★ **New Glenluce case: Thomas Adair & Margaret Hannay.** Persistent cohabitation
  / "frequent and continued fornications," aggravated by **a new fornication with
  another woman** — referred to Mr John Scott (minister of Old Glenluce) to process.
- ★ **Paternity witnesses named: Cathrin and Jonet McBryd** (present at Marion's
  delivery) — the case's evidentiary net widens (note the recurring **McBryd(e)**
  surname, also the fugitive James McBryde).
- Routine penance/administration: McGie/McWilliam and McIlhaffie/McMillan penances
  continue; the fugitive **James McBryde** still not found; widows/orphans roll

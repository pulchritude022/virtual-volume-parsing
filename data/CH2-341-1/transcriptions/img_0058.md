## Notes & context
- **The Porterfield question sharpens — patron vs presbytery over money.** Threads from img
  0057 continue: the presbytery **writes to the Earl of Galloway to "satisfy Mr John
  Porterfield,"** and — tellingly — resolves that **if the Earl will not, it will refer the
  matter to the Lords of Session** (the civil court). This reads like a **stipend/teind or
  building charge** attaching to **Kirkcolm** (the parish the Earl claims to patronise), owed
  to **Porterfield** (the former minister or his estate) and now entangled with **Blair's
  settlement and the kirk's building/reparation.** A concrete money-and-patronage dispute for
  the events timeline; resolve the Porterfield identity in the refining pass. See

  kirk/manse)** and the presbytery arranges the **reparation of [Stoneykirk?]** — the material
  fabric of the parishes, not just their discipline. Good social/economic-history colour.

  were at the Synod**" — a nice glimpse of how the **Provincial Assembly** pulled ministers away

# CH2/341/1 — Image 125 (folios 124v–125), 29 December 1647–16 February 1648 — George Rumble's adultery penance; and Livingstone's report that he obtained a Stranraer stipend at Edinburgh and delivered Scott's presentation

Left page (folio **124a**): **Presbytery holden at Stranraer, December 29, 1647** — absent Mr John
Livingstone, Mr Alexander Turnbull, Mr John Crichton, and the ruling elders of Kirkmaiden and Portmontgomery;
**Mr James Blair handled the common head *De redemptione* (John 10:15) and was approved**; the Inch elder
excused; Blair and Dick reported they had prayed at Stranraer as appointed; then **★ George Rumble,
parishioner of Stranraer, at the direction of the session of Stranraer, compeared before the presbytery,
[confessed] the guilt of adultery with [—] Malinson(?), and was sharply admonished by the moderator and
enjoined to give signs of repentance in sackcloth**; **★ Mr John Livingstone appointed to preach at
Stranraer, and Mr John Crichton at the [old] kirk of Glenluce, [each] fortnight at least**; **the answer of
the letter sent to Ireland concerning John Bain(?) and Alexander McIlwrick is not yet come — nothing
reported**; the next meeting appointed at Stranraer this day month (Mr John Dick to make, Mr John Crichton to
add, or if he return not, Mr Hew Hutcheson). Then **Presbytery holden at Stranraer, January 26, 1648** —
absent Mr Alexander Turnbull and the ruling elders of Stranraer, Kirkmaiden and Portmontgomery; **Mr John
Dick made and Mr John Crichton added the exercise (1 Thess. 4:2–5) and both were approved**; Livingstone and
Crichton declared the reasons of their former absence, and were approved. Right page (folio **125**): **★★
the said day Mr John Livingstone reported that, according to the commission of the presbytery [of 10 Nov
1647], he went to Edinburgh and had obtained a competent stipend for the kirk of Stranraer from the [Lords]
for the plantation of kirks; 2. that he had delivered to Mr John Scott the presentation, subscribed by the
moderator and clerk of the presbytery at Glenluce the 22 of September 1647; 3. that he had dealt with the
[heritors] of Glenluce that they would pay to James Ross of Balneil the [arrears] of the mortification, [and
otherwise] to obtain the same in a legal way**; **the answer to the letter sent to the presbytery of Ireland
anent John Bain(?) and Alexander McIlwrick was not [yet] come**; **★ the said day the presbytery, taking to
consideration the [list of the] widows and orphans given in according to the act… appointed Mr John
Livingstone to write to the Lords [of Parliament]** [concerning them]; the next meeting appointed in February
(Mr John Crichton to make). Then **Presbytery at Stranraer, February 16 [1648]** — absent Mr John
Livingstone, Mr James Blair, and Mr Alexander Turnbull, and the ruling elders of Kirkmaiden, Portmontgomery,
New kirk of Glenluce, and Stoneykirk.

**Presbytery holden at Stranraer, December 29, 1647.** Absent Livingstone, Turnbull, Crichton, and the
elders of Kirkmaiden and Portmontgomery. **Mr James Blair handled the common head *De redemptione* (John
10:15) and was approved.** The Inch elder excused; Blair and Dick reported they had prayed as appointed.

John Crichton at the [old] kirk of Glenluce, at least fortnightly.** **The answer of the letter sent to

to make, Crichton to add, or Hutcheson). **Presbytery holden at Stranraer, January 26, 1648.** Absent
Turnbull and the elders of Stranraer, Kirkmaiden, Portmontgomery. **Mr John Dick made and Mr John Crichton
added the exercise (1 Thess. 4:2–5), both approved.** Livingstone and Crichton's former absence excused.

## Notes & context
- **★★ Mission accomplished — Livingstone reports back from Edinburgh.** The commission of img 0124 succeeds
  on all three heads: Livingstone reports he **obtained "a competent stipend for the kirk of Stranraer"** at
  Edinburgh (from the plantation-of-kirks authorities — the Galloway-bishopric petition bore fruit);
  **delivered Scott's presentation** (subscribed by the moderator and clerk, dated Glenluce 22 Sept 1647,
  img 0119); and **pressed the Glenluce heritors to pay James Ross of Balneil the mortification arrears**, to
  be pursued at law if not. Both the Stranraer and Glenluce finance threads — running since imgs 0101,
  0107 — reach a favourable resolution here.
- **★ George Rumble's penance.** A fresh Stranraer discipline case: **George Rumble confesses adultery (with
  [—] Malinson?) and is put to sackcloth** — the ordinary running of parish discipline alongside the
  high-politics.
- **The year turns to 1648.** The minutes cross into **1648** (sittings of 26 January and 16 February) — the
  eve of the Engagement crisis whose Kirk-side mobilisation appeared at img 0122. The **war-widows/orphans
  list** (a thread since img 0096) is finally acted on: **Livingstone to write to the Lords of Parliament**
  about relief.
- **Ireland still silent.** The **answer from the presbytery of Ireland** about the fled **McIlwrick** (and
  John Bain?) has **still not come** across two sittings — the cross-channel discipline machinery slow as
  ever.
- **Supply.** With Livingstone often away (Edinburgh, then absences) and the New/Old Glenluce kirks in
  transition, the presbytery arranges **fortnightly supply of Stranraer (Livingstone) and Glenluce
  (Crichton)**.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `obtained a competent stipend for ye kirk of Stranrawer… for
  plantationes of kirks` = securing a fixed stipend via the plantation authorities; `delivered to Mr Johne
  Scott ye presentatioun… at Glenluce ye 22 of September 1647` = handing over the formal presentation;
  `common head De redemptione, John 10:15` = the doctrine of redemption (the Good Shepherd text); `compeared…
  in sackcloth… enjoined to gyve signes of repentance` = public penance for adultery; `ye list of ye widowes
  and orphanes… Wryt to ye Lords` = pursuing relief for the war-bereaved. Left page firm; right page heavily
  water-stained — the Rumble partner's name, the Ireland names, and the February day are provisional. Capital
  **W** for *w/wh*. Years **1647→1648**.

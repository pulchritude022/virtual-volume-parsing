# CH2/341/1 — Image 156 (folios 155v–156)

The presbytery's ★★ **case-by-case verdicts on the examined Glenluce heritors** (left
page, foliated **155a** = folio 155 verso), the practical outcome of the whole
examination sequence: **Balneil** — decision *delayed to 27 December*, summoned *apud
acta*; **Thomas Hay of Park** — *delayed until the bill between him and Airieman is
cleared*; ★ **Airieman** — reported to have *uttered malignant speeches approving the
Engagement and railing against Argyll and the ministry* (witnesses **William Lin of
Larg** and **Thomas Gledstone**), to be summoned; **Dynan** — *may be admitted to the
Covenant on his confession*; **Michael Dinragot** — *received on repentance* (referred
to the minister); **Litle Dinragot** and **Alexander Hanna** — *admitted on
confession*; **Patrick McKie** — *delayed* pending the committee. The paper is extracted
and signed by **How Nisbett, session clerk**. The right page (folio 156) opens the
★ **Stranraer sitting of 27 December 1648**: exercise; **Mr James Lowrie** still awaits
possession of the **Toskerton and Clashquhat** glebes; the **Mochrum irregular-baptism**
case (by **Andrew Cowan**) advances with a memorable quoted phrase; and **John
Ronald(s)** answers for the **poor-relief collection** for his brother.

### Modern English
[**Balneil**, cont.:] ...the elders, being particularly enquired whether they knew him
to be a spreader of civil novels, all answered they knew no such thing. The Session,
having considered his answers (and the answers of the rest of the guilty), **recommends
to the presbytery** that they may enquire upon them and draw up the process, extracted
from the session book by **How Nisbett, clerk to the Session**.

**[Balneil]** — the presbytery, having heard his answer, thinks fit to **delay
consideration of the particular answers until the next meeting at Stranraer, 27
December**; and James Ross is summoned *apud acta* to be present that day.
**[Park]** — the presbytery think fit to **delay his consideration for the Covenant until
the bill depending between him and Airieman be cleared**.
**[Airieman]** — reported that he **uttered a number of malignant speeches approving the
Engagement, and railing against Argyll and the ministry** (witnesses **William Lin of
Larg** and **Thomas Gledstone**); he and they to be **summoned** for 27 December.
**[Dynan]** — already tried; the presbytery concludes **he may be admitted to the Covenant
on his confession**.
**[Michael Dinragot]** — **may be received by the minister on his repentance** (referred to
the minister to receive him next Sabbath or delay a day or two).
**[Litle Dinragot]** — **may be received to the Covenant on his confession**.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 39 (folios 38v–39) — the Blair/French judgment; Glenluce visitation; commissioners to end the Power case

Left page (folio **38a**): the presbytery's **arbitral judgment** between **French and
Mr James Blair** — it finds **"no solid ground" in French's paper**, French's
expressions "inconsiderate and rash," and Blair's credit **no way impaired** (unless
French gives a *formal* accusation de novo); **French protested.** Then: register the
**500-merk Glenluce bond**; **Mr George Dick** to raise **letters of exhibition** against
**James Rosse** to produce the **"gift of mortification" for Glenluce**; and the
**visitation of Glenluce, 9 June 1643** (**Mr John Porterfield** preached — found "**had
not taken pains**"; reports some elders given to drink). Right page (folio **39**,
mostly blank): **15 June 1643** — the **commissioners from Kirkcudbright and Wigtown
presbyteries**, sent by the **General Assembly** at the presbytery's request, **convene
to advise the "full and final determination" of the Power process.**

### Modern English
The presbytery, having considered the differences between **French and Mr James Blair**
upon their submission, **declares it finds no solid ground in French's paper**; wherefore
it has **no reason to entertain any prejudice against Blair** — the paper "**was not given
in by way of a formal accusation**," and **French's expressions are "inconsiderate and
rash" and no way impair Blair's credit** — until French gives in a **formal accusation,
containing the same points, de novo.** **Against this declaration, French protested.**

Business: the **500-merk bond for repairing the [kirk] of Glenluce** to be registered;
**Mr George Dick, [minister] of that parish, to raise letters of exhibition at the
instance of the parish of Glenluce against James Rosse, for producing the "gift of
mortification" [charitable endowment] in favour of Glenluce.**

**Presbytery at Glenluce, 9 June 1643, for a visitation.** Absent: the ruling elders of
[Inch], Glenluce, Stranraer. **Mr John Porterfield preached on his ordinary, Luke 11:4
[etc.]; the presbytery thought he "had not taken pains," was not well satisfied, and
exhorted him to take more.** **Mr John**, asked about the elders' carriage, declared
**some were given to excessive drinking, remiss in keeping the Session, and he was
jealous [suspected] they kept not family exercise.** The elders, asked about the
minister, **approved him.** Order: **Mr John Dick to take 10 merks of the contribution,
and 40 merks to Mr James Blair.**

### Modern English

## Notes & context
- **French loses the Blair round.** The presbytery's arbitral judgment is a clear **win
  for Mr James Blair**: French's charges are dismissed as "**inconsiderate and rash**,"
  not even a formal accusation, and Blair's reputation "**no way impaired**." **French
  protested** (as he does at every adverse ruling). A useful data-point on how the court
  distinguished Power's case (which it prosecuted) from Blair's (which it dismissed) —
  and on French's litigiousness.
- **The Power case reaches "full and final determination."** The pivotal procedural
  event: **commissioners from Kirkcudbright and Wigtown presbyteries**, dispatched by the
  **General Assembly** at Stranraer's request, **convene (15 June 1643) to advise the
  final judgment on Power.** This is the higher-court intervention Power's appeals had
  been steering toward — the case is now being resolved with outside oversight.
- **The "gift of mortification" for Glenluce.** A **mortification** = a charitable/pious
  endowment (land or money settled to a perpetual use, e.g. the poor or the kirk). **James
  Rosse** is being compelled by **letters of exhibition** to produce Glenluce's
  mortification-deed — likely why he has been dodging the presbytery (imgs 0031, 0037). A
  glossary entry and a motive for the Rosse sub-plot.
- **Who is Glenluce's minister?** At this **9 June 1643** visitation **Mr John
  Porterfield** preached (and reported on Glenluce's elders) — yet at img_0031 (May 1642)
  the minister of Glenluce read as **"Mr George [Park]."** Either the charge changed, or
  Porterfield preached as the appointed visitor while the resident minister (approved by
  the elders) is another. **Flag to resolve** — it affects the Glenluce minister's
  biography. (Also note: **Mr George Dick** is here called "[minister] of that parish"
  when raising the exhibition-letters — adding to the George Park/George Dick confusion.)
- **A blank page.** Folio 39 is left mostly empty after the 15 June entries — the clerk
  reserved space (perhaps for the commissioners' determination or James Rosse's business,
  entered elsewhere). Nothing lost.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `de novo` = anew, afresh (a fresh formal charge);
  `mortificatioun` = a charitable endowment in perpetuity; `letters of exhibition` = a
  writ compelling production of a document; `jealous` = suspicious; `familie exercise` =
  household worship; `taken paines` = prepared diligently (of a sermon). Capital **W**
  for *w/wh*. Dates 1643 as written.

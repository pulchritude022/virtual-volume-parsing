## Notes & context
★★★ **A section landmark — the Synod's revision note and the annual re-election.** Two administrative anchors fall here. First, the register carries the **Synod of Galloway's revision: "[Nov] 13, 1651, this book examined by Mr James Lawrie clerk of presbytery, [and] R. Ferguson [clerk of Synod]"** — the periodic synodal approbation (cf. imgs 166, 188, 211, 227). Second, at the **5 November 1651** sitting the presbytery holds its **post-Synod election: Mr James Bell chosen moderator, Mr James Blair chosen clerk** (Mr James Lawrie continuing as clerk of presbytery) — succeeding the Blair-moderator/Lawrie-clerk slate of April 1651 (img 227). A fresh **commission of ruling elders** is enrolled parish by parish.

★★ **Robert Campbell still will not satisfy.** The Engager-suspect of the qualified Donaghadee testimonial (imgs 223–226) — long a foil to the other Campbell (John) — **appears with his own minister Mr John Park but again refuses the satisfaction formerly enjoined,** and is once more rebuked and set toward public repentance, the brethren "labouring to convince him." The old defiance ("What! malignancy in me!", img 210) undimmed. The excommunicate **George Adestoun** (Alexstoune) "would not be found," his sentence again deferred.

★ **The lame-soldiers roll and the privy trial.** The presbytery again **fails to gather "the names of the lame soldiers"** (the war-relief census, cf. imgs 240–241) and renews the order; and conducts its **privy trial of ministers and elders**, rebuking elders "remiss in keeping the presbytery meetings." A **contribution for Mr William Forbes** is collected and forwarded.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 49 (folios 48v–49), April–May 1643 — the case goes to the Synod; the register meets the appeal

The chronological register **reaches the material copied at the front of the volume**. Left
page (folio **48a**): **Mr John Livingston reports on the Stoneykirk tumult-women** — some
fled the country, but several **submit** (**the Lady Sorbie, the Lady Killaster, [—]
Dunbar, James Grey**); then, on **6 April 1643**, the **lairds of Garthland, French,
Killaster (Ellaster), James Cathcart** and **Kirkcolm elders** give in a **fresh
supplication against Mr Gilbert Power** — and **Power "brake out"** (protests). Right page
(folio **49**): the moderator puts it to a vote whether Power be removed; **Power protests
and appeals to the Synod**, so the presbytery **refers all to the Provincial Assembly
(Synod) at Wigtown, 18 April 1643**. Then **Presbytery at Stranraer, 3 May 1643** (office
rotation: **Mr John Dick moderator**, Turnbull continued clerk); **Patrick McKelling, the
fugitive adulterer, reported now in Ireland.**

### Modern English (partial — page faded)
**Mr John Livingston**, appointed to speak to the **women who made the tumult at
Stoneykirk**, reported his diligence: several of those named "could not be reached" (some
gone out of the country), but some were content to **submit to the presbytery** — namely
**the Lady Sorbie, the Lady Killaster, [—] Dunbar, and James Grey** — who, called in,
**desired that Mr Gilbert be removed from sitting among them** in that matter, and **he of
his own accord removed.** [After business touching correspondence to the General Assembly
via their commissioner **Mr James Bower(?)**, and the "Gordon variance,"] some submitted to
the presbytery's order; the rest, absent, were ordered to **make public confession in the
kirk of Stoneykirk next Sabbath**, the moderator to rebuke them.

Then **the lairds of Garthland, French, Killaster (Ellaster), [others], James Cathcart**,
and **Kirkcolm elders gave in a supplication, under their hand, against Mr Gilbert Power**,
craving the presbytery not to delay or dispense with the accusation — **6 April 1643.**
That day, as the moderator was presenting the supplication, **Mr Gilbert broke out**
[protesting]…

### Modern English (partial — page faded)
[6 April 1643, cont.:] The moderator **asked the votes whether Mr Gilbert should be
removed** until the presbytery advised on the supplication. **But Mr Gilbert protested,
appealed to the Synod, and declined the presbytery.** Whereupon the presbytery **referred
both [supplication and answers] to the Provincial Assembly (Synod), to be held at Wigtown
on 18 April.** The presbytery then held the **privy trials** of members present (approved).

**Mr John Livingston** reported his diligence in **speaking to the [tumult] women**, and
that satisfaction was given to the **Synod at Wigtown, 19 April.** **Presbytery at
Stranraer, 3 May 1643:** exercise on Colossians 3:17; absents (Mr James Blair; the elders
of Inch and Stoneykirk). Ruling-elder **commissions produced** (Kirkmaiden → Patrick
McDowell; the **laird of Craichlaw** / Turnbull → John Campbell; etc.); the Stoneykirk
commission **not admitted.** **Mr John Dick chosen moderator, Mr Alexander Turnbull
continued clerk.** It was **reported that Patrick McKelling, the fugitive, was [now] in
Ireland.**

## Notes & context
- **The register meets the appeal — chronology locked.** This opening is the **hinge**
  between the chronological register (folios 29+) and the **appeal documents bound at the
  front of the volume (folios ~22–28)**: on **6 April 1643** the anti-Power lairds give in a
  fresh supplication, **Power appeals to the Synod and declines the presbytery**, and all is
  **referred to the Provincial Assembly (Synod) at Wigtown, 18 April 1643** — exactly the
  Synod the front folios argue before. Confirms: **deposition July 1642 → 1642–43 wrangling
  → 6 April 1643 supplication → Synod at Wigtown 18 April 1643 (the front-folio appeal).**
  A key note for [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]] and the wiki's event timeline.
- **The tumult-women resolved.** Livingston reconciles most of the **Stoneykirk tumult**
  women; several **submit** (the **Lady Sorbie, the Lady Killaster, [—] Dunbar, James
  Grey**), the rest ordered to **public confession**. Note the surprising **"Lady
  Killaster"** among them (Ellaster is an accuser) — flag. Power **voluntarily withdrew**
  from sitting when they asked.
- **Power still sits — and still fights.** Even deposed, Power was **"sitting amongst"** the
  presbytery and **"brake out"** against the supplication, then **appealed to the Synod and
  declined the presbytery's jurisdiction** — the same due-process/higher-court strategy as
  his 1642–43 appellation. He never accepts the verdict.
- **Office rotation & McKelling.** **Mr John Dick becomes moderator (May 1643)**, Turnbull
  continues clerk. **Patrick McKelling, the fugitive adulterer (img_0048), has fled to
  Ireland** — beyond the presbytery's reach.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `declyned the presbytrie` = formally refused its
  jurisdiction (a "declinature"); `privat/privie tryell` = the members' private examination;
  `voyces` = votes; `dispensing With [an] accusation` = setting it aside; `fugitive` = one
  fled from discipline/justice. **Both pages browned** — many readings provisional. Capital
  **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1643 (Scots New Style).

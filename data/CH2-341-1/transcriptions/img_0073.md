# CH2/341/1 — Image 73 (folios 72v–73), May 1645 — heritors refuse to repair the kirk; Sabbath-breaking; the Ferguson children to be baptized after satisfaction

Left page (folio **72a**): the **lairds of French and Cardiness refuse to contribute to repairing
the ruinous fabric of the kirk** until it be [surveyed] and Mr Gilbert [Power] better advised
(following his complaint, img 0072); a report that **there was much Sabbath indiscretion**, so the
**elders are to try and cite the transgressors before the session** and censure the guilty; **sessions
being visited, to be amended**; **Mr John Dick reports tracing where John McIlroy resides** (Mr James
Bell to be sent to take band of him for satisfaction); another **transgressor has fled the parish**,
a course to be taken for his trial; and an **adulterer appointed to give satisfaction before the kirk
of Stoneykirk.** Right page (folio **73**): **Mr George Dick reports a woman's will/testament** (made
"with mouth"; witnesses David Kennedy and Mr [—] Kennedy) — the presbytery to advise, continued to
next meeting; **Mr Alexander Turnbull reports**; and the presbytery **ordains that the children
procreated between John Ferguson and Agnes [and her sister?] be baptized by Mr George Dick, upon the
parents' public satisfaction on the Sabbath before the whole church.** Then **the presbytery convened
[at —], the [1]3 day of [May/June] 1645** — Mr James Bell's absence not reasonably excused; **absent
Blair, Livingstone, Hutcheson, and Bell**; **Turnbull preached (1 Cor. 7:15–16) and was approved.**

## Notes & context
- **★ The heritors won't pay to repair the kirk.** A direct sequel to Power's complaint (img 0072):
  the **lairds of French and Cardiness refuse to contribute to repairing the ruinous church** until
  it be surveyed — the material neglect of the parish laid at the heritors' door. A concrete social
  detail (heritors were legally bound to maintain the fabric) and a named-landowner node: **laird of
  French**, **laird of Cardiness (Cardoness?).**
- **★ The Ferguson affair resolved for the children.** The grim **John Ferguson** case (imgs
  0069–0070) reaches a pastoral conclusion: the **illegitimate children "procreated between John
  Ferguson and Agnes [and her sister]" are to be baptized by Mr George Dick once the parents make
  public satisfaction before the congregation** — the kirk balancing discipline against the
  children's need for baptism. Ties the Ferguson thread forward; note for the case timeline.
- **Sabbath discipline and absconding offenders.** **"Much indiscretion on the Sabbath"** (Sabbath-
  breaking) prompts the elders to identify and cite offenders; **John McIlroy's residence is traced**
  (Bell to take a band of him); and **another transgressor has fled the parish** — the ordinary,
  dogged machinery of parish discipline, and the recurring problem of offenders slipping the bounds.
- **A nuncupative will.** **Mr George Dick reports a woman's will made "with mouth"** (an oral/
  nuncupative testament), witnessed by **David Kennedy** and **Mr [—] Kennedy** — the presbytery to
  advise. Likely a bequest touching the kirk (a mortification or legacy); flag for follow-up when the
  next meeting resolves it.
- **A thinned bench.** With **Blair and Livingstone both in Ireland** and **Hutcheson and Bell also
  absent**, the presbytery is running short-handed in mid-1645 — Turnbull, the two Dicks, and the
  aged Power carrying the load.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `contribute to the repairing of the fabrick` = pay towards
  repairing the church building; `Indiscretioun on the Sabbath` = Sabbath-breaking/misconduct; `tak
  band of him` = take a written obligation (bond) binding him to appear/satisfy; `childeren
  procreate betwixt… A on B` = children begotten by A on B (out of wedlock); `made her Will… with
  mouth` = a nuncupative (spoken) testament. **Both pages faded; folio 73 outer edge water-stained**
  — provisional throughout. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1645.

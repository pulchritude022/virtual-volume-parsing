## Notes & context
★★ **The case against Balneil finally acquires signed ministerial evidence.** After the witness route collapsed (img 173), the presbytery pivots to **written, subscribed declarations from its own ministers** — the surest corroboration available. **Mr Alexr Turnbull** subscribes numbered articles: that Balneil (though moved by Sir Robert Adair to subscribe the Covenant at first) went with the Engagement; that he **married Mr Thomas Hay's daughter at Sorbie**; that **[Callander] was Lt-Major to his regiment for "the Last unlawful Engagement," about June 1648**; and that **he convened his men to the Engagement before the Act of Estates was discharged.** **Mr John Dick** subscribes that he heard Balneil **argue in defence of the Duke Hamilton war, "that it was dangerous to stand out against a standing authority and a standing army,"** and that Balneil drew up a paper to supply the heritors who marched into England in the first Engagement. This is the documentary spine the Commission demanded (img 173).

★ **The process to be extracted from the minutes.** Turnbull and James Ross (elder) are appointed, with Mr John Scott, to **draw the whole Balneil process out of the presbytery minutes and subscribe it "in form"** — i.e. assemble the formal case-file for the General Assembly, exactly as the Commission ordered.

★ **The General Assembly is now sitting (July 1649).** At the 18 July sitting, **Mr James Blair and Mr John Scott are absent "at the General Assembly"** — the 1649 GA to which the whole Balneil dossier is bound. A thin, exercise-less meeting (Crosse absent; Dick repeats his text).

# CH2/341/1 — Image 129 (folios 128v–129)

A dense opening carrying **four sitting-headings**. Left page (foliated **128a**
= folio 128 verso): (1) **"At the Abbey of Glenluce, March 23"** — a physical
**visitation/fabric survey of Glenluce Abbey**: the brethren view the roof and go
through the several rooms (the choir, the halls, the rooms above), noting for each
the state of doors, windows, glass and roof — most windows broken, some rooms
lacking doors, ceilings rotten, though the main roof is "made good"; (2) **"At the
New kirk of Glenluce, March 23"** — the kirk deals with **James Rosse** about a
gleib, and nominates men (with the laird of Barnbarroch as oddsman) to settle it;
(3) the **next meeting appointed at Stranraer, 1 April**, Mr John Hutcheson to make
the exercise; (4) **"Presbytery at Stranraer, the 5 day of April 1648"** — absents
noted (Mr John Craig and a ruling elder gone to the **Synod of Glasgow** to keep
correspondence). The right page (folio 129) continues the 5 April Stranraer
sitting with a long, badly-faded **probation deposition in the Marion [—] /
James McMillan paternity case** (involving travel to Ireland via Portpatrick and
Stranraer, and named witnesses incl. Thomas Wilsone), ending with an act
appointing the moderator to have **Mr Edward [—]** cause James McMillan compear
before the session of Glenluce.

elder of Portmontgomery absent.)

The deposition is a **probation in the Marion [—] / James McMillan paternity
case** referred up from the Glenluce session (continued from image 128). A witness

## Notes & context
- ★ **Fabric survey of Glenluce Abbey (23 March 1648).** A rare, concrete
  architectural record: the presbytery walks the ruinous abbey with Mr John Scott
  (now minister of the old kirk), room by room — the **choir**, the **[north]
  hall**, the **room above the east hall**, the **close**, the **[fire-house]** —
  noting doors, broken windows, missing glass, a rotten ceiling, but a roof "made
  good." This is the practical follow-through on giving Scott possession of the
  abbey (img 128) — assessing what repairs the building needs to serve as kirk.
- ★ **New kirk of Glenluce, same day:** the gleib/feuing dispute with **James
  Rosse** — two arbiters nominated, with the laird of Barnbarroch as oddsman if
  they disagree. Continues the New-kirk endowment thread.
- ★ **Synod of Glasgow correspondence:** Mr John Craig and a ruling elder are
  absent from the 5 April Stranraer sitting because they are at the **Synod of
  Glasgow "for keeping correspondence"** — evidence of inter-synodal liaison during
  the 1648 Engagement crisis.
- ★ **Marion / James McMillan probation (5 April 1648):** the paternity case from
  img 128 comes to formal probation, with a convoluted narrative of cross-border
  travel (Portpatrick ⇄ Ireland via Stranraer) and named witnesses. Remitted for
  James McMillan to be summoned before the Glenluce session via Mr Edward [—].

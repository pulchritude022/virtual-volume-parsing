# CH2/341/1 — Image 63 (folios 62v–63), September 1644 — **the reposition takes effect: Power exercises and is approved**

Left page (folio **62a**): the **Visitation of the kirk of Kirkcolm, 5 September 1644** — **Mr
James Bell preaches (Matthew 5, the Beatitudes)**, and **Mr James Blair (minister of Kirkcolm) is
examined and approved** (elders approve his life, doctrine, discipline; he "painful in
catechising"; the usual plea for **family worship**); then a **fornication case** (a "Mr Donell"
and a woman in **Leswalt**, who claimed she was **married in Ireland**). Right page (folio
**63**): the woman **could produce no testimonial** of her Irish marriage and is put to
**satisfaction**; then — the key entry — **Presbytery at Stranraer, 18 September 1644**, where
**Mr Gilbert Power does the weekly exercise (Colossians 4:3–4) and is approved by Mr Hew
Hutcheson** — the **reposition (imgs 0061–0062) now visibly in effect: Power is a functioning,
approved member again.** Absence discipline closes the page (John Dick reprehended).

## Notes & context
- **★ The reposition takes visible effect — Power exercises and is approved.** Just weeks after
  the **General Assembly's reposition act was registered** (imgs 0061–0062), **Mr Gilbert Power
  is appointed to "make and add" the weekly presbytery exercise (Colossians 4:3–4), does so, and
  is approved by Mr Hew Hutcheson** (18 Sept 1644). After two years of being **"not approven" and
  "not desyred"** at every privy trial, Power is now **a fully functioning, approved member** —
  the concrete proof that the restoration was real and honoured. The only note is a mild caution
  about **preaching length.** This closes the Power arc on **vindication and reintegration**. See
  [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]].
- **Kirkcolm confirmed as Blair's parish.** The **5 Sept 1644 visitation of Kirkcolm** examines
  and approves **Mr James Blair** as its minister — firming up **Kirkcolm = Blair's charge** (cf.
  the img-0060 "Portmontgomery" visitation, also of Blair; the two suggest "Portmontgomery" is an
  alias for, or the same round as, Kirkcolm — still worth Dad's confirmation). Same visitation
  form; the recurring pastoral emphases are **catechising** and **family worship.**
- **A cross-border marriage dispute.** A woman (**Marion Birr?**, Leswalt) accused of fornication
  **claims she was married in Ireland but cannot produce a testimonial** — so the kirk treats the
  union as unproven and puts both parties to **satisfaction/censure.** A neat illustration of how
  **testimonials governed mobility and marriage** across the Scotland–Ulster channel, and of the
  evidentiary weight the kirk placed on them (cf. the testimonials at img 0056). Genealogically
  suggestive (an Irish marriage, named parties).
- **Routine absence discipline.** Livingstone, Turnbull, and George Dick excuse their absences;
  **John Dick, without reasonable cause, is reprehended** — the ordinary maintenance of
  attendance.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `make and add` = deliver the exercise and the "addition"
  (the two-part weekly sermon-exercise); `painfull in catechising` = diligent/laborious in
  catechising; `testimoniall` = a certificate (here, of a marriage) — without it the marriage is
  unproven; `satisfactioun` = the discipline of public repentance; `reprehended` = formally
  rebuked. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1644.

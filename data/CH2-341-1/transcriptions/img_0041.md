# CH2/341/1 — Image 41 (folios 40v–41) — the GUILTY VERDICT against Mr Gilbert Power (16 July [1643])

**The climax of the whole case.** Left page (folio **40a**) closes the **Stoneykirk
Session supplication** (they are "out of hope to be edified by his ministry") with its
**nine subscribers** (Garthland, Ardwell, Ballespie, James Gordon in Culgroat, Hew
McClure, Uchtred McCulloch, George McBride, Patrick McCubbin, Mungo McDowell); then the
presbytery **with the neighbouring commissioners revises the process**, adjourns to
**16 June** (5 a.m.), enacts that absent elders must make public confession, and takes
two last depositions — **Ardnell (Ardwell) on the ten-merk Tarbret bribe** and **Uchtred
McDowell "in the Chapel" on victual prices 1621–23**. Right page (folio **41**) is the
**sentence**: the presbytery, with the two presbyteries' brethren, having revised the
whole process at the instance of **Uchtred McDowell of French and James Gordon in
Culgroat**, and **Power thrice called and not compearing**, **finds and declares Mr
Gilbert Power GUILTY** — (1) **collusion in cloaking an adulterer for money**; (2)
**usury, divers times practised**; [more crimes follow on the next opening].

### Modern English
[Session supplication, concl.:] "…the Lord wrought in him. **We are out of hope either to
be edified by his ministry**, but put rather jealousies, prejudices, and heart-burnings,
prejudicial to the Lord's work among us. And [we crave] your instance." **Subscribed:**
**(1) Garthland, (2) Ardwell, (3) Ballespie[?], (4) James Gordon in Culgroat, (5) Hew
McClure, (6) Uchtred McCulloch, (7) George McBride, (8) Patrick McCubbin, (9) Mungo
McDowell.** Witnesses: **Mr Thomas McNaught, [—] McCulloch.**

That day the presbytery **with the commissioners went to revising the process against Mr
Gilbert Power**; adjourned to **the morrow, 16 June [1643]**, members and interested
parties to attend **at 5 a.m.** On **16 June** it was enacted that any **ruling elder
absent without lawful excuse must make public confession** before the congregation.

That day **Ardnell (Ardwell) came in and declared** that **when he and Mr Gilbert agreed
for the ten merks for cloaking Robert Tarbret's adultery, there was no witness**; Mr
Gilbert "would not agree… until he spoke with the Session, which was allowed." **Uchtred
McDowell "in the Chapel" declared** that **the price of victual in 1621, 1622, 1623 was
ordinarily twelve pounds or twenty merks** [per boll], sometimes more, sometimes cheaper
— "the common price would run to above… twelve…"

### Modern English
[Victual, concl.:] "…pound, always." That day the **clerk was ordained to send out a
roll** from which a competent number might be chosen as **sub-commissioners for
[teind] valuation.**

That day the **presbytery of Stranraer, the whole brethren of both presbyteries being
present, having revised the whole process led and deduced against Mr Gilbert Power at the
instance of Uchtred McDowell of French and James Gordon in Culgroat** — "**being fully
advised and satisfied of all doubts and scruples they could propone**" — **thought good
to pass to the sentence.** The officer was appointed to call Mr Gilbert, orderly, to
appear before the presbytery **to hear sentence pronounced against him**; and **he being
thrice called and not compearing, the presbytery, being advised, proceeded to the
sentence**, as follows:

"**At Stranraer, the 16 of July 1642 [= 1643].** That day the presbytery, **with the
advice of some of the brethren of the neighbouring presbytery, having seriously pondered

## Notes & context
- **THE VERDICT — the case's climax.** After a year of libel, witnesses, suspension, and
  appeals, the presbytery — reinforced by **commissioners of the neighbouring presbyteries
  (Kirkcudbright & Wigtown, sent by the General Assembly, img_0039)** — **finds Mr Gilbert
  Power GUILTY.** Power, **thrice called, did not appear**; sentence was pronounced in
  absentia. The enumerated crimes begin: **(1) cloaking an adulterer (Robert Tarbret) for
  money; (2) usury** — and continue onto the next opening (expect the full list, then the
  censure, likely **deposition from the ministry**). This is the headline event of Power's
  biography.
- **Chronology crux — "1642" vs 1643.** The MS writes **"the 16 of July 1642"** (and "16
  of June 1642" on the left page), yet this stretch of the *live* register is otherwise
  **1643** (imgs 0036–0040: "May 19 1643"; the June-1643 GA commissioners whose **advice
  the verdict itself cites**; Livingston's June-1643 Irish commission). The verdict's
  reliance on those commissioners **pins it to 1643** — so **"1642" here is a clerk's slip
  for 1643** (a common error in the year after the 25-March year-change). **Flag for the
  reviewer:** confirm the sentence date is **16 July 1643**, and that Power's Synod appeal
  (folios ~22–28) was against the earlier **1642 suspension**, remitted and now finalised.
- **Power's own Session damns him — the subscribers named.** The Stoneykirk supplication
  is subscribed by **nine men**: gentry (**Garthland, Ardwell**), the third accuser
  (**James Gordon in Culgroat**), a witness (**Uchtred McCulloch**), and elders (**Hew
  McClure, George McBride, Patrick McCubbin, Mungo McDowell, Ballespie**). This is the
  **anti-Power coalition on his own parish's Session**, a crucial people-cluster for the
  wiki — overlapping the accuser/witness pool.
- **The Tarbret bribe, from the taker's mouth.** **Ardnell (Ardwell)** confirms the
  **ten-merk deal with Power "for cloaking Robert Tarbret's adultery,"** done privately
  ("no witness") until Power "spoke with the Session" — the very point on which Power was
  first suspended (img_0012). Note the sum here is **10 merks** (earlier notes/memory had
  **£5**; reconcile).
- **Grain-price evidence.** **Uchtred McDowell "in the Chapel"** deposes that **victual
  (grain) sold at ~12 pounds / 20 merks per boll in 1621–23** — evidence to test whether
  Power's payments-in-kind/annualrent were usurious (the going rate matters to the usury
  charge). A nice economic datum for the wiki's "money/prices" glossary.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `deduced` (of a process) = formally carried
  through/led; `probationes` = the proofs/evidence; `pass to the sentence` = proceed to
  judgment; `dispensing… him` = relaxing church discipline for him; `sub-commissioners for
  valuation` = a teind-valuation sub-committee; `ryde in above` = amount to more than. MS
  writes "1642." **Left-page inner margin blotted.** Capital **W** for *w/wh*.

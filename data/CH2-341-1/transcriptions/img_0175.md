### Modern English
Mr Alexr Turnbull reported that he had kept the exercise; and that **he and Mr James Ross had extracted the process concerning Balneil out of the presbytery minutes, according to order.** He had preached at Stranraer [2] July.

Mr James Lawrie reported he preached at Portpatrick, 8 July. Mr John Dick appointed to preach at [New] Glenluce and Mr James Bell at Portpatrick.

**The presbytery [and town] of Stranraer desired the presbytery to concur with the town and parish of Stranraer in giving the call to Mr John Park** [as minister]. The presbytery appointed the moderator to write to its commissioner at the Assembly to give him [warrant], in the presbytery's name, **entreating the act of transportation in his favour before the Assembly.**

Delinquents: **William Williamson [in] Stoneykirk** and **Roger Campbell in [Kirkinner]**, cited and not compearing, ordained to appear again next presbytery day.

Next meeting a fortnight hence; Dick to make and Crosse to add (1 Thess 5:25).

**Presbytery at Stranraer, 8 August 1649. No exercise this day, because the fair-goers [the commissioners] were not yet returned from the Assembly.** Absent: Blair, Scott, Dick, and all ruling elders except the New Kirk of Balneil. **Thomas McNish and William Stonehouse**, for disobedience to the Stoneykirk session, compeared, were rebuked, and remitted back to the session for further censure.

**Mr John Crosse reported the supplication presented to Parliament for the widows'-and-orphans' moneys, and that an act was passed for the ministers' stipends.**

## Notes & context
★★ **The General Assembly resolves the Balneil case (Aug 1649).** After a year of referrals, refusals, and failed witness-gathering, the 1649 General Assembly disposes of the case: **Balneil's process is remitted back to the presbytery "to receive him to public satisfaction … and thereafter to admit him to the Covenant."** So the malignancy prosecution converts into a **repentance-and-reconciliation** track — Balneil is to make public satisfaction and be re-admitted to the renewed Covenant. This is the practical endpoint the img 162–174 loop was grinding toward; the presbytery's extracted process (img 174) went up, and the GA sent this direction down.

★★ **A call to Mr John Park for vacant Stranraer.** The town and parish of Stranraer — vacant since **Livingstone's transportation to Ancrum in 1648** — move to **call Mr John Park**, and the presbytery asks its GA commissioner to entreat the **act of transportation in his favour**. This begins the planting of Stranraer's pulpit (Park has appeared earlier as the Wigtown correspondent, imgs 168, 170).

★★ **A fresh Commission for the trial of witches.** Among the acts brought back is a **Commission for the trial of witches**, which Mr James Blair and the ministers are to deliver to the named justices — the 1649 witch-hunt (a national peak year) reaching the Rhins, echoing the 1644 witch processes of imgs 64–65 and 89.

★ **The 1649 Parliament's poor-relief legislation** lands locally: acts for the **poor's maintenance** and for **widows and orphans** (the war-relief funds this presbytery has been administering since 1646), plus the **act for ministers' stipends** and a **Solemn Fast on 26 August**. Also a discharge for the **Highland regiment's** arrears out of John [Cathcart]'s rents (a lingering war-finance item).

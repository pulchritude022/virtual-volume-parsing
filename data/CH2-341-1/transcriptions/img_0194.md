## Notes & context
★★★ **Thanksgiving for the defeat of Montrose — the Commission of the Kirk's circular letter, verbatim.** The centrepiece is the **Commission of the General Assembly's letter (dated Edinburgh, 24 May 1650, signed by Mr Robert Douglas, Moderator)** appointing a national day of thanksgiving for **"the deliverance of this Kirk from the intended violence and oppression of that excommunicate traitor James Grahame [Montrose], who, with the assistance of strangers and some unnatural countrymen, had invaded the western parts of this kingdom."** This is thanksgiving for the crushing of **Montrose's 1650 rising — his defeat at Carbisdale (27 April 1650) and execution at Edinburgh (21 May 1650)** — reaching the Rhins pulpits within a fortnight. The presbytery sets **Wednesday 12 June 1650** for the solemn thanksgiving in all its kirks. **Mr Robert Douglas** (the pre-eminent Covenanter minister, who would crown Charles II at Scone on 1 Jan 1651) signing as GA Moderator is a notable national fingerprint. (Cf. the Montrose "scandalous pamphlet" declaration read at img 184, on the *eve* of Carbisdale — here the aftermath.)

★★ **The robber named and pursued.** The robbery of the "poor stranger/Englishman" (imgs 184, 186, 188, 193) is pinned firmly on **Thomas Baillie, son to John Baillie in Laigh Cross**, already cited before the Committee of War; Scott to cite him to next presbytery.

★ **Ongoing 1650 vigilance.** A **papist "malignantly affected" incomer from Wigtown** taking up residence in the bounds is to be reported to the Synod (cf. "foreign malignants," imgs 188, 190). And **another Lieutenant-Colonel Engager (a McDowall)** supplicates to profess repentance — answer deferred. Also: the *pro re nata* sittings (4 June, 12 June) and the reshuffled meeting calendar (thanksgiving on the 12th pushing the ordinary meeting to 19 June).

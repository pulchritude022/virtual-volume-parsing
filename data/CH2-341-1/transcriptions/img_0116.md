## Notes & context
- **★ The Argyll collection receipted — a signed discharge from Provost Porterfield.** A documentary gem:
  the presbytery records the **verbatim discharge (receipt)** for the Argyll relief. **David Lennox** paid
  it in "in name of the [session] of Stranraer," and **George Porterfield, provost of Glasgow** (the
  distributor of the national Argyll collection — cf. imgs 0096, 0109, 0114) **signed for "[three?] hundred
  twenty-nine lib. 3s." at Glasgow on 20 May 1647.** This closes the loop on the long relief thread with a
  hard figure and two named agents. (The leading digit of the sum is faded — likely "three hundred," giving
  ~£329 Scots.)
- **★ The Kirkmaiden visitation — the anatomy of a kirk inspection.** A textbook Covenanter **parish
  visitation**: the minister (**Turnbull**, of Kirkmaiden) **preaches and is approved**; then he is
  **removed while the elders are examined one by one** on his preaching, sacraments, and discipline — and
  they **approve him**; the **session book is called for** (but the clerk is absent); and the **people are
  examined** on whether they concur with the elders in discipline. A clean example of the three-part
  scrutiny (minister / elders / people) for the wiki's "visitation" glossary node. It ends with a live
  discipline case — **Marion Mc[—]'s confession of adultery with Thomas Adair** (continued overleaf).
- **★ Thomas Adair again.** The **Adair** thread deepens: the man of the £100 bond to shun Margaret Hannay
  (img 0097), later a fugitive suspected of adultery (imgs 0114–0115), is now named in **Marion Mc[—]'s
  confession of adultery with him** at Kirkmaiden. A person-page for Adair will need to knit together the
  bond, the flight, and these successive accusations.
- **Threads continued.** The **McColby scandal** (Anna Mc Colby + John Mc[—], img 0115) is held over;
  **Euphame Mc Robb** is pursued for **not adhering to her husband** (a rare marital-desertion/adherence
  case, distinct from the sexual-scandal cases); another **adultery** is referred down to the **Portmontgomery
  session**; and the court's **correspondence network** widens — Ireland, **Ayr** (Dick), and **Wigtown**
  (Bell, who is to attend Wigtown on 23 June, apparently for a neighbouring minister's admission).
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `ane discharge fra George Porterfield… Received be me David Lennox…
  for ye use of ye distressed people of Argyle… Sic subscribitur` = a formal receipt engrossed in the
  minutes; `required to adhere to her husband… compeired not… summoned pro 2` = an adherence (anti-desertion)
  citation with a second summons; `being removed… severalie inquyred if they had any thing agt him… they Did
  approve him` = the elders' private testimony about their minister at visitation; `ye session booke could
  not be gotten` = the session register unavailable for inspection. **Both pages faded; the right torn on the
  outer margin** — the Argyll sum's leading digit and several surnames provisional. Capital **W** for
  *w/wh*. Year **1647**.

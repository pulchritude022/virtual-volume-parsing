# CH2/341/1 — Image 74 (folios 73v–74), June 1645 — a storm stops the exercise at the Water of Luce; an absconder traced to Douglas

Left page (folio **73a**): the tail of the previous sederunt — the **elders privily tried and
approved** (a few to be admonished "to walk more exactly"), the **session book visited and
approved**, and the **next presbytery appointed at Stranraer for 25 June**, **Mr George Dick to
handle the common head "de libero arbitrio"** (on free will). Then the heading **"Presbytery at
Stranraer, the 25 [June] 1645"** — where **the common head was not handled "in respect of the great
storm," which raised the Water of Luce so that Mr George Dick, who should have handled the
controversy, could not get over the water till it was fair in the afternoon**; **absent Turnbull;
Blair (excused the day before), Charles Campbell**; and **Mr James Bell reports he had spoken with
[McIlroy's host].** Right page (folio **74**): Bell to **take band of [McIlroy] to be forthcoming**;
**Mr John Dick reports the [absconder] is now residing at Douglas**, so the presbytery orders **Mr
James Blair to write to the session of Douglas**; and further business on **Mr George Dick and the
[Ferguson] children** (Agnes and Jonet), touching satisfaction and baptism.

## Notes & context
- **★ A storm stops the sermon — the Water of Luce in spate.** A wonderfully concrete weather
  vignette: at the **25 June 1645** meeting the **common head ("de libero arbitrio" — on free will)
  could not be handled "in respect of the great storm," which so raised the Water of Luce that Mr
  George Dick, who was to deliver it, could not cross till the afternoon.** Rare, vivid evidence of
  how weather and the Galloway rivers shaped presbytery life — good colour for a "life of the
  presbytery" page, and a datable storm (late June 1645). Place node: **Water of Luce.**
- **An absconder traced to Douglas; cross-presbytery letters.** The discipline machine again reaches
  across the country: a **transgressor (McIlroy?) is found to be residing at Douglas** (Lanarkshire),
  so **Blair is ordered to write to the session of Douglas** — echoing the Ferguson hue-and-cry
  (imgs 0069–0070) and confirming how the kirk pursued offenders through the network of sessions and
  presbyteries. **Mr James Bell** meanwhile is to **take a band of the man to be forthcoming.**
- **The Ferguson children again.** **Mr George Dick** carries forward the matter of the children of
  **Agnes and Jonet [Chairteris?]** (the Ferguson affair, imgs 0069, 0073) — the local session to
  proceed to baptism once **signs of repentance** appear. (The surname read here, *Chairteris*, is
  very uncertain — possibly the "Agnes/Jonet" of the earlier Ferguson entries under a fuller name.)
- **Governance rhythm.** A **privy trial** of the elders (a few to "walk more exactly") and the
  **visitation and approval of the session book** — the standard oversight, before the storm-struck
  sederunt.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `common head "de libero arbitrio"` = the set theological
  commonplace on free will (disputed at the Synod); `the great storme… raised the Watter of Luce`
  = a flood on the River Luce; `culd not… over the Watter till it Was faire after nune` = could not
  ford it till the afternoon; `tak band of him to be forthcoming` = take a bond binding him to
  appear; `Writt a letter to the Session of Douglas` = correspond with a distant kirk session.
  **Folio 74 is badly faded/ghosted** — large stretches illegible and provisional. Capital **W** for
  *w/wh*. Dates 1645.

# CH2/341/1 — Image 79 (folios 78v–79), August 1645 — Garthland gets a loft; the Assembly's circular on the sins of the land

Left page (folio **78a**): **the laird of Garthland petitions for a place in the kirk to build a
loft** (a private gallery pew) — the presbytery **grants him the place west of the pulpit and above
the window** to build it; then it records **a letter from the commissioners of the General Assembly,
with an enclosed paper of articles**, ordered engrossed in the presbytery book, whose tenor begins
**"Reverend and beloved brethren…"** Right page (folio **79**): the body of that **circular letter** —
the Assembly's commissioners, having by diligent search examined **the causes of God's wrath against
the land**, set apart a solemn day, had their ministers one by one "open their mouth," and drew up **a
catalogue of the sins of the land**, which they **transmit to every presbytery**, exhorting that the
same course of **confession and bewailing of sin, and the pressing of every family privately to
examine itself, be followed in the several sessions, "that so at last all Scotland may turn unto the
Lord, that His fierce wrath may depart from us"** — subscribed **"Your loving brethren, the
[commissioners of the] General Assembly, [Mr —] Bennet, moderator, Perth, 5 August 1645."** The
presbytery orders the letter and articles engrossed for its governance; then the **visitation of the
kirk of Inch** resumes.

### Modern English (paraphrase — long circular, much faded)
The Assembly's commissioners write that, having by diligent and accurate **search examined the sins
of the land that provoke God's wrath**, they **set apart a solemn day**, had **each of their ministers
in turn "open his mouth"** to acknowledge the faults, and **drew up a catalogue of sins**, which they
**enclose and transmit to every presbytery**, exhorting that the same course be **followed
presbyterially and in the several kirk sessions — a solemn confession and bewailing of sin, and every
family privately pressed to examine itself — "that so at last all Scotland may turn unto the Lord,
that His fierce wrath may depart from us."** They commit the brethren and their labours to God's
blessing. Subscribed **"Your loving brethren, the [commissioners of the] General Assembly, [Mr —]
Bennet, moderator, Perth, 5 August 1645."** The presbytery **orders the letter and articles engrossed
in its book for its governance.** [Then the **visitation of the kirk of Inch** resumes.]

## Notes & context
- **★ A loft in the kirk for the laird of Garthland.** A concrete piece of church material culture:
  **the laird of Garthland (McDowell of Garthland, the leading Stoneykirk heritor) is granted the
  space "west of the pulpit and above the window" to build a private loft** (an elevated family pew/
  gallery). Church seating was a marker of rank and a frequent source of dispute; this grant documents
  Garthland's standing and the physical layout of the kirk. A person/place detail for Garthland and
  the parish page.
- **★ The Assembly's "causes of wrath" circular (Perth, 5 Aug 1645).** The presbytery engrosses a
  **circular letter from the commissioners of the General Assembly** enclosing **a catalogue of the
  sins of the land**, ordering a coordinated course of **humiliation, confession, and family
  self-examination "that all Scotland may turn unto the Lord, that His fierce wrath may depart."** This
  is the classic **Covenanting "causes of a solemn fast"** genre — in the disastrous summer of 1645
  (Montrose's victories, plague), the Kirk read national defeat as divine chastisement for sin and
  drove repentance down through presbyteries, sessions, and households. A rich theme/glossary node
  (national fasts; the "causes of wrath"), and it dovetails with the 26 July humiliation and the levy
  (img 0078). Subscription **"[Mr —] Bennet, moderator"** — verify the name.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `a place… to build a loft` = permission to erect a private
  gallery pew; `lyeth west fra the pulpit, above the Window` = the position in the kirk assigned;
  `commissioners of the Generall Assemblie` = the standing Commission of the Assembly; `articles of
  the controversie` = points of the church's contendings; `engrossed in the presbytery buke` = written
  fair into the register; `a catalogue of sinnes… that His fierce wrath may depairt fra us` = an
  enumerated confession of national sins, in the fast-day idiom. **Both pages faded** — the long
  circular is provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1645.

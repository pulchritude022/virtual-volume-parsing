# CH2/341/1 — Image 83 (folios 82v–83), Aug–Oct 1645 — ★ a thanksgiving for the victory at Philiphaugh; supplying vacant Glenluce

Left page (folio **82a**, badly washed): the brethren appoint a **presbytery meeting at Stranraer for
Monday 18 August**; then the **presbytery met at Stranraer on Saturday [26 September] upon occasion of
a letter [touching] the distress of the land**, and **★ appointed a thanksgiving to be kept for the
victory obtained at Philiphaugh against the rebels — for 27 September, the Lord's day**; then a
heading, **Presbytery at Stranraer, 9 October 1645** (an exercise on Col. 4, approved; absentees).
Right page (folio **83**): **Mr Alexander Turnbull excused for his absence at the Inch visitation** (and
the absent elders likewise); a **visitation of Glenluce appointed for 20 October**; the brethren
**report they kept the thanksgiving [for Philiphaugh] on 27 September**; and, **the parishioners of
[Stoneykirk/Glenluce] desiring supply, the presbytery arranges to supply the vacant charge of Glenluce
by rotation** — Mr John Dick to preach there the next Lord's day, others to follow — "till the Lord be
pleased to provide a minister of their own."

## Notes & context
- **★★ A thanksgiving for Philiphaugh (Sept 1645).** The volume's most dramatic war-news entry: **"a
  thanksgiving to be kept for the victory obtained at Philiphaugh against the rebels,"** appointed for
  **Sunday 27 September 1645** and duly kept. The **Battle of Philiphaugh (13 September 1645)** was
  **David Leslie's crushing defeat of Montrose** — the turning point that ended the royalist rising in
  Scotland. Here the Rhins of Galloway learn of it by letter and give public thanks: a vivid,
  precisely-dated instance of national military news reaching a remote presbytery and being marked in
  worship. A headline event node (Philiphaugh; the 1645 thanksgiving), the counterpart to the earlier
  levy and fast (imgs 0078–0079).
- **Glenluce still vacant — supply by rotation.** The long **Glenluce planting** thread (imgs
  0044–0047, 0071) continues: the charge is **still without a settled minister**, so the presbytery
  arranges **supply preaching by the brethren in turn** (John Dick, James [Stuart?], and another) "till
  the Lord provide a minister of their own," and appoints a **Glenluce visitation for 20 October.** The
  new kirk is built (img 0071) but not yet filled — useful for the Glenluce parish page and timeline.
- **Attendance enforced, gently.** Turnbull and the elders **excused** for missing the Inch visitation
  — the presbytery balancing discipline with reasonable excuse.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `a thanksgiving… for the victorie obtained at Philiphauch

  rebells` = Montrose's royalist army; `supplie the charge… by severall fortnights` = provide pulpit

## Notes & context
★★★ **A parish visitation from the inside — school, poor-fund, and a ruinous kirk.** This is the fullest ordinary-visitation record in the batch. At **old Glenluce** the elders privately approve their minister's life and name none of their number scandalous; then the minister, given his turn, "had nothing" against his elders but **regrated three parish wants: no [school] for "the breeding of young children," poor-fund money owing that "would not be gotten in," and the ruinous fabric of the kirk.** The present heritors **condescend to set aside a parish rent to maintain a school** — a concrete glimpse of parochial education, poor relief, and church upkeep as everyday presbytery business, distinct from the war and discipline that dominate 1651.

★★ **The kirk-fabric repair put in hand.** The presbytery does not let the ruinous kirk sit: it **appoints the moderator (Mr James Blair), Mr John Park, and another to meet the Glenluce heritors at the kirk "on Thursday" to settle the repair** — and directs the session to "present the same in law." Church-building administration in real time.

★ **The Galloway regiment-chaplaincy stalls.** Following img 236, **Blair's letters go unanswered: my Lord Galloway sends no reply and no one comes to the meeting; and Blair's approach to Mr Archibald Hamilton, late moderator of Wigton presbytery, to have Wigton join in "providing my Lord Galloway's regiment with a minister" draws only a hedging answer by letter.** The wartime demand for a chaplain drifts unresolved — inter-presbytery coordination proving slow. The **20 August 1651** stated meeting then opens.

- **Lowest-confidence readings (Dad):**

  - **"Mr Archibald Hamilton, late moderator in that presbytery [of Wigton]"** — the name is a best reading; he is treated as Wigton's late moderator, consistent with the img 236 referral of the regiment matter to Wigton.
  - **"school" (scoole)** for "the breeding of young children" is the priority reading and fairly secure from context (repeated twice).
  - The day-of-month of Blair's report on folio 237 is illegible; the **20 August 1651** sitting-date is secure.

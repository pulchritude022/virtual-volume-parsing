A minister who rises to moderator by April 1649 (with Mr Hew Hutcheson as clerk) and who
plays a direct hand in two of the volume's major later storylines.

## A witness for Balneil

Crosse is one of the ministers whose personal testimony helps clear **James Ross of Balneil**
of malignancy, corroborating that Balneil "expressed his dislike of the Engagement" and
acted, where he complied at all, from fear of the shire rather than genuine sympathy for the
Engager cause [img_161, entity:event/james-ross-of-balneil-s-malignancy-process]. He is also
named as the presbytery's chosen commissioner to carry copies of the Balneil case documents
forward [img_162].

## Settling Glenluce's property dispute

Alongside Mr James Blair, Crosse is deputed to settle the running glebe/manse dispute at
Glenluce, with Balneil as the counterparty [img_157,
entity:event/the-division-and-planting-of-glenluce].

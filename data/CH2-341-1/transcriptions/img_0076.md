# CH2/341/1 — Image 76 (folios 75v–76), July–Sept 1645 — the fugitive John Ferguson faces excommunication; public penances at Glenluce

Left page (folio **75a**): the discipline sweep continues — the **riddle-turner (Ross?) appointed to
give signs of repentance publicly**; a report of someone **at Douglas beginning to repent**; **William
Mc[—] in Boreland** examined; the **session of Glenluce to deal tenderly** with a party; cases
**delayed (James McCulloch, James Stuart) till Mr Alexander Turnbull returns from Edinburgh**; **Mr
George Dick reports nothing further could be proved against Agnes and James Broughan**, who enter to
give signs of repentance; and **Mr George Dick reports he had cited John Ferguson publicly three
several Sabbaths.** Right page (folio **76**): **the presbytery ordains Mr George Dick to pray for
John Ferguson the next Sabbath, and — if he still appear not — to excommunicate him**; **Power reports
[repentance/disorder] at Stoneykirk**; **John Stuart and [J.] Nobell to do public penance at Glenluce
for a scandal** (Nobell's fault found the greater); **the whole brethren report the fast was kept in
their several parishes on 21 September**; and a **new adultery case** opens (**Robert Milligan? in
Glenluce**).

## Notes & context
- **★ John Ferguson to be excommunicated.** The grim **Ferguson** case (imgs 0069–0070, 0073–0074)
  reaches its ecclesiastical climax: cited **publicly three several Sabbaths** and still absent, he is
  now **to be prayed for once more and then excommunicated by Mr George Dick** — the kirk's ultimate
  censure, cutting him off from the congregation. A clear terminal event for the Ferguson thread; note
  for the case timeline and for how excommunication was staged (repeated citation → public prayer →
  the sentence).
- **Public penance at Glenluce.** **John Stuart and [J.] Nobell** must do **public penance (sackcloth)
  at Glenluce** for a scandal, **Nobell's the graver** — the ordinary theatre of parish discipline,
  with the session left to "set the penalty." A pair of named parishioners for the Glenluce page.
- **The riddle case resolves in repentance.** The sieve-diviner (img 0075) is set to **give public
  signs of repentance** — the superstition case closing as a satisfaction/penance rather than a
  criminal prosecution, confirming the kirk treated "turning the riddle" as a scandal to be repented,
  not a capital witchcraft.
- **Stoneykirk still troubled; Ferguson-affair women repent.** **Power** is still trying to bring the
  disorderly at **Stoneykirk** to repentance (cf. img 0075); and **nothing further could be proved
  against Agnes and James Broughan** (the Ferguson-affair associates), who now enter to give signs of
  repentance — the discipline winding down for them even as Ferguson himself is cut off.
- **National fast, 21 September 1645.** The **whole brethren report the fast kept in every parish on
  21 Sept** — another of the 1645 national fasts (a plague-and-war year) observed across the bounds.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `cited publicklie thrise severall Sabbaths` = summoned by
  public proclamation on three separate Sundays; `sall excommunicate him` = shall pronounce the
  sentence of excommunication; `stand [in sackcloth] one Sabbath… received upon signes of repentance`
  = perform public penance in the place of repentance and be readmitted on evidence of contrition;
  `the fast Was kept in their severall parochines` = the appointed fast observed in each parish.

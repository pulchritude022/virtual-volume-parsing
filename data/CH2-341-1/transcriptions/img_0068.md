# CH2/341/1 — Image 68 (folios 67v–68), Dec 1644 – Jan 1645 — negotiating Power's helper; a third Power letter begins

Left page (folio **67a**): the **heritors' answer concluded** — they agree the helper's provision
(the **annualrent secured on the gentleman's heritage, not the glebe**; the **greater part of the
stipend, ~300 merks, to the helper**, from Whitsunday last), noting the burden on the parish;
**Mr James Blair declares that unless Power's paper is agreed in all points he has no commission
to deal further, only to report**; **the laird of Garthland desires an answer and promises to
provide a maintenance for a helper**; and a **petition (via the laird / Mr George Dick) for a
qualified man to be added to Stoneykirk "because of Mr Gilbert's age and infirmity"** is approved
— the laird to seek one whom the presbytery would admit. Right page (folio **68**): the heritors
"**being called in, acquiesced**"; the presbytery asks **whether Power would absolutely consent to
a helper**, and sends **Blair to speak him and bring his answer in writing**; then **Presbytery at
Stranraer, 10 January 1645** (exercise Col. 4:10; a **fast kept** per the commissioners of the
kirk; a letter for **choosing Synod commissioners**), and the reading of **a further letter from
Mr Gilbert Power** on whether he absolutely assents to a helper (a third first-person Power text,
continued on folio 69).

## Notes & context
- **The collegiate charge, negotiated in real time.** This opening is the middle of the drawn-out
  **negotiation for a colleague to the aged Power** (imgs 0066–0067): the **heritors agree the
  ~300 merks and the helper's provision** (securing the **annualrent on the gentleman's heritage,
  not the glebe**); **Blair's commission is strictly conditional** on Power's paper being met in
  every point; and **the laird of Garthland promises a maintenance and a petition to plant a
  qualified helper** is approved. The presbytery keeps pressing to know **whether Power will
  "absolutely assent"** to a helper — hence yet another Power letter. A detailed case study in how
  a **second charge / collegiate ministry** was set up around an infirm minister. See
  [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]].
- **A third first-person Power document begins.** Power writes **again** (his third sustained text
  in the volume, after the Garthland letter and his conditions-paper), answering the demand
  whether he **absolutely assents** to a helper and referring to **the commission he had given to
  Mr James Blair.** Transcribe it in full from folio 69 for his person-page — his voice recurs.
- **National rhythm continues.** A **fast kept** per the commissioners of the kirk, and a letter to
  **choose Synod/Provincial-Assembly commissioners** — the ordinary church-national business
  running under the local drama (Jan 1645).
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `acquiesced in… Desyre` = agreed to/accepted; `absolutely
  assent` = consent unconditionally; `commission… no commission to deall further` = Blair's
  delegated authority was limited; `annualrent… secured on the… heritage` = the yearly charge
  fixed on the landowner's estate (not the glebe); `Whitsunday` = the term-day (movable, late
  spring); `commissioners of the kirk` = the standing commission of the General Assembly. **Both
  pages partly stained/trimmed** — provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1644–45.

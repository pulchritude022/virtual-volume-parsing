# CH2/341/1 — Image 42 (folios 41v–42) — Mr Gilbert Power DEPOSED; the full charge-list and sentence

**The judgment completed.** Left page (folio **41a**) gives crimes **3–9 and "lastly"**,
then the **sentence**: the crimes "**deserve no less than deposition**," and the
presbytery, **"all in one voice, none excepted,"** **deposes Mr Gilbert Power from the
ministerial function in all time coming** and **declares the parish of Stoneykirk
vacant.** Right page (folio **42**): the certification (Power liable to further censure
if he contravenes; the clerk to send him a copy "that he may not pretend ignorance"); an
act on **absent ruling elders**; **the presbytery's answer to Power's appeal delivered up
(via Mr George Rutherford to Mr Hugh Henderson)**; and the **visitation of Kirkmaiden, 23
June [1643]** (Hunter McDowell of Barlockhart admitted ruling elder of Glenluce).

### Modern English
[The crimes, cont.:] **(3)** profaning the Lord's Day **by making merchandise** thereupon;
**(4)** guilty of **swearing judicially and solemnly a manifest untruth** [perjury]; **(5)**
of **carding and dicing for a long time**; **(6)** of **cloaking fornication**, committing
it in the company of a fornicatrix, though still residing in his own parish; **(7)** of
**contempt, in contravening the act of his suspension**; **(8)** of **vitiating his own
Session book, inserting an untruth in it for the better colouring of his collusion with
the person under the scandal of adultery** [Tarbret]; **(9)** of **sowing sedition among
his parishioners**; and lastly, of **profaning the Lord's Day publicly, since the first
accusation, while under suspension.**

"**These points being duly considered according to the Word of God and the constitutions
of this kirk, we think [they] deserve no less than deposition. And therefore, with advice
foresaid, we, all in one voice, none excepted, think good to depose the said Mr Gilbert
Power; and by these presents wholly depose him from the ministerial function in all time
coming, and from whatsoever he did or could pretend right unto by virtue of the said
office. And likewise, by these presents, we declare the said parish of Stoneykirk to be
vacant, with certification to** [him…]"

### Modern English
"…the said Mr Gilbert, **if he contravene this present act, will be liable to the censure
of the kirk** in such cases." And the clerk is ordained to **send Power a copy of these
presents under his hand, "that he may not pretend ignorance."**

The presbytery, considering the **prejudice from ruling elders' frequent absence**,
declares that any elder **absent without lawful excuse must make public confession before
the congregation**; if he gives no satisfactory confession, he must come before the
presbytery — his censure for a first fault; on a second, **confession before the
presbytery on a presbytery day.**

**Mr George Dick delivered up [to the] presbytery its answer to Mr Gilbert Power's
appellation**; the presbytery **gave it to Mr George Rutherford, to be delivered to Mr
Hugh Henderson.**

**Presbytery at Kirkmaiden, for the visitation of that kirk, 23 June [1643].** Absent: Mr
James Blair, Mr George Dick, and the ruling elders of Stranraer, [Inch], Portpatrick, and
Leswalt. That day **Hunter McDowell of Barlockhart gave in his commission as ruling elder
of Glenluce, which was accepted.**

## Notes & context
- **Mr Gilbert Power DEPOSED — the definitive outcome.** The presbytery, unanimously
  ("**all in one voice, none excepted**"), **deposes Power from the ministry for all time**
  and **declares Stoneykirk vacant.** This is the terminal fact of his ministerial
  biography: after a year+ of libel, suspension, appeals, and his own Session's petition,
  he is **stripped of office.** (Whether he later appealed the *deposition* to the General
  Assembly may appear in following folios.)
- **The complete charge-sheet (headline for the wiki).** Nine numbered crimes + a coda —
  a rare, itemised 1643 conviction of a parish minister:
  1. collusion in **cloaking an adulterer (Tarbret) for money**; 2. **usury**; 3. **Sabbath
  merchandise**; 4. **perjury** (a solemn judicial untruth); 5. **carding & dicing**;
  6. **cloaking fornication**; 7. **contempt** (preaching while suspended); 8. **falsifying
  his Session book** to cover the adultery-collusion; 9. **sowing sedition** among his
  parishioners; + **public Sabbath-breaking while suspended**. This list should anchor
  Power's person-page and seed glossary/event entries (usury, horning, Sabbath discipline,
  session-book falsification).
- **The appeal-answer transmitted.** The presbytery's written **answer to Power's appeal**
  (the rebuttal at imgs 0027–0028) is **handed to Mr George Rutherford to deliver to Mr
  Hugh Henderson** — i.e. sent up the chain (to the Synod/General Assembly). New names to
  track: **Mr George Rutherford**, **Mr Hugh Henderson**.
- **Routine business resumes.** An act tightening **elder attendance** (public confession
  for absentees); the **Kirkmaiden visitation (23 June)**; **Hunter McDowell of Barlockhart**
  admitted ruling elder of Glenluce. Note the **date order is non-linear** again (a 23 June
  visitation entered after the 16 July sentence) — the clerk completed the Power narrative,
  then back-filled June business (see the chronology note on [[ch2-341-1-power-case-and-cast]]).
- **Chronology:** MS again writes **"1642"** (23 June) where context is **1643**; the same
  clerk slip flagged at img_0041. Treat the sentence as **16 July 1643.**
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `hale depose` = wholly depose; `pretend right unto`
  = lay claim to; `certificatioun` = formal warning of consequences; `vitiating` =
  corrupting/falsifying; `carding and dycing` = playing at cards and dice (gambling);
  `fornicatrix` = a female fornicator; `pretend ignorance` = plead ignorance. Right-page

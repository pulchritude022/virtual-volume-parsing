# CH2/341/1 — Image 56 (folios 55v–56), Jan–Feb 1644 — testimonials, an action before the Lords of Session, and supplying Kirkmaiden

**⚠ Water-stained opening** (inner margin of folio 55a; outer edge of folio 56); the bodies are
partly legible — low confidence.

Left page (folio **55a**): routine **testimonial** business — the presbytery grants
**certificates (testimonials)**, one touching **James Boyd's process** against **Mr John
Watson, minister at Inch** (an **action of law depending before the Lords of Session**), and
another for **John Nisbet(?), spouse of Elizabeth [—]**, concerning debts; then **Presbytery at
[Stranraer], the last of January 1644** (Hew Hutcheson exercises). Right page (folio **56**):
**pulpit supply for vacant Kirkmaiden** — **Livingstone, Blair (now of Kirkcolm), and John
Dick** preach there in rotation; **Presbytery at Stranraer, 28 February 1644** (John Dick
exercises on Col. 4:2; George Dick and the elders of Glenluce, Kirkcolm, Portmontgomery
absent); a **James Wright(?) summoned a second time**; and further **Kirkmaiden supply** (Blair
10 March, John Dick a fortnight later).

## Notes & context
- **Testimonials — the kirk as a certifying authority.** Two nice examples of the **presbytery
  issuing testimonials (character/status certificates)**: one for **James Boyd**, whose
  **action of law was depending before the Lords of Session** (Scotland's supreme civil court,
  Edinburgh) and who was "**unable to travel**"; another for **John Nisbet(?)** touching
  **debts**, also toward the Lords of Session. Shows how a church court's paperwork underpinned
  people's **civil** affairs — genealogically valuable (real names, a marriage, a court suit).
  New names: **James Boyd**, **Mr John Watson (minister at Inch)**, **John Nisbet / Elizabeth
  [—]**.
- **Vacancy juggling: now Kirkmaiden.** With **Kirkcolm just planted (Blair)**, the presbytery
  rotates **supply preaching at vacant Kirkmaiden** — Livingstone, Blair, and John Dick take
  turns (late Jan – March 1644). **Glenluce** is still being planted too (img 0055). A useful
  snapshot of a presbytery managing several **vacancies** at once.
- **Blair already at work as a presbyter.** Freshly admitted to Kirkcolm (3 Jan 1644, img
  0055), **Mr James Blair** immediately takes his turn supplying Kirkmaiden and sitting in
  presbytery — confirming his settled status.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `testimoniall` = a certificate of character/status/facts
  issued by the kirk; `the Lords of Sessioun` = the College of Justice (supreme civil court,
  Edinburgh); `actioun of law depending` = a lawsuit in progress; `unhabile to travell` =
  unable to travel; `processe` = a formal cause/case; `pro 2do` = a second summons; `made [the
  exercise]` = delivered the set sermon. **Both margins water-stained** — several readings
  provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Dates 1644.

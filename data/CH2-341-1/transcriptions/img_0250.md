## Notes & context
★★ **The Resolutioner supplication carried on; Garthland surfaces again.** The presbytery's fight over the late General Assembly (imgs 247–248) continues at the **25 February 1652** sitting: **the moderator and Mr John Park "represent the reasons" of the presbytery's supplication to the Synod, made "upon the consideration of the proceedings of the late General Assembly"** — the local Resolutioner campaign pressing upward. And the old **Laird of Garthland** — the principal Rhins Engager brought to public repentance in 1650–51 (imgs 220, 225, 231) — **resurfaces: Mr James Blair, appointed to speak with him, reports his meeting;** Garthland to be examined and to declare himself.

★ **Mr John Dick in Ireland; the Dunbar captives accounted; the fabric and the fugitives.** A cluster of ongoing threads: **Mr John Dick is "not returned from Ireland,"** so Lawrie supplies the exercise; **David Kennedy renders account "to the use of the captives [prisoners] … in England"** (the Dunbar relief, imgs 243, 249); the **old-Glenluce kirk fabric** is to be partly repaired with the gentry called in; and **Alexr McDowall, the adultery-suspect, is "again in Ireland"** and still will not compear (imgs 246–249) — the fugitive-to-Ireland pattern once more.

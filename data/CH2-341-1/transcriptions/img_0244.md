## Notes & context
★★★ **The scandal behind Robert Campbell's "refusal of the Bible."** Two threads that ran separately now knit together: **Robert Campbell "made the refusal of the Bible"** (img 243) — refusing to purge himself by oath — and here we see why: he and **Isobell Matronnall** are a **fornication/adultery scandal pair** ("her scandal with Robert Campbell"). She, tried on the process led by the Stranraer session, is **ordered to public confession the next Lord's day;** he, refusing the oath, is handed to **Mr John Crosse** to deal with, with **Blair, Dick, and Crosse** appointed to confer. The Engager-suspect's long "disobedience" (imgs 221–226, 242–243) is thus bound up with a carnal scandal he will not swear himself clear of. *(If this Robert Campbell is the malignancy-suspect, the two strands merge; if a namesake, note the ambiguity — verify.)*

★★ **The presbytery must judge "the late General Assembly."** A politically loaded administrative moment: the moderator **represents that the Synod has recommended a business "anent the late General Assembly"** on which the presbytery must give judgment, and **a special conference-day is set for Tuesday the 25th [November], "the members of the presbytery only," to consider it before the next meeting.** In the winter of 1651 — after the Dundee Assembly was broken up by Cromwell's forces and the Resolutioner–Protester validity dispute raged — the little presbytery is being drawn into judging the lawfulness of the national Assembly itself.

★ **Discipline routine.** **John McDowall of Stranraer** is set to public repentance for disobedience; **Mr John Lindsay** is named to a commission "within the bounds"; and Kirkmaiden-session and other referrals are handled.

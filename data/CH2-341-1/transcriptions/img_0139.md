# CH2/341/1 — Image 139 (folios 138v–139)

Continuation of the **7 June 1648** sitting and a new **Stranraer sitting of 28
June 1648**. Left page (foliated **138a** = folio 138 verso): the commissioner
**John [Kennedy?] of [Barsell?]** fails to compear on the appointed day, so the
**Islandmagee/Dick transportation business is deserted** — and, the presbytery
holding that transporting **Mr John Dick (minister of Inch)** was "not in their
power," ★ the commissioner **appeals the matter to the next General Assembly** and
takes an instrument. Then ★ the **paternity probation**: **Jonet [—], sister to
Marion [—]**, is examined under oath and deposes that Marion first named **Robert
McColm** (servant to Airieholland) as the child's father, then said the father was
**a trooper** (the story keeps shifting — earlier Marion had named James McMillan).
Then an **adultery case (Inch)**: **Robert McIlhaffie** and **Isobell McMillan**,
both confessing, enjoined sackcloth penance at Stranraer. The right page (folio
139): preaching and correspondence appointments — ★ **Mr Alexander Turnbull to keep
correspondence with the presbytery in Ireland** — and the **28 June sitting**
(exercise by Blair and Turnbull; absents; the paternity witness delayed again;
Turnbull reports he has written to the kirk of Ireland).

### Modern English
[Dick transportation, continued:] The said **John [Kennedy?]** compeared not,
although a day was peremptorily appointed and himself desired to receive an
answer; so, as they convened, **the business was deserted**. And next, the matter
contained in his supplication — to transport **Mr John Dick, present minister at
Inch**, from his charge there to the parish of **Islandmagee in Ireland** — was
not in [the presbytery's] power. Whereupon the said John [Kennedy] gave in a paper
**appealing from the kirk to the next General Assembly**, whereof an instrument was
taken under the clerk's hand, which was granted.

The same day, **Jonet [—], sister to Marion [—]**, being called, compeared and,
being judicially sworn, deponed that she was not present with the said Marion at
the time of the delivery, but came to see her about two days after; and that, as
she enquired, the child was born before its time — not a quarter of a year before
the [full] time. She declared also that, when she questioned her sister
concerning the father of the child, **Marion at first said Robert McColm, servant
to Airieholland, was the child's father, and afterwards said the child's father was
a trooper** who came [—] out of their [—]. The said Jonet, being asked whom she
thought to be the child's father, answered it was uncertain...

The same day, **Robert McIlhaffie, parishioner of Inch**, adulterer with **Isobell
McMillan**, referred by the session of Inch, being called, compeared and confessed
the adultery with the said Isobell, and was again sharply rebuked and appointed to
show signs of repentance in the kirk of Stranraer until the session be satisfied.
**Isobell McMillan**, adulteress with the said Robert, being called, compeared...
(continues).

## Notes & context
- ★ **Islandmagee/Dick transportation goes to the General Assembly too.** With the
  commissioner **John [Kennedy?]** failing to appear and the presbytery declaring
  the transportation "not in their power," the case is **appealed to the General
  Assembly** (like the Ardwell matter, img 138) — so two of this presbytery's
  disputes head to the July 1648 Assembly.
- ★ **Paternity probation — shifting testimony.** The sworn deposition of **Jonet
  [—]** exposes why the case has dragged: her sister **Marion** gave different
  fathers at different times — first **Robert McColm** (servant to **Airieholland**),
  then **a trooper**, having earlier (imgs 128–131) named **James McMillan**. The
  child was born prematurely. Classic difficult paternity case; the "woman present
  with Marion" is still being chased for corroboration.
- ★ **Ongoing Scotland–Ulster church correspondence.** **Mr Alexander Turnbull** is
  formally appointed to **keep correspondence with the presbytery in Ireland**, and
  reports he has written — concrete evidence of the standing tie between the
  Stranraer presbytery and the Ulster Scots presbytery across the North Channel.
- **Adultery case (Inch):** **Robert McIlhaffie** and **Isobell McMillan**, both
  confessing, sackcloth penance at Stranraer.

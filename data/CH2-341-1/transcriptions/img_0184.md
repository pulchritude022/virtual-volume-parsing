### Modern English
**Presbytery at Stranraer, 5 March [1650].** Absent: Dick, Crosse. Currie made, Bell added, approven.

**Mr John Park reported he had used diligence in trying the abuses touching the Covenant, and had found some scandal carried on by Capt Stewart**, [now] remaining in the parish of Mochrum; the presbytery orders Park **to write to Mr William MacKie to cause Capt Stewart compear to satisfy for his scandalous behaviour.**

**Park and the Provost reported they spoke with the Committee about restraining the malcontents at Stranraer, and [the Committee] promised a speedy course.** **Mr John Crosse to represent to the Committee an abuse done by some in the parish of Glenluce — robbing a poor Englishman — and desire redress.**

Major John McDowall would not yet compear; ordered to meet Turnbull and Park before next presbytery. Turnbull and Currie to confer with the **Laird of Garthland about his going to the Committee of the Kirk.** A letter from the Commission of the Kirk read.

**The Session of Stranraer reported that a quarrel was like to break out between George Montgomerie and [Chappell] — for a combat [duel]; the presbytery orders them both summoned.**

### Modern English
**Mr John Park reported that the Session of Stranraer had inhibited an inhabitant of the town from conversing (within a mile or two) with one coming from Ireland under the scandal of malignancy.**

**Robert Campbell to be summoned to answer concerning his son John Campbell, a known malignant still remaining in his house.**

Blair preached at Mochrum; Bell to preach at New Glenluce, Park at Inch. **The act of Parliament anent the poor to be put in execution by all the brethren.** Next presbytery at Stoneykirk, 27 March, for visitation.

**Presbytery at Stoneykirk, for visitation, 27 March 1650.** Mr James Lawrie preached (1 Thess 4:3), approven. **The brethren reported that the [session] had read the Declaration in answer to a scandalous pamphlet set out by James Grahame [the Marquess of Montrose].** The brethren to keep the fast on the first Lord's day, per the Commission's letter. The moderator produced a declaration subscribed by John Guthrie for all the brethren.

Park reported on Capt Stewart (Mr William MacKie promising to make him answer). Mr John Scott went to old Glenluce to attend the Committee day as appointed, but the Committee members did not meet.

## Notes & context

This puts the sitting on the very eve of Montrose's defeat at **Carbisdale (27 April 1650)** and execution (21 May 1650) — the Stranraer presbytery reading the Kirk's counter-declaration from its pulpits.

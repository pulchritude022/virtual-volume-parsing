Seat of the presbytery itself, and by far the volume's most-mentioned place — almost every
sederunt is headed "at Stranraer" regardless of which parish's business is actually being
transacted, so its mention count reflects administrative venue more than narrative
importance.

## The presbytery's home parish

Stranraer's own minister for nearly the whole volume is **Mr John Livingstone**, whose
repeated absences (Ireland, Edinburgh, fending off Glasgow's and Ancrum's attempts to poach
him) are covered on his own page. A Stranraer child's baptism, delayed "in respect of Mr
Gilbert Power's infirmity," is one of the small human details the register preserves from
ordinary parish life alongside the bigger cases [entity:person/mr-gilbert-power].

# CH2/341/1 — Image 35 (folios 34v–35), 25 May 1642 — Power refuses to compear; Gordon's witnesses examined

Left page (folio **34a**) closes **Power's protestation** (subscribed *Mr Gilbert
Power*); the presbytery resolves to **proceed notwithstanding**. Sitting near Power's
dwelling, it sends **Mr John Dick and Mr Hugh Hutcheson** to require him to appear for
the swearing of witnesses on **James Gordon's accusation**; **Power refuses**, and the
officer **calls him thrice at the church door** — he compears not. Right page (folio
**35**): the witnesses are sworn and examined **in his absence**. The first, **Uchtred
McCulloch**, deposes on the **Sabbath-alehouse** episode (Power took **silver for
advising on a Portspittal man's daughter's marriage**, "confessed he took it"),
**preaching after suspension**, **Mr James Adamson subscribing the covenant**, and
Power **labouring land beyond his glebe**.

### Modern English
[Power's protestation, concl.:] …of proceeding, appealing thereto (without prejudice to
his former appellations) to the **General Assembly**, and for the reasons in his
appellations, which he repeats, protesting (per his former protestations) for **liberty
to enlarge and add** as he finds expedient. **Subscribed: Mr Gilbert Power.**

The presbytery holds that, **notwithstanding Power's protestation, it ought — as a
"privileged presbytery" — to proceed.** **John McDowell, the presbytery officer**, called
and asked whether he had summoned Power to Stoneykirk for 25 May, declared he did. The
**clerk** declared he had **sent Power a copy of James Gordon's accusation** per the
presbytery's order. The presbytery, **sitting near Power's dwelling**, sent **Mr John
Dick and Mr Hugh Hutcheson** to require Power, in its name, to appear **for the hearing
of witnesses to be sworn on James Gordon in Culgroat's accusation**; **Power refused to
appear.** The presbytery again ordered its officer **to the church door, to call Power
thrice** — first to come in and object to the witnesses if he had anything; **Power
compeared not**; secondly, called to come in and hear the witnesses [sworn…]

### Modern English
[The witnesses] sworn — Power compeared not. The presbytery **read the accusation to the
witnesses**; nothing being objected against them, they were sworn. **The witnesses:
Uchtred McCulloch, [Hugh] Wallace, Thomas McNaught, Alexander Conning[?], Mr Alexander
Turnbull.**

**Uchtred McCulloch, sworn**, asked whether either party had given him "sinistrous
counsel," answered no. Posed on the **Sabbath-breaking**, he declared he would accuse Mr
Gilbert of being **in an alehouse with the tenants of Portspittal on the Lord's day**;
Mr Gilbert answered that **one of the Portspittal men wanted his advice about his
daughter's marriage**, and they **drank a pint or quart of ale**, and some offered him
**silver** — which Mr Gilbert said he was unwilling to take till another day, but the man
said he might not see him for half a year again, and **Mr Gilbert confessed he took it**;
Mr Gilbert said he **neither counted nor reckoned accounts, nor had pen or ink to write
discharges**. Posed whether Power **preached after his suspension**, McCulloch declared
he heard him **oftentimes in his "seat house."** He declared that **Mr James Adamson rose,
made a confession, and subscribed the Covenant** — "but whether he was required thereto I
know not." Posed whether Power **laboured more land than his glebe**, he declared Power
**set his forebears [servants?] to labour a parcel of ground beside his glebe.**

## Notes & context
- **Proceeding in absentia — the drama at the church door.** With Power **refusing to
  appear** (twice sent for, then called thrice at the church door), the presbytery
  invokes its status as a **"privileged presbytery"** and **examines Gordon's witnesses
  without him.** This is exactly the "condemned unheard / witnesses led in my absence"
  grievance that Power's 1643 appeal (imgs 0023–0025) turns on — here caught in the act,
  from the court's side.
- **The Sabbath-alehouse charge, with Power's own defence.** The recurring
  "Sabbath-drinking with the Portspittal tenants" charge (French, Ellaster, Gordon) gets
  its fullest telling: Power says he was **giving marriage advice**, took **silver
  pressed on him** only reluctantly, and kept no accounts — his innocent gloss on what the
  accusers call Sabbath-breaking and money-dealing. Good "both sides" material.
- **Mr James Adamson and the Covenant.** The witness recalls **Mr James Adamson rose,
  confessed, and subscribed the Covenant** — very likely the **conform minister who fled
  Ireland** whom Power was charged with receiving (cf. the Ellaster charge, img_0019;
  memory: Power's brother-in-law). A probable name for that refugee minister — flag to
  confirm.
- **New witnesses / people.** **Uchtred McCulloch** (a *second* Uchtred — a McCulloch
  witness, distinct from the accuser **Uchtred McDowell of French**), **Hugh Wallace**,
  **Thomas McNaught**, **Alexander Conning**, with **Mr Alexander Turnbull** (moderator)
  also a witness. And Power's **"seat house"** (a room in his house used for preaching
  while barred from the pulpit).
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `priviledged presb.` = a presbytery empowered to
  proceed (not bound to halt for the appeal); `sinistrous councell` = biased/corrupt
  prompting (the standard impartiality question); `siller` = silver/money; `comptit` =
  reckoned accounts; `seat house` = a private room used for worship; `forbears` here
  probably = household folk/servants (or literally forebears — uncertain). Capital **W**
  for *w/wh*. Dates Old Style. `y`+superscript → *th-*.

**★ the same day compeared [John and Robert Mc[—], — Torrid(?), Humphrey [Craven](?)], Jean Hutcheson, [and

Estates, and after the reading of the same, [appointed the moderator to write a letter]** [enclosing it].

not nor satisfies not, [but is] approved [reported] by the session, be [brought] before the presbytery to

### Modern English
**The moderator to write a letter to Mr [—], that the petition be given in to the Committee of Estates.**
**★ The presbytery, considering that many [scandalous persons] by long abode and absence — through not being
recovered, whereby the discipline of the kirk is oft contemned — do therefore appoint that whatsoever person
cited before any kirk session, who compears not nor satisfies not (but is [reported] by the session), be
brought before the presbytery to advise, and be censured for their disobedience; and that whosoever, cited
before the presbytery, compears not nor satisfies not, and are not otherwise approved by the presbytery, be
presently proceeded against by public censures — from the pulpit, admonitions, prayers; and if they, being
excommunicated, continue obstinate and do not compear and submit themselves, but refuse to be censured, then
for their disobedience the act to be intimated from the pulpit in every kirk as the presbytery directs.**
**The brethren reported that the thanksgiving was kept upon the 25 day of September.** **Master James Blair
appointed to give in his report.** The exercise assigned the next presbytery day after the Synod; the
presbytery day to be set when the brethren meet at the Synod. **The privy trial — the ministers and ruling
elders severally tried and inquired, and were approved.** *Mr Hew Hutcheson, clerk.*

## Notes & context
- **★★ The Ardwell scandal goes to evidence — and up to the Synod.** The adultery-slander cause (McCulloch of
  Ardwell vs the Lady Freugh, imgs 0113–0119) now reaches its investigative climax: **the Laird of Ardwell's
  own tenants and cottars are examined "upon the presumptions of the scandal lying upon" him.** Strikingly,
  **some deposed they "never had any suspicion of the scandal till they were first informed — and that by the
  Laird himself,"** a telling detail (the Laird was apparently the one publicising it). Unable to resolve it,
  **the presbytery refers the whole business and its presumptions to the Synod for its opinion**, along with
  the bill against the Lady Freugh — the cause moving up the hierarchy again (cf. img 0118's GA referral).
- **★ A petition to the Committee of Estates.** The court **subscribes a petition to the Committee of
  Estates** (the executive committee of the Covenanter Parliament governing Scotland in this period), the
  moderator to write a covering letter — evidence of the presbytery's direct dealings with the civil
  government (very likely over the Glenluce stipend enforcement, the war-widows relief, or a related
  temporal matter).
- **★ A standing discipline act — the escalation ladder.** A general procedural act worth flagging for the
  glossary: it sets out the **full escalation for the contumacious** — from **kirk session** to **presbytery**,
  to **public censure from the pulpit (admonitions, prayers)**, to **excommunication**, and finally to
  **intimation of the act from every pulpit** if they remain obstinate. A compact statement of Covenanter
  discipline procedure.
- **Year-end housekeeping.** The **thanksgiving kept 25 September** (appointed at img 0119); the **exercise
  and next meeting deferred to after the Synod**; and the **privy trial** of the brethren, closing the
  presbytery-year — **subscribed by Hutcheson as clerk** (a formal sign-off).
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `tenants and cottars of ye Laird of Ardwell… examined upon
  presumptiones of ye scandall` = examining dependants as witnesses to a rumour; `made manifest to ye Synode,
  and yair opinioun craved` = referring a difficult cause to the superior court; `subscribed a petition to ye
  Committee of Estates` = petitioning the civil government; `proceeded against by publick censures out of
  pulpits… excommunicated… intimated out of pulpit in every kirk` = the graded discipline of the obstinate;
  `The privie tryall… severalie tryed… Wer approved… Hutchesone, clerk` = the year-end mutual trial of the
  brethren, signed by the clerk. **Both pages heavily water-stained** — the witness surnames and the object
  of the Estates petition are provisional. Capital **W** for *w/wh*. Year **1647**.

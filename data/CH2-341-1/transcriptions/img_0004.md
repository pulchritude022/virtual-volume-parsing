## Notes & context

Left page (marked **"3a"**, i.e. verso of folio 3) is heavily foxed and faded:
it finishes the previous sederunt's business (a trial-sermon approbation, a
disobedience citation, and a fugitives case) and adjourns; the foot carries a
**struck-through** false start of the next sitting. Right page (folio **4**)
firms up: the tail of a witness examination of **Mr Gilbert Power** over
barn-breaking, oxen and a bond, then Power's **supplication** to the presbytery
touching the libel raised against him by **McDowell of French**.
- **Case identified — minister under discipline.** Folio 4 reveals that the
  **"Mr Gilbert"** who appeared as a creditor in the McDowall-of-Lochans
  deposition (img_0002) is **Mr Gilbert Power, minister of Stoneykirk (Stanikirk)**.
  He is here the *accused*: a libel was raised against him by **McDowell of
  French** and adherents, remitted from the **Provincial Assembly** to the
  Presbytery of Stranraer for trial. Charges touch **barn-breaking**, disputed
  **oxen**, a **bond of 500 merks**, and the writing of **Peter McCulloch's
  testament** — connecting directly to img_0002 (loan/annualrent) and img_0003
  ("the false writing of Peter McCulloch's testament", 500 merks, Mr Alexander
  Turnbull sent to the Edinburgh commissioners). This looks like the running
  scandal of a money-lending minister accused of a forged testament.
- **Power's supplication** asks for two things: (1) a certified copy of the
  answers given to the Provincial Assembly on his appeal, and (2) re-examination
  of specific witnesses he had objected to — **Mr John Porterfield, Michael
  McIlrae, Robert McIl[?]**.
- **Left page (folio 3v) business** before this: a **trial-sermon approbation**
  of **Mr John Dick** on Job 1:13–19 (approved despite 8–9 elders absent); a
  **disobedience citation** of [John?] Webe[?] of Calmpether[?] (failed to
  compear → summoned *pro secundo*); and a **fugitives-between-parishes** order
  concerning **John McIlroy** and **John McCahen[?]** (ministers to give public
  warning against harbouring them, on pain of kirk censure). Then adjournment to
  Stoneykirk, 17 Oct 1642, and a **struck-through** false start of the next
  sederunt (naming Mr Alexander Turnbull, Mr John Livingston, Mr John Dick, Mr
  George Dick).
- **Palaeography applied:** `8ber` = October (*8bris*); `pro 2do` = *pro secundo*
  (a second time); `comperit` = appeared in court; `posed`/`inquyred` = questioned;

  remaining; `viz.` = *videlicet*; `yow` = you. `y`+superscript expanded to *th-*.

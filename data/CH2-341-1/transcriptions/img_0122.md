### Modern English
**★ Mr John Crichton reported that Alexander McIlwrick — enjoined two several sackcloths in the guilt of
adultery, and giving signs of his repentance in the New kirk of Glenluce — is now gone to Ireland; the
presbytery appointed the moderator to write to the presbytery of Ireland concerning him, that they would
cause his return to this kingdom.** **★ The said day a letter was presented by Master John Livingstone from
the Commission of the Kirk, subscribed by Mr Robert Douglas, moderator of the same, appointing that — seeing
it hath pleased the Lord to call James Murray out of this life — the brethren of the presbytery of Stranraer

owing to the said James Murray by the next presbytery day.** **★★ There was also read another letter,
delivered by Mr John Livingstone from the Commission of the Kirk, declaring that the Commission, being

## Notes & context
- **★★ The Kirk mobilises — "the army might not be disbanded."** The headline: a **remonstrance from the
  Commission of the Kirk** (delivered by Livingstone, subscribed by its moderator **Mr Robert Douglas**),
  read into the minutes, urging the **Committee of Estates not to disband the army**, warning of the
  "imminent danger of religion," calling for **prayers for the persecuted in England "for the truth and our
  Covenant,"** for the people to **fund the army's subsistence**, and for the presbytery's **diligence in
  trying and censuring Malignants.** This is the Rhins backwater plugged straight into the national crisis of
  late 1647 — the maneuvering between the Kirk party and the "Engagers" that would erupt in the 1648
  Engagement and the Second Civil War. A significant political-historical anchor (and a firm date-stamp:
  **Robert Douglas** was moderator of the Commission at this juncture). The bundle of enclosed papers
  (**two remonstrances, a proposition, directions for government, nine reasons for the thanksgiving/fast,
  twelve directions**) shows how the Commission drove a coordinated pulpit campaign.
- **★ Adultery and flight to Ulster — again.** **Alexander McIlwrick**, doing **"two several sackcloths"**
  (double public penance) for adultery **in Crichton's New kirk of Glenluce**, has **fled to Ireland** — the
  recurring cross-channel escape route (cf. Adair, McCredie, the McColby women). The presbytery writes to the
  **presbytery of Ireland** to have him sent back. This names the penitent left as "John Mc[—]" at img 0119.
- **★ The Ardwell case — hunting the witness in Ireland.** The Synod, consulted (img 0121), sends the case
  back with orders to **find more presumptions**: the presbytery will write to Ireland to **search for Jean
  [—]** (the alleged partner, the "special witness"), and — pointedly — tells **the Laird of Ardwell himself
  to fetch her** to clear his own name, "if she be free of the fact." The whole affair still turns on a woman
  the court cannot reach across the North Channel.
- **The late James Murray.** A letter from the Commission notes **James Murray "called out of this life"** and
  directs the brethren to **bring in what they owe him** — apparently settling accounts with a deceased agent
  or supplier of the Kirk (the exact relationship is unstated).
- **Housekeeping.** The **post-Synod election** (Blair moderator, John Turnbull clerk); the **discipline act
  of img 0121 ordered read in every parish** (vacant parishes included); and the **October fast confirmed
  kept**.
- **Palaeography / language applied:** `Wer advised to use yr farder diligence for finding out mae
  presumptiones` = the Synod ordering more evidence-gathering; `two severall sackcloths… is now gone to
  Ireland… caus his returne to yis kingdome` = a double-penance defaulter fled abroad; `a remonstrance to ye
  Committee of Estates… ye armie… myt not be disbanded… trying and censuring Malignants` = the Kirk's
  political intervention of late 1647; `nyne reasons… of ye thanksgeving and fast, and twelve directions` =
  the standard bundle of Commission-issued instructions. Firmer hand than the preceding openings; the woman's
  surname, James Murray's role, and a few phrases in the remonstrance remain provisional. Capital **W** for
  *w/wh*. Year **1647**.
